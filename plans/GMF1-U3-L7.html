Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuing group) · 60 min · Third unit test of the year: calm, model-first, no competition · desks + side table
Week 23.11–29.11 · Lesson 24L7 Test~12 pupils · desks apart🦸 Phonics Hero ур.20b · Magic E: a-e

Verify them against the PDF one more time and cue the audio before pupils arrive — no fumbling with the CD on test day.
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. Assess Unit 3 language — parts of the body (head, shoulders, arms, hands, legs, feet, eyes, ears, nose, mouth · toes), describing people with big / small and hair & eyes (long / short / curly / straight hair · blue / brown eyes), and have got (“I've got a big nose”, “He's/She's got long hair”, “The clown has got small ears”) in the circus context — with the GMF1 Unit 3 Standard Level Test (Listening ×2 · Reading · Writing · Reading & Writing · Speaking), in a calm, confidence-first atmosphere.
  • Subsidiary. Phonics Hero ур.20 (b) закрепление, calm — Magic E a-e: read the word grid, a short confidence dictation (cake, name), story «Торт и змея» goes home; tricky words could, should; exam micro-skills: work alone, listen twice, colour carefully, check your page.
  • Personal. Model-first: show every task type before the paper is out; no competitive games today; keep voice low and pace unhurried; no child ever sits with nothing to do (quiet “funny clown” poster during Speaking turns). It's the third real test of the year → keep it warm, protect confidence.
By the end, pupils can…
  • complete all written parts of the Unit 3 test independently;
  • answer the Speaking questions one-to-one (What's this? · Has he/she got…? · big/small · hair & eyes);
  • read a-e words (cake · game · snake) and write cake, name from dictation;
  • leave smiling — the test is a “show what I know” day, not a scary day.

Concept checks (ICQs) · Assumptions
Before the paper: Do we work alone or with a friend? → alone! Is it OK to not know one? → yes — skip it, come back!
Listening: How many times do we listen? → two times — first just listen, then number / colour.

Assumptions: all task types rehearsed in L6 (review + AB p. 34) and via the unit trainer at home; children write by copying / completing from a model; they have crayons and know the colours from Unit 1. This is their third “real” test → calmer than U1, but still keep breathing ritual + model-first + Speaky cheering, not judging.

Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — arm low all lesson = whisper day.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
Breathing — smell the flower 🌸 → blow the candle 🕯 ×3.
Differentiation
  • Support (needs more). Seat these children within arm's reach; read every instruction aloud again right beside them, point to the worked example (Ex 4 “two eyes” is done for them; Ex 5 gives the first letter), and give extra time — hold the next listening item until their pencil/crayon moves. Praise effort, not speed.
  • Extension (fast finishers). After the poster: a bonus “My funny monster” poster (draw + label 3 body parts, add a “He's got a big nose” caption) or a page in the class picture book; still buzzing → the calm Jenga ritual below. Never “more test”, always create + apply.
Self-evaluation (thinking)

At the very end each child evaluates their own effort with a traffic-light card — 🟢 easy · 🟡 okay · 🔴 tricky:

Show me your light. What was EASY today? What was a bit TRICKY? 2–3 children name one thing each. No right/wrong — it tells YOU what to fold into the Unit 4 warm-ups.

Calm, non-competitive Jenga ritual (optional buffer): a fast finisher gently pulls one block → answers ONE Unit 3 question (What's this? / Has he got big or small eyes?) → done. No tower race, no losers.

Teacher
Slow, smiling: See? You know EVERY one. Easy-peasy — now let's show it on paper. Then close the board and hand out the test.

Confidence read (тетрадь стр.56 №1 «Читаем слова»). Recall the magic wand: The e is silent — a says its NAME! Read the word grid together, all show 🖐 on each: cake · game · whale · snake · late · plate. Calm and choral — success first, test after.

Gap-fill — стр.56 №2 (quick, together). Picture up (cake · whale · snake · game) — Which letter is missing? — a! And the magic e is already there! All write the a in their тетрадь.

Mini-dictation — стр.56 №3 (words they CAN write). Say the word once — cake… — all segment on fingers → write → Show me! → reveal, self-check. Words: cake, name (add game, late only if the class is calm and quick).

Слова-молнии + tricky. Strip made · came · ate (стр.56 №4) — read for speed, there and back. Flowers could · should: Tricky words — we just know them! Choral ×2 + air-write. Praise every board: Super writing! You are READY. (The story «Торт и змея» стр.57 is tonight's home read-aloud — no time pressure today.)

12–20′
Model-first revision
at the board

Demo every test task type — ONE example each, teacher first. ① Listen & number: hold up a body card — Listen: hands… find the hands… write 1. ② Listen & colour: The girl has got brown hair. Colour the hair brown — like this. (colour one thing on the board). ③ Read & match: read a sentence — “I've got a big nose.” Which clown — Bill or Bob? Draw a line. ④ Write from the word box: two eyes — copy the words, letter by letter. ⑤ Complete the word: The clown has got s… mall ears. Small — write m-a-l-l.

▶ RallyCoach warm pairs (no points, just practice): partner A points to a body part on a flashcard (or on themselves) and says He's got a big nose! / She's got long hair!; partner B coaches — Yes! / Try again! — then swap. 2 minutes, quiet voices.

Test rules + ICQs. Alone or with a friend? — Alone! Listen how many times? — Two! Don't know? — Skip and come back! Finished? — Clown poster on your desk, whisper-quiet.

23–43′
UNIT 3 TEST — written parts
desks

Read every rubric aloud. They are emergent readers — before EACH exercise, read the instruction to the whole class and point to the example: Exercise 1 — listen and number. Like this. Re-read privately beside any child who needs it.

Listening (paced together). Ex. 1 Listen and number the body parts — hands, head, arms, foot/toes, feet, legs (Track 09) · ex. 2 Listen and colour the girl's and boy's hair & eyes (Track 10) — play each track TWICE: first listen, pencils/crayons down; second time number / colour. Watch the room, pause between items.

Reading → Writing → Reading & Writing (own pace). Ex. 3 look & read, match the “I've got…” sentences to clown Bill or Bob · ex. 4 look & write the body words from the box (two arms · ten toes · two legs · one head · two eyes · two feet — “two eyes” is done) · ex. 5 read & write the missing describing word (The clown has got s___ ears → small; b___ eyes → big…). Circulate silently; encourage with a nod, never with answers: Take your time. You're doing great.

Countdown at the end: Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down. Collect written pages (keep the Speaking picture on p. 3).
