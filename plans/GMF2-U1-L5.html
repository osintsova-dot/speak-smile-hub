Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the ai/ay tongue twister («Snails play in the rain all day»), talk about British Sports Day races, and read & perform the poem «Ball!» — three mini-blocks, one sporty thread.
  • Subsidiary. Textbook phonics only (по учебнику): notice ai/ay = same sound /eɪ/, two spellings; listening — match days ↔ sports (CD1 23); culture comparison — Think about your culture; literacy — text type a poem, rhythm & performance.
  • Personal. Three blocks — keep transitions crisp (song/сhant bridges); the tongue twister is play, not phonics drilling.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tongue twister fast ×3;
  • name 2–3 Sports Day races and compare with our school;
  • perform a verse of the poem with actions.

Form
ai (inside a word: rain, snail) / ay (at the end: play, day) — same sound /eɪ/. Imperatives in the poem: Catch me!
Pron. watch
/eɪ/ long and clean in all four twister words; race /reɪs/; poem rhythm — beat on BALL.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“rain and play — same sound? Same letters?” → same sound /eɪ/, different letters!
“Sports Day — is it a normal lesson or a holiday at school?” → a fun sports holiday.

Assumptions: Sports, actions and days are active (L1–L4). Sound-letter awareness from GMF1 phonics is enough to enjoy ai/ay noticing — строго по учебнику, без отдельной фонопрограммы. Pupils can read the short poem with support. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Toby's tongue twister (act. 1, CD1 22). PB p.13: Snails play in the rain all day! Listen, then the speed ladder: slow → fast → whisper. Pairs test each other ×3.

Notice ai/ay. Board: s n ai l · r ai n | pl ay · d ay. Same sound! Where does AY like to stand? At the END! Sort 4 more word cards as a class (train, May, say, paint) — every child points left/right before each card is placed.

Listen & match (act. 2, CD1 23). Days ↔ sports; model item 1, pairs compare, class check.

Talk Partners game (act. 3). Beth goes swimming on Wednesday! — partner finds the cell in the book grid. Swap roles.

💪 Support: twister at half speed is a win. ⭐ Extension: invent one more ai/ay word for the board.

Block 3 · Literacy · poem «Ball!»
carpet → desks

Before you read. Cover of PB p.15: What balls can you see? Big? Small?

Listen & read (CD1 26). Follow with a finger. Second read chorally with the beat — clap on BALL!

Perform it. Verse 1 whisper, verse 2 GIANT voice, verse 3 robot — whole class, then two volunteer groups pick their style and perform.

Write your own (notebooks, AB p.12 format). Frame: ___ ball, ___ ball, ___ ball, any ball! Catch me! ___ me! ___ me! Pairs fill with sports + actions from the unit; 2 pairs read theirs aloud.

💪 Support: choose words from the board bank. ⭐ Extension: add a 4th line that rhymes.
