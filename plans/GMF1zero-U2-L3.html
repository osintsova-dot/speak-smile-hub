Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., first-year beginners · линия с нуля) · 60 min · Story: listen → follow → act out (drama) · carpet + desks
Week 23.11–29.11 · Занятие 24✨ STORY~12 pupils · trios for dramaЛиния с нуля🦸 Phonics Hero ур.18b · ue·er·ar
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow and understand the story “The slow scooter” (Pupil's Book pp. 20–21, CD1 33) and act it out using key story language: “My scooter is slow/fast.”, “The wheels are small/big.”, “What is it? — It's a motor!”, “Well done! Thank you!”.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — toys + “What is it? / Is it a…? — Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.” (L1–L2) — woven into the story work (guess Beth's present, question the mystery motor); phonics — Phonics Hero ур.18 (b) закрепление: ue·er·ar (match → dictation → tricky so, be → story «Звезда над фермой»; the story's fast CAR = ar!); values: saying thank you; preview adjectives (slow/fast, small/big) for Lesson 4.
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (gesture responses, prediction); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a trio at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 1-min showcase.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (order the frames, answer with gestures);
  • say the key lines in role: “My scooter is slow/fast!”, “What is it? — It's a motor!”;
  • read, match & write ue/er/ar words (car, star, glue, her) and chorus the story «Звезда над фермой»;
  • say “Thank you!” when somebody helps (values).

Form

Pron. watch
ng /ŋ/ one nasal sound (no hard “g” at the end of ring, sing); sentence stress: My scooter is SLOW.

Frame 6: “Look! What is it?” → “It's a motor!” — “Is the scooter slow now?” → “No! It's fast!”

Assumptions: toys + Is it a…? known from L1–L2; children know the Magic Bike characters from Unit 1. Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures while listening; the teacher points, children don't read the bubbles alone. slow/fast/small/big are met here in context and studied properly next lesson.

Phonics Hero — ue·er·ar (b, light)
board → desks

Speed reading. The full digraph strip ai … ou · oi · ue · er · ar: There and back — beat your time! Doctor recall: Open wide — a-a-ar! Story tie-in: A fast CAR — ar! (today's racing story!).

Read & match — тетрадь стр.50 №2 (all point first). Read each word together (car · star · glue · fern), everyone points to its picture in the air → draw the line. Neighbour-check: Car? — Yes!

Dictation — стр.50 №3 (short, story day). 3 words only, once each: car · star · her → segment on fingers (/c/ /ar/ — car!) → write → Show me! Tricky strip №4: so · be — there and back.

Story taste — стр.51 «Звезда над фермой» (1–2′, settler). Chorus the 5 frames, finger under the words: It is winter. A star in the dark… The star is far. So far! The draw-task («A star in the dark», стр.51 №2) goes home. Colour the star «Я молодец!» (стр.50).

Sight words. Flash more · before: chorus, air-write — One MORE time! (our replay phrase).

💪 Support: match with the word card in hand; dictation with c__. ⭐ Extension: 4th word by ear (glue). No long written work — full story lesson. 🎮 Phonics Hub (speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/) — набор ue·er·ar, ✅ готов.

24–34′
📺 Story — listen & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 20). Big story picture on screen / books open. Pre-teach with gesture: wheels (fists rolling) · motor (brrrm!) · race — Ready, steady… go!

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “How many toys?” ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U2 “Find it!” engine on the big story picture. · How: Speaky asks, a child taps an object; it lights up. · Pupils: whole class points in their book / calls it — A bike! A scooter! The wheels! The dog! — then chorus the toy count. · Teacher: 🦝 How many toys? Point! — 4–5 things, quick (spot the Magic Bike's bell — hook). · Offline reserve: no projector → point in the open Pupil's Book (p. 20), pairs count 15 sec & chorus the number.

Predict (thinking) — before we listen. Beth's new scooter is SO slow… What will make it fast? New wheels? A push from the dog? Or… the MAGIC BIKE? Thumbs vote (wheels / dog / magic bike). Let's listen and check your guess!

Listen 1 — global (CD1 33). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: Is Beth happy at the start? Show me! (thumbs) And at the end? Was your guess right — what helped her?

Detail check — True or False? (thumbs up / down, all react). Say a sentence, class shows a thumb: Beth's scooter is fast at the start. (👎 — slow!) · The wheels are small. (👍) · The Magic Bike helps Beth. (👍) · It's a robot. (👎 — a motor!) · Beth wins the race. (👍). Re-point the frame to fix any wrong thumb.

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the story gestures at the right moments: slow 🐌, small wheels (little circles), Ring! Ring! 🔔 (their phonics sound!), big wheels, fast ⚡, cheer Well done, Beth! CCQs between frames (see box above). At the mystery frame stop: Look! What is it? Is it a robot? → No, it isn't! → It's a MOTOR!

35–43′
▶ Build the story
desks, pairs

▶ Story Builders (Mido №54 “Reconstruction”, picture re-skin for non-readers — all pairs work at once). Each pair gets 8 mini frame-cards of the story, shuffled.

Order it. Put the story in order — one card each, take turns! (RallyRobin with cards: A places one, B places the next.) Countdown 2 min.

Check with the audio. Replay CD1 33: pairs touch each card as they hear it — self-check, fix the order. Then choral retell, teacher holds up big frames one by one, class says the line: Let's have a race! · My scooter is slow! · Ring! Ring! · The wheels are big! · It's a motor! · You win the race! Thank you!

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Slow-Scooter Story-Sequence 1–5 🔨 СОБРАТЬ
Screen: the 5 key story frames (the race → the slow scooter with small wheels → call the Magic Bike → Ring! Ring! big wheels & motor → fast, wins the race & “Thank you!”) appear shuffled in a row, each with an empty number badge 1–5. · How: tap two frames to swap them (or drag); a “Check” button lights correct frames green with a cheer. · Pupils: the class calls the order — Number one — the race! Number two — the slow scooter! — and gesture each frame. · Teacher: take votes (Which is first? Hands up! — Numbered Heads), tap as they decide, then replay CD1 33 to confirm. Mirrors the paired frame-cards on screen for a whole-class check. · Offline reserve: no projector → use the big frame cards on the board (blu-tack), children come up in a fast rotation to place each one.

Support: hand that pair only the 5 clearest frames (not 8) and number the first one (the race) for them. Extension: after ordering, say one whole line for each frame from memory before the audio check.

43–51′
Act out — drama
carpet, trios

Roles. Trios: Beth (red badge) · Jake (green) · Molly + Magic Bike bell (blue, says “Ring! Ring!”). Teacher narrates and models once with a strong trio.

All trios act at the same time. Narrate the story slowly, frame by frame; every trio plays it in their own spot (races on the spot!): Let's have a race! — Ready, steady… go! — Oh no! My scooter is slow! — What's the matter, Beth? — I've got an idea… Magic Bike! — Ring! Ring! — Look! The wheels are big! — What is it? — It's a motor! — My scooter is fast now! — Well done, Beth! Thank you!

Create (thinking) — make it yours. Before round 2: Beth's scooter is fast now… but what ELSE is slow? You choose! Each trio adds a line — a slow bike, a slow skateboard, a slow train: My skateboard is slow! — Ring! Ring! — Now it's fast! (support: point to a toy card; extension: invent the whole exchange).

Swap roles, run it again faster (2 rounds). Volunteers: ONE trio shows 30 sec for the class — big applause. Values (PB p. 21 ex. 2): When a person helps you, what do you say? → Thank you!

Support: quieter children get Molly + the bell (short line Ring! Ring! + gesture); a line card in hand. Extension: confident children play Beth / narrate and lead the exchange.
