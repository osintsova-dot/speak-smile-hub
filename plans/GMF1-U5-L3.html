🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 5 “Fun on the farm” · Lesson 3 — Story: Animal magic
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Story: listen → follow → act out (drama) · carpet + desks · продолжающая линия
Week 18–24.01 · Lesson 36✨ STORY~12 pupils · small groups for drama
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow and understand the story “Animal magic” (Pupil's Book pp. 52–53, CD2 42) and act it out using key story language: “Can you see the cat? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't.”, “Is it a cow? — Yes, it is. But cows can't fly!”, “This sheep can climb trees.”, “Bad luck, Toby!”.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — farm animals + “Can you see a…?” (U5 L1–L2) and “Is it a…? — Yes, it is.” (U2 toys) and “Do you like…?” (U4 food) plus colours (Starter) — woven straight into the story work (a magic farm where animals do impossible things). Meet can / can't for ability (fly · swim · climb) receptively before the full focus in L4. Phonics — light recognise & blend the /ie/ sound (JP Book 2 p. 19): pie · tie · lie · die + quick tricky-word review. Values: wash your hands after you touch animals.
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (gesture responses, prediction); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a small group at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 30-sec showcase.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (order the frames, True/False thumbs);
  • say the key lines in role: “Is it a cow? — Yes, it is. But cows can't fly!”, “Can you see the cat? — Yes, I can!”;
  • recognise & blend the /ie/ sound (pie · tie · lie);
  • invent one magic animal — “My pig can fly!” (predict + create);
  • say why we wash our hands after touching animals (values).
Target language
Lexis
cowhorsesheepgoatduckcatdogfrogmouseflyswimclimb (recycled: farm animals U5 L1–L2 · colours · Toby the dog + the magic bike · story words magic · Shoo! · Amazing!)
Structure
Can you see the (cat)? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't. · Is it a (cow)? — Yes, it is. But cows can't fly! · This sheep can climb trees. · Dogs can't climb trees. Bad luck, Toby!
Form
can / can't + base verb (ability): (Cows) can't fly. · (This sheep) can climb. — receptive/echoed here, full focus in L4. Question echo: Can you see…? → Yes, I can. Recycled: Is it a…? — Yes, it is.
Pron. watch
weak can /kən/ in statements vs. strong can't /kɑːnt/ (stress the “t”); connected Is it /ɪzɪt/; sentence stress: Cows CAN'T FLY.
Phonics
Jolly (JP Book 2 p. 19): recognise & blend the /ie/ sound — pie · tie · lie · die (light, story lesson) · quick tricky-word sight review.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Frame 1: “Can Toby see the cat?” → “Yes!” “Is the cat happy?” → “No — Shoo! Stop, Toby!”
Frame 3: “Look — a cow in the sky! Can cows REALLY fly?” → “No! Cows can't fly.” “So why can it fly?” → “It's MAGIC!”
Frame 8: “The cat is up the tree. Can Toby climb the tree?” → “No — dogs can't climb trees!” “So the cat is…” → “safe / happy!”

Assumptions: farm animals + Can you see a…? known from U5 L1–L2; Is it a…? — Yes, it is. from U2; colours from the Starter; children know Toby the dog & the magic bike from earlier units. Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures while listening; the teacher points, children don't read the speech bubbles alone.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
New words block the story (magic · Shoo! · Amazing!)Pre-teach 3 things before listening with gesture: magic = sparkle fingers + “ooooh”, Shoo! = shooing hands, Amazing! = jaw-drop + thumbs up.
Children call answers over the audio“Listening hands” rule — hands cupped behind ears while the CD plays; talk only when it stops.
can vs. can't sound the same to themOne gesture each: can = thumbs up “yes!”, can't = arms crossed “X, no!”; over-stress the -t. “Show me!” drill before the story.
Drama turns to chaos (running around)Animal moves are ON THE SPOT (flying cow = flap arms in place); roles fixed by colour badges; “Freeze!” resets.
Frame cards ordered by one child in the pairRule “one card each turn, take turns” (RallyRobin with cards); praise pairs where both hands work.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset (essential in drama).

5–13′
📺 Phonics — the /ie/ sound (light)
at the board

Recognise. New Jolly sound /ie/ with its action (point a finger up as if to say “I know!”): ie — ie — ie! Can you hear /ie/? Say words, children do the action ONLY when they hear it: pie · sit · tie · dog · lie. Snappy, all react.

Blend — “Robot Speaky” (all respond). 🦝 Speaky robot-voice, one sound at a time: /p/ /ie/… class blends & shouts pie! then points to the picture. Do pie · tie · lie · die — a picture appears each time for meaning.

Read — thumbs check (all react). Flash the /ie/ word cards; class blends chorally, then thumbs up = real word, thumbs down = silly word (mix in zie · lot). Every child shows a thumb. Then flash 3–4 tricky sight words — chorus + air-write. Keep it light — this is the fullest lesson of the unit, the story is the star.

Support: keep the picture beside the word; ask for just the first sound. Extension: fast finishers sky-write pie · tie and add one more /ie/ word they know.

📺 Story — listen & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 52). Big story picture on screen / books open. Title: “Animal magic”. What animals can you see in the story? — pairs point & name 15 sec, then chorus a few. Pre-teach with gesture: magic (sparkle fingers) · Shoo! (shooing hands) · Amazing! (jaw drop + thumbs up).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “What's on the magic farm?” ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U1 “Find it!” engine on the big story picture. · How: Speaky asks, a child taps the animal; it lights up. · Pupils: whole class points in their book / calls it — A cow! A goat! A sheep! then answer Can you see the cat? — Yes, I can! · Teacher: 🦝 Where's the goat? Point! — 4–5 animals, quick.
Оффлайн-резерв: точечный поиск по большой картинке в PB p. 52 + пальцы всех детей.

Predict (thinking) — before we listen. It's a MAGIC farm! Can a cow fly here? Can a goat swim? Thumbs guess: can (thumbs up) vs. can't (arms crossed). Let's listen and check your guess!

Listen 1 — global (CD2 42). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: Is it a normal farm or a MAGIC farm? Show me! (thumbs) Was your guess right — can the cow fly?

Detail check — True or False? (thumbs up / down, all react) — assessment. Say a sentence, class shows a thumb: Toby is a cat. (👎 — He's a dog!) · The cow can fly. (👍 — It's magic!) · Goats can swim in the story. (👍) · Dogs can climb trees. (👎 — Dogs can't climb trees!) · The cat is sad at the end. (👎 — happy!). Re-point the frame to fix any wrong thumb.

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the story gestures at the right moments: Shoo! (frame 2) · flying cow flap arms (frame 3) · swimming goat (frame 4) · climbing sheep (frame 5) · Ring! Ring! the magic bell (frame 6) · point in the tree (frame 7) · Bad luck, Toby! pat-pat (frame 8). CCQs between frames (see box above). At frame 3 stop: Is it a cow? — Yes, it is. But cows can't…? — FLY!

27–35′
▶ Build the story
desks, pairs

▶ Story Builders (Mido №54 “Reconstruction”, picture re-skin for non-readers — all pairs work at once). Each pair gets 8 mini frame-cards of “Animal magic”, shuffled.

Order it. Put the story in order — one card each, take turns! (RallyRobin with cards: A places one, B places the next.) Countdown 2 min. ICQ: Do we grab all the cards or take turns? (→ take turns!)

Check with the audio. Replay CD2 42: pairs touch each card as they hear it — self-check, fix the order. Then choral retell, teacher holds up big frames one by one, class says the line: Here's Toby! · The cat is on the magic bike. Shoo! · Is it a cow? Cows can't fly! · Is it a goat? Goats can't swim! · This sheep can climb trees! · Stop, magic, stop! · The cat is in the tree. · Dogs can't climb trees. Bad luck, Toby!

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Animal-Magic Story-Sequence 1–5 🔨 СОБРАТЬ
Screen: the 5 key story frames (Toby sees the cat → cat on the magic bike → flying cow → swimming goat → cat safe in the tree, “Bad luck, Toby!”) appear shuffled in a row, each with an empty number badge 1–5. · How: tap two frames to swap them (or drag); a “Check” button lights correct frames green + a cheer plays when the order is right. · Pupils: the class calls the order — Number one — here's Toby! Number two — the magic bike! — and gesture each frame. · Teacher: take votes (Which is first? Hands up!), tap as they decide, then replay CD2 42 to confirm. Mirrors the paired frame-cards on screen for a whole-class check.
Оффлайн-резерв: 5 больших кадров на доске магнитами — класс голосует и переставляет.

Support: hand that pair only 5 clearest frames (not 8) and number the first one for them. Extension: after ordering, ask them to say one whole line for each frame from memory before the audio check.

35–45′
Act out — drama
carpet, groups

Roles. Groups of 3–4: Toby the dog (blue badge, “Woof!”) · the cat (black badge, rings the magic bell “Ring! Ring!”) · 2 children (red + green — Molly & Jake, they spot the animals). Model once with a strong group.

All groups act at the same time. Narrate the story slowly, frame by frame; every group plays it in their own spot (flying-cow flap ON THE SPOT!): Here's Toby! Oh no, he can see the cat! Shoo, Toby! … Suddenly — look! Is it a cow? Yes, it is. But cows can't fly! It's magic! And look — a goat! Goats can't swim! Amazing! Look at the sheep — this sheep can climb trees! Ring the bell, stop the magic! Can you see the cat now? Yes — it's in the tree! Dogs can't climb trees. Bad luck, Toby!

Create (thinking) — make it yours. Before round 2: The magic farm makes ONE more animal do something silly — you choose! What animal? What can it do? Each group adds a line with their own magic animal: Is it a pig? Yes, it is. But pigs can't fly! … This pig CAN fly! (support: point to an animal card + do the action; extension: invent the whole exchange). This feeds tonight's homework (draw a magic animal).

Swap roles, run it again — keep it to 2 rounds (don't let the drama over-run; Freeze! the moment energy tips). Volunteers: ONE group shows 30 sec for the class — big applause. Values (PB p. 53 ex. 2): We touched the animals — now what do we do? → Wash your hands!

Support: quieter children get the animal role (mime + one sound); a line card in hand. Extension: confident children play Molly / Jake and lead the “Is it a…? Cows can't…!” exchange.

45–52′
▶ Magic farm — can or can't?
desks, all at once

▶ Can or Can't? (targeted settler — can/can't + animals; every child reacts every round). Each child has a small set of animal cards. 🦝 Speaky says a sentence; class shows thumbs up = can (true) / arms crossed = can't (silly) AND holds up the matching animal card.

Ducks can swim! (👍 hold up duck) · Cows can fly! (❌ arms crossed — No! Cows can't fly… only on the MAGIC farm!) · Frogs can jump! (👍) · Fish can climb trees! (❌) · Horses can run! (👍). 6–7 sentences, instant show-me check each time — every child answers with body + card, nobody hides.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Flashcards — animals & actions (model & check) ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U1 “Flashcards” engine — an animal card + an action (fly / swim / climb / run). · How: tap to reveal, Speaky says the sentence, one child taps ✅/❌. · Pupils: first show thumbs + card at their desk, then one child mirrors it on screen — class checks: Yes! Ducks can swim! · Teacher: use it to model round 1, then just for the show-me check so every child stays on their own cards.
Оффлайн-резерв: обычные флешкарты животных + учитель называет — все реагируют телом + карточкой.
