🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 4 “Fantastic food!” · Lesson 2 — Grammar: I like / I love / I don't like…
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · continuing group · 60 min · Dialogue → controlled drill → freer → production · carpet + desks
Week 30.11 · Lesson 26✨ GRAMMAR~12 pupils · pairs + mingle
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say what food they like, love and don't like and ask a friend about theirs: “I like chicken. I love ice cream. I don't like fish. What about you?” (Pupil's Book p. 41 dialogue, CD2 21), and act out the “magic restaurant” food dialogue.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — L1 (lesson 25) food words chicken · pizza · rice · soup · salad · sandwiches · pasta · fish + Unit 2 Is it a…? / What's your favourite…? — woven into today's structure (“What's your favourite food? — I love pizza!”); phonics — Magic e: e_e /iː/ (Pete, these, Steve, Eve, evening), reviewing last lesson's a_e (cake, name, game) and contrasting short e (pet, hen); listen for detail (PB p. 41 ex. 2 “Listen and draw a mouth”); gently preview the question Do you like…? — Yes, I do. / No, I don't. for L3–L4.
  • Personal. Insist on don't (“I don't like fish”, not “I no like”); keep food nouns bare (“I like fish”, not “a fish”); every task keeps ALL children responding (own faces / thumbs / all mingle at once) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say “I like / I love / I don't like (food)” about 6–8 foods;
  • ask & answer “Do you like (pizza)? — Yes, I do. / No, I don't.” and act out the food dialogue;
  • hear, blend & read magic-e e_e words and tell them from short e.
Target language
Lexis

Structure
I like (chicken).  ·  I love (ice cream).  ·  I don't like (fish).  ·  What about you?  ·  It's delicious! · It's my favourite!  ·  preview: Do you like (pizza)? — Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
Form
like / love / don't like + food noun (no article: “I like fish”). don't = do not. Question preview: Do you like + noun? → short answers Yes, I do / No, I don't (not “Yes, I like”).
Pron. watch
don't /dəʊnt/ — say the whole word, not “I no like”; love /lʌv/ vs like /laɪk/; stress the feeling word — “I LOVE ice cream!”; short answer “No, I don't” ↘.
Phonics
Jolly focus: Magic e — e_e /iː/ (Pete, these, Steve, Eve, evening) — the silent e makes the vowel say its name (recognise → blend → read); review a_e (cake, name, game) · contrast short e (pet, hen) · tricky words: they, their (JP Book 2 p. 35).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Rub your tummy at ice cream: “I LOVE ice cream!” → is that a lot or a little? a lot! Then a smile at rice: “I like rice” → happy. Then pull a yuck face at fish: “I don't like fish” → no, yuck.
“Do you like pizza?” → “Yes, I do!” (thumbs up)  ·  “Do you like soup?” → “No, I don't!” (thumbs down) — not “Yes, I like”.
Magic e: point to the e at the end of Pete: “Does this e make a sound?” → no, it's silent — “but it's magic: it wakes the other e to say its name — /iː/… Pete!”
⭐ Option — favourite CCQ: “What's your favourite food — the one you love MOST?” → one food only. (Recycles Unit 2 favourite; use if the class is quick today.)

Assumptions: the 8 food words and “I like / don't like / love…” were first met in L1 (lesson 25); Unit 2 gave What's your favourite…?; last lesson gave magic-e a_e; the 26 single sounds are known. Pupils are non-/emergent readers → picture cards & audio, short printed words only. Carpet routines & pair-/mingle signals known.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
“I no like fish” instead of “I don't like fish”Cross your arms + shake head on don't: I DON'T like fish. Back-chain …like fish → don't like fish → I don't like fish; the yuck-face always comes with don't.
Adds an article: “I like a fish / a chicken”“For food we love in general — no ‘a’: I like fish.” Model with a big open hand: I like fish. (keep it light — praise the correct version).
Confuses like and loveBodies: love = hands on heart + big “Mmm!”; like = simple smile. Sort a food to the right feeling before speaking.
Answers “Yes, I like” to “Do you like…?”Thumbs-up drill: the short answer is Yes, I do. / No, I don't. — chant it with the thumb, no full sentence needed.
Reads Pete as “pet” (misses magic e)“The magic e wakes the vowel — it says its NAME: /iː/… Peeete.” Contrast the pair pet → Pete, them → theme with a magic-wand gesture over the final e.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset (key for the mingle & corners).
Procedure — staging
highlighted = exact words to say (teacher / class) 🦝 = Speaky lines ▶ = game […] = fill-in slot 🎮 = on-screen (projector) interactive
⏱ Pacing — honest 60 min. This is a full plate, so pick, don't cram. The core = dialogue model (26–34′) + one production game (Food Corners) + Listen & draw a mouth. Buffers to drop first if you're short: the 2nd Food-Survey round, the “Do you like…?” preview (52–55′), Timed Pair Share. Better one phrase said well by every child than three stages half-done — never rush the model or the game.

Phonics — Magic e (e_e)
at the board

Recognise — the magic e trick. Write pet: sound it out /p/ /e/ /t/ — pet. Add a silent e → Pete, wave a “magic wand” over it: This e is MAGIC and silent — it wakes the vowel to say its NAME: /iː/… Peeete! Do them → theme. Quick review of a_e from last lesson (yummy link): cake! — name, game. Children copy the wand gesture.

Short e or magic e? — whole-class show-me (all react). Every child gets two cards: 🪄 (magic e /iː/) and 🔤 (short e /e/). Say words one by one — Pete · pet · these · hen · Steve · bed · theme · ten… → Show me! — all hold up 🪄 or 🔤. 8–10 words, fast; nobody just watches.

Blend (all together). Push the sounds together: /P/ /ee/ /t/… Pete! Then these, Steve, Eve, evening — a face-picture / name-card for each. (Note: e_e words are few — that's fine; the RULE is the prize.)

Read & write (everyone). Now YOU read. Flip 4 word-cards — children blend them aloud. Then dictation: Listen… Pete. word once → all write on mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & check. Focus: Pete, these. Finish with tricky words by sight: they · their — flash, chorus, air-write.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Magic-e Sorter ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the Unit 1 “Flashcards” engine)
Screen: a word + picture flips up one at a time (Pete · pet · these · hen · Steve · bed · theme · ten). Mechanic: tap the card to hear the word, then tap 🪄 or 🔤 to sort it; a green tick / red shake shows if right. Pupils: hold up their own 🪄/🔤 first, then a helper taps to check. Teacher: read the word, wait for hands, then reveal. Reuse: same engine as L1 Flashcards — just swap the card set.

◆ Support: in dictation, give the sound-buttons (P-e-te) — they only fill letters. ▲ Extension: fast writers read a bonus pair by ear (them → theme).

Presentation · Dialogue
carpet → pairs

Model with Speaky. 🦝 Speaky tastes from his plate. Teacher: Mmm! I like fish. Speaky pulls a face: I don't like fish. I like chicken. Teacher: I like ice cream, too. What about you? Speaky: Oh yes! I love ice cream. It's delicious!

Listen & repeat — PB p. 41 ex. 1 (CD2 21). Books open / picture on screen: the space-picnic scene. Play the dialogue; children repeat line by line. Drill the three feelings with bodies (heart / smile / yuck): I love ice cream! · I like chicken. · I don't like fish. Point at the Grammar box: I like chicken. I love ice cream. I don't like fish. — today's magic words.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Do you like…? Spinner ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the “What's this? spinner” engine)
Screen: a wheel of 8 foods sits next to Speaky. Mechanic: tap to spin → it lands on one food; tap it → Speaky's face pops up (😋 love / 🙂 like / 🤢 don't like). Pupils: BEFORE the reveal the whole class asks together Do you like (pizza)? ↗ → on reveal they voice Speaky: Yes, I do! I love pizza! / No, I don't! Teacher: spin, hold the reveal until you hear the FULL question, then tap. 🧠 Predict: Love, like or don't like? Point & guess! — children vote with 🙌/👍/👎 before the face shows.

Act out (ALL pairs at once). Pairs on the carpet; each pair gets 3 food cards. A tastes a card: Mmm! I like […]. What about you? B answers with a real feeling: I love […]! / I don't like […]! Swap cards, change roles. ICQ: Do you say “I no like” or “I DON'T like”? (→ don't!)

◆ Support: weaker child answers with a thumb + one word first (Love!), then the full line. ▲ Extension: add It's delicious! / It's my favourite!

▶ Food Corners
whole room

▶ Food Corners — I love / I like / I don't like (Kagan Corners — the whole class moves and speaks at once, nobody sits out). Post three signs: 🙌 I LOVE · 🙂 I like · 🤢 I don't like in three corners.

How it runs — everyone decides & says. 🦝 Speaky holds up a food card and calls it: Fish! Each child runs to their corner and, on Go!, everyone in that corner shouts their true sentence: I don't like fish! / I like fish! / I love fish! Teacher scans: Corner, say it! I DON'T like fish! 6–8 foods (pizza, ice cream, soup, cake, salad, chicken…). Reset if loud: Freeze!

Kagan — Take Off–Touch Down twist (option): for a quick round, no corners — just Stand up if you LOVE ice cream! → lovers stand + shout I love ice cream!, others stay seated. Fast, everyone chooses honestly.

◆ Support: a child unsure of the word points to the card & copies the corner's chant. ▲ Extension: add a reason — I love ice cream — it's delicious!

⭐ Swap (rotation): if you ran Corners recently, swap in Yummy or Yucky! taste-test drama — Speaky “feeds” a mimed spoonful to a volunteer who acts the feeling and says the line; the class copies. (Keeps drama in the rotation vs. last lessons' hide-&-seek / spinner games.)
