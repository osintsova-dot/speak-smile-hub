🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 7 “Let's explore my town!” · Lesson 6 — Review: Places, transport & There's/There are · 🏆 Flagship Quiz
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Consolidation + flagship team quiz with reward · carpet + desks
Week 15.03–21.03 · Lesson 53L6 Review🏆 Quiz day~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will have consolidated all Unit 7 language — the places in town (zoo, café, sweet shop, toyshop, bookshop, hospital, restaurant, swimming pool, cinema, supermarket), the transport (bus, boat, motorbike, train, helicopter, lorry), “There's a (zoo). / There are (two sweet shops).”, “How many (buses) are there? — There are (fifteen).” and numbers 11–20 — and can use it confidently in the review tasks and the quiz.
  • Subsidiary. Phonics — say the unit “z” sound (z–z–z. There's a lazy zebra in the zoo, PB p. 77) and read the Jolly digraph “ow / ou” (count, mouse, house, loud; brown, flower, down); listening for detail (PB p. 80 ex. 1, CD3 39); self-assessment (My progress traffic light, AB p. 77 ex. 3).
  • Personal. Run the flagship quiz by PIES: every child writes/shows every answer (Showdown on mini-boards), the responder is picked by Numbered Heads — no “captains answer everything”; keep the reward moment short so language time wins.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the ten places in town and the six kinds of transport without support;
  • say “There's a (zoo)” for one and “There are (two sweet shops)” for more than one;
  • ask & answer “How many (buses) are there? — There are (fifteen)” using numbers 11–20;
  • say the “z” sound and read simple “ow / ou” words;
  • self-assess with the traffic light — ready for the Unit 7 test.

Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Show a scene: ONE zoo, TWO sweet shops. One zoo — There's a zoo. Two sweet shops — There's a… or There ARE? → There are two sweet shops.
Point at a picture of 3 buses: How many buses are there? Count! — one, two, three… → There are three buses.
Quiz rule ICQ: Who writes the answer — one child or EVERYBODY? → everybody, on their own board!

Assumptions: all Unit 7 language met in L1–L5; numbers 1–20 and “There's / There are” done — today we retrieve, not re-teach. Teams of 4 with numbered members (1–4) are routine. The flagship quiz with reward runs only on Review day — children know it's special.

Phonics — the “z” sound + read “ow / ou”
🎮 board → open space

Unit sound “z” (say it — ties to the zoo). Buzz like a sleepy zebra: z–z–z… then chant Toby's tongue twister — z–z–z. There's a lazy zebra in the zoo! (PB p. 77). Choral ×2 + finger-buzz gesture.

Read the digraph (all together, word first — picture after). Write c-ou-nt with ou joined: arm-blending gesture — /k/… /ow/… /n/… /t/ — count! Then mouse, house, loud; switch to ow — brown, flower, down. Picture appears only AFTER the class reads — reading, not guessing.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор) · “Flashcards” (Genki/S&S engine)  ✅ READY
Screen: the word fills the screen in big letters — count · mouse · house · loud · brown · flower · down — tap to flip and reveal the picture.
Mechanic: word shows first (letters only) → class blends chorally → tap → picture confirms. Never picture-first.
Target language: /ow/… blend it… mouse!
Teacher: tap in rhythm, keep the arm-blend gesture; no projector → paper word cards work identically.

▶ Take Off–Touch Down (all move): Stand up if you hear /z/! Say a mix: zoo · sun · zebra · cat · buzz · dog · nose. Everyone stands / sits every time — nobody watches.

▶ Swat It! — мухобойки (fly-swatters, whole class reads). 5–6 “ow / ou” words on the board. Point to one → the WHOLE class reads it chorally first (m-ou-se… mouse!) → THEN two children race to swat it. Quick rotation, new pair each word, so many get a turn while the class is always the reading “jury”.

17–24′
Review in motion
🎮 open space · teams

▶ Build-a-Town — There's a…/There are… (everyone answers, nobody waits). Stick place cards around the room to make a “town” (one bookshop, TWO sweet shops, one zoo…). Point: Look! What's in our town? Think–Pair first: everyone whispers the sentence to a neighbour. Then call a number → that child says it aloud: There's a zoo! / There are two sweet shops! Class confirms chorally.

▶ How many? — transport count. Hold up a handful of transport cards (buses, boats, motorbikes…). Ask: How many buses are there? Everyone shows the number on their fingers → call a number → child answers: There are fifteen buses! Recycles numbers 11–20 + transport. Attention reset: Class? — Yes!

Support ▸ weaker pupils hold the picture card + answer with a partner (echo the chunk).   Extension ▸ stronger pupils give the full sentence — There are two sweet shops next to the café.
🎮 Интерактив (проектор) · “What's this? Spinner” + “Find it!” (U7 review engines)  ✅ READY

Mechanic: Spinner → What's this? — It's a helicopter! · Find it! → point & say There's a cinema! (whole class points at the screen every round).
Target language: What's this? / There's a… / There are… / How many… are there?
Teacher: use as a warm station or when a real-card round runs dry; keep it to 60–90 sec so movement stays the star.

33–51′
🏆 FLAGSHIP QUIZ with reward
🎮 teams · projector

Set-up (2 min). 3 teams of 4, numbered heads 1–4, one mini-whiteboard EACH. Quiz grid on screen — categories: Places in town 🏙 · There's / There are 🏠 · How many? & numbers 🔢 · Sounds & spelling 🔤. Rules + ICQ: Who writes? — Everybody! When do you show? — On “Showdown!”

Round flow (Kagan Showdown + Numbered Heads — all answer every question). Question up (e.g. picture half-hidden: What is it? · one vs many: There's a… or There are…? · count: How many buses? — 1, 2, 3… fifteen! · read an “ow / ou” word · say the “z” sound). Every child writes/draws → Showdown! → all boards up → call a number: Number 3! — that child of the playing team answers aloud in a full sentence.

Predict (thinking). On the half-hidden picture, before revealing: Look — just a corner! What do you THINK it is? Write your guess! Then reveal — Were you right? Turns each round into a little guess-and-check.

Reward shot. Correct = the answering child takes the shot: animated football penalty on screen — a GOAL = team points, class counts them in English: One, two, three… GOAL! (No projector → paper goal + soft ball, ≈ Mido №91 Bowling for Animal reskin.) Rotate teams; over ~10–12 questions every child shoots at least once.

Support ▸ word-box / picture bank stays visible on a side card so weaker writers copy, not freeze; accept one correct word from them.   Extension ▸ when a strong pupil is “Number”, require the full sentence — There are two sweet shops. There are fifteen buses. — not just the noun.
🔨 Flagship · projector · BUILD · “Unit 7 Team Quiz + Reward”  🔨 BUILD
Screen 1 — Grid: 4×3 board of numbered tiles under the 4 category headers (Places in town 🏙 / There's · There are 🏠 / How many? & numbers 🔢 / Sounds & spelling 🔤). Tap a tile → it flips to the question (place photo / half-hidden building / count-the-transport / “ow · ou” word / “z” prompt). Used tiles grey out.
Screen 2 — Answer: big “Show your boards!” + a 5-second timer bar; tap ✔ / ✗ to score; a full-sentence model appears for choral echo.
Screen 3 — Reward mini-game: football penalty — child “kicks”, goalie dives, animated GOAL (or a bowling reskin). One shot = +points.
Screen 4 — Scoreboard: 3 team bars, huge numbers readable from the back row, plus a “Teamwork ⭐” toggle for best cooperation.
Mechanic: pick tile → question → everyone writes → Showdown → Numbered Head answers → score → reward shot → rotate team. ~10–12 tiles ≈ 15 min.
Target language: all Unit 7 — What's this? / There's a… / There are (two)… / How many (buses) are there? — There are (fifteen). + read an “ow · ou” word / say “z”.
Teacher: host only — YOU tap tiles & call the number so no captain dominates. Spec is a self-contained HTML slide (S&S purple #6C3FC5 / honey #F5C842). Offline reserve: printed grid + paper goal + soft ball works identically.

Finish with totals + teamwork star: Give me five, champions!
