Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to respond to robot commands — I am a robot! Turn on! Turn off! Move! Stop! Turn left! Turn right! — moving robot-stiff on each command and saying it.
  • Subsidiary. Remember-time recycle (left/right from Adventure · colours · can you…?); folder worksheet (colour by command); “program your friend” TPR.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; accept one-word answers, model the full chunk back; every child speaks/moves every round; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘.
By the end, pupils can…
  • move / freeze on Turn on! / Turn off!;
  • do Move! Stop! Turn left! Turn right! as a robot;
  • say the command while doing it;
  • program a partner robot;
  • join the whole I am a robot song.

TPR gesture
turn on press a button on the tummy · turn off flop & freeze · move stiff robot walk · stop freeze · left/right whole-body turn.
Pron. watch
robot voice = flat, choppy syllables; keep Stop! a sharp cut. Joining the movement matters more than accuracy.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: “Turn off — keep moving OR freeze?” → freeze.
ICQ: “Move your body AND say the word?” → yes.

Assumptions: children know left/right & colours from earlier levels. Left/right at 5 is fuzzy — mirror by facing the same way. Some English + L1 prompt. Non-readers. Group = 12.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Move but stay silentSay the command WITH every move; the song & the robot voice carry it.

Robots crash into each otherBubble-arms; robot walk is slow; Turn off! = freeze = brakes.
Over-excitement after ⚡🧘 settler after each ⚡; volume-control arm.
Transitions get chaoticTrain: Come with me! — one signal, one destination.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 👋 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…” with fingers shown.
“Come with me!” 🚊 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana’s real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🌱 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Cumulative Q&A recycle lives at the start (Remember time); the final game is on today’s language.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello song (chorus), “choose the greeting → jump/run/swim/fly to your chair”.
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives & shakes hands with each child (Hello! How are you?).
  • Remember time — cumulative review with A4 flashcards / Genki Karuta / app on the board.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, topic song, board app, active games.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheet, a different type each lesson, always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🌱 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Final game ⚡ — back to the carpet for a fast, fun game on today’s language, ending on a high note.
  • Closing — Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
