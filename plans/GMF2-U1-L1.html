Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 sports and say which they do using the play/go split: “I play (tennis). / I go (swimming).”
  • Subsidiary. Sing the coursebook song «Do you play any sports?» (CD1 12); retrieve school subjects (PE!) and “What's your favourite…?” from Starter, woven onto sports; stickers chant (CD1 14); early literacy — read the sport words, spot the -ing “go”-sports.
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; ICQ before every task; stirrer→settler rhythm — sports mime is high-energy, plan the freezes.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 sports;
  • say “I play football / I go swimming” about themselves;
  • read the 10 sport words and match word ↔ picture.

Structure
I play (tennis). / I go (rollerblading).  ·  preview: Do you play…? — Yes, I do.  ·  recycle: My favourite subject is PE!
Form
play + ball/team games (football, tennis…) · go + -ing sports (swimming, cycling, skateboarding, rollerblading). Spot the rule together — don't lecture it.
Pron. watch
volleyball /ˈvɒlibɔːl/ (not “волейбол”); baseball–basketball minimal pair; -ing endings unstressed: swim-ming, cy-cling.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Football — play or go?” → play. “Swimming?” → go. “Why? Look — swim-m-i-n-g!” → -ing sports take go.
“Do I play a sport EVERY day for it to be MY sport?” → no — sometimes is fine: I play tennis.

Assumptions: Starter routines are back; pupils know PE from subjects and action verbs (run, jump, swim) from GMF1. The play/go split is NEW and is the core challenge. Early readers — word cards work alongside pictures.

10–20′
Presentation · 10 sports
carpet → board

Present with flashcards. Each card: What's this? It's… football! Choral ×3 + gesture; 2–3 solo. Contrast pair held together: BASEball — BASKETball!

play or go? (the key move). Two columns on the board. Hold each card: Play or go? …I PLAY football! …I GO swimming! Kids call the column, you stick the card in. Look at the GO column — what do you see at the end? -ing!

▶ Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up for GO sports… crouch for PLAY sports! — flash 8 cards fast, everyone moves every time.

💪 Support: answer with the gesture + word only. ⭐ Extension: full sentence — I play hockey with my dad.

28–36′
Word ↔ picture · Memory
carpet, pairs

▶ Memory · I play / I go — in pairs. Each pair: 5 picture cards + 5 WORD cards (readers!) face-down in two rows. Turn one of each; a match is claimed only with the right sentence: I play tennis! / I go cycling! Wrong verb → card goes back. Most pairs wins.

ICQ: Can you keep the cards if you say “play swimming”? (→ no — go swimming!)

🎮 Interactive (projector): S&S memory pairs — sports. One demo round on screen before the pair game: a rotating child flips two cards, the whole class says the sentence aloud. · 🎮 sports-memo

✔ Assess: stand behind one pair at a time — is the play/go choice automatic yet?

 5+5 cards per pair · 🎮 sports-memo
36–44′
Mime & guess
carpet, 2 teams

▶ Slow-motion mime. One player mimes a sport IN SLOW MOTION; their team asks together: Do you play basketball? (chorus — previews L2!) Mimer: Yes, I do! / No, I don't. Point per correct guess; rotate the mimer every round — everyone mimes once.

Whole team must say the question together — no solo shouting: One voice, one team!

 sport flashcards (prompt deck)
44–52′
My sports · speak & write
desks

Speak. ▶ RallyRobin: Take turns with your partner: I play… / I go… — how many can you say about YOU?

Write (notebooks). Two sentence frames from the board: I play ___. I go ___. Each child writes their own 2 true sentences, copying sport words from the board columns. Fast finishers add a third with and.

💪 Support: copy the frame + choose from 3 word cards on the desk. ⭐ Extension: add “with my friend / on Saturdays”.
