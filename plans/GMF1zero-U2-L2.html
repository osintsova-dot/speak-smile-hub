🦝 Give Me Five! 1 (с нуля) · Unit 2 “Happy birthday!” · Занятие 23 / L2 — Grammar: Is it a…? + What's your favourite toy?
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., first-year beginners · линия с нуля) · 60 min · Dialogue → controlled drill → freer → production · carpet + desks
Week 16.11–22.11 · Занятие 23✨ GRAMMAR~12 pupils · pairs + 2 teams🦸 Phonics Hero ур.18a · ue·er·ar
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to ask about a mystery present and answer with short forms: “What is it? Is it a (bike)? — Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.” (Pupil's Book p. 19 dialogue), act out the birthday-present dialogue, and say their favourite toy: “What's your favourite toy? — My favourite toy is a (ball).” (в сокращённом юните с нуля эта структура — целевая уже сегодня).
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — L1 toys + “What is it? — It's a…”, Genki colours — woven into today's question game (“Is it a red car? — No, it isn't!”); phonics — Phonics Hero ур.18 (a): digraphs ue /юː/ · er /əː/ · ar /ɑː/ (meet → blend → trace, тетрадь стр.49–50); listen for detail (PB p. 19 ex. 2 “Listen and circle”); What's your favourite toy? — My favourite toy is a… (целевая структура занятия 23, КТП с нуля).
  • Personal. Insist on the FULL question (“Is it a car?”, not “Car?”); model both Yes- and No-answers equally; every task keeps ALL children responding (own cards / thumbs / all pairs at once) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • ask “What is it? Is it a (scooter)?” about a hidden toy;
  • answer “Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.” honestly and act out the present dialogue;
  • answer “What's your favourite toy?” — My favourite toy is a […];
  • blend & read the first ue/er/ar words (glue, fern, her, car, star, farm) and trace the digraphs.

Structure
What is it? Is it a (bike)? — Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.  ·  It's a fantastic present. Thank you!  ·  What's your favourite toy? — My favourite toy is a (ball).
Form
Is + it + a + noun? → short answers Yes, it is. / No, it isn't (= is not). Rising tone on the question ↗, falling on the answer ↘.
Pron. watch
keep -n't in isn't; linking “Is it a” /ˈɪzɪtə/; don't let the question shrink to one word.
Phonics

⏱ Pacing — honest 60 min. This is a full plate, so pick, don't cram. The core = dialogue model (24–33′) + one production game + Listen & circle. Buffers to drop first if you're short: the 3rd secret-toy round, the tic-tac-toe grid 2, the 3rd Mix-Pair-Share swap in the favourite stage (keep at least two rounds — целевая структура). Better one full question (“Is it a car?”) said well by every child than three stages half-done — never rush the model and the pair practice. Cognitive load: only the Yes/No short answers are truly new — the toys, colours & “What is it?” are all recycled, so the question carries the challenge; scaffold it (back-chain, hands-behind-back rule) and keep everything else light. Offline reserve: every 🎮 has a paper twin (flashcards / box / dice) — no board needed.

Phonics Hero — ue·er·ar (a)
board → desks

Speed reading — тетрадь стр.49 №1. The strip is LONG now: ai · oa · ie · ee · or · ng · oo · ch · sh · th · ou · oi. Twelve digraphs — there and back, beat your time! Vary the voice: Boys… girls… whisper… LOUD!

Meet ue · er · ar — стр.49 №2. Three new sounds: ue /юː/ — glue! (sticky-hands gesture) · er /əː/ — her! (thinking hum “errr…”) · ar /ɑː/ — «открой ротик у доктора»: a-a-ar! All copy each sound + gesture ×3. Check: hold up ar — Two letters… how many sounds? → every child shows ONE finger.

Blend — Hub Station 3 / стр.50 №1 (all together). Sound windows open one by one: /g/ /l/ /ue/… glue! /c/ /ar/… car! /s/ /t/ /ar/… star! Then fern, her, farm — picture for meaning; every child slides a “blend finger” in the air.

Trace — стр.49 №3 (desks, settle). Trace by the arrows, start at the yellow dot: ue · er · ar · glue · star — whisper each as you write. (The match, dictation & story «Звезда над фермой» of ур.18 come next lesson — today is first meeting only.)

Sight words. Flash other · were: chorus ×3, air-write — Show me the OTHER toy! (swap-game word from today's pair work).

Presentation · Dialogue
carpet → pairs

Model with Speaky. 🦝 Speaky shakes his box. Teacher: What is it? Is it a bike? — Speaky: No, it isn't. — Is it a scooter? — Yes, it is! Open the box (toy scooter card): It's a fantastic present. Thank you!

Listen & repeat — PB p. 19 ex. 1 (CD1 31). Books open / picture on screen: birthday scene. Play the dialogue; children repeat line by line. Drill: rising tone on Is it a bike? ↗, head-shake on No, it isn't ↘, nod on Yes, it is! ↘ Back-chain the question. Point at the Grammar box: this is our question of the day.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Mystery Present Spinner ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the “What's this? spinner” engine)
Screen: a wrapped present next to Speaky, with a hidden wheel of the 10 toys. Mechanic: tap to spin → the toy is chosen but stays HIDDEN behind the wrapping; the class asks, and each tap reveals a yes/no; the last tap unwraps it with a “ta-daa”. Pupils: the WHOLE class asks together Is it a robot? → Speaky (screen) shakes head No, it isn't! until the right toy → Yes, it is! + unwrap + cheer. Teacher: hold each reveal until you hear the FULL question, then tap. 🧠 Predict: What's inside? Point to your guess before we unwrap! — children point to a toy card before the reveal. Offline: the real wrapped box with a toy card inside, or a paper spinner.

Act out (ALL pairs at once). Pairs on the carpet; each pair gets a “present” (envelope with a secret toy card). A shakes it, B asks: What is it? Is it a […]? A: No, it isn't. / Yes, it is! → open → both: It's a fantastic present. Thank you! Swap envelopes with another pair, change roles. ICQ: Do you say “car” or “Is it a car”? (→ the full question!)

◆ Support: weaker child is the asker with the toy cards fanned in front of them to read the pictures; partner answers. ▲ Extension: add a colour to the guess — Is it a red car?

33–40′
▶ Guess the secret toy
carpet, 2 teams

▶ Speaky's Secret Toy (Mido №16 “Hungman”, idea 2 — guessing rescue version). 8 toy pictures in a row on the board; 🦝 Speaky secretly picks ONE (card in his pocket). On the board: a balloon ladder — 5 balloons 🎈 next to a little Speaky climbing to the cake 🎂.

Two teams take turns. Kagan — Numbered Heads: team huddles 10 sec, agrees on a guess, then the called number asks for the team: Is it a robot? Before Speaky answers, the WHOLE class votes thumbs up/down — what will he say? Speaky: No, it isn't! → cross that toy out + pop one balloon 🎈✂️ — the whole class jumps up with a “POP!” and sits back (keeps everyone moving between guesses). Yes, it is! → Speaky reaches the cake, the guessing team scores.

3 quick rounds, new secret each time; wrong guesses shrink the choice, so the logic is visible. Reset if loud: Freeze!

◆ Support: let the team point to their chosen toy card as they ask, so the question is anchored to a picture. ▲ Extension: the guesser adds a colour clue — Is it a blue robot?

⭐ Swap (rotation): if you ran a board-guessing game last lesson, swap in ▶ Fly-swatters — 8 toy cards on the board; call Is it a scooter? and two players race to swat it, while everyone else holds up their own matching mini-card at the same time.

▶ Toy tic-tac-toe
desks, pairs

▶ Toy Tic-Tac-Toe (Mido №118, pre-A1 pairs re-skin — every pair plays at once, nobody waits). Each pair: one 3×3 grid of toy pictures + pencils (X / O).

Model on the board first. To take a square you must PLAY it, not just point: A covers the picture with a hand, B asks: Is it a train? A peeks: Yes, it is! → B marks O and they swap. Wrong toy named → No, it isn't! → no mark, turn passes. Three in a row wins; then flip the sheet — grid 2, change who starts.

Kagan — RallyCoach: partner = checker; praise the full question: Good question! Teacher monitors, feeds the fast finishers grid 2 with What is it? — It's a […].

🧠 Create — my mystery box: a fast pair hides one toy card behind a book and their neighbours must guess it with real questions — Is it a teddy? Is it a kite? — their own choice, their own present.

◆ Support: weaker child is the checker first (just peeks & says Yes, it is / No, it isn't), then swap to asking. ▲ Extension: add a colour to win the square — Is it a green train?
