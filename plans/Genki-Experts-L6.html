Genki preschoolers (~5–6 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Experts level · Lesson 6 of 7✨ circle · triangle · square · star · heart12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to name & spot shapes — What shape is it? — It’s a circle. It’s a star! — drawing each shape in the air and finding it around the room.
  • Subsidiary. Cumulative Remember-time recycle of ALL earlier topics (incl. Experts L2–L5: numbers, money, tens); folder worksheet (colour by command); our big-book shapes story.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; draw each shape with a big arm; every child names & finds a shape every round; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name 5 shapes;
  • answer What shape is it? — It’s a…;
  • draw each shape in the air;
  • find a shape around the room;
  • colour a shape on command (star → gold…).

TPR gesture
draw each shape in the air: circle round · triangle 3 sides · square 4 corners · star zig-zag · heart two-hand heart.
Pron. watch
triangle = “TRY-ang-gul”; square = “skwair”; circle = “SUR-kul”. Say the shape WHILE drawing it in the air.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: “Round with no corners — is it a square or a circle?” → circle.
ICQ: “Do we draw it AND say it?” → yes.

Assumptions: children met shapes in the Christmas block (recognition) — here they name & use them in context (What shape is it?). Near-zero English + L1 prompt. Non-readers — shapes by picture & air-drawing. Group = 12.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Square / triangle confusionCount corners together: triangle = 3, square = 4; draw big in the air.
Silent pointing at shapesSay the shape WITH the gesture every time; song & karuta carry the words.
Shape hunt → running chaosWalk to touch; Freeze! when found; hunt in the room, not out of it.
Over-excitement after ⚡🧘 settler after each ⚡; volume-control arm.
Transitions get chaoticTrain: Come with me! — one signal, one destination.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 👋 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…” with fingers shown.
“Come with me!” 🚊 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana’s real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🌱 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Cumulative Q&A recycle lives at the start (Remember time); the lesson ends on a high-note game on today’s language.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello song (chorus), “choose the greeting → jump/run/swim/fly to your chair”.
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives from the bus-box & shakes hands with each child (Hello! How are you?).
  • Remember time — cumulative review of ALL topics with A4 flashcards / Criss Cross / app on the board.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, topic song, board app, 2–3 active games.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheets, a different type each lesson (listen & tick / colour by command / trace / match соедини / draw & tell / put-in-order / mini-craft), always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🌱 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Final game ⚡ — back to the carpet for a fast, fun game on today’s language, so the lesson ends on a high note (kids don’t want to leave).
  • Closing — Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
