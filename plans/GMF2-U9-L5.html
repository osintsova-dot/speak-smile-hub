Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the ear/air tongue twister («The bear with a pear climbs the stairs at the funfair»), tell about British summer holidays, and read a diary & write their own diary entry in the past.
  • Subsidiary. Textbook phonics: ear/air = /eə/; culture — Blenheim Palace (maze! giant chess! butterflies); literacy — text type a diary: Yesterday was… I was at… It was…! — the year's grammar finale in writing.
  • Personal. The diary is the crown of the year's writing — protect the full 16 minutes; it's also Speaking-exam gold (уроки 70–72).
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the twister fast ×3;
  • tell 2 things about British summer holidays;
  • write a 4-line diary entry in the past.

Pron. watch
/eə/ open: bEAr, pEAr, stAIrs; ⚠ ear-words here read /eə/ (bear!), not /ɪə/ (hear) — flag lightly; diary /ˈdaɪəri/.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“bear and chair — same sound? Same letters?” → same /eə/, different letters.
“A diary — about today or about MY yesterday?” → my yesterday, my real days!

Assumptions: was/were + feelings active (L2–L4). Diary format lands on rich soil — it's the Speaking-exam template. Blenheim maze/chess/butterflies are visually magnetic.

Block 3 · Literacy · My diary
carpet → desks

Before you read. PB p.99: Find and circle the days — quick! (act. 1 race).

Listen & read (CD4 24). The diary: circus Saturday, funfair Sunday, tickets, photos. Chorus the formula lines: Yesterday was Saturday. It was sunny and hot. I was at the circus. It was great!

Write YOUR entry (AB p.96 format, notebooks). The four was-slots: Yesterday was ___. It was ___ (weather). I was at ___. It was ___! True or dream; draw the “ticket” beside. Two volunteers read theirs like TV reporters.

💪 Support: slot-by-slot copy support. ⭐ Extension: add a Who-was-with-me line: My cousins were with me!
