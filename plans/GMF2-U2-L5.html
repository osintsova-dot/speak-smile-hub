Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the ee/ea tongue twister («Queen Jean eats meat and the green sheep eats cheese»), tell about Pancake Day, and read & write a recipe («Funny face sandwich») with sequencers First/Then/Now/Finally.
  • Subsidiary. Textbook phonics: ee/ea = same /iː/, two spellings; prices listening consolidation (CD1 42); cooperative learning song «What do I need to do?» (CD1 43); culture comparison — pancakes at home.
  • Personal. Recipe block is the writing heart — protect 15 minutes for it; keep the pancake race safe and short.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the twister fast ×3;
  • tell 2 facts about Pancake Day and compare with blini;
  • write a 4-step recipe with First/Then/Now/Finally.

Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“green and meat — same sound? Same letters?” → same /iː/, different letters.
“In a recipe — do we ask or COMMAND?” → command: put! add!

Assumptions: Food + prices are active. Sequencers are new as explicit tools (heard in stories). Pancake culture maps neatly onto Масленица — rich compare talk expected.

Block 3 · Literacy · Recipe
carpet → desks

Before you read. PB p.25 photo: What can you see? Eyes? A nose?! What food is it?

Listen & read (CD1 46). Follow the 4 steps; sequencer cards (First/Then/Now/Finally) go up on the board in order as they're heard.

Order check. You read steps out of order — class shouts the sequencer that SHOULD come: FIRST!

Write your own funny-face recipe (notebooks, AB p.22 format). Word bank: bread · cheese · egg · carrot · olive · salad. Pairs write 4 steps with the sequencers, draw the face. Two pairs read theirs aloud — class mimes making it.

💪 Support: gap-frame: First, put the ___ on the ___. ⭐ Extension: add a 5th step: Eat it! Yum!
