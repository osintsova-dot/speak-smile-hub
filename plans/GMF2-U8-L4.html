Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to give & follow directions: “How do I get to the (post office)? — Go straight on. Turn left. Turn right. There's the (post office)!”
  • Subsidiary. Signs reading (CD3 51); human-robot navigation on the floor map; map tracing (AB format); left/right in ENGLISH anchored on the body.
  • Personal. Left/right needs body anchors (L-hand trick!); robot game = the funnest discipline machine of the year — instructions only work if precise.
By the end, pupils can…
  • give the 3 core directions confidently;
  • navigate a “robot” partner across the floor map;
  • trace a route on a paper map from spoken directions.

Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Show me left! Right! (L-hand check)” → the L-hand is LEFT!
“Directions — polite suggestions or COMMANDS?” → clear commands: Turn left!

Assumptions: Town map + places automatic. Left/right known in Russian, shaky in English — the L-hand trick and Genki «Left And Right» fix it. Robot game needs the safety brief (slow steps!). Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Human robots · floor map
floor map

Model. You are the robot; a pupil navigates you to the baker's — slow-motion, literal obedience (walk into a “wall” if told wrong — comedy teaches precision!).

▶ Robot pairs. Navigator + robot; secret destination card; navigate across the floor map: Go straight on… turn right… STOP! Where are you? — The chemist's! Swap roles. Two rounds each.

✔ Assess: listen at the map: left/right accurate? Chunks complete? The robots' faces tell you everything.

💪 Support: navigator holds the arrow cards. ⭐ Extension: add a distance: Go straight on — two steps!
