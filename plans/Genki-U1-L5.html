🦝 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Unit 1 “Start speaking” · Lesson 5 — I, you, we are happy!
Pre-A1 preschoolers (~5 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + games + TPR (no reading / writing) · chairs + carpet + tables
Week 14.09–20.09 · Lesson 5 of 72✨ pronouns + to be12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 All by ear — no text, no phonics
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to match a pronoun to who they point at and say the be chunk — I am happy · You are happy · He/She is happy · We are happy · Everybody is happy! — recognising 8 pronoun/quantifier words (I · you · we · he · she · they · everybody · nobody) with the feelings from L2.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle L2 feelings (happy · sad · great) inside the new pronoun frame; a language-linked craft settler (group-photo collage — stick figures & chorus by pronoun); a draw-&-tell finish — draw “us” & tell We are happy!
  • Personal. Embody the grammar with grouping (I = alone, we = pair, everybody = all); every child points/speaks every round; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘; hold to max 3 game-peaks.
By the end, pupils can…
  • point to me / you / us / everybody on cue;
  • say I am happy / We are happy!;
  • chant Everybody is happy! as a group;
  • recognise he / she / they by pointing;
  • draw & tell We are happy! to teacher/partner.

Chunks
I am happy. · You are happy. · He/She is happy. · We are happy! · They are happy. · Everybody is happy! · Nobody is sad.
TPR gesture
I = point to self · you = point to partner · we = circle arms round two · he/she = point aside · everybody = big arms round all · nobody = shake head + hands out.
Pron. watch
the tiny word swap: am / are / is — model, don't drill; I'm / you're / we're as one word; keep everybody a joyful 4-beat clap: EV-ry-bo-dy.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ (grouping game): “We — do we stand alone or with a friend?” → with a friend.
ICQ (point): “Point AND… say it?” → yes!

Assumptions: children have had L1–L4 (feelings solid). Pronouns/be are abstract at 5 — we teach them physically (who you point at), NOT as grammar. Accept whole chunks by ear; don't correct am/are/is. Near-zero English beyond unit chunks · non-readers/non-writers · attention 5–7 min · group = 12, whole-group + shoulder-partner; grouping games use pairs & the whole circle.

Likely problemWhat you do
Pronouns feel meaningless (abstract)Never explain — show with the body: I = me, you = you, we = us together, everybody = the whole circle. The pointing IS the meaning.
They mix am/are/isDon't correct at 5 — the song fixes it over time. Praise the pronoun + feeling; model the correct chunk back warmly.

Shy child won't pair upSpeaky or teacher is the instant partner; doing the gesture counts more than speaking.

Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter.
“Speaky says…” — do it only when Speaky says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone freezes, reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…”.

Lesson framework — the shape of EVERY Genki preschool lesson (all 72)

Fixed skeleton — reuse this order in every plan. Keep the ⚡/🧘 rhythm, but max 2–3 game-peaks per lesson — don't over-game.

  • 1 · Greeting 🚪 🧘 — door welcome + Speaky arrives.
  • 2 · Warm-up song ⚡ — Genki Disco Warm Up (ritual + recycles earlier language).
  • 3 · Core games ⚡/🧘 — 2–3 games max, each with its song.
  • 4 · Craft / worksheet block 🧘 — language-linked (listen → do → say). Here: group-photo collage.
  • 5 · 📖 Story time 🧘 — S&S big-book (Speaky), choral refrain.
  • 6 · Workbook worksheet — draw & tell 🧘.
  • 7 · Reflection + Goodbye + Exit 🧘.
