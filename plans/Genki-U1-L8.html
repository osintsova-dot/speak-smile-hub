Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will have consolidated the whole of Unit 1 and shown they can use it with confidence in games: name (What's your name?), feelings, actions & directions (I can… · Left! Right!), pronouns (We are happy!), polite phrases (Thank you!) and seasons (It's summer!).
  • Subsidiary. A picture Bingo & a flagship team reward game (review lesson — where a competitive reward game belongs); a light achievement check (tick what each child can do); a language-linked craft (sticker-match — build your own bingo card & name each picture); a draw-&-tell finish (all-about-me showcase).
  • Personal. No new language — celebrate & confirm; teams 4 max with fast rotation (no waiting); every child speaks/moves every round; alternate ⚡/🧘; hold to max 3 game-peaks.
By the end, pupils can…
  • recognise & name any Unit 1 picture in Bingo;
  • say a chunk on cue in the reward game;
  • say name · feeling · one action · one season;
  • chant a pronoun line & a polite phrase;
  • draw & tell an “all about me” page to teacher/partner.

Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ (Bingo): “When you see YOUR picture — do we shout the word & cover it?” → yes!
ICQ (reward game): “Say the chunk FIRST, then roll/shoot?” → yes!

Assumptions: children have had all of Unit 1 (L1–L7). This is a celebration & light check — no testing pressure, no shame. Everything gesture/picture-based. Non-readers/non-writers — Bingo uses pictures, covered with counters; no words to read. Group = 12; reward game uses 3 teams of 4, cooperative. Losing rule: Try again!

Anticipated problems & solutions (finale focus)
Likely problemWhat you do
Bingo too hard / a child can't find their pictureUse only 6-picture cards (3×2) with big clear images; hold the called card up & do its gesture so they match visually. Help hands-on; nobody “loses”.
“I didn't win Bingo!”Play to “everybody wins” — keep going till every card is full; a cheer for each full card. It's about naming, not racing.
Reward game → tears on losingTeam scores together, everyone gets a turn & a cheer; Try again! — model losing happily.
Long lesson, some words forgottenRe-model with gesture, whole-group choral first. This is revision — warmth over accuracy.
Over-excitement after ⚡🧘 settler after each ⚡; volume-control arm; Freeze!

Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter.
“Speaky says…” — do it only when Speaky says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone freezes, reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…”.

Lesson framework — the shape of EVERY Genki preschool lesson (all 72)

Fixed skeleton — reuse this order in every plan. Keep the ⚡/🧘 rhythm, but max 2–3 game-peaks per lesson. Unit finale review = Bingo + ONE flagship reward game + achievement check.

  • 1 · Greeting 🚪 🧘 — door welcome + Speaky arrives.
  • 2 · Warm-up song ⚡ — Genki Disco Warm Up + full-unit medley.
  • 3 · Core games ⚡/🧘 — Bingo + flagship reward game.
  • 4 · Craft / worksheet block 🧘 — language-linked (sticker-match bingo-build).
  • 5 · 📖 Story time 🧘 — S&S big-book (Speaky), choral refrain.
  • 6 · Workbook worksheet — draw & tell 🧘 — “all about me” showcase.
  • 7 · Reflection + achievement check + Goodbye + Exit 🧘.

▶ Speaky Basketball (review reward game — review lessons only). 3 teams of 4. Speaky flips a Unit 1 card; the player says the chunk & does it — I can fly! · I'm happy! · It's winter! · Thank you! — THEN shoots a soft ball into the box. Each basket = a cheer for the whole team.

Well done! Come with me! 🚂 To the tables. Language link — listen → do → say. Give each child an empty 4-window “my bingo” base + a sheet of Unit 1 picture stickers.

Mechanic. Speaky calls one — Choose… a feeling! — each child picks & sticks a matching sticker, then names it: happy — I'm happy! Then an action, a season, a polite word. Choose → stick → say — every child engaged; they take home their own bingo card.
