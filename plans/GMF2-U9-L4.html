Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to use the irregular plurals man/men · woman/women · child/children and ask about people: “Who's that (man)? — He's my uncle. / Who are those (children)? — They're my cousins.”
  • Subsidiary. Sing «Who's that?» (CD4 17); family words return (aunt · uncle · cousins — GMF1 family upgraded); photo-talk: real/imaginary holiday photos; Talk Partners (CD4 18).
  • Personal. women /ˈwɪmɪn/ is the sneaky one — drill the VOWEL change; photo-talk should feel like real family chat, warm not drilly.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the three irregular pairs correctly;
  • ask Who's that…? / Who are those…? by counting;
  • answer with family words: “She's my aunt.”

Structure
Who's that (woman)? — She's my (aunt).  ·  Who are those (men)? — They're my (uncles).
Form
Irregular plurals — no -s! man→men, woman→women (vowel magic), child→children; that (one) / those (many).
Pron. watch
women /ˈwɪmɪn/ — the о reads as /ɪ/!; children /ˈtʃɪldrən/; those /ðəʊz/.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Two man? Two mans?” → two MEN — magic words, no -s!
“Who's that — for one or many?” → one; many → Who are THOSE?

Assumptions: Family basics (mum, dad, brother…) from GMF1; aunt/uncle/cousins are the upgrade. that/those maps onto the one/many logic trained all year (is/are, was/were).

Magic pairs (act. 1, CD4 16). Board pairs with pictures: man→men · woman→women · child→children. Chant with a clap-switch on the plural. Vowel spotlight on WOMEN /ˈwɪmɪn/ — silly-face drill ×3.
