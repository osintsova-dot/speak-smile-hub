🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 8 “Camping fun!” · Lesson 4 — Vocab+Grammar: Outdoor activities · What's he/she doing? — He's/She's riding a horse
Pre-A1 young learners (~7–8 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · New vocab + structure together (PPP + TPR/mime) · carpet + desks
Week 05.04–11.04 · Lesson 58 · продолжающая✨ VOCAB+GRAM~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4 + pairs
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 5 outdoor camp activities — sail a boat, ride a horse, play the guitar, play volleyball, sing songs (Pupil's Book p. 86) — and ask & answer about what someone is doing: “What's he/she doing? — He's/She's riding a horse.” (3rd-person present continuous).
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — clothes + “What are you wearing? — I'm wearing (shorts) and a (T-shirt)” (L1–L2), the story Fun at the camp where She's riding a horse / He's sailing a boat (L3), the weather chunks It's hot / It's raining — all woven into today's target: “He's swimming — and he's wearing shorts!”; phonics — reading the /uː/ (long u) sound across spellings ue · ew · u-e (blue · few · cute, JP Book 2); sing the coursebook song Camping is great! (CD3 49); value: everyone can have fun outdoors.
  • Personal. Model the -ing ending & the contracted He's / She's clearly, one mime per activity; give an ICQ before every game; every task keeps ALL children responding (all mime at once / all point / everyone has a card in Quiz-Quiz-Trade / pair ask-answer).
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 5 activities with a mime (sail a boat, ride a horse, play the guitar, play volleyball, sing songs);
  • ask the question: “What's he/she doing?”;
  • answer with a full chunk: “He's playing the guitar. · She's sailing a boat.”;
  • read short /uː/ words: blue · glue · few · cute.
Target language
Lexis
sail a boatride a horseplay the guitarplay volleyballsing songs (recycled: swimming — “He's swimming”; clothes — T-shirt, shorts, sun hat, swimsuit…)
Structure
What's he/she doing? — He's / She's (riding a horse). · Talk Partners: What's Dan doing? — He's swimming. · combine: She's sailing a boat. She's having fun!
Form
What + is + he/she + doing? · He/She + is (’s) + verb-ing. 3rd-person present continuous (now). Spelling: play→playing · sing→singing · ride→riding (drop “e”) · sail→sailing.
Pron. watch
contracted He's /hiːz/ · She's /ʃiːz/ (one beat, not “he is”); -ing = /ɪŋ/ (NOT “-ink”); weak “a” /ə/ in a horse.
Phonics
Jolly focus: reading /uː/ (long u) — spellings ue · ew · u-e (blue · glue · few · blew · cute · tube, JP Book 2) · tricky words: you · are.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“She's riding a horse — is she doing it NOW or yesterday?” → NOW (point to the picture, mime).
“He's playing the guitar — is it a boy or a girl?” → boy = he 👦 (a girl = she 👧).
“I ask ‘What's he doing?’ — do I answer about me or about him?” → about HIM.

Assumptions: clothes + “What are you wearing? / I'm wearing…” are solid from L1–L2; the L3 story already SHOWED “She's riding a horse / He's sailing a boat”, so 3rd-person -ing was FELT — today it gets named, drilled and used to ask & answer. Emergent readers → pictures, mime, audio, minimal print. ⚠️ КТП & BigBen list a wrong AI vocab set (riding a horse, climbing a tree, swimming, fishing, making a fire, putting up a tent); PB p. 86 core is these 5 → plan follows the book. The wrong-list “стр. 41” is the JP Book 2 page, not the Pupil's Book.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Drop the verb “is” — “He riding a horse”Drill the contraction with a clap on He's: clap-say the rhythm He's • ri-ding • a • horse; hand-squeeze gesture for the ’s.
“-ing” said as “-ink” or droppedExaggerate the /ŋ/ tail, chant each verb with its mime: play-ING 🎸, sail-ING ⛵, rid-ING 🐴.
Mix up he / sheFix a gesture: boy = he 👦 (point), girl = she 👧; hold a boy card / girl card and swap fast.
Mime game = 1 child mimes, 11 just watchUse the all-mime versions: everyone mimes at once (Freeze & guess), pair mime (RallyRobin), everyone holds a card (Quiz-Quiz-Trade); the board is a fast rotation, not a queue.
/uː/ read as separate letters (b-l-u-e)Push the digraph/split spelling as ONE sound: bl + ue → blue; picture appears for meaning; praise self-corrections.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… sit down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-mime reset.

Phonics — /uː/ read: ue · ew · u-e
at the board

Recognise — warm up the sound. Flash the /uː/ (long u) sound with the Jolly action; children voice it (not the app). Contrast: short u in cup vs long u in cute.

Blend to READ (all together). Build a word on the board, push the sound as ONE: /b/ /l/ /ue/… blue! Then glue · few · cute (3 more — keep it tight) — a picture flips in for meaning each time; show the 3 spellings of the same sound: ue (blue, glue) · ew (few, blew) · u-e (cute, tube). Kagan — Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if you HEAR /uː/… cute!… cat? (a few words — all react).

Segment · quick Showdown (protect the clock). Say ONE word → children write on mini-whiteboards → Showdown: Show me! all reveal at once, check on screen: blue · few (2 words only — today's MAIN aim is activities + What's he/she doing?). Tricky words to finish (whole-word flash, not sounded): you · are.

💪 Support: give the first sound aloud + show the picture before they blend. ⭐ Extension: add glue / tube, or write the whole word with no letter-cards on show.
