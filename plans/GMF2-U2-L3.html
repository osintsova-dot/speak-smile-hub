Week 12.10–18.10 · Lesson 13✨ Story + value: saying “please”~10 pupils · groups of 3–4Pupil's Book pp.20–21 · AB p.18
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow, read and act out the story «The naughty parrot», using the shop language in context: “Can I have…, please? / Of course! / Here you are.”
  • Subsidiary. Story skills: predict → gist listen → read along → sequence → act; value talk — remember to say please; meal words in context (breakfast · lunch · dinner); true/false comprehension (AB format).
  • Personal. Big theatre today — parrot voice is a gift, use it; every child gets a story job every run.
By the end, pupils can…
  • retell the story in 4–5 sequenced steps;
  • read their role's lines;
  • say what they eat for breakfast/lunch/dinner (bridge task).

Story · listen & read
carpet

Predict. Cover frame: Look — who's on the shelf? What does he want?

Listen 1 — gist (CD1 36, books closed). Listen: WHAT does the parrot take? Harvest answers.

Listen 2 — read along (pp.20–21). Pause per frame: What do they buy for lunch? Who takes it?! Class gasps on cue — train the gasp, it keeps attention.

Check. True/false paddles ×6: The parrot says please… true or false? — all vote.

 Pupil's Book pp.20–21 · CD1 36 · paddles
20–27′
Sequence it
desks

Solo → pair. Frame-strips (5 mini-pictures) per child: order them silently (60 sec), then compare with partner and agree; final check against the book.

Retell chain. Around the circle, one step each: First — they buy bread… Then — the parrot takes it! Sentence frames on board (First / Then / Next / Finally).

✔ Assess: the solo ordering shows who followed the plot without support — note the ones who waited to copy.

27–39′
Act out
carpet, groups of 4

Model a frame with a pupil as the parrot (squawk training!).

Groups of 4: Jake · Molly · shopkeeper · parrot. Two frames per group, books as scripts. Stage-whisper rehearsal, rotate the parrot once.

Showtime. Each group performs one frame; audience task: shout NAUGHTY PARROT! at the theft moment.

Value. The parrot doesn't say please. Is that OK? What do WE say? — 30-second harvest, recast: Please! Thank you!
