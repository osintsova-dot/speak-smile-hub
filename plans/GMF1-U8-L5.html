🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 8 “Camping fun!” · Lesson 5 — Phonics · Culture · Literacy: A Postcard
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Phonics + CLIL culture + guided literacy · carpet + desks
Week 05.04–11.04 · Lesson 59L5 Phon+Cult+Litпродолжающие · ~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the /v/ sound (Toby's tongue twister “Vicky the vet loves volleyball”), understand what British children do when they go camping (camp in the garden, sing songs round the campfire, sleep in tents, tell stories) and read & make their own holiday postcard (Dear … / I'm at a fantastic camp / See you soon).
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning inside the topic — Unit 8 clothes + outdoor activities + “What's he/she doing? — He's/She's (swimming / riding a horse)” (L1–L4) applied to camping; Jolly track — quick recognition review of the known letter-sounds in class + the y = /ee/ ending (funny · muddy · jolly, JP Book 2) → differentiated homework, to protect pace; sight words loves · my.
  • Personal. Keep the craft stage tight (timer + a SHORT board frame); model writing letter-by-letter on the board; every child responds in every task (own postcard / show-me buzz / thumbs vote) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tongue twister with a clear English /v/ (teeth on lip) and spot words with the /v/ sound;
  • name 2–3 things British children do when they go camping;
  • read a simple holiday postcard and make their own (copy a short frame + draw the campsite);
  • write the first letter-sound they hear (incl. V v).

Structure
Vicky the vet loves volleyball.  ·  What's he/she doing? — He's/She's (swimming / riding a horse).  ·  In the summer we camp in the garden.  ·  Postcard: Dear …, I'm at a fantastic camp. I swim in the lake every day. See you soon, …
Form
Initial/medial /v/ + word. Postcard = fixed frame (Dear… / I'm at a… / See you soon), copied not built. Present continuous He's/She's + -ing — recycled chunk.
Pron. watch
English /v/ — top teeth gently on the bottom lip, a voiced buzz; not the rounded-lip /w/. Keep it clear in vet (not “wet”), volleyball (not “wolleyball”), van; loves ends /vz/.
Phonics
Coursebook sound: /v/ (PB p. 87, CD3 52). Jolly track: in-class recognition review of known letter-sounds + the y = /ee/ ending (funny·muddy·jolly, JP Book 2) → HW. Sight words: loves · my.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold the postcard: Is this a book, or a postcard you send? → A postcard! Do we send it from school, or from our holidays? → Holidays!
The /v/ sound — teeth on your lip and buzz, or round lips like /w/? → teeth on lip + a voiced buzz (vet, not wet).

Assumptions: clothes + “What are you wearing?” + outdoor activities + “What's he/she doing?” known from U8 L1–L4; children can copy letters but not write freely → all writing is trace/copy from a board frame. Emergent readers: the postcard is read together, finger-following. “Alice the camel” + the camping role-play are new — model first.

Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Phonics — /v/ + sound review
at the board

Recognise /v/ (~2½′). Toby's tongue twister (PB p. 87, CD3 52): v – v – v. Vicky the vet loves volleyball! Teeth-on-lip gesture; a voiced buzz — not the rounded-lip /w/ (contrast wet → vet). Choral ×3: slow → normal → fast. 2–3 brave solos with the “microphone”. 💪 Support: hold up the /v/ picture-anchor card (a van / a vet) as the sound cue — weak pupils watch it, not the letter.

🎮 Projector: Phonics Studio — Mode 1 · Sound recognition 🔨 to build
  • Screen: known letter-sounds scattered on the board; ONE lights up at a time.
  • Kids: say the highlighted sound + its Jolly action. Children voice the sound — the app does NOT.
  • Teacher: tap to highlight the next; 6–8 sounds only, recognition, not writing (~1½′). Keep it a quick warm-review — the full letter-sound writing moves to differentiated homework.

“v or not v?” — whole-class show-me (~2′, all react). Pictures one by one — drive · love · vegetable · flower · seven · ball · vet · DVD (AB p. 84 set). Has the /v/ sound → everyone stands and buzzes v-v-v; no /v/ → sit, hands on knees. Show me! Fast, nobody just watches.

🎮 Projector: Flashcards — “v or not v?” ✅ ready
  • Screen: big picture flashcards, one tap = next card.
  • Kids: whole class stands+buzzes for /v/ words, sits for the rest — every child reacts at once.
  • Teacher: use the existing Flashcards tool; exaggerate the sound (vvv-vet).

Blend for meaning (~2′). Push sounds together with picture support: /v/ /e/ /t/… vet! /v/ /a/ /n/… van! /s/ /e/ /v/ /e/ /n/… seven! Children echo + do the action.

🎮 Projector: Phonics Studio — Mode 2 · Blending 🔨 to build
  • Screen: letters v · e · t slide together → vet, then a picture pops up for meaning.
  • Kids: say each sound as it moves, then the whole word + action.
  • Teacher: control the slide speed. Single sounds stay silent (teacher voices them); the whole word MAY be spoken by the app (browser speech).

Segment — CHILDREN break the word (~2½′, analyse + write). No teacher spelling: YOU break it! vet → say it slowly, tap it on your fingers. Class taps /v/ /e/ /t/ → then write the FIRST letter on mini-whiteboards → Show me! 3 rounds: vet + van + one earlier word (sun→s). 💪 Support: write just the first letter. ⭐ Extension: write the WHOLE word (v-e-t / v-a-n), or a capital V.

🎮 Projector: Phonics Studio — Mode 3 · Segmenting dictation 🔨 to build
  • Screen: a picture + a “say it” button; after kids write, the word appears letter-by-letter for self-check.
  • Kids: hear the whole word → segment it themselves → write → check their own board against the screen.
  • Teacher: press “say it” (whole word). The app never voices separate sounds — segmenting is the children's thinking job.

Tricky / sight words (~1½′). Flash cards loves · my + the y = /ee/ ending funny · muddy · jolly: Look — the y at the END says /ee/: funn-y, mudd-y, joll-y! And we just KNOW these: loves… my. Choral ×2 + air-write. Then straight to the song — keep the pace up.

Optional / fast finishers: ▶ What's my word? (AB p. 84 ex. 3) — pairs guessing game with the v-picture set: Is it seven? — No, it isn't. Is it a DVD? — Yes, it is! both partners speak, then swap. · Kinaesthetic swap for “v or not v?”: a Fly-swatters reskin where everyone has a paper swatter and all “swat the air” on hearing a /v/ word (not two racers at the board).

Culture — camping in Britain
carpet → circle

Listen & look (PB p. 88, CD4 1). Photo by photo: In the summer, we camp in the garden. It's good fun. → point: Dad plays the guitar and we sing songs. We sleep in our sleeping bags in big tents. At night, we tell stories — they're scary!

▶ Sing “Alice the camel” (CD4 2) — everyone joins in. The children's favourite campfire song: count the humps down with fingers — Alice the camel has five humps… So go, Alice, go! Boom! Boom! Boom! … down to no humps — now Alice is a HORSE! (sit down fast on “horse”). Fun, whole class.

▶ Let's go camping! — everyone joins in (role-play). All “put up a tent” (mime), sit round a pretend campfire (a torch). ICQ: Round the campfire — quiet story voice or a shout? — Quiet! Hum a tune, then a tiny scary story together: …and then — BOO! Every child mimes, sings, listens.

Think about YOUR culture (all vote — thumbs). And YOU? Do you go camping? Do you swim in the lake? Do you play in the sea? — thumbs up / down each question, everyone answers.

Literacy — a postcard
desks

Before you read (PB p. 89 ex. 1). What can you see on the postcard? Circle and say! — a lake · a forest · boats · a town · a farm · horses · a playground. Point & name together.

Listen & read the postcard (CD4 4) — fingers follow. Read Sue's postcard together: Dear Sally, I'm at a fantastic campsite with my family. There's a beautiful forest and a big lake. I swim in the lake and I sail a boat every day. It's hot and sunny. I'm wearing my new swimsuit. See you soon, Sue. CCQ: Is Sue happy? — Yes! Is it hot or cold? — Hot!

Make YOUR postcard (literacy product). Fold an A6 card. Front — draw the campsite (a lake, a forest, a tent — a landscape). Back — copy the SHORT frame from the board (written LARGE, line by line): Dear […], I'm at a fantastic camp. I swim every day. See you soon, […] and draw a stamp. 💪 Support: dotted-line strips to trace + teacher scribes for the weakest. ⭐ Extension: add a weather line It's hot and sunny. or I'm wearing my new swimsuit. Timer 8 min, countdown finish: 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down!
