🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 1 “Ready for school!” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Phonics · carpet + desks
Week 14.09–20.09 · Lesson 4✨ NEW~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4🦸 Phonics Hero ур.10 · Заглавные A–Z
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 classroom objects and ask & answer where a thing is using “Where's my (pencil)? — It's here!”
  • Subsidiary. Review greetings / colours / shapes (fluency mingle); phonics (recognise → blend → segment); listen for detail (sequence pictures to the song); pre-literacy & fine motor (word–picture, stickers).
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; give an ICQ before every task; run a stirrer→settler rhythm with songs and signals.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 classroom objects;
  • ask & answer “Where's my…? — It's here!”;
  • blend & segment target CVC words.
Target language
Lexis

Structure
Where's my (object)? — It's here!  ·  What's this? — It's a (…)
Form
Where + 's (is) + my + noun? → It + 's + here. Connected “Where's” /weəz/.
Pron. watch
final /g/ in bag; /ʃ/ in sharpener; stress on where + object.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.10: заглавные буквы A–Z — «звучит так же, как маленькая, просто нарядная»: имена (Sam!) и начало предложения; match CAPITAL ↔ small (тетрадь стр.26–27) · sight words: me, be, my.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Is the pencil near or far?” → near → “It's here.”
“Do I want to know where it is?” → yes.

Assumptions: numbers, colours, shapes & greetings are known from last year (Starter = revision). Pupils are non-/emergent readers → rely on pictures & audio, not printed text. Carpet routines are known. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Confuse rubber / rulerPresent as a contrasting pair with one clear gesture each.
“Where's my…” said too fastBack-chain: …pencil → …my pencil → Where's my pencil?
Final /g/ in bag droppedShort exaggerated choral drill.
Over-excitement on the carpetUse a settle signal + follow every stirrer with a settler.
Teams argue over cardsAssign the 4 roles inside each team; rotate.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Speed reading. Hub «⚡ Speed reading» / letter strip: the small letters they know — Read with me — fast! (whole class, choral, there and back).

Meet the CAPITALS (тетрадь стр.26). Show a and A: Same sound! The capital is just… fancy! «Заглавная звучит так же — просто нарядная»: for names — Sam! Pat! [Masha]! — and the start of a sentence. Air-draw a few: small → BIG.

▶ Take Off–Touch Down: flash letter cards — CAPITAL → stand up tall; small → crouch small. 8–10 cards, fast — every child moves every time.

🎮 Phonics Hub (projector): speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/ — Station 2 «Заглавные — matching» (строчная ↔ ЗАГЛАВНАЯ): a capital appears — the class calls the letter + sound, a rotating child taps its small partner. ✅ ready. Offline: card pairs on the board ledge.

Write — тетрадь стр.26–27 (desks). №1 обведи заглавные (name the letter + sound as you trace) · №2 пишем: A · B · C + the word SAT — start at the top, capitals always go top-down! Then стр.27 №2 «Соедини пары» (G–g, R–r, D–d, Q–q, E–e, B–b). Colour the star «Я молодец!»

Sight words. Flash me · be · my: chorus, air-write. Tie-in: Where's MY pencil? — today's question, now in print!

44–53′
Ask & Find
carpet, in pairs

Model (board). ▶ Find it! (find-it-u1) tap Speaky — 🦝 asks Where's my [crayon]? the class finds it on screen → It's here! (2–3 times, to model the phrase).

▶ Memory · Where's my…? — in pairs, on the carpet. Each pair has 4–5 object cards. Child 1 shows a card, names it — It's a pen. — and places it face-down. Repeat for all 4–5 (show → name → place).

When all are down, Child 1 asks: Where's my pen? Child 2 tries — from memory, first try — to turn over the right card. Correct → It's here! and keeps it. Then swap roles. (Most cards wins.)

Reset if it gets loud: Freeze!

🎮 Digital option (projector): class-memo — S&S memory pairs on screen, 1–4 teams: a rotating child flips two cards, the whole class names every picture aloud — It's a pencil! Swap it in for one round of this game when the screen is free.
