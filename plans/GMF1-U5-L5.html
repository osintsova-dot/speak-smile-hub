🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 5 “Fun on the farm” · Lesson 5 — Phonics · Culture · Literacy: Animal Riddles
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Phonics + CLIL culture + guided literacy · carpet + desks · continuing group
Week 25.01–31.01 · Lesson 38L5 Phon+Cult+Lit~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the /ʃ/ sound (Toby's tongue twister “Shush! Don't shout, sheep!”), understand what an urban farm in Britain is like (visit the farm — feed the goats, brush the pony, collect the eggs, look at the garden) and read & write their own animal riddle (I've got … legs. I can … I can't … I'm … What am I?).
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning inside the topic — this unit's farm animals + actions + Can you see a…? / (Ducks) can fly. (Cows) can't climb. (L1–L4) applied to /ʃ/ words (sheep!) and to the riddles; Jolly track — quick recognition review of the known letter-sounds incl. the recent vowel digraphs /ai/ /ee/ /ie/ (fuller written digraph dictation → differentiated homework, to protect pace); revise colour words black · white · brown (used in the riddles) + love · another.
  • Personal. Keep the writing stage tight (timer + word-bank frame on the board); model writing line-by-line; every child responds in every task (own riddle card / hush-or-freeze show-me / thumbs vote) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tongue twister with a clear soft English /ʃ/ and spot words that begin with sh;
  • name 2–3 things you can do on a British urban farm (feed the goats, collect eggs…);
  • read a simple animal riddle and write & say their own (copy the frame + choose words);
  • write the sh at the start of a word they hear (ship, shop, fish).

Structure
Shush! Don't shout, sheep!  ·  Can you see a (goat)? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't.  ·  (Ducks) can fly. (Cows) can't climb.  ·  Riddle: I've got (two) legs. I can (swim). I can't (climb). I'm (small and brown). What am I?
Form
Initial sh /ʃ/ + word. Riddle = fixed frame (I've got… / I can… / I'm… / What am I?). Chunks copied & slotted, not built.
Pron. watch
English /ʃ/ — a soft “shhh”, finger to the lips like hushing a sleeping baby, lips pushed forward and round. Keep it gentle in shush, sheep, shop, fish — not the harder Russian «ш» and not «щ».
Phonics
Coursebook sound: sh /ʃ/ (PB p. 55, CD2 47). Jolly track: in-class recognition review of known letter-sounds incl. recent digraphs /ai/ /ee/ /ie/ (fuller written digraph dictation → HW, JP Book 2 p. 19, y /ie/: my·sky·shy·flying). Revise colour sight-words black·white·brown + love·another.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold a riddle: Is this a story or a riddle? → A riddle! A riddle — do we just read the answer, or GUESS it? → Guess it!
The sh sound — a hard «ш», or a quiet shhh with a finger on your lips? → soft shhh (English /ʃ/), lips round.
Can a duck fly? — Yes, it can! Can a cow climb? — No, it can't! (checks recycled can/can't).

Assumptions: farm animals + actions + Can you see…? / can·can't known from L1–L4; colours black·white·brown known → all writing is copy from a board frame + word bank. Emergent readers: the riddle is read together, finger-following. The sh sound and the urban-farm role-play are new — model first.

“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Phonics — sh /ʃ/ + sound review
at the board

Recognise sh /ʃ/ (~2½′). Toby's tongue twister (PB p. 55, CD2 47): sh – sh – sh. Shush! Don't shout, sheep! Finger-to-lips gesture; soft quiet “shhh”, lips round — not a hard «ш». Choral ×3: slow → normal → fast. 2–3 brave solos with the “microphone”. Support: hold up the sh picture-anchor card (a sheep) as the sound cue — weak pupils watch it, not the letters.

🎮 Projector: Phonics Studio — Mode 1 · Sound recognition 🔨 to build
  • Screen: known letter-sounds + recent digraphs (/ai/ /ee/ /ie/) scattered on the board; ONE lights up at a time.
  • Kids: say the highlighted sound + its Jolly action. Children voice the sound — the app does NOT.
  • Teacher: tap to highlight the next; 6–8 sounds only, recognition, not writing (~1½′). Quick warm-review — the fuller written digraph dictation moves to differentiated homework.

“sh or not sh?” — whole-class show-me (~2′, all react). Pictures one by one — sheep · shark · sun · shop · hat · shell · sock · short (AB p. 52 set). Begins with sh → everyone puts a finger to their lips and hushes shhh; not sh → sit, hands on knees. Show me! Fast, nobody just watches.

🎮 Projector: Flashcards — “sh or not sh?” ✅ ready
  • Screen: big picture flashcards, one tap = next card.
  • Kids: whole class hushes “shhh” for sh-words, sits for the rest — every child reacts at once.
  • Teacher: use the existing Flashcards tool; exaggerate the first sound.

Blend for meaning (~2′). Push sounds together with picture support: /sh/ /o/ /p/… shop! /sh/ /i/ /p/… ship! /f/ /i/ /sh/… fish! Children echo + do the action.

🎮 Projector: Phonics Studio — Mode 2 · Blending 🔨 to build
  • Screen: letters sh · o · p slide together → shop, then a picture pops up for meaning.
  • Kids: say each sound as it moves, then the whole word + action.
  • Teacher: control the slide speed. Single sounds stay silent (teacher voices them); the whole word MAY be spoken by the app (browser speech).

Segment — CHILDREN break the word (~2½′, analyse + write). No teacher spelling: YOU break it! shop → say it slowly, tap it on your fingers. Class taps /sh/ /o/ /p/ → then write the FIRST sound sh on mini-whiteboards → Show me! 3 rounds: shop · ship · fish. Support: write just sh. Extension: write the WHOLE word (sh-o-p), or a capital Sh.

🎮 Projector: Phonics Studio — Mode 3 · Segmenting dictation 🔨 to build
  • Screen: a picture + a “say it” button; after kids write, the word appears letter-by-letter for self-check.
  • Kids: hear the whole word → segment it themselves → write → check their own board against the screen.
  • Teacher: press “say it” (whole word). The app never voices separate sounds — segmenting is the children's thinking job.

Colour / tricky words (~1½′). Flash cards black · white · brown (we'll need them for the riddles!) + love · another: We just KNOW these — look, say: black… white… brown… Choral ×2 + air-write. Then straight to the farm — keep the pace up.

Optional / fast finishers: ▶ Fast finger! (PB p. 55 ex 3 / AB p. 52 ex 3) — in pairs, Pupil A says a sh-word, Pupil B points to it fast, then swap; everyone plays at once. · Kinaesthetic swap for “sh or not sh?”: a Fly-swatters reskin where everyone has a paper swatter and all “swat the air” on hearing a sh-word (not two racers at the board). · A floor Hopscotch of picture squares — hop only on sh-pictures — as an alt stir if space allows.

Literacy — an animal riddle
desks

Before you read (PB p. 57 ex 1). Farm animals with TWO legs — and with FOUR legs. Point and tell me! — quick sort: two legs (duck, hen), four legs (cow, horse, sheep, goat, pig, dog). Everyone points.

Listen & read the riddles (CD2 52) — fingers follow. Read Mrs Green's blog riddles together: I've got two legs. I can swim. I can fly. I'm small and brown. What am I? → guess: A duck! · I've got four legs. I can walk. I can't climb. I'm big. I'm black and white. What am I? → A cow! CCQ: Read the answer or GUESS it? — Guess!

Write YOUR animal riddle (literacy product). Each child secretly picks a farm animal and copies the frame from the board (written LARGE + word bank), one line at a time, then draws it on the back: I've got [two/four] legs. I can [swim]. I can't [climb]. I'm [big/small] and [brown]. What am I? Support: dotted-line strips to trace + teacher scribes for the weakest. Extension: add a second ability or colour (AB p. 54 frame). Timer 8 min, countdown finish: 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down!

Create — links back to phonics (fast finishers). On the card add a sh picture (sheep · shark · shop): Draw something that starts with sh-sh-shhh — and tell me its name! — a mini creative check of the sound.
