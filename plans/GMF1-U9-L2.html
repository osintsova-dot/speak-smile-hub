🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 9 “Day and night” · Lesson 2 — Grammar: I (wash my face) every day. Me, too.
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Dialogue → controlled drill → freer → production · carpet + desks
Week 19.04 · Lesson 63✨ GRAMMAR~12 pupils · pairs + 3 teams🦸 Phonics Hero · свободное чтение: истории Части 2
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to talk about their daily routines with the present simple + every day: “I wash my face every day.” and agree “Me, too.” (Pupil's Book p. 93 dialogue), and act out the “getting ready” dialogue.
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve real prior learning — the 10 daily-routine actions just met in L1 (get up, wash my face, brush my teeth…) re-used inside today's structure (“I go to school every day. — Me, too!”); gently contrast with Unit 8's now language (“He's washing his face” = now → “I wash my face every day” = always); phonics — Phonics Hero свободное чтение: истории Части 2 (тетрадь стр.32–51) — the digraph-story carousel; listen for detail (PB p. 93 ex. 2 “Listen and tick”).
  • Personal. Keep the FULL habit sentence (“I wash my face every day”, not just “wash my face”); don't add a wrong -s to I (“I wash”, not “I washes”); every task keeps ALL children responding (own cards / mime together / all pairs at once) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say what they do every day: “I (brush my teeth) every day.”;
  • agree with a partner “Me, too.” and act out the getting-ready dialogue;
  • read 2–3 digraph stories from Part 2 aloud with a partner.

Structure
I (wash my face) every day. — Me, too.  ·  And I (brush my teeth) every day. — Me, too.  ·  Are you ready? — Yes, I am. Let's play!
Form
Present simple for habits: I + verb + every day. No third-person here → keep the plain verb after I (I wash, not “washes”). Agreement chunk: Me, too. = “I do that too.”
Pron. watch
Link “every day” /ˈevri deɪ/ — don't drop it; Me, too /miː tuː/ with a warm falling tone ↘; keep the sentence whole, not just the verb chunk.
Phonics
Phonics Hero — свободное чтение, истории Части 2 (стр.32–51): «Коза и лодка» (стр.35) · «Пчёлы и пирог» (стр.38) · «Король поёт» (стр.41) · «Кот и луна» (стр.42) · «Fish and chips» (стр.45) · «Королева и туча» (стр.48) · «Звезда над фермой» (стр.51) · tricky words by sight: said, some.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Point to a clock & the week: “I wash my face on Monday… and Tuesday… and every day?” → yes! → “every day” = all the days, always.
Contrast with Unit 8: mime washing NOW — “Am I doing it now or every day?” → now → “He's washing.” Then: “But every day?” → “I wash my face every day.”
“Me, too” — you say “I brush my teeth every day”; a child does it as well → “Me, too!” (means I do that too).
ph: “Do p and h come together? What do they say?” → not /p/-/h/ → they say /f/ — phone!

Assumptions: the 10 routine actions and the song “It's a beautiful day” were first met in L1; the whole Phonics Hero programme (ур.1–31) is finished — this week is a free-reading festival; Unit 8's present-continuous “What's he doing? — He's …ing” is fresh (useful contrast). Pupils are non-/emergent readers → picture grids and audio, not printed text. Carpet routines & pair-work signals known. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Drops the time phrase: says just “wash my face”Two-hand gesture: verb (one hand) + every day! (other hand, big sweep). No sentence counts until every day is on it; back-chain …every day → wash my face every day → I wash my face every day.
Adds a wrong -s: “I washes my face”Thumb to chest for I → plain verb; “I is easy — I wash, I brush, I go.” Chorus the clean form; don't over-explain third person (that's later).
Confuses now and every day (says “I'm washing every day”)Two picture cards — a clock = now (…ing), a calendar = every day (plain verb). Point to the calendar: “Every day → I wash.”
ph said as /p/ + /h/ (misses /f/)“p and h make a NEW sound — /f/, like phone”; bite the lip for /f/: ph… phone, photo; contrast p /p/ (pen) vs ph /f/ (phone).
“Me, too” said flat, or child just copies silentlyModel the warm tone + thumbs-to-chest gesture; rule “agree → SAY it: Me, too!”; teacher gestures “speak” before the point.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

▶ Mime it! — I … every day
whole room / teams

▶ Крокодил / Charades (Игротека “Крокодил”) — daily-routine version, everyone stays busy, nobody sits out. Class in 3 teams on the carpet.

How it runs (all react). One child draws a routine card and mimes it (no words). Their whole team calls the sentence together: You wash your face every day! → the mimer confirms as themselves: Yes! I wash my face every day! and everyone who does it too shouts Me, too! Point for a correct full sentence. Fast rotation — a new mimer every 20–30 sec so every child gets a turn.

Kagan — Quiz-Quiz-Trade energy: keep it a whole-team chorus, not one guesser — the whole team says the sentence so all mouths move. Reset if loud: Freeze!
