🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 4 “Fantastic food!” · Lesson 5 — Phonics · Culture · Literacy: A Lunch Menu
Pre-A1 young learners (~7–8 y.o., emergent readers · continuing line) · 60 min · Phonics + CLIL culture + guided literacy · carpet + desks
Week 07.12–13.12 · Lesson 29L5 Phon+Cult+Lit~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4🦸 Phonics Hero ур.23a · Magic E: повторение
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the /dʒ/ sound (Toby's tongue twister “j – j – j. Jim juggles jam, juice and jelly”, PB p. 45), understand a traditional English breakfast (mix the eggs · make the toast · cut the tomatoes · cook the food · take it to Mum) and read a lunch menu and make their own mini lunch menu (Day + salad · soup · chicken · fruit · ice cream…).
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning inside the topic — Unit 4 food + fruit (L1–L4) with “I like / don't like / love… · Do you like…? — Yes, I do. / No, I don't.” applied to the /dʒ/ words & the menu; Phonics Hero ур.23 (a) — Magic E повторение: transform → sort ✊/🖐 → sort by houses a-e/i-e/o-e/u-e (тетрадь стр.64); tricky words would · put.
  • Personal. Keep the menu stage tight (timer + ONE day per menu, copied from a board frame + drawing); model writing letter-by-letter on the board; every child responds in every task (own menu / juggle-gesture / thumbs vote) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tongue twister with a clear English /dʒ/ and spot words that begin with /dʒ/;
  • name 2–3 things in a traditional English breakfast;
  • read a simple lunch menu and make their own mini lunch menu (Day + foods + draw);
  • transform & sort Magic-E words: can → cane, houses a-e / i-e / o-e / u-e (стр.64).
Target language
Lexis

Structure
j – j – j. Jim juggles jam, juice and jelly.  ·  Do you like (chicken)? — Yes, I do. / No, I don't.  ·  I like / don't like / love (…).  ·  Let's make a traditional English breakfast. I mix the eggs. Dad cuts the tomatoes.  ·  We've got (soup) on (Monday).
Form
Initial /dʒ/ + word. Magic e: u-e (silent e → long u: cube). Menu = day + food list. Fixed chunks, copied not built.
Pron. watch
English /dʒ/ — a soft, voiced “j” in Jim, jam, juice (a “dzh”). NOT the Russian «ж» (жук), and don't devoice it to «ч». Keep it in Jim, jam, juggle, juice, jelly.
Phonics
Coursebook sound: /dʒ/ (PB p. 45, CD2 28–29) · Phonics Hero ур.23 (a) — Magic E повторение: can → cane · pin → pine · hop → hope · tub → tube, sort ✊/🖐 (cap/cape, pin/pine, cub/cube), sort by houses a-e/i-e/o-e/u-e (тетрадь стр.64). Tricky words: would · put.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold a picture: Is this jam or ham? → jam! jam — does it start with «дж» or «ж»? → soft «дж» (English /dʒ/), voiced.
Hold the menu: Is this a story or a menu? → a menu! What's for lunch on Monday? → read & point.

Assumptions: food + fruit vocab and “Do you like…?” known from L1–L4; children can copy letters but not write freely → the menu is copied from a board frame + drawn. Emergent readers: menu & breakfast texts are read together, finger-following. Magic e is new → recognition only in class, dictation to HW. The English-breakfast role-play is new — model first.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
English /dʒ/ said as Russian «ж»Model the contrast jam (дж) not «жам»; hand on throat to feel the voice; “juggle it — dzh-dzh!”.
Can't tell j-words from othersPicture anchors (jam·jug·jump·juggle) + juggle-or-freeze show-me; exaggerate the initial sound.
Menu copying slow / letters unknownBoard frame written LARGE, ONE day only; dotted-line strips for weakest writers; teacher scribes.
Over-excited in breakfast role-play / mixerRule first + ICQ: In the kitchen — careful or crazy? — Careful! Use the volume-control arm to settle.
Magic-E houses mixed up in the sort (стр.64 №3)Anchor: say the vowel's NAME first (bike — /ай/ — house i-e); pairs check each word (RallyCoach); unfinished rows go home.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”

10–22′
Phonics — /dʒ/ + sound review
at the board

Recognise /dʒ/ (~2½′). Toby's tongue twister (PB p. 45, CD2 28): j – j – j. Jim juggles jam, juice and jelly! Juggling-hands gesture; soft voiced “dzh”, hand on throat to feel the buzz — not the Russian «ж». Choral ×3: slow → normal → fast. 2–3 brave solos with the “microphone”. Support: hold up the /dʒ/ picture-anchor card (a jar of jam) as the sound cue — weak pupils watch it, not the letter.

“j or not j?” — whole-class show-me (~2′, all react). Pictures one by one — jeans · cherries · jacket · jug · jump · jam · girl · juggle (AB p. 42 set). Begins with /dʒ/ → everyone stands and juggles the air, dzh-dzh; not /dʒ/ → sit, hands on knees. Show me! Fast, nobody just watches.

🎮 Projector: Flashcards — “j or not j?” ✅ ready
  • Screen: big picture flashcards, one tap = next card.
  • Kids: whole class stands+juggles for /dʒ/ words, sits for the rest — every child reacts at once.
  • Teacher: use the existing Flashcards tool; exaggerate the first sound.

Преврати слово — тетрадь стр.64 №1 (~2′, all read both). The whole Magic-E family in four transformations: can → cane · pin → pine · hop → hope · tub → tube — read the short word ✊, wave the wand, read the magic word 🖐. Kagan — RallyRobin: A short word, B magic word, swap.

Sort ✊/🖐 — стр.64 №2 «Раздели слова» (all vote, then write). Six words — cap · cape · pin · pine · cub · cube: read together → all vote ✊/🖐 → check → everyone writes the word into the right column in the тетрадь.

Houses — стр.64 №3 «Раздели» (a-e · i-e · o-e · u-e, in pairs). Word bank cake · bike · nose · cube · late · time · home · flute… — pairs sort 6–8 words into the four houses in the тетрадь (the rest go home). RallyCoach: partner checks each word: Good job!

Tricky words (~1½′). Flash cards would · put (+ recycled like · love): We just KNOW these — look, say: would… put… like… love. Choral ×2 + air-write. Then straight to the food song — keep the pace up.

Culture — English breakfast
carpet → circle

Listen & look (PB p. 46, CD2 31). Photo by photo: It's Sunday morning. Let's make a traditional English breakfast! → point: Here are the ingredients: eggs, mushrooms, baked beans, tomatoes and bread. I mix the eggs. Lucy makes the toast. Dad cuts the tomatoes. Dad cooks the food. We take the breakfast to Mum. She's very happy!

▶ Let's make the breakfast! — everyone joins in (TPR role-play). Stand in a circle = the kitchen; each action together: Mix the eggs… (whisk!) …make the toast… (pop!) …cut the tomatoes… (chop!) …cook the food… (sizzle!) …take it to Mum! ICQ: In the kitchen — careful or crazy? — Careful! Every child mimes every step.

Think about YOUR culture (all vote — thumbs). And you — what do YOU have for breakfast? Eggs? Cereal? Toast? Juice? — thumbs up / down each one, everyone answers.

35–47′
Literacy — a lunch menu
desks

Before you read (PB p. 47). Look — Munch! Munch! I like a healthy lunch! How many days is the menu for? — Five days! Point to Monday–Friday.

Listen & read the lunch menu (CD2 33) — fingers follow. Read across the days together: On Monday — salad, meat and vegetables, jelly. On Wednesday — soup, meat and rice, ice cream… CCQ: What's for lunch on Friday? — Soup, fish and salad, carrot cake!

Make YOUR lunch menu (literacy product). On an A5 card, each child writes ONE day at the top (copy from the board frame, written LARGE) and copies/draws 3 foods for that day's lunch — like the book. Support: dotted-line strips to trace + teacher scribes for the weakest. Extension: add a second day, or start each food word with its capital letter. Timer 8 min, countdown finish: 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down!

Create — hide a /dʒ/ food (fast finishers, links to phonics). On the back of the card: Draw a j-food — jam or juice or jelly — and tell me its name! They show & say it at the door — a mini creative check of the sound.
