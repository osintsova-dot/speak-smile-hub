Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the ur/ir tongue twister («Is the purple turtle's birthday on the first or the third?»), name 4 British festival days with dates, and say the traditional rhyme «Thirty days hath September» (adapted).
  • Subsidiary. Textbook phonics: ur/ir = /ɜː/; culture — Christmas Day (25th Dec), Bonfire Night (5th Nov), April Fools' (1st Apr), Carnival (Aug) ↔ our holidays; literacy — a traditional rhyme: rhythm + месяцы по 30/31 дню.
  • Personal. Dates from L4 get their workout here — every festival lands on the calendar; the rhyme is a memory gift — send it home proudly.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the twister fast ×3;
  • name 3+ UK festivals with their dates;
  • recite the rhyme's first four lines with the class.

Pron. watch
/ɜː/ in pURple, tURtle, bIRthday, fIRst, thIRd — one sound family; rhythm of the rhyme drives stress.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“purple and first — same sound? Same letters?” → same /ɜː/, different letters.
“All months have 31 days?” → no — the rhyme tells us which!

Assumptions: Dates fresh from L4 — the culture block applies them immediately. Rhyme memorisation by rhythm suits early readers. April Fools' jokes need boundaries (kind jokes!).

Block 3 · Literacy · The rhyme
carpet → desks

Listen & read (CD3 17). PB p.67; follow the lines; knee-clap the rhythm on the second listen.

Learn 4 lines. Echo-build line by line with the clap: There are thirty days in September, April, June and November… Whisper → normal → proud-loud.

Fist calendar check. Knuckle trick (костяшки = 31): check 3 months — the rhyme is TRUE!

Write (AB p.64 seed, notebooks). Fill the missing month-words in the printed rhyme frame; illustrate one month.

💪 Support: chorus carries everyone; frame is copy-fill. ⭐ Extension: recite all 4 lines solo for the class.
