🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Starter Unit “Hello, friends!” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuing line) · 60 min · FIRST LESSON OF THE YEAR · Welcome + reactivation + Phonics · carpet + desks
Week 03.09–06.09✨ NEW · Hello / What's your name? — I'm… + class signals as year rituals~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4Pupil's Book p.4 · AB p.2
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to greet and introduce themselves — “Hello! What's your name? — I'm (Jake). Goodbye.” — and confidently retrieve numbers 1–10 (this lesson's anchor), reactivating them after the summer in the friendly market context.
  • Subsidiary. Sing What's your name? & say The ten balloons chant; re-warm colours, shapes (circle · square · triangle) & days of the week woven into greetings; phonics — Phonics Hero: повтор ур.1–3 (s·a·t · i·p·n · c·k·e, тетрадь стр.3–9) — speed-reading reactivation (they KNOW these sounds — no presentations) + set up the year's Phonics Hero routine; tricky words the · a · I · is; establish the Classroom Management Toolkit (Class?–Yes!, WiFi, Give me five, Freeze) as rituals for the whole year.
  • Personal. Front-load Bond on day one — learn/greet every name, keep teacher-talk low, ICQ before every task; run a stirrer→settler rhythm with songs & signals; catch & recast a run-together Whatsyourname and the /s/ vs /z/ in snake · sun.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say “Hello / Goodbye”, ask & answer “What's your name? — I'm (Jake)”;
  • count & say numbers 1–10 and answer “How many? — Ten!”;
  • reactivate colours, shapes & days; hear & blend the Group-1 sounds s·a·t·i·p·n;
  • use this year's attention signals (Class?–Yes!, WiFi, Freeze).

Structure
Hello. / Goodbye.  ·  What's your name? — I'm (Jake).  ·  recycled: What's this? — It's a (ball). · How many? — (Ten). · What colour is it? — It's (red). · What day is it today? — It's (Monday).
Form
Fixed greeting chunks. I'm = I + am (contraction) — keep as one chunk, don't unpack. “It's a…” for shapes; count 1→10 as a rhythmic sequence.
Pron. watch
What's your name? — don't run together, chunk it: What's · your · name; I'm /aɪm/ (one beat); /θ/ in three; /s/ vs /z/ in snake · sun; stress-count rhythm 1–10.
Phonics
Phonics Hero — повтор ур.1–3: sounds s·a·t · i·p·n · c·k·e (тетрадь стр.3–9) — speed reading «туда и обратно», blends sat, pin, cat. Продолжающие ЗНАЮТ эти звуки — это реактивация + запуск ритуала на год. Tricky words: the · a · I · is.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“When you meet a friend, do you say Hello or Goodbye?” → Hello. “When you leave?” → Goodbye. “Do I ask What's your name? or answer I'm Jake?” (point to who speaks).
‘s’: “snake — is it /s/ (soft, like a snake) or /z/ (buzzy)?” → /s/. Numbers: hold up fingers — “How many? Show me!”

Assumptions: continuing pupils have met numbers 1–10, basic colours, shapes & days before (recognition level) — today is reactivation, not first teaching. They are non-/emergent readers → rely on pictures, mime, rhythm & audio, not printed text. It is Day 1: some names/routines are new to you and to them → build Bond first, set signals early, keep writing to a minimum.

‘s’ read as /z/ (or split from blends)Weave the snake hand: /s/ — like a snake, sssss. Blend with a finger slide: /s/ /u/ /n/… sun!
Classroom management — signals (set up TODAY as year rituals)
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

17–28′
Phonics Hero — повтор ур.1–3
at the board

Set the year's routine. Hold up the new тетрадь: This year you are… Phonics HEROES! 🦝 Speaky shows the cover — каждый урок: speed reading → задания → звезда «Я молодец!». You know these sounds — show me how FAST you read! (реактивация, НЕ презентации).

Speed reading — тетрадь стр.3–9 (ур.1–3). Sound strips s · a · t → i · p · n → c · k · e on the board / in the тетрадь: There and back — fast! Vary the voice: Boys… girls… whisper… LOUD! Every child reads every sound.

🎮 Phonics Hub (projector): speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/. Station 1 «Звуки»: flash the nine sounds — children voice each (not the app). Station 6 «🎈 Шарики» as the finisher — letters float up, the whole class names the sound, a rotating child pops the balloon. Status ✅ ready. Offline reserve: letter cards on the board ledge.

▶ Take Off–Touch Down (Kagan): Stand up if you hear /s/… sun!… cat!… snake!… net! (mix in non-/s/ words — everyone reacts every time). Repeat quickly for /t/ and /p/.

Blend & tricky words. Finger-slide 2–3 CVC blends they can already do: /s/ /a/ /t/… sat! · /p/ /i/ /n/… pin! · /c/ /a/ /t/… cat! Then flash tricky words the · a · I · is — These are tricky — we just KNOW them, like magic words!

💪 Support: finger under each sound on the strip; pair with a strong reader. ⭐ Extension: read a strip solo on the timer — beat the class record.

✔ Assess: the strips + Take Off–Touch Down are your live check — note who has lost sounds over summer; fold them into the next lessons' speed readings (ур.4–9 идут занятия 2–3).

30–37′
Numbers 1–10 (anchor)
carpet → book

Retrieve — the lesson's anchor. Choral count 1→10 on fingers, then whisper-count, then shout-count (control the volume). CCQ with fingers: How many? Show me! Hold up 3 fingers → class says Three!

🎮 Interactive (projector): “Find it!” — the market scene (✅ Find it! engine). Tap Speaky — 🦝 Can you find the balloons? How many? Count! The class scans the PB p.4 market scene on screen, points & answers Ten balloons! Repeat for balls · books · dogs · hats · socks (2–3 rounds). Every child points & counts on fingers = all engaged. Offline reserve: the big Pupil's Book p.4 scene — point & count.

Book grounding (light, no long writing). Open your book at page 4. Find, count — how many bananas? Point! Count together; a quick number trace only if time. Save the writing for homework.

ICQ: Do we count or colour now? (→ count!)

Game · consolidate
standing → pairs

▶ Genki Rock, Paper, Scissors (bonding line game, first-day favourite). Two lines of children. Each pair face off: greet Hello! What's your name? — I'm ___!, then play Rock–Paper–Scissors. Winner stays & steps up the line; loser cheers Well done! and rejoins. Everyone is greeting or playing the whole time — no one waits.

🎮 Interactive (projector): Quiz-Quiz-Trade cards (🔨 reskin — number/colour/shape reveal). Between rounds, flash a card set on screen; the winner of each duel taps a card & the pair answers together Three! · Red! · It's a triangle! before trading. Offline reserve: each child holds a paper Q-card, quizzes their partner, then trades cards.

Reset if loud: Class? — Yes! Freeze!

🎮 Digital option (projector): starter-memo — S&S memory pairs on screen, 1–4 teams: a rotating child flips two cards, the whole class names every picture aloud — It's number five! Swap it in for one round of this game when the screen is free.
