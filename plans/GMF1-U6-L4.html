🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 6 “A day in the park” · Lesson 4 — Vocab+Grammar: Prepositions of place · Where are they? They're behind the tree!
Pre-A1 young learners (~7–8 y.o., emergent readers · продолжающая линия) · 60 min · New vocab + structure together (PPP + TPR) · carpet + desks
Week 15.02–21.02 · Lesson 44✨ VOCAB+GRAM~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4 + pairs
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 5 prepositions of place — behind · near · in front of · next to · between (Pupil's Book p. 64) — and ask & answer where two people/animals are: “Where are (Jake and Molly)? — They're (behind the tree).”
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — the playground objects (L1: slide, swing, roundabout, seesaw, pond…), “Where's (Beth)? — She's on the swing.” (L2), the story Hide and seek where the children were behind the tree / near the rocks (L3) — woven into today's target: singular He's/She's… now grows into plural They're… with new prepositions; phonics — reading long /oo/ (moon · spoon · food, JP Book 2); sing the coursebook song Hide and seek (CD3 8); values: play fair & take turns.
  • Personal. Say each preposition clearly & match it to one gesture (behind = hide hand 🙈 · near = hands close 🤏 · in front of = hand forward 👉 · next to = side by side ✋✋ · between = one hand in the middle ✊✋✊); give an ICQ before every game; every task keeps ALL children responding (own hiding-kit / all point at once / pair ask-&-answer).
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 5 prepositions with a gesture (behind, near, in front of, next to, between);
  • ask a question: “Where are Jake and Molly?”;
  • answer where they are: “They're next to the bush.” → point to the hidden pair;
  • read long /oo/ words: moon · spoon · food · zoo · roof.
Target language
Lexis
behindnearin front ofnext tobetween (anchor nouns: tree · grass · flowers · bush · rocks; recycled: slide, swing, roundabout, pond…)
Structure
Where are (Jake and Molly)? — They're (behind the tree). · Where are they? — They're (next to the bush). · Talk Partners: Where are Anna and David? — They're in front of the desk.
Form
Question: Where + are + (two people) ? · Answer: They're (= They are) + preposition + the + noun. Plural today (two people/animals) — contrast with L2 singular He's/She's.
Pron. watch
weak They're = /ðeə/ (one beat, NOT “they are”); in front of = /ɪn ˈfrʌnt əv/ (silent-ish, one chunk); between = /bɪˈtwiːn/ (stress 2); weak “the” /ðə/.
Phonics
Jolly focus: reading long /oo/ (moon · spoon · food · zoo · roof, JP Book 2) · tricky words: every · mother.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hide a toy dog behind a book. “Is the dog behind the book — can I see it, or is it hidden?” → hidden (say behind).
Put the toy between two rocks. “Between — how many rocks? one or two?” → two! (in the middle 👉).
Point to ONE child vs TWO children. “Where's he? — He's… / Where are they? — They're…” → two people = they're.

Assumptions: playground objects (L1), “Where's…? — He's/She's on the swing” (L2) and “They're behind the tree” from the L3 story Hide and seek are solid; behind / on / under were already FELT in the story — today more prepositions get named, drilled and used to ASK & ANSWER about two people. Emergent readers → real toys, pictures & audio, minimal print. ⚠ КТП note: the КТП listed nature animals (butterfly/bird/bee/snail/ladybird) for L4, but PB p. 64 L4 is prepositions of place + Where are they? with anchor nouns (tree/grass/flowers/bush/rocks) → plan follows the book.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Mix up the prepositionsFix ONE gesture per word: behind = hide hand 🙈 · near = hands close 🤏 · in front of = hand forward 👉 · next to = side by side ✋✋ · between = middle ✊✋✊. Chant with the gesture, then hide the word — only the gesture.
Say “He's…” / “She's…” for two peopleHold up ONE finger → He's/She's; TWO fingers → They're. Always show fingers before the answer; the game uses PAIRS of toys so “they're” is natural.
in front of said as 3 slow wordsModel it as ONE chunk /ɪnˈfrʌntəv/; clap the beat once; back-chain: …of → front-of → in-front-of.
Over-excitement with the hiding gameSoft toys only; “Freeze!” resets; one hide → one question → one answer, calm. ICQ before every round.
Long /oo/ read as short (food → “fud”)Stretch the sound: f–oo–d, long lips 👄; contrast moon (long) vs a short word; picture appears for meaning; praise self-corrections.

Phonics — long /oo/ (read)
at the board

Blend to READ (all together). Build a word letter-card by letter-card, push the sounds: /m/ /oo/ /n/… moon! Then spoon · food (3 words — keep it tight) — picture appears for meaning each time. Kagan — Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if the word has /oo/… food! (a few words — all react).

Segment · quick Showdown (protect the clock). Tricky words to finish (whole-word flash, not sounded): every · mother.

Presentation · prepositions + Where are they?
carpet

Present prepositions (PB p. 64 ex. 1, CD3 7). Listen–point–say with the picture (Toby by the tree/grass/flowers/bush/rocks): behind the tree · near the grass · in front of the flowers · next to the bush · between the rocks. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children. Fix ONE gesture per word.

Present the grammar — ask & answer. Hide TWO toys (Jake + Molly) behind a real book: Where ARE they? (two fingers ✌) → reveal → They're behind the book! Repeat: near / next to / in front of / between. Say → hide → children answer & copy the gesture.

▶ Where are they? call-response (all react). You hide the pair + ask; class shouts the place: Where are they? — They're NEXT TO the bush! Speed up. CCQ (see box): One child — he's / she's… TWO children — ? (→ they're!)

▶ Speaky says — Hide it! (TPR command game; every child has their own kit → all move at once, nobody watches). Each child: 2 mini-people cards + a park mat (tree · bush · flowers · rocks drawn).

🦝 Speaky commands, all place: Speaky says put them behind the tree! · Speaky says put them next to the bush! · Speaky says put them between the rocks! Trick them (no “Speaky says” → freeze): Put them in front of the flowers! — anyone who moves does a funny wiggle. ICQ: Do you move if I DON'T say “Speaky says”? (→ No — freeze!)

Kagan — RallyRobin (pairs, take turns): Partner A hides & asks Where are they?, Partner B answers They're behind the tree! — swap, swap, swap. Everyone asks AND answers.

💪 Support: teacher's gesture stays visible with each command (behind 🙈 / next to ✋✋ / between ✊✋✊). ⭐ Extension: the "boss" gives the answer in a full sentence with a new noun — They're between the two big rocks.
🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Put it! (prepositions) Screen: a park scene — tree · bush · flowers · rocks — and a tray of draggable people (Jake · Molly). On a valid drop the sentence prints & Speaky reads it; a "no-Speaky-says" trap bounces them back. Language: They're behind the tree. · They're next to the bush. · They're between the rocks. Teacher: mirror the pupils' own kit; let a child drag while the class asks & answers aloud. 🔨 СОБРАТЬ — reuse U1 «Put it!» drag-&-drop engine; new deck: park art + 5 preposition drop-zones + sentence-print + Speaky voice.
