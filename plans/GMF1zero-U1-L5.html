Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will have consolidated all Unit 1 language — the classroom objects (book, pen, pencil, rubber, ruler, crayon, sharpener, pencil case, school bag, computer + desk/chair/board/bin/shelf), “Where's my…? — It's here / It's (in/on/under) the…”, “Put the (book) on the (shelf)” and “May I borrow your…? — Of course!” — and can use it confidently in the review tasks and the quiz.
  • Subsidiary. Phonics Hero ур.16 (b) закрепление — ch·sh·th: sort → dictation → tricky strip they, all → story «Fish and chips»; sight words live, give; self-assessment (My progress traffic light, AB p. 13 ex. 3); listening for detail (PB p. 16 ex. 1, CD1 25).
  • Personal. Run the flagship quiz by PIES: every child writes/shows every answer (Showdown), the responder is picked by Numbered Heads — no “captains answer everything”; keep the reward moment short so language time wins.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name all the classroom objects and answer “Where's my…?” without support;
  • ask & answer “Where's my…? — It's (in/on/under) the (bag)”;
  • say “Put the (book) on the (shelf)” and “May I borrow your…? — Of course!”;
  • sort, read & write ch/sh/th words and chorus the story «Fish and chips»;
  • self-assess with the traffic light — ready for the Unit 1 test.

Pron. watch

Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Put a pencil under a book: Where's my pencil? Is it ON or UNDER? → under → “It's under the book.”
Quiz rule ICQ: Who writes the answer — one child or EVERYBODY? → everybody, on their own board!

Assumptions: all Unit 1 language met in L1–L5; single sounds j v w x y z + r and CVC blending done — today we read the ch digraph. Teams of 4 with numbered members (1–4) are routine. The flagship quiz with reward runs only on Review day — children know it's special.

17–24′
Review in motion
📺 open space · teams

▶ Where's my…? — Spy Hunt (everyone answers, nobody waits). Before the lesson 🦝 Speaky “hid” objects in / on / under the desk, chair, shelf, bin. Ask: Where's my crayon? Think–Pair first: everyone whispers the answer to a neighbour. Then call a number → that child answers aloud: It's under the desk! Class confirms chorally: Yes! It's under the desk!

Mix all three Unit 1 moves across 5–6 rounds: Where's my ruler? · Put the book on the shelf! (a child does the action) · May I borrow your pen? — Of course! Attention reset: Class? — Yes!

Option — big room? Secret-Word Stations (Obby / Escape). Set 4 corner stations (School things · Where's my…? · Put the … · May I borrow…?). Teams rotate; clear one mini-task per station (name it / answer / do the action) and earn ONE letter. Four stations → four letters → unscramble the secret word (e.g. B·O·O·K / R·U·L·E·R). First team to read the word wins — a lively rotation alternative to Spy Hunt when space allows.

Support ▸ weaker pupils keep the picture card of the hidden object in hand + answer with a partner (echo the chunk).   Extension ▸ stronger pupils give the full sentence, not one word — It's IN the bin, next to the ruler.

33–51′
🏆 FLAGSHIP QUIZ with reward
📺 teams · projector

Set-up (2 min). 3 teams of 4, numbered heads 1–4, one mini-whiteboard EACH. Quiz grid on screen — categories: School things 🎒 · Where's my…? 📍 · Sounds & spelling 🔤 · Class rules ✨. Rules + ICQ: Who writes? — Everybody! When do you show? — On “Showdown!”

Round flow (Kagan Showdown + Numbered Heads — all answer every question). Question up (e.g. picture half-hidden: What is it? · position: Where's the ruler? — in / on / under? · sound: Write the word — sh-i-p! · read a digraph word (chip · ship · fish) · class rule: Put your hand up to… ?). Every child writes/draws → Showdown! → all boards up → call a number: Number 3! — that child of the playing team answers aloud in a full sentence.

Predict (thinking). On the half-hidden picture, before revealing: Look — just a corner! What do you THINK it is? Write your guess! Then reveal — Were you right? Turns each round into a little guess-and-check.

Reward shot. Correct = the answering child takes the shot: animated football penalty on screen — a GOAL = team points, class counts them in English: One, two, three… GOAL! (No projector → paper goal + soft ball, ≈ Mido №91 Bowling for Animal reskin.) Rotate teams; over ~10–12 questions every child shoots at least once.

Support ▸ word-box / picture bank stays visible on a side card so weaker writers copy, not freeze; accept one correct word from them.   Extension ▸ when a strong pupil is “Number”, require the full sentence — It's a pencil case. It's on the desk. — not just the noun.
🔨 Flagship interactive · projector · BUILD · “Unit 1 Team Quiz + Reward”  🔨 BUILD
Screen 1 — Grid: 4×3 board of numbered tiles under the 4 category headers (School things 🎒 / Where's my…? 📍 / Sounds & spelling 🔤 / Class rules ✨). Tap a tile → it flips to the question (picture / half-hidden object / audio word / digraph word / rule prompt). Used tiles grey out.
Screen 2 — Answer: big “Show your boards!” + a 5-second timer bar; tap ✔ / ✗ to score; a full-sentence model appears for choral echo.
Screen 3 — Reward mini-game: football penalty — child “kicks”, goalie dives, animated GOAL (or a bowling reskin). One shot = +points.
Screen 4 — Scoreboard: 3 team bars, huge numbers readable from the back row, plus a “Teamwork ⭐” toggle for best cooperation.
Mechanic: pick tile → question → everyone writes → Showdown → Numbered Head answers → score → reward shot → rotate team. ~10–12 tiles ≈ 15 min.
Target language: all Unit 1 — What's this? / Where's my…? — It's (in/on/under) the… / Put the … on the … / May I borrow your…? + read a digraph word (ch·sh·th).
Teacher: host only — YOU tap tiles & call the number so no captain dominates. Spec is a self-contained HTML slide (S&S purple #6C3FC5 / honey #F5C842). Offline reserve: printed grid + paper goal + soft ball works identically.

Finish with totals + teamwork star: Give me five, champions!

🎮 Ready-made engine for this quiz: class-quiz — the S&S bowling/basketball quiz on this unit, 1–4 teams: a picture question on screen → the team answers chorally — Where's my bag? → It's here! → one child makes the throw on screen; the score counts itself.
