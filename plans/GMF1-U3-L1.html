🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 3 “At the circus” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary
Pre-A1 young learners (~7–8 y.o., emergent readers · продолжающие) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Phonics · carpet + desks
Week 02.11–08.11✨ NEW~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4PB p. 28 · AB p. 26🦸 Phonics Hero ур.17b · ou·oi
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to identify & name 10 parts of the body and count them in context — “How many (arms)? — Two arms!” / “Ten fingers, one nose” — through the circus song This is me.
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve numbers 1–10 (Starter) & size adjectives big/small/long (Unit 2), woven onto the body; phonics — Phonics Hero ур.17 (b) закрепление: digraphs ou /aʊ/ · oi /ɔɪ/ (blend → gap-fill → dictation → story «Королева и туча»); listen for detail (point & stick to the song); fine motor (stickers, draw-a-monster); sight words live · give.
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; give an ICQ before every task; run a stirrer→settler rhythm with songs and signals.
By the end, pupils can…
  • point to & name the 10 body parts;
  • count parts: “Two feet, ten toes, one nose”;
  • ask & answer “What's this? — It's my (nose)”;
  • blend, write from dictation & read ou/oi words (out · cloud · coin · oil) and read the story «Королева и туча».
Target language
Lexis
headmouthnoseeyesearsarmslegsfeettoesfingers (bonus: hands · body)
Structure
What's this? — It's my (nose).  ·  How many (arms)? — Two (arms)!  ·  This is my body. Count with me. (preview → L2: I've got two arms.)
Form
my + noun; number + plural noun (arm → arms, toe → toes). Count aloud: one head, two arms, ten fingers.
Pron. watch
voiced final /z/ on plurals (eyes, ears, legs, toes); /θ/ at end of mouth; long /iː/ in feet; stress = number + noun.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.17 (b) закрепление: digraphs ou /aʊ/ («ау!» в лесу) · oi /ɔɪ/ («ой!») — cloud, mouth, out, coin, soil, oil · dictation out, loud, coin, oil · tricky strip your, she · story «Королева и туча» (тетрадь стр.47–48). Sight words: live · give.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“How many noses have you got?” → one. “How many fingers?” → ten.
“Point to your feet — up or down?” → down (contrast with head).
“ou — two letters: how many sounds?” → ONE — /aʊ/, like calling «ау!» in the forest.

Assumptions: numbers 1–10 & colours (Starter) and size adjectives big/small/long/short (Unit 2) are known → recycled, not taught. Pupils are emergent readers → rely on pictures, TPR & audio, not printed text. Phonics: ou·oi met last lesson (Phonics Hero ур.17a, стр.46, calm test-day round) → today consolidates the full ур.17. Carpet routines known.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Confuse feet / fingers or eyes / earsPresent as contrasting pairs with one clear gesture each; point to your own body.
Plural -s dropped (“two arm”)Exaggerate final /z/ in the choral count; hold up fingers as you say the number.
Digraph read as two letters: ou = /о-у/, oi = /о-и/“Two letters — ONE sound”: merge two fingers into one fist; anchor gestures — hands round the mouth ou! («ау!»), surprised oi! («ой!»).
Over-excitement in Simon Says (touching bodies)Use Freeze! + follow the stirrer with a settler; sit for the next task.
Everyone shouts over the flashcardsClass? — Yes! + hands-up rule; nominate after the chorus.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

10–21′
Phonics Hero — ou·oi (b)
board → desks

Recall the pair (gestures first). Hands round your mouth, call into the forest: ou! (ау!) · the surprised sailor: oi! (ой!) All copy ×2. Quick re-read of the стр.46 №1 strip (all ten old digraphs): There and back — fast!

Blend — тетрадь стр.47 №1 «Соединяем звуки». Sound windows open one by one: /c/ /l/ /ou/ /d/… cloud! /c/ /oi/ /n/… coin! Words: cloud · mouth · out · coin · soil · oil (+ picture for meaning) — every child slides a “blend finger” in the air, all blend chorally.

Gap-fill — стр.47 №2 «Допиши слово» (all vote first). Picture up (cloud · coin · mouth · soil) → every child votes: left hand up = ou, right hand up = oi → check → all write the missing digraph in the тетрадь.

Dictation — стр.47 №3 (everyone writes). Now YOU write. Listen: out… word once → segment on fingers together → all write → Show me! → reveal on the board & self-check. Words: out, loud, coin, oil.

Tricky strip — стр.47 №4. your · she — read the strip for speed: left→right, then right→left! + today's sight pair live · give — flash, chorus, air-write.

Story — стр.48 «Королева и туча» (settler). Read the 5 frames like a comic, finger under the words, chorus each line: A queen. The queen looks out. A big cloud is loud!… ⭐ Star task: find & circle every queen, then circle how many (4). Finish: colour the star «Я молодец!» (стр.47).

🎮 Phonics Hub (проектор): speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/ — наборы qu·ou и oi·ue ✅ ГОТОВ
Station 4 «Сегментинг (диктант)» — the screen checks the dictation words after the тетрадь round; Station 5 «Tricky words» + «⚡ Speed reading» — the strip your · she on a timer. Pupils: read/write first on paper, the Hub is the reveal & check — children voice everything, not the app. Offline: тетрадь стр.47 + a hand-written strip on the board do the same job.

💪 Support: dictation with sound windows drawn on the board (c-oi-n) — child only fills letters. ⭐ Extension: a 5th word by ear (loud → cloud); read the story solo.

🎮 Reading-game option: read-race — ⚔ battle mode, level «Диграфы»: a word pops up, everyone reads it aloud, the fastest correct team races ahead. Swap it in for the speed-reading strip when the screen is free.

23–33′
Presentation · body parts
carpet

Presentation — 10 parts of the body. Hold up each A4 card AND touch your own body: What's this? — It's my head. Head. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children individually. Cover all 10: head · mouth · nose · eyes · ears · arms · legs · feet · toes · fingers.

Contrast the tricky pairs (touch each): These are my eyes. These are my ears. · These are my feet. These are my fingers.

▶ Teacher Says (Simon Says) — all move at once. Teacher says touch your nose!… Touch your ears! (no “teacher says” = don't move). Everyone touches their OWN body; funny speed-ups. Reset with Freeze!

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): What's this? Spinner — body wheel ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the “What's this? spinner” engine — swap deck to body parts) · 🎮 whats-this-spinner-u3
Screen: a wheel of the 10 body parts next to Speaky. Mechanic: tap to spin → it lands on one part → tap it to zoom + play the word. Pupils: BEFORE the reveal all point to that part on their OWN body and ask What's this?; on reveal, chorus It's my (nose)! Teacher: spin, hold the reveal until the whole class points, then tap. 🧠 Predict: Where will it stop? Point & guess! Offline fallback: flip the A4 flashcards face-down, pull one at random.

ICQ: Do we point and say “It's my nose” or “Where's my nose?” (→ It's my nose.)

💪 Support: stand next to a strong peer for Simon Says — copy the action. ⭐ Extension: be the caller for one round of Teacher Says.

33–39′
Song & stickers · “This is me”
carpet → desks

Listen & point — PB p. 28 ex. 1 (CD1 47). Play “This is me”: Two feet, ten toes, two eyes, one big nose… ten fingers, one head, two arms, two long legs. Children point to each part on their body as they hear it. Again: Now let's sing and count! — hold up fingers for the numbers.

Stickers — PB p. 28 ex. 2 (CD1 49). To your tables! Open your books. Find the ten stickers. Listen and stick! Model the first (head); everyone sticks their own. Then check with a neighbour RallyRobin: What's this? — It's a leg. Swap.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Flashcards — body deck ✅ ГОТОВ (reuse of the Unit 1 “Flashcards” engine — new deck)
Screen: the 10 body flashcards flip up one at a time (picture → word). Mechanic: tap a card to hear the word; “flash mode” shows each for 2 s. Pupils: chorus the word the instant it flips — hands stay down, voices only. Teacher: run a fast flash round after stickers to lock the set. 🧠 Retrieve: flip to word-only → children say it before the picture appears. Offline fallback: the physical A4 flashcards, flashed by hand.

Countdown. Finishing in 5… 4… 3… 2… 1… pencils down.

Production · Draw a monster
desks

Create — listen & draw (everyone draws their own). Play Genki “Let's make a monster!” then dictate: Draw two arms! Ten fingers! One big nose! Three eyes! Two long legs! Each child draws their OWN monster on a mini-whiteboard / paper — counting & size words come straight back (numbers + big/long from Unit 2).

🧠 Create: Now add ONE crazy thing — how many ears? How many toes? Children invent & draw.

▶ RallyRobin — describe your monster (in pairs). A shows, B counts: One head, four eyes, ten fingers! then swap. Light preview of L2: My monster has got two arms! All pairs talk at once.

💪 Support: a body outline to add parts onto (not from blank). ⭐ Extension: say a full sentence — It's got three eyes and two big feet.
