🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 3 “At the circus” · Lesson 6 — Review: Parts of the body & have got · 🏆 Flagship Quiz
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Consolidation + flagship team quiz with reward · carpet + desks
Week 16.11–22.11 · Lesson 23L6 Review🏆 Quiz day~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4🦸 Phonics Hero ур.20a · Magic E: a-e
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will have consolidated all Unit 3 language — parts of the body (head, shoulders, arms, hands, legs, feet, eyes, ears, nose, mouth + fingers, toes), “I've got (two eyes / a red nose)”, “He's / She's got (long hair / blue eyes)”, and descriptions of hair (long / short / curly / straight) and eyes (blue / brown / green) — and can use it confidently in the review tasks and the quiz.
  • Subsidiary. Phonics — Phonics Hero ур.20 (a) первое знакомство: Magic E a-e — the silent e makes a say its name /эй/ (can → cane, cap → cape, cake, name); tricky words many, any; self-assessment (My progress traffic light, AB p. 33 ex. 3); listening for detail (PB p. 36 ex. 1, CD2 11).
  • Personal. Run the flagship quiz by PIES: every child writes/shows every answer (Showdown on mini-boards), the responder is picked by Numbered Heads — no “captains answer everything”; keep the reward moment short so language time wins.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name all the body parts and say “I've got (two eyes / small ears)”;
  • describe a person: “He's / She's got (long hair) and (green eyes)”;
  • choose the right hair & eyes word (long/short/curly/straight · blue/brown/green);
  • turn a short word into a Magic-E word (can → cane), read & trace a-e words (cake, name);
  • self-assess with the traffic light — ready for the Unit 3 test.
Target language
Lexis
headshouldersarmshandslegsfeeteyesearsnosemouth + fingerstoes · hair: longshortcurlystraight · eyes: bluebrowngreen
Structure
I've got (two eyes / a red nose). · He's / She's got (long hair) and (blue eyes). · Have you got (long hair)? — Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.
Form
subject + 've / 's got (have/has got) + (adjective) + noun. Contraction “I've” /aɪv/, “he's / she's” /hiːz, ʃiːz/.
Pron. watch
plural /z/ in eyes, arms, legs, fingers, toes; /s/ in feet, hands; clear /h/ in head, hair, hands; “got a” linking.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.20 (a) первое знакомство — Magic E: a-e — волшебное e молчит, а a называет своё имя /эй/: can → cane · tap → tape · cap → cape · mad → made; trace cake · name (тетрадь стр.55). Tricky words: many, any.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Point to the teacher's hair: Is it LONG or SHORT? → short → “You've got short hair.” Then a child with long hair → “She's got long hair.”
Count together: How many eyes have you got? One or TWO? → two → “I've got two eyes.” (recycles Starter numbers.)
Quiz rule ICQ: Who writes the answer — one child or EVERYBODY? → everybody, on their own board!

Assumptions: all Unit 3 language met in L1–L5; have got affirmative practised; phonics: the vowel secret ✊/🖐 (Phonics Hero ур.19, стр.53–54) done in L4–L5 — today the silent e explains WHY the vowel goes long. Teams of 4 with numbered members (1–4) are routine. The flagship quiz with reward runs only on Review day — children know it's special.

Meet the Magic E (Спики's secret). 🦝 Speaky: This is the magic e! It sits at the END, says NOTHING… and makes the vowel say its NAME! Board: can /кэн/ → add e → cane /кейн/ — wave a “magic wand” on the e, all show 🖐. Check: Does the e talk? → No — it's silent!

Волшебное превращение — стр.55 №2 (all read both). Four transformations: can → cane · tap → tape · cap → cape · mad → made. Read the short word ✊, tap the wand, read the long word 🖐 — whole class, exaggerated. Kagan — RallyRobin: in pairs A reads the short word, B the magic word, swap.

▶ Body TPR — Speaky Says (everyone moves, nobody waits). Touch your head! … your ears! … your shoulders! … your toes! Freeze! Fast, then trickier: Touch your knee to your elbow! Recycles every body word with the whole class moving at once.

▶ Make a Monster — describe & draw (Numbered Heads, all draw). Each child draws a silly circus monster on a mini-board while you feed prompts: Give it three eyes! … two big noses! … long green hair! Think–Pair first: everyone whispers a sentence to a neighbour — It's got three eyes and long green hair! Then call a number → that child describes their monster aloud; class echoes. (Recycles Starter colours + numbers and Unit 2 adjectives big/small/long/short inside the new have got.)

Support ▸ weaker pupils keep a body-word picture strip in hand + describe with a partner (echo the chunk “It's got…”).   Extension ▸ stronger pupils give two full sentences — It's got two heads. It's got small ears and a big red nose.

33–51′
🏆 FLAGSHIP QUIZ with reward
📺 teams · projector

Set-up (2 min). 3 teams of 4, numbered heads 1–4, one mini-whiteboard EACH. Quiz grid on screen — categories: Body parts 🧑 · He's / She's got 💇 · Sounds & spelling 🔤 · What's missing? 🔍. Rules + ICQ: Who writes? — Everybody! When do you show? — On “Showdown!”

Round flow (Kagan Showdown + Numbered Heads — all answer every question). Question up (e.g. picture: What is it? — feet! · describe a clown: Has he got long or short hair? · sound: Write the word — c-l-o-ck! · read a recap word · missing part: What's missing on the face?). Every child writes/draws → Showdown! → all boards up → call a number: Number 3! — that child of the playing team answers aloud in a full sentence.

Predict (thinking). On a half-hidden clown picture, before revealing: Look — just the hair! What do you THINK — a boy or a girl? Write your guess! Then reveal — Were you right? She's got curly hair! Turns each round into a little guess-and-check.

Reward shot. Correct = the answering child takes the shot: animated football penalty on screen — a GOAL = team points, class counts them in English: One, two, three… GOAL! (No projector → paper goal + soft ball, ≈ Mido №91 Bowling for Animal reskin.) Rotate teams; over ~10–12 questions every child shoots at least once.

Support ▸ body-word / picture bank stays visible on a side card so weaker writers copy, not freeze; accept one correct word from them.   Extension ▸ when a strong pupil is “Number”, require the full sentence — She's got long curly hair and green eyes. — not just the noun.
🔨 Flagship interactive · projector · BUILD · “Unit 3 Team Quiz + Reward”  🔨 BUILD
Screen 1 — Grid: 4×3 board of numbered tiles under the 4 category headers (Body parts 🧑 / He's–She's got 💇 / Sounds & spelling 🔤 / What's missing? 🔍). Tap a tile → it flips to the question (picture / clown to describe / audio word / recap word / half-face). Used tiles grey out.
Screen 2 — Answer: big “Show your boards!” + a 5-second timer bar; tap ✔ / ✗ to score; a full-sentence model appears for choral echo.
Screen 3 — Reward mini-game: football penalty — child “kicks”, goalie dives, animated GOAL (or a bowling reskin). One shot = +points.
Screen 4 — Scoreboard: 3 team bars, huge numbers readable from the back row, plus a “Teamwork ⭐” toggle for best cooperation.
Mechanic: pick tile → question → everyone writes → Showdown → Numbered Head answers → score → reward shot → rotate team. ~10–12 tiles ≈ 15 min.
Target language: all Unit 3 — What's this? / I've got… / He's / She's got (long hair) and (blue eyes) + read a recap word.
Teacher: host only — YOU tap tiles & call the number so no captain dominates. Spec is a self-contained HTML slide (S&S purple #6C3FC5 / honey #F5C842). Offline reserve: printed grid + paper goal + soft ball works identically.

Finish with totals + teamwork star: Give me five, champions!
