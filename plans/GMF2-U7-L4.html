Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 5 home objects (radio · camera · box · clock · phone) and ask & answer about ownership: “Whose is this (phone)? — It's mine / yours / his / hers.”
  • Subsidiary. Real-objects magic bag; his/hers gender split (echo of his/her from Starter!); lost-property role-play; listen & check (CD3 33).
  • Personal. Possessives click through REAL ownership — use the kids' actual pencils/toys (with permission ritual); his/hers pointing must be kind, not grabby.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 5 objects;
  • ask “Whose is this?” and answer with all four pronouns;
  • run a lost-property exchange.

Structure
Whose is this (watch)? — It's mine. / It's yours. / It's his. / It's hers.
Form
Whose = чей; mine/yours stand alone (no noun after!); his/hers split by gender — hers with -s.
Pron. watch
Whose /huːz/ = who's twin (flag the joke); mine long /aɪ/; hers with /z/.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“It's mine — do I add the thing? ‘mine phone’?” → no — mine stands alone!
“The watch is Masha's — his or hers?” → hers!

Assumptions: his/her known from Starter (names) — mine/yours/hers extend the family. Objects partly known (phone, clock). Ownership games need the kindness frame. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Presentation · objects + possessives
carpet → board

Objects. radio · camera · box · clock · phone (+watch): choral ×3 + mime (turn the dial! click! tick-tock!).

The possessive four. Board faces: я→mine · ты→yours · 👦→his · 👧→hers. Model with a real pencil: Whose is this pencil? It's… mine! (chest-fist). Hand it to a child: Now it's yours! Boy's pen: his! Girl's: hers! Choral each.

Chain. Museum table: each child lends ONE item; you lift items: Whose is this? — class answers with the right pronoun (owner confirms: It's mine!).

ICQ: “It's mine pencil” — OK? (→ no — just “It's mine”!)

Lost property office
two counters

Role-play. Museum items “lost” at two counters; owners queue at the OTHER counter: clerk asks Whose is this camera? — queue answers It's his! / It's hers! pointing kindly; owner claims: It's mine! Thank you! Rotate clerks every 2 min.

💪 Support: pronoun faces card at the counter. ⭐ Extension: clerk adds a check question: Is it YOURS?
