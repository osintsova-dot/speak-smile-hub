🦝 Give Me Five! 1 (с нуля) · Unit 1 “Ready for school!” · Занятие 19 / L4 — Vocab+Grammar: Classroom furniture · Put the book on the shelf!
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., first-year beginners · линия с нуля) · 60 min · New vocab + structure together (PPP + TPR) · carpet + desks
Week 02.11–08.11 · Занятие 19✨ VOCAB+GRAM~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4 + pairs🦸 Phonics Hero ур.16a · ch·sh·th
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 6 classroom furniture words — shelf, cupboard, bin, desk, board, chair (Pupil's Book p. 12) — and give & follow instructions with prepositions of place: “Put the (book) on the shelf / in the bin / under the desk.”
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — the 10 classroom objects (L1), “Where's my…? — It's here!” (L2), the story Toby goes to school where things ended up in the bin / on the shelf (L3) — woven into today's target: “The book is on the shelf now — tidy!”; phonics — Phonics Hero ур.16 (a): digraphs ch·sh·th — meet, blend & trace (тетрадь стр.43–44, «CHair» starts today's topic!); sing the coursebook song Let's tidy up (CD1 17); values: keep the classroom tidy.
  • Personal. Say the preposition clearly and match it to a gesture (on = flat hand up · in = drop inside · under = hand down low); give an ICQ before every game; every task keeps ALL children responding (own object-kit / all place at once / pair barrier game).
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 6 furniture words with a gesture (shelf, cupboard, bin, desk, board, chair);
  • follow an instruction: “Put the crayon in the bin.” → do it;
  • give an instruction: “Put the book on the shelf!”;
  • hear, blend & trace the digraphs ch·sh·th (chip, ship, fish…).
Target language
Lexis
shelfcupboardbindeskboardchair (recycled: 10 classroom objects — book, pencil, crayon, paper, bag…)
Structure
Put the (book) on the (shelf). · Put the (paper) in the (bin). · Put the (chair) under the (desk). · Talk Partners: Put the pencil under the chair. — Good!
Form
Put + the + noun + preposition + the + noun. Imperative — no “I / you”. Prepositions: on · in · under.
Pron. watch
final /f/ in shelf; /ʌ/ in cupboard = /ˈkʌbəd/ (silent “p”, two beats — NOT “cup-board”); weak “the” /ðə/.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.16 (a) первое знакомство: digraphs ch /tʃ/ · sh /ʃ/ · th («язычок между зубами!») — chip, chin, shop, ship, fish, moth (тетрадь стр.43–44) · sight words: like, have.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold a book high on a shelf vs drop it in the bin. “Is the book on the shelf or in the bin?” (point + gesture).
“The chair is under the desk — is it on top or below?” → below (hand low).
“I say Put the book… — do I DO it, or do YOU do it?” → YOU do it! (it's an instruction).

Assumptions: the 10 objects + “Where's my…? It's here” are solid from L1–L2; in / on / under were already FELT in the L3 story (the book in the bin, the pencil case on the shelf) — today they get named, drilled and used to give instructions. Emergent readers → real objects, pictures & audio, minimal print. КТП listed window/door/computer too, but PB p. 12 core is these 6 → plan follows the book. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Mix up in / on / underFix ONE gesture per word: on = flat hand up ✋ · in = drop inside 👇🥣 · under = hand low ⬇️. Chant with the gesture, then hide the word — only the gesture.
cupboard said “cup-board” (2 clear words)Model /ˈkʌbəd/, back-chain: …board → cup-board → cupboard; exaggerate the silent “p”.
Children DO the action when teacher says “Put the…”, but don't SPEAK when it's their turnSplit clearly: teacher/commander SAYS, the rest DO. In the pair game each child gets a turn as the “boss”.
Objects fly / over-excitement with real stuffSoft objects only (paper ball, foam book); “Freeze!” resets; one command → one calm placement.
Blend reading: children name letters, not soundsPush the sounds fast: /s/ /t/ /o/ /p/ → stop!; picture appears for meaning; praise self-corrections.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

9–19′
Phonics Hero — ch·sh·th (a)
board → desks

Speed reading (тетрадь стр.43 №1). Strip ai · oa · ie · ee · or · ng · oo: There and back — fast!

Meet ch · sh · th (стр.43 №2). The train ch — ch-ch-ch! · the quiet-finger sh — shhh! · the clown th — «язычок между зубами». Tie-in to today's furniture: CHair — ch! All copy each sound + gesture; quick show-me: say chair / shop / three / chip — all do the matching gesture.

Blend — Hub Station 3 / стр.44 №1 (all together). Sound windows: /ch/ /i/ /p/… chip! /sh/ /i/ /p/… ship! Then chin, shop, fish, moth — picture for meaning; contrast: chip… ship — same? No!

Trace (стр.43 №3, desks — quick). By the arrows: ch · sh · th · chip · ship — whisper as you write (finish lines at home; today's MAIN aim is furniture + prepositions).

Sight words. Flash like · have (whole-word, not sounded): chorus, air-write.

💪 Support: gesture first, then sound; blend only chip·ship. ⭐ Extension: write fish on the spare line. 🎮 Phonics Hub (speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/) — набор ch·sh·th, ✅ готов; offline: тетрадь стр.44 №1.

Presentation · furniture + Put the…
carpet

Present furniture (PB p. 12 ex. 1, CD1 16). Real / pictured, listen–point–say: shelf · cupboard · bin · desk · board · chair. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children. Contrast the tricky word: cupboard = /ˈkʌbəd/ (back-chain).

28–35′
▶ Speaky says: Put it!
at desks, standing

▶ Speaky says — Put it! (TPR command game; every child has their own kit → all move at once, nobody watches). Each child: a mini-book + crayon + paper ball + a furniture mat (shelf · bin · desk · chair drawn).

🦝 Speaky commands, all place: Speaky says put the book on the shelf! · Speaky says put the crayon in the bin! · Speaky says put the paper under the desk! Trick them (no “Speaky says” → freeze): Put the book on the chair! — anyone who moves does a funny wiggle. ICQ: Do you move if I DON'T say “Speaky says”? (→ No — freeze!)

Kagan — RallyRobin (pairs, take turns): Partner A gives ONE command, Partner B does it & says Done! — swap, swap, swap. Everyone is the boss.

💪 Support: teacher's gesture stays visible with each command (on ✋ / in 👇 / under ⬇️). ⭐ Extension: the "boss" gives a 2-step command — Put the book on the shelf AND the crayon in the bin.
🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Put it! Screen: a room scene — shelf · bin · desk · chair — and a tray of draggable objects (book · crayon · paper · pencil). Mechanics: drag an object; a drop only "sticks" ON / IN / UNDER a piece of furniture. On a valid drop the sentence prints & Speaky reads it; a "no-Speaky-says" trap makes the object bounce back. Language: Put the book on the shelf. · Put the crayon in the bin. · Put the paper under the desk. Teacher: mirror the pupils' own kit; let a child drag while the class commands out loud. ✅ ГОТОВ — U1 Speaky says (TPR: Put the… on / in / under): u1-speaky-says.
