🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 7 “Let's explore my town!” · Lesson 4 — Vocab+Grammar: Transport · How many buses are there?
Pre-A1 young learners (~7–8 y.o., продолжающая line, emergent readers) · 60 min · New vocab + structure together (PPP + TPR) · carpet + desks
Week 08.03–14.03 · Lesson 51✨ VOCAB+GRAM~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4 + pairs🦸 Phonics Hero · повтор Magic E ур.19–20
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 6 transport words — bus, boat, motorbike, train, helicopter, lorry (Pupil's Book p. 76) — and ask & answer about quantity: “How many (buses) are there? — There are (fifteen).” with numbers 11–20.
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve real prior learning — town places (L1: zoo, park, library), numbers 11–20 (L2), the story The treasure hunt where children counted steps and asked How many…? (L3) — woven into today's target: “How many buses are there in the town? — There are fifteen!”; phonics — Phonics Hero повторение Части 3, ур.19–20: краткий ✊ / долгий 🖐 + Magic E a-e (can → cane, cake; тетрадь стр.53–57) + re-read «Торт и змея»; tricky — all colour sets lap; values: transport in our town.
  • Personal. Model plurals cleanly (bus → buses, lorry → lorries) and stress the -TEEN endings; give an ICQ before every game; every task keeps ALL children responding (own card set / all count at once / pair ask-answer with roles swapped).
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 6 transport words (bus, boat, motorbike, train, helicopter, lorry);
  • answer a quantity question: “How many buses? — There are five buses.”;
  • ask the question themselves: “How many lorries are there?”;
  • show ✊/🖐, transform can → cane and read the a-e column at speed.
Target language
Lexis
busboatmotorbiketrainhelicopterlorry (recycled: numbers 11–20 · town places — zoo, park, library…)
Structure
How many (buses) are there? — There are (fifteen). · Talk Partners: How many motorbikes are there? — There are 15 motorbikes. — Correct!
Form
How many + plural noun + are there? → There are + number + plural noun. Plurals: bus→buses · lorry→lorries · boat→boats.
Pron. watch
helicopter = /ˈhelɪkɒptə/ (4 beats — back-chain); lorry /ˈlɒri/; plural buses /ˈbʌsɪz/ (extra beat); voiced final -s → /z/ in boats, trains; stress -TEEN (fif-TEEN).
Phonics
Phonics Hero — повтор Части 3, ур.19–20: ладошка-игра ✊/🖐 (kit/kite, cub/cube) · Magic E превращения can → cane, tap → tape · a-e column at speed (cake, name, game, late) · re-read story «Торт и змея» (тетрадь стр.53–57) · tricky: все цвета — one lap of Red+Yellow+Green+Brown.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold up 3 toy buses. “How many buses? — Do I want a colour or a number?” → a number → three!
“There are fifteen. Is that one, or lots?” → lots → so we say buses (add the /ɪz/ beat).
Magic E: “cane — does the e talk?” → no, it's silent — “it makes a say its NAME /эй/!”

Assumptions: numbers 11–20 were met in L2 and counted in the L3 story; “How many…? — There are…” was already FELT counting steps in The treasure hunt — today it is drilled onto NEW transport words. Emergent readers → pictures, real toys & audio, minimal print. ⚠️ КТП listed transport as car/bike/taxi/plane, but PB p. 76 core is bus · boat · motorbike · train · helicopter · lorry → plan follows the book.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Drop the plural — “There are fifteen bus”Over-model bus-es, lorr-ies with a clap on the extra beat; hold up many fingers = “lots → add the sound”.
Confuse the -teen numbers (13 / 15 / 18)Stress -TEEN and count on fingers; flash the number card while saying it; slow the song verse if needed.
helicopter too long / garbledBack-chain: -ter → cop-ter → heli-copter; four claps, one per beat.
Miscount transport in a busy scenePoint-and-count together, ONE finger per vehicle; ICQ: Do we count fast or one-by-one? (→ one-by-one).
Magic-e words read short after the break (cane → “can”)Wand gesture on the silent e + fist/palm replay: can ✊ — cane 🖐; the transformation pairs stay on the board.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

21–28′
Presentation · transport + How many?
carpet

Present transport (PB p. 76 ex. 1, CD3 30). Pictured/real toys, listen–point–say: bus · boat · motorbike · train · helicopter · lorry. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children. Back-chain the tricky word: helicopter = -ter → cop-ter → heli-copter (4 claps).

Present the grammar. Lay out a row of toy buses. Count together, one finger each: One, two, three… How many buses are there? — There are three buses! Swap the toys & number: How many trains are there? — There are five trains! CCQ (see box): Colour or number? — Number! Drill plural beat: bus-es · lorr-ies.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): What's this? spinner Screen: a wheel of the 6 transport pictures; the arrow spins and lands on one. Mechanics: tap Spin → arrow stops on bus / boat / motorbike / train / helicopter / lorry → whole class names it, then a named child; a small number badge pops so class also answers How many? Language: What's this? — It's a helicopter! · How many? — There are twelve! Teacher: spin 6–8×, speed up; land on helicopter to re-run the back-chain. ✅ ГОТОВ — “What's this?” spinner (swap deck to transport). 🔌 Оффлайн-резерв: 6 transport flashcards face-down in a bag — pull one, class names + counts on the card.
