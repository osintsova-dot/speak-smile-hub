🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 3 “At the circus” · Lesson 2 — Grammar: I've got a (red nose).
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Dialogue → controlled drill → freer → production · carpet + desks
Week 02.11 · Lesson 19✨ GRAMMARContinuing line · ~12 pupils · pairs + 3 teams🦸 Phonics Hero ур.18a · ue·er·ar
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to describe their own face & body with have got: “I've got a red nose.”, “I've got big ears.”, “I'm a clown.” (Pupil's Book p. 29 dialogue, CD1 50) — and act out the two-clowns dialogue.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — the 10 body parts from L1 (head, ears, nose, eyes, mouth, hands, arms, legs, feet, fingers) + the size adjectives big / small and colours from Units 1–2 — woven straight into today's structure (“I've got a big nose”, “I've got a red nose”); phonics — Phonics Hero ур.18 (a) первое знакомство: digraphs ue /uː/ · er /ɜː/ · ar /ɑː/ (meet the sounds → blend → trace & write); listen for detail (PB p. 29 ex. 2 “Listen and draw”); gently preview He's / She's got… for Lessons 3–4.
  • Personal. Insist on the full chunk “I've got…” (keep the 've — not “I got”); pair the size/colour with the part (“a big nose”, not just “nose”); every task keeps ALL children responding (own draw / point & say / all pairs at once) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say “I've got a (big) (nose).” about their own face & body;
  • act out the two-clowns dialogue and draw a clown from spoken clues;
  • blend & read their first ue/er/ar words (glue, fern, her, car, star, farm) and trace the digraphs.
Target language
Lexis
headearsnoseeyesmouthfingershandsarmslegsfeethairclown (recycled: body parts from L1 · big/small · colours)
Structure
I've got a (red nose).  ·  I've got (big ears).  ·  And I've got a (big mouth). I'm a clown.  ·  I'm a clown, too.  ·  preview: He's / She's got (purple hair).
Form
I've got = I have got (’ve = have); I've got + (adjective) + body part. Affirmative only today. Plural parts take no “a”: big ears, small eyes (not “a big ears”).
Pron. watch
keep the ’ve: /aɪv gɒt/ — not “I got”; link “I've got a” /aɪvgɒtə/; ears / eyes end in /z/ (plural); contrast big ↔ small with a big / tiny voice.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.18 (a) первое знакомство: digraphs ue /uː/ · er /ɜː/ · ar /ɑː/ («открой ротик у доктора: a-a-ar!») — glue, fern, her, car, star, farm (тетрадь стр.49–50) · sight words: old, only.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Point to your own nose: “Is this YOUR nose or MY nose?” → mine → “So I say I've got a nose.” (’ve = it's on ME).
Big vs small: hold a tiny clown nose then a huge one — “Small nose or BIG nose?” → “I've got a big nose!” (say it in a big voice).
Digraphs: hold up ar — “Two letters… how many sounds?” → ONE — /ɑː/, open wide at the doctor's: a-a-ar!
⭐ Option — He/She CCQ: point to a boy clown then a girl clown: “I've got a big nose… and HE?” → He's got a big nose. (Primes He's/She's got for L3–4 — use only if the class is quick today.)

Assumptions: the 10 body parts + “Make a face!” were first met in L1; size adjectives big/small come from Unit 2 (L4) and colours from the Starter; phonics: ou·oi consolidated last lesson (Phonics Hero ур.17, стр.46–48) → today's speed-reading strip; ue·er·ar are new. Pupils are non-/emergent readers → pictures, drawing & audio, not printed text. Carpet routines & pair-work signals known. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Likely problemWhat you do
Drops the ’ve: says “I got a nose”Tap your chest on I've every time; back-chain …a red nose → got a red nose → I've got a red nose; no answer from Speaky until the ’ve is there.
Says just “nose” — no size / colourPoint-and-say drill: finger on the part + I've got a BIG nose! — the chunk always carries an adjective; model with a big/tiny voice.
Puts “a” before a plural part: “a big ears”Show two ears on fingers: “Two ears — no ‘a’! I've got big ears.” Keep “a” only for one thing (a nose, a mouth).

Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.
Procedure — staging
highlighted = exact words to say (teacher / class) 🦝 = Speaky lines ▶ = game […] = fill-in slot 🎮 = on-screen (projector) interactive
⏱ Pacing — honest 60 min. This is a full plate, so pick, don't cram. The core = dialogue model (24–33′) + one production game + Listen & draw. Buffers to drop first if you're short: the 2nd drawing round, the He's/She's preview (53–55′), Timed Pair Share. Better one clown described well by every child than three stages half-done — never rush the model and the pair practice.

Recycle L1 body parts — everyone points. Fast TPR: Touch your nose! Touch your ears! Show me your eyes! Wiggle your fingers! — all move & name: It's my nose! (8–10 parts, snappy).

10–22′
Phonics Hero — ue·er·ar (a)
board → desks

Speed reading — warm the old sounds (тетрадь стр.49 №1). Strip ai · oa · ie · ee · or · ng · oo · ch · sh · th · ou · oi: Look how many you know — twelve! There and back… fast! Whole class reads left→right, then right→left; everyone reads every sound.

Meet the new sounds (стр.49 №2). Three pictures, three anchors: ar — the doctor: Open wide — a-a-ar! (all open wide) · er — the whirring mixer: errr! (stir with your arm — no rolled r, just a lazy /ɜː/) · ue — sticky glue: ue! /uː/ (pull sticky fingers apart). Copy each sound + gesture ×3. Check: hold up ar — Two letters… how many sounds? → every child shows ONE finger.

🎮 Phonics Hub (проектор): speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/ — набор er·ar (+ ue) ✅ ГОТОВ
Station 1 «Звуки»: flash old + new sound cards — children voice each sound, not the app. Station 3 «Блендинг»: sound windows open one by one — /g/ /l/ /ue/… glue! /s/ /t/ /ar/… star! — every child slides a “blend finger” in the air = all blend at once. Words: glue · fern · her · car · star · farm (+ picture for meaning). Offline reserve: тетрадь стр.50 №1 «Соединяем звуки» — the same six words in sound windows, finger under each window.

Write it — тетрадь стр.49 №3 «Пишем» (desks). Phonics Hero time! Open page 49. Trace ue, er, ar by the dots, then the arrows show the stroke — start at the yellow dot and write: ue · er · ar · glue · star. Walk & monitor pencil grip; children whisper each word as they write.

Sight words. Flash old · only: chorus ×3, air-write. (The match, dictation & story «Звезда над фермой» of ур.18 come next lesson — today is first meeting only.)

Balloon finisher (60 sec, stir). Hub Station 6 «🎈 Шарики» — letters float up; the whole class names each sound, one rotating child pops the balloon. End on the cheer.

◆ Support: finger-trace the big letters first; blend only car · star · farm. ▲ Extension: write farm or her on the spare line.

24–33′
Presentation · Dialogue
carpet → pairs

Model with Speaky. Put a red nose & big ears on 🦝 Speaky: Look at me! I've got a red nose. — point — I've got big ears. And I've got a big mouth. I'm a clown! Second (girl) clown card joins: I'm a clown, too!

Listen & repeat — PB p. 29 ex. 1 (CD1 50). Books open / picture on screen: two children dressed as clowns. Play the dialogue; children repeat line by line. Drill with a big pointing gesture: chest-tap on I've got, big voice on a BIG mouth. Point at the Grammar box: I've got a red nose. I've got big ears. — this is our structure of the day.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Clown Builder Spinner ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the “What's this? spinner” engine) · 🎮 whats-this-spinner-u3
Screen: a blank clown face with two wheels — one of sizes/colours (big · small · red · purple) and one of parts (nose · ears · eyes · mouth · hair). Mechanic: tap to spin both → they land, e.g. big + nose; tap the face → that part pops on (reveal + “boing” sound), building the clown. Pupils: BEFORE the reveal the whole class says together I've got a big nose! → on reveal, cheer. Teacher: spin, hold the reveal until you hear the FULL chunk, then tap. 🧠 Predict: Big or small? Point & guess before it stops!

Act out (ALL pairs at once). Pairs on the carpet, each with a red nose card / clown mask. A puts it on: Look at me! I've got a […] nose. I'm a clown! B: I'm a clown, too! Swap the card, change roles, repeat with a new part. ICQ: Do you say “nose” or “I've got a big nose”? (→ the full chunk!)

◆ Support: weaker child is the echo (I'm a clown, too!) first, then swap. ▲ Extension: A adds a 2nd line — And I've got big ears!

Mechanic: tap Speaky → he describes one — I've got a big nose and small eyes! — children scan & point to the right clown; tap it → it glows + cheers. ▶ Clown Mix & Freeze (movement — the whole class moves at once, nobody sits out). Music on → all clown-walk around the room. Music stops → 🦝 Speaky calls a body clue and everyone freezes in that pose, shouting the chunk: I've got big feet! (stomp) · I've got a big nose! (finger on nose) · I've got long arms! (stretch wide). 5–6 rounds; anyone still moving does a funny wobble & rejoins.

Kagan — Mix-Pair-Share: on one freeze, each child turns to the nearest partner and tells them one thing: I've got a small mouth! — then mix again. Everyone speaks every round. Reset if loud: Freeze!

◆ Support: hold a body-part picture up as you call, so the pose is clear. ▲ Extension: add size + colour in the pose — I've got a big red nose!

⭐ Swap (rotation): if you ran a freeze game last lesson, swap in Simon-clown says — Speaky says: show me big ears! (no “Speaky says” → don't move).

▶ Pair “Draw my clown”

▶ Draw my Clown (production drill — every pair works at once, nobody waits). Each child: a blank clown-face sheet + crayons + a book/folder screen so the partner can't see their drawing.

Model on the board first. A invents a clown & describes it line by line: I've got a big nose. I've got small eyes. I've got purple hair. B draws it from the clues; then drop the screen & compare: Look! Same clown! Swap roles & draw a new one. Keep the full “I've got…” chunk every time.
