🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 8 “Camping fun!” · Lesson 3 — Story: Fun at the camp
Pre-A1 young learners (~7–8 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Story: listen → read → act out (drama) · carpet + desks
Week 29.03–04.04 · Lesson 57✨ STORY~12 pupils · small groups for drama
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow and understand the story “Fun at the camp” (Pupil's Book pp. 84–85, CD3 47) and act it out using key story language: “What's the weather like? — It's hot / cold / raining.”, “Let's swim / sail a boat.”, “You can't swim today — it's dangerous.”, “She's riding a horse. He's sailing a boat.”, “Oh no! What a pity! Bad luck!”, “But we can sail the magic bike!”.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — can / can't for ability & permission (Unit 5 Farm) is the engine of the whole story (the weather decides what the children can't do); Where's my…? (U1) → “Where's my swimsuit?”; colours red / yellow / green (Starter) → the safety flag; He's/She's + activity (U6 → U8 L2) — all woven straight into the story. Phonics — Jolly /ue/ spelled ue (blue · glue · true · rescue, recognise → blend → read) + tricky words by sight you · are · there. Values (PB p. 85 ex. 2): stay safe near water — a red flag means don't go in.
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (gesture responses, prediction over the repeating weather pattern); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a small group at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 30-sec showcase. Keep phonics short and light — this is a story lesson.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (number the frames, True/False thumbs);
  • say the key lines in role: “It's hot! Let's swim.”, “You can't swim — it's dangerous!”, “She's riding a horse.”;
  • read the ending twist — “We can't sail a boat… but we can sail the magic bike!”;
  • blend & read ue words (blue · glue · rescue) and read you · are · there by sight;
  • know a red flag means it's dangerous to swim (values).

Structure
What's the weather like? — It's hot / cold / raining. · Let's swim / sail a boat. · You can / can't swim today. It's dangerous / raining. · She's riding a horse. He's sailing a boat. · I'm wearing trousers and a shirt. · Where's my swimsuit? · Oh no! What a pity! Bad luck! · We can sail the magic bike!
Form
can / can't + base verb — ability & permission (recycled U5, the story's backbone). Present continuous He's / She's + verb-ing (riding, sailing, swimming — receptive here; full focus L4). I'm wearing + clothes (L2). Let's + verb (suggestion, receptive).
Pron. watch
weak can /kən/ vs strong can't /kɑːnt/ (make the /t/ clear); contractions She's · He's · I'm · It's · Let's; connected What's the /wɒts ðə/.
Phonics
Jolly: /ue/ spelled ue — blue · glue · true · rescue (recognise → blend → read) · tricky words by sight: you · are · there (all appear in the story language).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Frames 2–3: “It's hot — Let's swim! Can they swim?” → “No! The flag is RED — it's dangerous.”
Frames 4–5: “The next day it's cold and raining. Can they ride a horse?” → “No — it's raining!”
Frames 6–8: “Now it's hot and sunny — Let's sail a boat! Can they sail?” → “No — it isn't windy!… but the MAGIC BIKE helps!” (the happy twist).

Assumptions: can / can't known from U5 (Farm); Where's my…? from U1; colours from the Starter; clothes + I'm wearing… from U8 L1–L2; children know Toby the dog & the magic bike from earlier units. Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures while listening; the teacher points, children don't read the speech bubbles alone.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
can and can't sound the same to themFix two gestures: can = thumbs-up “yes, go!”; can't = arms crossed “X, no!”. Over-stress the /t/. “Show me!” drill with weather before the story.
Present continuous -ing is newKeep it receptive — don't drill the form. Gesture each action (ride, sail, swim) and let the picture carry the meaning; the grammar is fully taught in L4.
Children call answers over the audio“Listening hands” rule — hands cupped behind ears while the CD plays; talk only when it stops.
Drama turns to chaos (running around)Movements are ON THE SPOT (magic-bike ride = pedal sitting; swim = arms on the spot); roles fixed by colour badges; “Freeze!” resets.
Frame cards ordered by one child in the pairRule “one card each turn, take turns” (RallyRobin with cards); praise pairs where both hands work.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset (essential in drama).

3–8′
Review → into the story
carpet, circle

Recycle U5 can/can't — the story's engine. Flash 4–5 activity/animal cards, class chants the rhythm (clap-clap-ask): Can you swim? — Yes, I can! · Can a dog sail a boat? — No, it can't! Fix two gestures: can = thumbs-up, can't = arms crossed. Call fast: Show me CAN! Show me CAN'T! — all react.

Recycle U1 + clothes (U8 L1). 🦝 loses things: Where's my hat? Where's my swimsuit? → hide & find. Then flash clothes: What are you wearing? — I'm wearing a T-shirt! (2–3 children).

Bridge. Some children are camping… but every day the weather is a problem! Can they swim? Can they ride a horse? Let's find out! Signal: Class? — Yes!

📺 Phonics — /ue/ (ue)
at the board

Recognise (snappy — keep it light, this is a story lesson). New spelling today: u–e together says /ue/! Show the digraph card; children make the sound with the Jolly action. 30 seconds, then straight to blending.

Blend (all respond). 🦝 robot-voice, one sound at a time: /b/ /l/ /ue/… class blends & shouts blue! → point to something blue. Do blue · glue · true · rescue — a picture each time for meaning (rescue = the magic bike rescues them at the end!).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Flashcards Blender — “ue words” ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U8 “Flashcards” engine — one ue word, letters split b · l · ue, then a picture reveal on tap. · How: tap to sound each part → tap to blend → tap to show the picture. · Pupils: voice each sound then blend chorally — blue! — and touch their nose if the picture matches. · Teacher: drive the taps, keep the beat, praise blends.

Read — thumbs check (all react). Flash word cards; class blends chorally, then thumbs up = real word, thumbs down = silly word (mix in bl-oo · gue). Every child shows a thumb.

Tricky words by sight — you · are · there. Flash, chorus, air-write. Meaning-link straight to the story: “There are lots of activities.” “Can you swim?” — no writing dictation (heaviest task; save the pen for the story work). Support: keep the picture beside the word; ask for the first sound only. Extension: fast finishers write blue · glue on a mini-whiteboard → Show me!

 JP ue digraph card · blue/glue/true/rescue word+picture cards · tricky-word cards (you/are/there) · a few mini-whiteboards (extension) · Speaky

20–31′
📺 Story — listen & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 84). Big story picture on screen / books open. The book's question: What's the weather like in the story? — pairs point & guess 15 sec, then chorus. Pre-teach with gesture: camp / tent (roof hands) · lake (wavy hands) · flag (wave) · swimsuit (mime).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “What's at the camp?” ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U8 “Find it!” engine on the big story picture. · How: Speaky asks, a child taps the thing; it lights up. · Pupils: whole class points to their book / calls it — A tent! A lake! A boat! A horse! then She's riding a horse. He's sailing a boat. · Teacher: 🦝 Where's the flag? Point! — 4–5 things, quick.

Predict (thinking) — before we listen. It's a hot day! What do the children want to do? Will they swim? Yes or no? Thumbs guess. Let's listen and check!

Listen 1 — global (CD3 47). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: Do the children have fun? Show me! (thumbs) Was your guess right — did they swim? (No — the flag is red!)

Detail check — True or False? (thumbs up / down, all react — mirrors the homework). There are lots of activities at the camp. (👍) · The children swim in the lake. (👎 — The flag is red! It's dangerous.) · The children ride a horse. (👎 — It's raining!) · There's a flag at the lake. (👍) · The children sail the magic bike. (👍). Re-point the frame to fix any wrong thumb.

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the gestures at the right moments: hot (fan) · swim (arms on the spot) · red flag → arms crossed “can't!” · Oh no! / What a pity! hands on cheeks · cold + raining (shiver + fingers falling) · ride a horse (bounce) · sunny (round arms) · magic bike Ring! Ring! · cheer Wheeee! CCQs between frames (see box above): stop at frames 3, 5, 7 — Can they swim? / ride? / sail? — No! Why?

32–39′
▶ Build the story
desks, pairs

▶ Story-Sequence (Mido №54 “Reconstruction”, picture re-skin for non-readers — all pairs work at once). Each pair gets the story's frames as shuffled mini-cards.

Order it. Put the story in order — one card each, take turns! (RallyRobin with cards: A places one, B places the next.) Countdown 2 min. ICQ: Do we grab all the cards or take turns? (→ take turns!)

Check with the audio. Replay CD3 47: pairs touch each card as they hear it — self-check, fix the order. Then choral retell, teacher holds up big frames one by one, class says the line: Wow! Lots of fun activities! · It's hot! Let's swim. · You can't swim — it's dangerous! · The next day — it's cold and raining. · You can't ride a horse today. What a pity! · Hurray! It's hot and sunny! Let's sail a boat. · We can't sail — it isn't windy. Magic bike, help! · Wheee! We can sail the magic bike!

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Camp Story-Sequence 1–5 🔨 СОБРАТЬ
Screen: the 5 key story frames (hot “Let's swim!” → red flag “can't swim!” → rainy day “can't ride!” → sunny “can't sail” → magic bike “Wheee!”) appear shuffled in a row, each with an empty number badge 1–5. · How: tap two frames to swap them (or drag); when the order is right, a ✅ + a cheer plays. A “Check” button lights correct frames green. · Pupils: the class calls the order — Number one — it's hot, let's swim! Number two — the red flag! — and gesture each frame. · Teacher: take votes (Which is first? Hands up!), tap as they decide, then replay CD3 47 to confirm. Mirrors the paired frame-cards on screen for a whole-class check.

Support: give that pair only 5 clearest frames and number the first one for them. Extension: after ordering, ask them to say one whole line for each frame from memory before the audio check. Offline reserve: printed frame mini-cards from PB pp. 84–85 (no projector needed).

 story frame mini-cards per pair (print from PB pp. 84–85) · 5-frame set for support pairs · CD3 47 · big frame cards for the board
39–48′
Act out — drama
carpet, groups

Roles. Groups of 3–4: 2–3 children (red/green/blue badges — want to swim, ride, sail) · the man at the lake (yellow badge — says “You can't… it's dangerous!”) · the magic bike (any child — “Ring! Ring! Wheee!”). Model once with a strong group.

All groups act at the same time. Narrate the story slowly, frame by frame; every group plays it in their own spot (swim/ride/sail ON THE SPOT!): Wow, lots of activities! It's hot — let's swim! … Sorry, you can't swim, it's dangerous! Oh no, what a pity! … The next day — it's cold and raining. You can't ride a horse! Bad luck! … Hurray, it's sunny! Let's sail a boat! … But it isn't windy — you can't sail. Magic bike, can you help? … Wheee! We can sail the magic bike!

Create (thinking) — make it yours. Before round 2: Add ONE more day! What's the weather? What do YOU want to do? Can you do it? Each group invents a short exchange: It's cold and windy! Let's fly a kite! — No, it's too cold! What a pity! (support: point to a weather + activity card; extension: invent the whole exchange).

Swap roles, run it again — keep it to 2 rounds (Freeze! the moment energy tips). Volunteers: ONE group shows 30 sec for the class — big applause. Values (PB p. 85 ex. 2): The flag is RED. Can we swim? → No! It's dangerous! — red = stop, green = go.

Support: quieter children get the “magic bike” or short-line role (Ring! Ring! + gesture); a line card in hand. Extension: confident children play the man at the lake and lead the “you can't…” exchange.

 role badges ×4 colours · weather + activity cards · PB p. 85 (values)
48–53′
▶ Weather says!
desks, all at once

▶ Weather says — can or can't? (targeted settler — can/can't + weather + activities + red/green flag; every child answers every round). Each child holds a red card and a green card (or thumbs).

🦝 Speaky sets the scene: It's hot and sunny! Let's sail a boat! → children hold up GREEN + say Yes, you can!; It's raining! Let's ride a horse! → RED + No, you can't!. 6–7 rounds, instant show-me check each time. Slip in the flag values: The flag is red — can we swim? → all RED.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “Weather & activities” (model & check) ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U8 “Find it!” board — weather icons + activity pictures. · How: Speaky says an activity, a child taps the weather that makes it can / can't; a red or green flag pops up. · Pupils: first show their OWN red/green card, then one child mirrors it on screen — It isn't windy — you can't sail! class checks: No, you can't! · Teacher: use it to model round 1, then just for the show-me check so every child stays on their own cards. Offline reserve: red/green paper cards only, no projector.
