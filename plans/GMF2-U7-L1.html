Week 01.03–07.03 · Lesson 48✨ NEW · 10 house words + There's a mouse!~10 pupils · pairs + room cornersPupil's Book p.72 · AB p.70
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 house/rooms words and say what's happening where: “There's a mouse (watching TV) in the (living room)!”
  • Subsidiary. Sing the house song (CD3 21) + stickers chant; retrieve furniture words (GMF1) and Present Continuous (U4–5), woven onto rooms; the cheeky MOUSE is the unit mascot — it hides in every stage.
  • Personal. There's + room is planted here lightly (full grammar in L2) — keep it a chunk, not a rule yet.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 rooms/house words;
  • say “There's a mouse in the kitchen!”;
  • read room words and match to pictures.

Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“House or flat — what do YOU live in?” → real answers (Norilsk: mostly flats!).
“There's a mouse in the kitchen — good news or funny trouble?” → funny trouble!

Assumptions: Furniture (bed, sofa, table) known from GMF1; rooms are the new layer. Kids live in flats — house/flat contrast is personally meaningful. Continuous is solid — the mouse “doing things” recycles it.

Presentation · 10 house words
carpet → board

Build the house. Big house cross-section on the board (or magnetic rooms): present each room card into place: What's this? It's the… kitchen! Choral ×3 + action per room (cook! sleep! wash! eat!). house vs flat photos: Who lives in a flat? Hands up!

The mouse moves in. Mouse cutout hops room to room: Where's the mouse? There's a mouse in the BATHROOM! — chunk chorus each hop; kids close eyes, you move it — reopen: Where is he NOW?!

▶ Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if you SLEEP there… bedroom! …garage!

💪 Support: point + room word. ⭐ Extension: add the action: There's a mouse eating in the kitchen!
