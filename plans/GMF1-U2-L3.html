🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 2 “Happy birthday!” · Lesson 3 — Story: The slow scooter
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Story: listen → follow → act out (drama) · carpet + desks
Week 12.10 · Lesson 13✨ STORY~12 pupils · trios for drama
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow and understand the story “The slow scooter” (Pupil's Book pp. 20–21, CD1 33) and act it out using key story language: “My scooter is slow/fast.”, “The wheels are small/big.”, “What is it? — It's a motor!”, “Well done! Thank you!”.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — toys + “What is it? / Is it a…? — Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.” (L1–L2) — woven into the story work (guess Beth's present, question the mystery motor); phonics — ng /ŋ/ (ring, sing, long, strong — and the story's “Ring! Ring!”); values: saying thank you; preview adjectives (slow/fast, small/big) for Lesson 4.
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (gesture responses, prediction); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a trio at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 1-min showcase.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (order the frames, answer with gestures);
  • say the key lines in role: “My scooter is slow/fast!”, “What is it? — It's a motor!”;
  • hear, blend & segment words with ng /ŋ/;
  • say “Thank you!” when somebody helps (values).
Target language
Lexis
scooterwheelsmotorraceslowfastsmallbig (recycled: bike · toys set · Magic Bike characters Beth, Jake, Molly)
Structure
My scooter is slow/fast. · The wheels are small/big. · What is it? — It's a motor. · Let's have a race! Ready, steady… go! · Well done! Thank you!
Form
be + adjective: it is slow / they are small (receptive — meaning through gesture, no form focus yet: full focus in L4).
Pron. watch
ng /ŋ/ one nasal sound (no hard “g” at the end of ring, sing); sentence stress: My scooter is SLOW.
Phonics
Jolly focus: ng /ŋ/ — ring, sing, long, strong (recognise → blend → segment) · tricky words: who, which (JP Book 2 p. 36).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Frame 2: “Is Beth's scooter fast?” → “No! It's slow.” (snail gesture) “Why?” → point at the picture: small wheels.
Frame 6: “Look! What is it?” → “It's a motor!” — “Is the scooter slow now?” → “No! It's fast!”
Frame 8: “Jake and Molly help Beth. What does Beth say?” → “Thank you!”

Assumptions: toys + Is it a…? known from L1–L2; children know the Magic Bike characters from Unit 1. Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures while listening; the teacher points, children don't read the bubbles alone. slow/fast/small/big are met here in context and studied properly next lesson.

Drama turns to chaos (races across the room)Races are ON THE SPOT (slow-motion → fast feet); roles fixed by colour badges; “Freeze!” resets.

Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset (essential in drama).

10–22′
Phonics — ng /ŋ/
at the board

Recognise. Story link: in our story a bell goes Ring! Ring! Model ng /ŋ/ — one nasal sound, touch your nose, hum: riiing… sing… long… strong. Children copy sound + Jolly action (ring the bell).

“ng or not?” — whole-class show-me (all react). Every child gets a bell card 🔔. Say words — ring · red · sing · sun · long · leg · strong · king… Hear /ŋ/ at the end → stand up, ring your bell card + hum “ng!”; no /ŋ/ → stay seated, hands on knees. 8–10 words, fast.

Blend (all together). Push the sounds: /r/ /i/ /ng/… ring! Then sing, long, strong + a picture each (bell · singer · long snake · strong arms 💪).

Segment · dictation (everyone writes). Now YOU write. Listen… one word at a time → all write on mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & check. Focus: ring, sing, long, strong. Finish with tricky words by sight: who · which — flash, chorus, air-write.

24–34′
Story — listen & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 20). Big story picture on screen / books open. How many toys can you see in the story? — pairs count 15 sec, then chorus the number. Pre-teach with gesture: wheels (fists rolling) · motor (brrrm!) · race — Ready, steady… go!

Listen 1 — global (CD1 33). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: Is Beth happy at the start? Show me! (thumbs) And at the end?

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the story gestures at the right moments: slow 🐌, small wheels (little circles), Ring! Ring! 🔔 (their phonics sound!), big wheels, fast ⚡, cheer Well done, Beth! CCQs between frames (see box above). At the mystery frame stop: Look! What is it? Is it a robot? → No, it isn't! → It's a MOTOR!

35–43′
▶ Build the story
desks, pairs

▶ Story Builders (Mido №54 “Reconstruction”, picture re-skin for non-readers — all pairs work at once). Each pair gets 8 mini frame-cards of the story, shuffled.

Order it. Put the story in order — one card each, take turns! (RallyRobin with cards: A places one, B places the next.) Countdown 2 min.

Check with the audio. Replay CD1 33: pairs touch each card as they hear it — self-check, fix the order. Then choral retell, teacher holds up big frames one by one, class says the line: Let's have a race! · My scooter is slow! · Ring! Ring! · The wheels are big! · It's a motor! · You win the race! Thank you!

43–51′
Act out — drama
carpet, trios

Roles. Trios: Beth (red badge) · Jake (green) · Molly + Magic Bike bell (blue, says “Ring! Ring!”). Teacher narrates and models once with a strong trio.

All trios act at the same time. Narrate the story slowly, frame by frame; every trio plays it in their own spot (races on the spot!): Let's have a race! — Ready, steady… go! — Oh no! My scooter is slow! — What's the matter, Beth? — I've got an idea… Magic Bike! — Ring! Ring! — Look! The wheels are big! — What is it? — It's a motor! — My scooter is fast now! — Well done, Beth! Thank you!

Swap roles, run it again faster (2 rounds). Volunteers: ONE trio shows 30 sec for the class — big applause. Values (PB p. 21 ex. 2): When a person helps you, what do you say? → Thank you!
