🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 4 “Fantastic food!” · Lesson 4 — Vocab+Grammar: Fruit · Do you like grapes? Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · New vocab + structure together (PPP + TPR) · carpet + desks
Week 07.12–13.12 · Lesson 28 (продолжающая)✨ VOCAB+GRAM~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4 + pairs🦸 Phonics Hero ур.22b · Magic E: o-e·u-e
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 6 fruit words — mango, grape, peach, cherry, lemon, coconut (Pupil's Book p. 44) — and ask & answer about likes: “Do you like (grapes)? — Yes, I do. / No, I don't.”
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — the food words (L1: chicken, pizza, rice, salad, fish, pasta…), “I like / don't like / love…” (L2), the story Do you like pizza? — I love it! I don't like salad — Yuck! (L3) — woven into today's target: the SAME question now asked about NEW fruit; phonics — Phonics Hero ур.22 (b) закрепление: Magic E o-e·u-e (word grid → gap-fill → dictation → слова-молнии home, hope, tune → story «Милый мул»); sing the coursebook song Do you like fruit? (CD2 25); value: eating fruit is fun and healthy.
  • Personal. Push rising intonation ↗ on “Do you like…?” (hand rises); one gesture per answer (like 👍 / don't like 👎); give an ICQ before every game; every task keeps ALL children responding (own fruit card / Mix-Pair-Share / pair survey — nobody just watches).
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 6 fruit words with a picture (mango, grape, peach, cherry, lemon, coconut);
  • ASK a question with rising intonation: “Do you like cherries?”;
  • ANSWER with a full short answer: “Yes, I do. / No, I don't.”;
  • read & write o-e/u-e words (nose, bone, cube, tune) and read the phonics story «Милый мул».
Target language
Lexis
mangograpepeachcherrylemoncoconut (recycled: chicken, pizza, rice, salad, fish, sandwiches, pasta, cake, ice cream)
Structure
Do you like (grapes)? — Yes, I do. / No, I don't. · I like / I don't like (peaches). · Talk Partners: Do you like cherries? — Yes, I do. What about you?
Form
Do + you + like + noun (plural) + ? → short answers Yes, I do / No, I don't (not just “yes / no”). Fruit go plural: grapes, peaches, cherries.
Pron. watch
rising ↗ intonation on “Do you like…?”; plural endings — /s/ in grapes vs extra beat /ɪz/ in peaches · cherries; coconut = /ˈkəʊkənʌt/ (3 beats).
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.22 (b) закрепление — Magic E o-e·u-e: word grid nose, bone, home, note, cube, mule, cute, flute · gap-fill (впиши o или u) · dictation nose, bone, cube, tune · слова-молнии home, hope, tune · story «Милый мул» (тетрадь стр.62–63) · tricky words: always · upon.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Show thumbs: “I like grapes — thumbs up or down?” → up 👍 (like = happy).
“Do you like…? — am I telling you, or asking you?” → asking! (voice goes UP ↗).
Hold two fruit: “Is it a peach or a cherry?” (point + name).

Assumptions: food words + “I like / don't like / love” are solid from L1–L3; “Do you like…?” was FELT in the L3 story (Do you like pizza?) — today it becomes the FOCUS, asked about 6 new fruit. Phonics: o-e·u-e met last lesson (ур.22a, стр.61) — today consolidates. Emergent readers → real / plastic fruit, pictures & audio, minimal print. КТП listed apples/bananas/oranges/strawberries/pears, but PB p. 44 and AB p. 41 core is these 6 → plan follows the book. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
“Do you like…?” said flat (no question tune)Model the rising ↗ hand going up; over-exaggerate; hum the tune first, THEN add the words; the class “sings” the question.
Answers just “yes / no” (drop the short answer)Drill the FULL answer with a thumb: Yes, I do 👍 / No, I don't 👎; keep a do/don't gesture card visible.
Plural dropped — “I like grape / peach”Model grapesss, cherriez; CLAP the extra beat in pea-ches; echo drill in plural.
Over-excitement with real fruitSoft / plastic fruit only; one card each; “Freeze!” resets; one question → one calm answer.
Magic-e read short — “home” said /hom/ (short o)Point to the magic e: “the e makes o say its NAME — o-e /əʊ/ → home!”; picture appears for meaning; praise self-corrections.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Phonics Hero — Magic E: o-e·u-e (b)
board → desks

Recall the trick (quick). Board: hop → hope, cub → cube: The magic e is silent — o says /оу/, u says /ю:/! All show ✊→🖐 and wave the wand.

Read the word grid — тетрадь стр.62 №1 «Читаем слова». Read together, picture beside each word: nose · bone · home · note · cube · mule · cute · flute. Kagan — RallyRobin: pairs take turns down the grid, partner checks with the picture.

Gap-fill — стр.62 №2 «Допиши слово» (all vote first). Picture up (nose · bone · cube · flute) → every child votes: left hand = o, right hand = u → check → all write the vowel in their тетрадь.

Dictation · Showdown — стр.62 №3 (protect the clock). Now YOU write. Listen: nose… word once → segment on fingers → all write → Showdown: Show me! → reveal & self-check. Words: nose, bone (+ cube, tune if quick — today's MAIN aim is fruit + Do you like…?).

Слова-молнии — стр.62 №4. Strip home · hope · tune — read for speed, there and back! + tricky words (whole-word flash, not sounded): always · upon. (Story «Милый мул» стр.63 — tonight's home read-aloud.)

💪 Support: dictation with sound windows drawn (n-o-se) — they only fill letters. ⭐ Extension: write flute, or read a story frame solo.

20–27′
Presentation · fruit + Do you like…?
carpet

Present fruit (PB p. 44 ex. 1, CD2 24). Real / pictured, listen–point–say: mango · grape · peach · cherry · lemon · coconut. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children. Watch the plurals: grapes · peaches · cherries.

Present the grammar — the question & the answer. Hold up grapes: Do you like grapes? (hand rises ↗) → model both answers with thumbs: Yes, I do 👍 · No, I don't 👎. Say → gesture → children copy the tune AND the thumb. CCQ (see box): Do you like…? — telling or ASKING? (→ asking, voice up!)

▶ Ask-the-class call-response (all react). You ask a fruit, class answers with a thumb + full answer; speed up. Then a named child asks YOU. Contrast: Do you like a coconut? — /ˈkəʊkənʌt/ (3 claps).

▶ Fruit survey — Mix-Pair-Share
standing, mingle

▶ Fruit survey (Kagan — Mix-Pair-Share; every child moves, asks & answers — nobody sits out). Each child: a mini survey slip (6 fruit pictures, 👍/👎 columns) + a pencil.

Music plays → children MIX around the room; music stops → hands up, find the nearest partner. Partner A asks, B answers, A ticks: Do you like cherries? — Yes, I do! / No, I don't. Swap roles → 1 more fruit → mix again for a new partner (3–4 rounds). ICQ: Do you ASK, or just point? (→ ask the full question ↗!)

Quick share: back on the carpet, 2–3 children report one result: Sasha likes grapes! Class cheers.

💪 Support: a “Do you like ___?” sentence strip + thumbs cards clipped to the slip; pair the child with a strong peer for round 1. ⭐ Extension: add “What about you?” and report two friends — Ben and Mia like peaches!
