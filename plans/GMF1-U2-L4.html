🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 2 “Happy birthday!” · Lesson 4 — Vocab+Grammar: Adjectives · What's your favourite toy?
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · New vocab + structure together (PPP) · carpet + desks
Week 19.10 · Lesson 14✨ VOCAB+GRAM~12 pupils · groups of 4 + circle🦸 Phonics Hero ур.15b · ng·oo
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to describe toys with adjectives — big, small, old, new, fast, slow (Pupil's Book p. 22; + bonus pair long, short) — and ask & answer “What's your favourite toy? — My favourite toy is a (ball). It's old.”
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — the 10 toys (L1), “What is it? / Is it a…?” (L2), story adjectives-in-context slow/fast, small/big (L3), Starter colours — woven into describing: “It's a big red robot!”; phonics — Phonics Hero ур.15 (b) закрепление: ng·oo (3-column sort → short dictation → tricky one, only → story «Король поёт»); sing the song My favourite toy (CD1 35); personalisation (draw & tell about YOUR toy).
  • Personal. Keep adjective+noun order right (“a big ball”, not “a ball big”); give an ICQ before every game; every task keeps ALL children responding (own bingo card / show-me gestures / all groups play memory at once).
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the adjective pairs with gestures (big–small, old–new, fast–slow);
  • describe a toy: “It's a big red ball. It's new.”;
  • ask & answer “What's your favourite toy? — My favourite toy is a…”;
  • write dictated words with ch · sh · th · ng correctly.
Target language
Lexis
bigsmalloldnewfastslow + long+ short (recycled: 10 toys · colours; long/short = extension pair from КТП, PB core is 6)
Structure
What's your favourite toy? — My favourite toy is a (train). · It's (fast). It's (red and blue). · It's a big red robot!
Form
It's + adjective (It's old.) · adjective BEFORE the noun: a big ball · order size → colour: a big red ball.
Pron. watch
“favourite” = /ˈfeɪvrɪt/ (two beats, not “fa-vou-ri-te”); linking “is a” /ɪzə/; clear final sounds in fast, short.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.15 (b) закрепление: ng /ŋ/ · oo long/short — sort ring, moon, book, song, zoo, foot (3 columns) · dictation king, song, moon, book · tricky strip one, only · story «Король поёт» (тетрадь стр.40–42) · sight-word review: why, where, who, which.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Two teddies: worn-out vs shiny with a price tag. “Which is old?” (point) “Which is new?”
“A big ball or a ball big — which is English?” → a big ball (adjective first, gesture: adjective hand BEFORE noun hand).
“Favourite = the toy I like a little or the MOST?” → the most ❤️ — you have ONE favourite.

Assumptions: toys + Is it a…? solid from L1–L3; slow/fast, small/big already FELT in the story (Beth's scooter) — today they get named and drilled; “What's your favourite…?” was previewed with the Genki song in L2. Emergent readers → picture bingo & memory, minimal print.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
old/new too abstractReal objects: a worn teddy vs a boxed new toy; “old = grandpa voice”, “new = ta-daa!” gestures.
Wrong order: “a ball big / a red big ball”Chant with claps: a BIG (clap) RED (clap) BALL! — size before colour, adjective before noun; fix instantly, redrill.
“favourite” mangled or answered with a listDrill /ˈfeɪvrɪt/ by back-chaining; rule “ONE favourite” — Speaky models choosing just one.
Dictation panic (4 digraphs mixed)Sound-cards warm-up first (show-me), only 5–6 words, word said twice, praise self-corrections.
Memory game: one child hogs the cardsStrict turn circle in the group of 4; “flip two, everyone says the sentence together”.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.
Procedure — staging
highlighted = exact words to say (teacher / class) 🦝 = Speaky lines ▶ = game 🎮 = on-screen (projector) interactive […] = fill-in slot
⏱ Pacing — honest 60 min. This is a full plate, so pick, don't cram. Cognitive load: only the 6 adjectives + the whole-sentence chunk “It's a big red ball” and “What's your favourite toy?” are truly NEW — toys, colours, slow/fast·big/small (from the story) and the ng·oo digraphs are all recycling/consolidation, so keep those light and let the adjectives + word-order carry the challenge (scaffold: gestures, clap-chant, sentence frame). The phonics is REVISION, not today's main aim — dictate only 4–5 words and move on; if you're behind, cut it to a fast show-me and skip the writing. Buffers to drop first if short: the long/short bonus pair, Memory round 2 (adding colour), the 2nd Bingo round, the exit-ticket line-up (fold it into the door goodbye). Better every child says one full It's a big red ball. It's new. well than six stages half-done. Offline reserve: every 🎮 has a paper twin (flashcards / cards / dice) — the plan runs with no board at all.

Phonics Hero — ng·oo (b)

Speed reading — now WITH ng·oo. Strip on the board / Hub: ai · oa · ie · ee · or · ng · oo. There and back — beat your last time! Quick recall of the gestures: nose-hum si-ng! + cuckoo oo-oo!

Sort — тетрадь стр.40 №2 «Раздели слова» (all vote first). Three columns: ng · oo долгий (moon) · oo короткий (book). Show a word — ring · moon · book · song · zoo · foot — read it together, then every child shows 1, 2 or 3 fingers for the column. Check → all write it in the right column. RallyCoach: partner checks before the next word.

Dictation — стр.40 №3 (everyone writes, protect the clock). 4 words only, each said once: king · song · moon · book → write in the тетрадь → Kagan — Showdown: write secretly, on Showdown! all reveal at once, compare in the group, fix together. Tricky strip стр.40 №4: one · only — read for speed, there and back; then flash the review set: why · where · who · which. (Behind time? Cut the dictation to 2 words — phonics is consolidation, the adjectives are today's aim.)

Story taste — стр.41 «Король поёт» (1–2′, settler). Chorus the 5 frames, finger under the words: A king. The king has corn. The king gets a fork. Ring, ring! The king sings. The king is on a long swing! — every line is our ng/or sounds in action. The draw-task («The king sings», стр.41 №2) and the second story «Кот и луна» (стр.42) go home.

🎮 Reading-game option: read-race — ⚔ battle mode, level «Диграфы»: a word pops up, everyone reads it aloud, the fastest correct team races ahead. Swap it in for the speed-reading strip when the screen is free.

💪 Support: sort with the word card in hand — they only place it under the column and copy; in dictation give k___. ⭐ Extension: a 5th dictation word by ear (zoo or ring).

24–32′
Presentation · Adjectives
carpet

Present in contrast pairs (PB p. 22 ex. 1, CD1 34). Real objects + pictures: big (arms wide) — small (pinch fingers) · old (grandpa voice, worn teddy) — new (ta-daa!, price tag) · fast (quick feet) — slow (snail arms). Listen, point and say with the CD. Bonus pair for our group: long — short (kite tail gesture).

▶ Opposite! call-response (all react). You say + gesture one adjective, class instantly shouts the OPPOSITE + gesture: Big! — Small! Speed up; then trick them by pointing at a toy: It's a big… ? — ROBOT!

Order drill. Clap-chant: a BIG · RED · BALL! a SMALL · NEW · CAR! — adjective before the noun, size before colour. CCQ (see box).

💪 Support: keep both gesture + picture up; child says just the adjective, teacher adds the noun (…ball!). ⭐ Extension: full three-word chunk with two adjectives — a big NEW ball!
🎮 Интерактив (проектор): “What's this?” spinner — describe the toy Screen: a wheel of toy pictures, each tagged with an adjective + a colour (a big red ball · a small new car · an old teddy…); arrow spins and lands on one. Mechanics: tap Spin → arrow stops → whole class says the full chunk, then a named child; land twice to compare a pair (big vs small). Language: What's this? — It's a big red ball! Teacher: spin 6–8×, speed up; stop on a size+colour toy to re-run the clap-chant word order. ✅ ГОТОВ — Unit 1 “What's this?” spinner (swap deck to adjective+toy cards). 📴 Offline: paper twin = flip an adjective card + a toy card together, class says the chunk.

32–38′
▶ Opposites memory
desks, groups of 4

▶ Opposites Memory (card game; Mido №45 “Concentration” — classic table version, = our Игротека «Мемори»; all groups play simultaneously). Each group of 4: 12 cards face-down — 6 adjective pictures in opposite pairs (big teddy/small teddy, old car/new car, fast scooter/slow snail-scooter).

Turn order round the group: flip two cards, say BOTH aloud — It's a big teddy… it's a small teddy — opposites, my pair! Opposite pair found → keep it (whole group repeats the two sentences chorally). Not a pair → face-down again. ICQ: Do you keep two BIG cards? (→ No — opposites make the pair!)

Most pairs wins a 🦝 sticker.

💪 Support: start with only 8 cards (4 pairs) face-down and let the child name one word (teacher adds the opposite). ⭐ Extension: round 2 adds colour — It's a big brown teddy! — and a full opposite sentence each flip.
🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Opposites Match — memory pairs Screen: a grid of face-down cards; tapping flips two; a matched opposite pair (big↔small) stays up & Speaky reads both, a non-match flips back. Mechanics: a volunteer taps while the whole class says BOTH cards aloud before the second flip — everyone commits an answer, not just the tapper. Language: It's a big teddy… it's a small teddy — opposites! Teacher: use for the whole-class warm-round; then groups play their paper sets simultaneously. 🔨 СОБРАТЬ — reuse the Unit 1 Put it! tap/flip engine as a match-two grid (re-skin art to opposite-pair toys). 📴 Offline: the physical 12-card memory set × 3 groups IS the game — board is optional.

44–49′
▶ Describe-it bingo
desks

▶ Describe-it Bingo (grid game; Mido №7 re-skin for non-readers — every child plays their own card). Each child a 3×3 picture card: toys drawn big/small/old/new/fast/slow (4–5 different card versions so neighbours differ).

Teacher (then a confident pupil) calls full sentences: It's a SMALL car! · It's an OLD teddy! · It's a FAST scooter! — children find and cover/mark that picture. Line of three → Bingo! — winner must SAY their line back: It's a big ball, it's a new kite, it's a slow train! 2 rounds. Countdown to close: 5…4…3…2…1.

🎮 Digital option (projector): toys-board — S&S dice board game in teams: tap the die, hop along the board, and the whole team says the square aloud — It's a kite! A quick consolidation round if the screen is free.

💪 Support: call the adjective slowly + hold the matching gesture; a buddy can point on their card. ⭐ Extension: the child-caller invents the sentences; winner adds a colour when saying the line back.
🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — match the description Screen: a board of toy pictures (big/small/old/new/fast/slow); a spoken sentence names one. Mechanics: say the sentence → children point/predict on their OWN card FIRST → tap the picture → it lights up to confirm; keeps every child answering, not just the tapper. Language: Find it — it's a small old teddy! Teacher: ask Which one? and count 3-2-1 BEFORE tapping so all commit. ✅ ГОТОВ — Unit 1 Find it! engine (re-skin deck to described toys). 📴 Offline: paper twin = the printed 3×3 bingo cards, teacher calls the sentences.

49–53′
Create + Production · my favourite toy
desks → carpet, 2 circles

Create — MY favourite toy! 🦝 Speaky: Now make YOUR OWN! Draw your favourite toy and choose TWO words for it. Each child draws a quick toy on a mini-card and decides 2 adjectives (this is their card for the circle — and a rehearsal for the homework “Draw and write”). Whisper-plan the sentence: My favourite toy is a … It's … and it's … Every child invents their own, not a given card.

Kagan — Inside-Outside Circle (everyone speaks ~4 times). Two circles face to face, each holds THEIR OWN drawing. Inside asks: What's your favourite toy? Outside: My favourite toy is a […]. It's [big] and it's [new]! Swap roles → outer circle rotates one step → new partner. 3–4 rotations, music between steps.

Finish: 2–3 volunteers show 🦝 Speaky their drawing: My favourite toy is a robot. It's big and blue!

💪 Support: a blank toy outline to colour + the frame on the board (It's a ___.); one adjective is enough. ⭐ Extension: two adjectives + a reason gesture, or ask the partner back And you? What's YOUR favourite toy? 		blank mini-cards + crayons · music mp3 · Speaky · ▶ Inside-Outside Circle
