🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 2 “Happy birthday!” · Lesson 4 — Vocab+Grammar: Adjectives · What's your favourite toy?
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · New vocab + structure together (PPP) · carpet + desks
Week 19.10 · Lesson 14✨ VOCAB+GRAM~12 pupils · groups of 4 + circle
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to describe toys with adjectives — big, small, old, new, fast, slow (Pupil's Book p. 22; + bonus pair long, short) — and ask & answer “What's your favourite toy? — My favourite toy is a (ball). It's old.”
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — the 10 toys (L1), “What is it? / Is it a…?” (L2), story adjectives-in-context slow/fast, small/big (L3), Starter colours — woven into describing: “It's a big red robot!”; phonics — revision dictation ch·sh·th·ng (JP Book 2, p. 36); sing the song My favourite toy (CD1 35); personalisation (draw & tell about YOUR toy).
  • Personal. Keep adjective+noun order right (“a big ball”, not “a ball big”); give an ICQ before every game; every task keeps ALL children responding (own bingo card / show-me gestures / all groups play memory at once).
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the adjective pairs with gestures (big–small, old–new, fast–slow);
  • describe a toy: “It's a big red ball. It's new.”;
  • ask & answer “What's your favourite toy? — My favourite toy is a…”;
  • write dictated words with ch · sh · th · ng correctly.
Target language
Lexis
bigsmalloldnewfastslow + long+ short (recycled: 10 toys · colours; long/short = extension pair from КТП, PB core is 6)
Structure
What's your favourite toy? — My favourite toy is a (train). · It's (fast). It's (red and blue). · It's a big red robot!
Form
It's + adjective (It's old.) · adjective BEFORE the noun: a big ball · order size → colour: a big red ball.
Pron. watch
“favourite” = /ˈfeɪvrɪt/ (two beats, not “fa-vou-ri-te”); linking “is a” /ɪzə/; clear final sounds in fast, short.
Phonics
Jolly focus: revision ch · sh · th · ng — dictation (chip, ship, three, ring…, JP Book 2 p. 36) · tricky words revision: why, where, who, which.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Two teddies: worn-out vs shiny with a price tag. “Which is old?” (point) “Which is new?”
“A big ball or a ball big — which is English?” → a big ball (adjective first, gesture: adjective hand BEFORE noun hand).
“Favourite = the toy I like a little or the MOST?” → the most ❤️ — you have ONE favourite.

Assumptions: toys + Is it a…? solid from L1–L3; slow/fast, small/big already FELT in the story (Beth's scooter) — today they get named and drilled; “What's your favourite…?” was previewed with the Genki song in L2. Emergent readers → picture bingo & memory, minimal print.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
old/new too abstractReal objects: a worn teddy vs a boxed new toy; “old = grandpa voice”, “new = ta-daa!” gestures.
Wrong order: “a ball big / a red big ball”Chant with claps: a BIG (clap) RED (clap) BALL! — size before colour, adjective before noun; fix instantly, redrill.
“favourite” mangled or answered with a listDrill /ˈfeɪvrɪt/ by back-chaining; rule “ONE favourite” — Speaky models choosing just one.
Dictation panic (4 digraphs mixed)Sound-cards warm-up first (show-me), only 5–6 words, word said twice, praise self-corrections.
Memory game: one child hogs the cardsStrict turn circle in the group of 4; “flip two, everyone says the sentence together”.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Phonics — ch·sh·th·ng revision + dictation
at the board

Recognise — wake up all four sounds. Four cards on the board: ch · sh · th · ng. Chorus each with its action (train “ch-ch” · “shhh” finger · tongue-teeth “th” · nose-hum “ng”).

Sound show-me (all react). Every child a fan of 4 digraph cards. Say words — chip · ship · three · ring · shop · chop · sing · bath · this · long… → Show me the sound! — all hold up the right card. 10 words, fast; instant check every round.

Blend (all together). Push the sounds: /sh/ /o/ /p/… shop! 3–4 words across the digraphs.

Dictation (everyone writes) — today's big job. Now the dictation — you are writers! 5–6 words, each said twice: chip · ship · three · ring · shop · long → mini-whiteboards → Kagan — Showdown: write secretly, on Showdown! all reveal at once, compare in the group, fix together. Tricky words flash to finish: why · where · who · which.

24–32′
Presentation · Adjectives
carpet

Present in contrast pairs (PB p. 22 ex. 1, CD1 34). Real objects + pictures: big (arms wide) — small (pinch fingers) · old (grandpa voice, worn teddy) — new (ta-daa!, price tag) · fast (quick feet) — slow (snail arms). Listen, point and say with the CD. Bonus pair for our group: long — short (kite tail gesture).

▶ Opposite! call-response (all react). You say + gesture one adjective, class instantly shouts the OPPOSITE + gesture: Big! — Small! Speed up; then trick them by pointing at a toy: It's a big… ? — ROBOT!

Order drill. Clap-chant: a BIG · RED · BALL! a SMALL · NEW · CAR! — adjective before the noun, size before colour. CCQ (see box).

 PB p. 22 ex. 1 · CD1 34 (Navio) · worn teddy + “new” toy · adjective picture cards
32–38′
▶ Opposites memory
desks, groups of 4

▶ Opposites Memory (card game; Mido №45 “Concentration” — classic table version, = our Игротека «Мемори»; all groups play simultaneously). Each group of 4: 12 cards face-down — 6 adjective pictures in opposite pairs (big teddy/small teddy, old car/new car, fast scooter/slow snail-scooter).

Turn order round the group: flip two cards, say BOTH aloud — It's a big teddy… it's a small teddy — opposites, my pair! Opposite pair found → keep it (whole group repeats the two sentences chorally). Not a pair → face-down again. ICQ: Do you keep two BIG cards? (→ No — opposites make the pair!)

Most pairs wins a 🦝 sticker. Fast groups: round 2 adding colour — It's a big brown teddy!

 memory set (12 opposite-pair cards) × 3 groups

39–44′
Song · My favourite toy
desks, books

PB p. 22 ex. 2 (CD1 35). Listen and point: whose toy is fast and new? Whose toy is old and big? Play My favourite toy; children point to the train / the teddy in the picture. Sing along with gestures (fast! new! red and blue! old! big!). Two key lines to own: My favourite toy is a train. It's fast. It's new.

Model the question (PB ex. 3, CD1 36). Listen and repeat: What's your favourite toy? — My favourite toy is a ball. It's old. Quick choral drill, back-chain favourite: …rite → favourite → my favourite toy.

 PB p. 22 ex. 2–3 · CD1 35–36 (Navio)
44–49′
▶ Describe-it bingo
desks

▶ Describe-it Bingo (grid game; Mido №7 re-skin for non-readers — every child plays their own card). Each child a 3×3 picture card: toys drawn big/small/old/new/fast/slow (4–5 different card versions so neighbours differ).

Teacher (then a confident pupil) calls full sentences: It's a SMALL car! · It's an OLD teddy! · It's a FAST scooter! — children find and cover/mark that picture. Line of three → Bingo! — winner must SAY their line back: It's a big ball, it's a new kite, it's a slow train! 2 rounds. Countdown to close: 5…4…3…2…1.

 printed bingo cards (3×3, 4–5 variants) · counters/pencils
49–53′
Production · favourite toys
carpet, 2 circles

Kagan — Inside-Outside Circle (everyone speaks ~4 times). Two circles face to face, each child holds ONE toy mini-card (their “favourite” for the game — or their own drawing). Inside asks: What's your favourite toy? Outside: My favourite toy is a […]. It's [big / new / fast]! Swap roles → outer circle rotates one step → new partner. 3–4 rotations, music between steps.

Finish: 2–3 volunteers tell 🦝 Speaky about a REAL favourite toy at home: My favourite toy is a robot. It's big and blue!
