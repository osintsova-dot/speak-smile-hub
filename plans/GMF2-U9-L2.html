Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to ask & answer about the past: “Where were you yesterday? — I was at the (theatre). / Where were they? — They were at the (funfair).”
  • Subsidiary. Understand the holiday dialogue (CD4 14); the time machine ritual (present ↔ past switch); alibi mini-game; write a yesterday-sentence (AB format).
  • Personal. am/is→was, are→were — teach as time-machine partners, not a table; every sentence needs the thumb-back anchor until it's automatic.
By the end, pupils can…
  • ask “Where were you?” and answer truthfully;
  • use was (I/he/she) vs were (you/we/they) correctly;
  • play a full alibi round in the past.

Structure
Where were you (yesterday)? — I was at the (circus).  ·  Where were they? — They were at the (castle).
Form
time machine: am/is → was · are → were. Question flips: You were → Were you…? (seed only).
Pron. watch
was weak /wəz/ in sentences, strong /wɒz/ alone; were /wɜː/; yesterday 3 beats.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“I AM at school — yesterday I…?” → WAS at school!
“They ARE at the funfair — yesterday they…?” → WERE!

Assumptions: The I-was chunk seeded in L1. Time-machine metaphor + gestures make the system learnable without tables. Places automatic. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Alibi! · detective game
carpet, pairs

Model. The cookie jar is empty! Detective (you) interrogates: Where were you yesterday at 3 o'clock?! Suspect shows an alibi card: I was at the sports centre!

▶ Detective pairs. Each child: alibi card. A interrogates B (2 questions: where were you? + Were you at the baker's?!), B answers by card; swap. Trade cards, new partner — 3 rounds.

💪 Support: alibi card has the full sentence. ⭐ Extension: detective adds a trick question: Were you with Toby?!
