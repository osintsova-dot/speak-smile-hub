🦝 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Unit 1 “Start speaking” · Lesson 1 — Warm-up commands & Rock Paper Scissors
Pre-A1 preschoolers (~5 y.o., non-readers, first English class) · 60 min · TPR + songs + games (no reading / writing) · chairs + carpet + tables
Week 03.09–06.09 · Lesson 1 of 72✨ FIRST LESSON OF THE YEAR12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 All by ear — no text, no phonics
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to understand and physically respond to 8 classroom commands (stand up, sit down, hands up, hands down, bow, clap, jump, cheer) and play Rock–Paper–Scissors in English using “Rock, paper, scissors! I win!”
  • Subsidiary. Build a warm, safe first-lesson routine (door greeting · Speaky · attention signals · goodbye); train listening for a command; a language-linked craft settler (applique-by-command — listen → stick → say the command); a draw-&-tell finish — draw “I win!” and say it to the teacher/partner (early production).
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk tiny & command-based; give an ICQ before every game; alternate a stirrer ⚡ with a settler 🧘; hold to max 3 game-peaks (don’t over-game); make sure every child moves/speaks every time (no queue).
By the end, pupils can…
  • do the action when they hear the command;
  • say the command word while doing it;
  • show & name rock · paper · scissors and play a round;
  • draw & tell one chunk (“I win!”) to the teacher/partner;
  • say hello · goodbye · thank you to Speaky.

Chunks
Stand up! · Sit down! · Hands up!  ·  Rock, paper, scissors, one-two-three!  ·  I win!  ·  Hello / Goodbye / Thank you · Come with me!
TPR gesture
each word = one clear body action (Speaky models). rock = fist · paper = flat hand · scissors = two fingers.
Pron. watch
final /p/ in up heard; scissors /ˈsɪzəz/ (not “skissors”); bow /baʊ/; keep cheer a happy shout, not a word to spell.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: “Do we MOVE or do we WATCH?” → move (thumbs-up).
ICQ (Please game): “Do it only when I say…?” → please.

Assumptions: most children have never had an English lesson and know little/no English → we rely on gesture, tone, pictures and music, NOT on words they must read. They are non-readers & non-writers (no phonics, no worksheets with text — “writing” = tracing a picture & sticking). Attention span is short (5–7 min blocks) → constant stirrer/settler rhythm. Group = 12 children (standard). Work is mostly whole-group on the carpet + shoulder-partner (the child next to you / teacher / assistant); when a game needs teams, max 4 per team (12 = 3 teams of 4). No fixed teams in this first lesson.

Anticipated problems & solutions (first-lesson focus)
Likely problemWhat you do
A child cries / clings to parent / won’t come inKneel to eye level, warm voice, no pressure: let them sit on a chair and just watch — Speaky waves, invites, never forces. Most join within one song.
Child is shy, won’t speakSpeaking is optional; doing the action counts. Praise participation, not words. Whole-group choral hides individual fear.
No English at all — blank facesEvery command has a gesture; say word + do action together, 3×. Meaning comes from the body, not translation.
Over-excitement / running wild after a stirrerAlways follow a ⚡ stirrer with a 🧘 settler; use Freeze! + volume control arm; sit back on chairs/carpet.
Losing at Rock-Paper-Scissors → tearsPre-teach the golden rule Try again! — losing is fine, we just play again. Model losing happily yourself.
Transitions (chairs → carpet → tables) get chaoticMove as a train: Come with me! — one signal, one destination at a time.
Classroom management — signals (teach each in a calm moment first)
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 🖐 — raise your arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1… sit down.” with fingers shown.
“Come with me!” 🚂 — line up as a train to change zone (door · carpet · tables).
Lesson framework — the shape of EVERY Genki preschool lesson (all 72)

Fixed skeleton — reuse this order in every plan. Keep the ⚡/🧘 rhythm, but max 2–3 game-peaks per lesson — don’t over-game.

  • 1 · Greeting 🚪 🧘 — door welcome + Speaky arrives.
  • 2 · Warm-up song ⚡ — always Genki Disco Warm Up (ritual + today’s language).
  • 3 · Core games ⚡/🧘 — 2–3 games max from the Genki bank, each with its song (here: one command game + the topic game). Everything else = optional/for variety, NOT in the main timing.
  • 4 · Craft / worksheet block 🧘 — every craft MUST carry a language link (listen → do → say), never craft for craft’s sake. Rotates each lesson: applique-by-command (stick on command) · draw-&-tell (draw & say) · magnet / puzzle sort by category (say as you sort) · sticker-match picture ↔ word (match & name).
  • 5 · 📖 Story time 🧘 — our own S&S big-book (Speaky), Brown-Bear mechanic, choral refrain.
  • 6 · Workbook worksheet — draw & tell 🧘 — child draws (in topic) & tells the teacher/partner in English. Every lesson.
  • 7 · Reflection + Goodbye + Exit 🧘 — “what did we learn” · goodbye song · exit ticket + reward sticker.

4 · Topic game ⚡ (game 3) — Rock, Paper, Scissors
carpet → path game
⚡ move

Teach the gestures. 🦝 Let’s play “камень-ножницы-бумага” in English! Drill: Rock! (fist) · Paper! (flat hand) · Scissors! (two fingers) · One, two, three! Everyone practises the chant + hands, no music.

▶ Genki song 02 — Rock, Paper, Scissors. Play rounds vs Speaky, then with your shoulder-partner. Winner shouts I win! Golden rule if you lose: Try again! (model losing happily yourself).

▶ Card-walk tournament. Action cards in a path on the floor; teacher one end, children the other. Step → name the card; step → next; meet in the middle → Rock, paper, scissors! Winner takes the card, start again till cards run out. Everyone plays each round — nobody just watches.

5 · Craft — applique-by-command 🧘
tables 🪑

Well done! Come with me! 🚂 To the tables. Language link — listen → do → say. Genki has no cut-&-stick sheet for the warm-up commands, so this uses our S&S applique-by-command sheet (Oksana’s own): hand out the black-&-white base sheet + a set of colour cut-out Speaky pictures, one per action (stand up · sit down · hands up · hands down · bow · clap · cheer).

Mechanic. Teacher commands Speaky, stand up, please! → each child finds the matching colour picture (Speaky standing), glues it in the right place and the class choruses Stand up! Then Speaky, sit down! … work through the actions. Listen → recognise → stick → say — every child engaged.

Countdown: 5… 4… 3… 2… 1… glue down.
