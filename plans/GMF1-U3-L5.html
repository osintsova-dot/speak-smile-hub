🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 3 “At the circus” · Lesson 5 — Phonics · Culture · Literacy: An Action Rhyme
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Phonics + CLIL culture + guided literacy · carpet + desks
Week 16.11–22.11 · Lesson 22L5 Phon+Cult+Litcontinuing group~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4🦸 Phonics Hero ур.19b · Magic E: краткий/долгий
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the /h/ sound (Toby's tongue twister “Helen the happy hippo hops and hops”), understand what British children do on Circus Day at school (paint faces, spin a plate, juggle scarves, ride a special bike, walk on stilts) and read, act out & complete their own “My feelings” action rhyme (When I'm sad, I sit and cry… happy · sad · angry · tired).
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning inside the topic — Unit 3 body parts + have got and describing hair/eyes (L1–L4) applied to /h/ words that are body words (hair · hand · head) and to Circus-Day faces (He's got a big nose / long legs); Phonics Hero ур.19 (b) закрепление — Magic E «Краткий ✊ или долгий 🖐?»: ладошка-игра → sort → dictation (тетрадь стр.54); tricky words more · before.
  • Personal. L5 packs three strands — keep each tight, protect the pace: heavy dictation → homework; ONE feeling line copied per child; model everything first. Every child responds in every task (own mini-whiteboard / pant-or-freeze show-me / thumbs vote / act the feeling) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tongue twister with a clear soft English /h/ and spot words that begin with /h/;
  • name 2–3 things British children do on Circus Day;
  • read the “My feelings” action rhyme, do the actions and complete it (happy · sad · angry · tired);
  • show ✊/🖐 for short vs long vowels, sort cat, rain, hop, bee, bed, moon into columns and write them from dictation.

Structure
Helen the happy hippo hops and hops.  ·  On Circus Day we (paint our faces / juggle scarves / walk on stilts).  ·  When I'm (sad), I (sit and cry).  ·  He's/She's got (a big nose / long legs). (recycled)
Form
Initial /h/ + word. Rhyme = fixed frame When I'm (feeling), I (action) — copied, not built. Feelings = adjectives after I'm.
Pron. watch
English /h/ — a soft breath out of the mouth (“fog up a little mirror”, pant like a tired hippo after hopping). Not the hard scrapy Russian «х». Keep it light in Helen, happy, hippo, hops, hand, head.
Phonics
Coursebook sound: /h/ (PB p. 33, CD2 5) — hand up to mouth, breathe out h-h-h as if you've been running · Phonics Hero ур.19 (b) закрепление: «Краткий ✊ или долгий 🖐?» — ладошка-игра cat, rain, kit, kite, bed, bee, cub, cube, hop, moon · sort & dictation cat, rain, hop, bee (тетрадь стр.54). Tricky words: more · before.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold up the hippo: Helen sound — a hard scrapy «х», or a soft breath h-h-h? → soft breath h-h-h (fog a mirror, pant like a hippo).
On Circus Day — do British children paint their faces or do maths? → Paint faces!   Rhyme — When I'm angry, do I smile, or stamp my feet? → Stamp my feet!

Assumptions: body parts + have got + hair/eyes known from L1–L4; children can copy letters but not write freely → all writing is trace/copy from a board frame. Emergent readers: rhyme is read together, finger-following. Phonics: the vowel secret ✊/🖐 met last lesson (ур.19a, стр.53) — today consolidates. Circus-Day activities are new → model each mime first. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Can't tell h-words from othersPicture anchors (hat·hair·hand·head·horse·happy) + pant-or-freeze show-me; exaggerate the first breath.
Rhyme copying slow / letters unknownBoard frame written LARGE, ONE feeling word per line; feeling words on the board to copy; teacher scribes for weakest.
Over-excited in the Circus-Day role-playMime seated/on the spot first, one action at a time; ICQ: Circus juggling — big arms or big feet? — Arms! Volume-control arm to settle.
Three strands feel rushed (main L5 risk)Timer each block; dictation → HW; fast finishers get the “draw your own feeling face” extension, not more teacher input.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

10–23′
Phonics — /h/ + Phonics Hero Magic E (b)
board → desks

Recognise /h/ (~2½′). Toby's tongue twister (PB p. 33, CD2 5): h – h – h. Helen the happy hippo hops and hops! Gesture — hand up to the mouth, breathe out h-h-h as if you've been running; soft breath, no scrapy «х». Choral ×3: slow → normal → fast. 2–3 brave solos with the “microphone”. 💪 Support: hold up the /h/ picture-anchor card (the hippo) as the sound cue — weak pupils watch it, not the letter.

“h or not h?” — whole-class show-me (~2′, all react). Pictures one by one — hat · hair · arm · horse · hand · happy · eye · head (AB p. 30 set). Begins with /h/ → everyone pants h-h-h with a hand at the mouth; not /h/ → freeze, hands on knees. Show me! Fast, nobody just watches.

🎮 Projector: Flashcards — “h or not h?” ✅ ready
  • Screen: big picture flashcards, one tap = next card.
  • Kids: whole class pants for /h/ words, freezes for the rest — every child reacts at once.
  • Teacher: use the existing Flashcards tool; exaggerate the first breath.

Ладошка-игра — тетрадь стр.54 №1 (~2′, all react). Recall the vowel secret: fist ✊ = short, palm 🖐 = the vowel says its NAME. Teacher reads the strip — cat · rain · kit · kite · bed · bee · cub · cube · hop · moon — every child shows ✊ or 🖐 for each word; the pairs (kit/kite, cub/cube) get an extra slow-motion replay.

Sort — стр.54 №2 «Раздели слова» (all vote, then write). Six words — cat · rain · hop · bee · bed · moon: read together → all vote ✊/🖐 → check → everyone writes the word into the right column in their тетрадь. Kagan — RallyCoach: partner checks the column: Good job!

Dictation — стр.54 №3 (everyone writes). Now YOU write. Listen: cat… word once → segment on fingers together → all write → Show me! → reveal on the board & self-check. Words: cat, rain, hop, bee. Finish: colour the star «Я молодец!» (стр.54).

🎮 Projector: Phonics Hub — speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/ · набор «Краткий или долгий? 🖐» ✅ ready
  • Station 4 «Сегментинг (диктант)»: the screen checks the dictation words after the тетрадь round.
  • Kids: write first on paper — the Hub is the reveal & check; children voice everything, not the app.
  • Offline: тетрадь стр.54 + the board reveal do the same job.

Tricky words (~1½′). Flash cards more · before: We just KNOW these — look, say: more… before. Choral ×2 + air-write. Then straight to the face song — keep the pace up.

Optional / fast finishers: ▶ Fast finger! (AB p. 30 ex. 3, in pairs) — Pupil A says an h-word, Pupil B points fast, then swap; both children work at once. · ⭐ Extension: think of one more long-vowel word for the 🖐 column (kite? cube?).

🎮 Reading-game option: read-race — ⚔ battle mode, level «Продвинутые»: a word pops up, everyone reads it aloud, the fastest correct team races ahead. Swap it in for the speed-reading strip when the screen is free.

Literacy — “My feelings” action rhyme
desks

Before you read (PB p. 35). How are you today? Look — happy, sad, angry, tired. Show me a happy face!… a sad face!… an angry face!… a tired face! — everyone pulls each face.

Listen, read & ACT the rhyme (CD2 9) — fingers follow. Read “My feelings” together with the action for each line: When I'm sad, I sit and cry. When I'm happy, I laugh and smile. When I'm angry, I stamp my feet. When I'm tired, I go to sleep. Do the actions ×2 — sit·cry, laugh·smile, stamp feet, sleep. CCQ: Angry — smile or stamp? — Stamp my feet!

Complete YOUR rhyme (literacy product — Activity Book p. 32). AB p. 32 ex. 1 Read and draw the faces (angry·sad·tired·happy) + ex. 2 Read the rhyme. Write. — copy the missing feeling word into each “When I'm ___,” line from the board frame (written LARGE). 💪 Support: feeling words listed on the board to copy; teacher scribes for the weakest. ⭐ Extension: add a capital letter, or invent one more line (When I'm happy, I…). Timer 8 min, countdown finish: 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down!
