Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Consolidation + flagship team quiz with reward · carpet + desks

Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will have consolidated all Unit 5 language — the farm animals (cow, horse, sheep, pig, duck, hen/chicken, goat, donkey, rabbit, dog + mouse, cat, frog, bird), “Can you see a…? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't.”, ability with the six actions (fly, swim, run, walk, climb, jump) — “(Ducks) can swim. (Pigs) can't fly.” — and describing an animal by what it can do + its colour (“This animal can jump. It's green. — A frog!”), and can use it confidently in the review tasks and the quiz.
  • Subsidiary. Phonics — Phonics Hero ур.28 (a) первое знакомство: sound /ю:/ in three houses ue · ew · u-e (blue, new, cube; trace blue, new); tricky words once, upon; self-assessment (My progress traffic light, AB p. 55 ex. 3); listening for detail (PB p. 58 ex. 1, CD2 53).
  • Personal. Run the flagship quiz by PIES: every child writes/shows every answer (Showdown with mini-boards), the responder is picked by Numbered Heads — no “captains answer everything”; keep the reward moment short so language time wins.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name all the farm animals and answer “Can you see a…?” without support;
  • ask & answer “Can you see a (goat)? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't.”;
  • say what animals can & can't do — “(Horses) can run. (Cows) can't climb.”;
  • read their first /ю:/ house-words (blue · new · cube) and trace blue · new;
  • self-assess with the traffic light — ready for the Unit 5 test.

Form
can / can't + base verb (no -s: a duck can swim). · Can you see a + noun? · It's + colour.
Pron. watch
clear final /t/ in can't (weak can /kən/ vs strong can't /kɑːnt/); the long /ю:/ vowel (blue, new); sh in sheep, shush.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.28 (a) первое знакомство: sound /ю:/ — three houses ue (blue, glue) · ew (new, chew) · u-e (cube, cute); trace blue · new (тетрадь стр.77). Tricky words: once, upon.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold up a pig: Can a pig FLY? Yes or no? → no → “Pigs can't fly.” (thumb-up = can · arms-cross = can't)
Quiz rule ICQ: Who writes the answer — one child or EVERYBODY? → everybody, on their own board!

Assumptions: all Unit 5 language met in L1–L5; the /и:/, /ай/ and /оу/ house-families (Phonics Hero ур.25–27) are done — today the same house-idea moves to /ю:/ (ур.28a, стр.77). Teams of 4 with numbered members (1–4) are routine. The flagship quiz with reward runs only on Review day — children know it's special.

17–24′
Review in motion
📺 open space · teams

▶ Can you see a…? — Farm Spy Hunt (everyone answers, nobody waits). Before the lesson 🦝 Speaky “hid” animal cards around the room (some easy to spot, some tricky, some not there at all). Ask: Can you see a horse? Think–Pair first: everyone whispers the answer to a neighbour. Then call a number → that child answers aloud: Yes, I can! / No, I can't! Class confirms chorally.

▶ Can / can't — thumbs-or-cross (all react at once). You say a sentence; the WHOLE class shows thumb-up (true) or arms-cross (false) + says it right: Ducks can swim! → 👍 Yes! Ducks can swim! · Cows can climb! → ❌ No! Cows can't climb! 6–7 rounds mixing all Unit 5 actions. Attention reset: Class? — Yes!

Option — big room? Animal Charades relay (Mido №47 Sentence Charades). A child draws a card (I can see a cow · Pigs can't fly · A frog can jump) and mimes it; the team races to say the whole sentence. New mimer each round — drama variety when space allows.

Support ▸ weaker pupils keep the animal picture card in hand + answer with a partner (echo the chunk).   Extension ▸ stronger pupils add a reason — No, I can't. But I can see a duck! It can swim.

33–51′
🏆 FLAGSHIP QUIZ with reward
📺 teams · projector

Set-up (2 min). 3 teams of 4, numbered heads 1–4, one mini-whiteboard EACH. Quiz grid on screen — categories: Farm animals 🐄 · Can you see…? 👀 · Can & can't 🏃 · Sounds & spelling 🔤. Rules + ICQ: Who writes? — Everybody! When do you show? — On “Showdown!”

Round flow (Kagan Showdown + Numbered Heads — all answer every question). Question up (e.g. picture half-hidden: What is it? · count: Can you see three goats? — Yes/No · ability: Can a pig fly? → Pigs can't fly. · sound: Write the word — b-l-ue! · read a /ю:/ word). Every child writes/draws → Showdown! → all boards up → call a number: Number 3! — that child of the playing team answers aloud in a full sentence.

Predict (thinking). On the half-hidden animal, before revealing: Look — just an ear! What do you THINK it is? Write your guess! Then reveal — Were you right? Turns each round into a little guess-and-check.

Reward shot. Correct = the answering child takes the shot: Bowling for Animals — soft “animal pins” by the board, child rolls the ball, class counts the fallen animals in English: One, two, three animals down! GOAL! (Mido №91 — on-theme for a farm unit.) On projector → animated football penalty as an alternative. Rotate teams; over ~10–12 questions every child shoots at least once.

Support ▸ picture/word bank stays visible on a side card so weaker writers copy, not freeze; accept one correct word from them.   Extension ▸ when a strong pupil is “Number”, require the full sentence — It's a horse. A horse can run, but it can't fly. — not just the noun.
🔨 Flagship interactive · projector · BUILD · “Unit 5 Team Quiz + Reward”  🔨 BUILD
Screen 1 — Grid: 4×3 board of numbered tiles under the 4 category headers (Farm animals 🐄 / Can you see…? 👀 / Can & can't 🏃 / Sounds & spelling 🔤). Tap a tile → it flips to the question (picture / half-hidden animal / count scene / ability prompt / read a /ю:/ word). Used tiles grey out.
Screen 2 — Answer: big “Show your boards!” + a 5-second timer bar; tap ✔ / ✗ to score; a full-sentence model appears for choral echo.
Screen 3 — Reward mini-game: bowling for animals — child “rolls”, animal pins tumble, count them (or a football-penalty reskin). One shot = +points.
Screen 4 — Scoreboard: 3 team bars, huge numbers readable from the back row, plus a “Teamwork ⭐” toggle for best cooperation.
Mechanic: pick tile → question → everyone writes → Showdown → Numbered Head answers → score → reward shot → rotate team. ~10–12 tiles ≈ 15 min.
Target language: all Unit 5 — What's this? / Can you see a…? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't. / (Ducks) can swim. / (Pigs) can't fly. + read a /ю:/ word.
Teacher: host only — YOU tap tiles & call the number so no captain dominates. Spec is a self-contained HTML slide (S&S purple #6C3FC5 / honey #F5C842). Offline reserve: printed grid + soft animal pins + ball works identically.

Finish with totals + teamwork star: Give me five, champions!
