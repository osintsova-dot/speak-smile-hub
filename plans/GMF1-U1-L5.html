🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 1 “Ready for school!” · Lesson 5 — Phonics · Culture · Literacy: A Classroom Poster
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Phonics + CLIL culture + guided literacy · carpet + desks
Week 28.09–04.10 · Lesson 8L5 Phon+Cult+Lit~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4🦸 Phonics Hero ур.12b · ДИПЛОМ 🏅
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the /r/ sound (Toby's tongue twister “A red rabbit on a red ruler”), understand what British children do at school (assembly — sit on the floor, listen to the head teacher, sing songs, play music, clap) and read & make their own classroom-rules poster (Listen to others / Put your hand up to speak / Work quietly…).
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning inside the topic — Unit 1 classroom objects + “Where's my…? / It's in/on/under the…” (L1–L4) applied to /r/ objects (ruler · rubber · crayon); Phonics Hero ур.12 (b): sentence dictation (A cat sat. · The dog is big.) + the «I can read!» DIPLOMA ceremony (тетрадь стр.30–31) — the literacy celebration of the lesson; tricky words me·be·my / you·your + sight word red.
  • Personal. Keep the craft stage tight (timer + ONE rule per poster); model writing letter-by-letter on the board; every child responds in every task (own poster / show-me roar / thumbs vote) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tongue twister with a clear English /r/ and spot words that begin with /r/;
  • name 2–3 things British children do at school (assembly);
  • read simple class rules and make their own classroom-rules mini-poster (copy one rule + draw);
  • write the first letter-sound they hear (incl. R r).

Structure
A red rabbit on a red ruler.  ·  Where's my (ruler)? — It's (in/on/under) the (bag).  ·  At school we (sit on the floor / listen to the head teacher).  ·  Class rules: Listen to others. / Put your hand up to speak.
Form
Initial /r/ + word. Poster = imperative rules (Verb + …!). Fixed chunks, copied not built.
Pron. watch
English /r/ — round lips, tongue does NOT tap: a soft “rrr”, not the Russian rolled «р». Keep it smooth in red, rabbit, run, ruler.
Phonics
Coursebook sound: /r/ (PB p. 13, CD1 20–21) · Phonics Hero ур.12 (b) закрепление: sentence dictation A cat sat. · The dog is big. + ДИПЛОМ «I can read!» (тетрадь стр.30–31: 26 звуков · 5 историй · 17 tricky words). Tricky words: revise me·be·my / you·your; sight word red.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold the poster: Is this a story or rules? → Rules! Rule 2 — do we shout, or put our hand up? → Hand up!
Red rabbit sound — a hard rolled «р», or a soft rrr? → soft rrr (English /r/), round lips, no tongue tap.

Assumptions: classroom objects + “Where's my…?” + in/on/under known from L1–L4; children can copy letters but not write freely → all writing is trace/copy from a board frame. Emergent readers: the poster is read together, finger-following. Assembly role-play is new — model first.

Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Phonics — /r/ + Phonics Hero ДИПЛОМ (b)
board → desks

Recognise /r/ (~2½′). Toby's tongue twister (PB p. 13, CD1 20): r – r – r. A red rabbit on a red ruler! Rabbit-paws gesture; round lips, soft growl, tongue still — no rolled «р». Choral ×3: slow → normal → fast. 2–3 brave solos with the “microphone”. Support: hold up the /r/ picture-anchor card (a rabbit) as the sound cue — weak pupils watch it, not the letter.

“r or not r?” — whole-class show-me (~2′, all react). Pictures one by one — rug · ring · pen · rabbit · rat · hat · read · run (AB p. 10 set). Begins with /r/ → everyone stands and growls r-r-r; not /r/ → sit, hands on knees. Show me! Fast, nobody just watches.

🎮 Projector: Flashcards — “r or not r?” ✅ ready
  • Screen: big picture flashcards, one tap = next card.
  • Kids: whole class stands+growls for /r/ words, sits for the rest — every child reacts at once.
  • Teacher: use the existing Flashcards tool; exaggerate the first sound.

Blend for meaning (~2′). Push sounds together with picture support: /r/ /a/ /t/… rat! /r/ /u/ /n/… run! /r/ /e/ /d/… red! Children echo + do the action.

Sentence dictation — Phonics Hero стр.30 №2 (~3′, everyone writes). The LAST step before the diploma: Now — whole sentences, like real writers! Dictate slowly, once: A cat sat. → all write in the тетрадь (capital letter at the start — ур.10! — full stop at the end) → reveal on the board, self-check. Then: The dog is big. Support: sentence frame A ___ sat. on the board. Extension: add your own animal: A rat sat!

🏅 ДИПЛОМ «I can read!» — стр.31 (ceremony, ~2′) ✅ the heart of the lesson
  • Frame: You did it! 26 sounds · 5 stories · 17 tricky words — you can READ! Each child writes their name («Имя героя») — with a CAPITAL letter!
  • Teacher: sign and date each diploma with a flourish; the class applauds every name: [Masha] can read — hooray!
  • Culture tie: a diploma is how we celebrate learning — like the first-day-of-school traditions we talk about today.
  • Parents note (стр.31): Part 1 done — from now on the group reads the coursebook, and digraphs (Part 2) run in parallel, one lesson at a time.

Tricky words (~1½′). Flash cards me · my · you · your + colour word red: We just KNOW these — look, say: me… my… you… your… red. Choral ×2 + air-write. Then straight to the school song — keep the pace up.

Optional / fast finishers: the bonus chant Red rabbit, red rabbit, jump and run… school is fun! (with jump + run actions) — an extra on top of Toby's book tongue twister, never a replacement. · Kinaesthetic swap for “r or not r?”: a Fly-swatters reskin where everyone has a paper swatter and all “swat the air” on hearing an /r/ word (not two racers at the board). · A floor Hopscotch of picture squares — kids hop only on /r/ pictures — works as an alt playground stir if space allows.

Culture — school in Britain
carpet → circle

Listen & look (PB p. 14, CD1 22). Photo by photo: Hello, I'm Alex! I'm Lucy! We go to school in England. Every day we have an ASSEMBLY. → point: We sit on the floor. We listen to the head teacher. We play music. We sing songs. We clap our hands for good work!

▶ Let's have an assembly! — everyone joins in (role-play). All sit on the floor like the photo; teacher = head teacher. ICQ: In assembly — quiet or loud? — Quiet! Sit down… listen… now clap for good work! — mime music, hum a tune, clap together. Every child sits, listens, claps.

Think about YOUR culture (all vote — thumbs). And you, at OUR school? Do you sit on the floor? Do you sing songs? Do you clap? — thumbs up / down each question, everyone answers.

Literacy — a classroom poster
desks

Before you read (PB p. 15). Who are the GOOD children? Look and point! — good children vs naughty ones on the page.

Listen & read the poster (CD1 24) — fingers follow. Read the Class rules together (funny monkeys!): Listen to others. Put your hand up to speak. Work quietly. Sit down on your chair. Walk in the classroom. Tidy up your things. CCQ: Rule 2 — shout or hand up? — Hand up!

Make YOUR rule poster (literacy product). Fold an A5 card; each child copies ONE class rule from the board frame (written LARGE, line by line) and draws a monkey doing it — like the book. Support: dotted-line strips to trace + teacher scribes for the weakest. Extension: copy a second rule, or add the capital letter at the start. Timer 8 min, countdown finish: 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down!

Create — your own /r/ thing (fast finishers, links to phonics). On the back of the card: Draw something that starts with r-r-r — and tell me its name! (rabbit · ruler · rug · ring…). They show & say it at the door — a mini creative check of the sound.
