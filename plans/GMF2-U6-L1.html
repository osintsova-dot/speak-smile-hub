Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 8 seasonal activities and link them to the 4 seasons: “We (plant seeds) in (spring). / We (open presents) in (winter).”
  • Subsidiary. Sing the seasons song (CD3 1) + stickers chant; retrieve weather (Starter), winter actions (U5) and clothes (GMF1), woven onto seasons; Present Simple we for habits — first gentle contrast with the -ing “now” of U4–5.
  • Personal. Simple-vs-continuous is a landmine — don't lecture; anchor with gestures: -ing = NOW-circle, seasons = calendar-flip “every year”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 8 activities and 4 seasons;
  • say “We go to the beach in summer” about their family;
  • sort activities into seasons and defend the choice.

Structure
We (plant seeds) in (spring). / We (celebrate Halloween) in (autumn).  ·  recycle: It's (cold). We're (skating) — right NOW (U5).
Form
we + verb (no -s, no -ing!) + in + season — habits, every year. Contrast flag only: NOW → -ing; EVERY YEAR → plain verb.
Pron. watch
autumn — silent n /ˈɔːtəm/; chestnuts /ˈtʃesnʌts/ (t прячется); clothes /kləʊðz/ one beat.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“We plant seeds in spring — this minute or every year?” → every year (calendar-flip!).
“Halloween — which season?” → autumn!

Assumptions: Seasons known receptively (GMF1/weather talk); activities are new chunks. In Norilsk seasons are vivid (polar night!) — real-life anchors help. Chunk-length phrases need clap-chunking. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).
