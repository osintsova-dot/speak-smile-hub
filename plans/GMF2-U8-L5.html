Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the y/igh tongue twister («Can a fly with a light fly at night?»), tell about British emergency services (999), and read & write a party invitation.
  • Subsidiary. Textbook phonics: y/igh = /aɪ/; culture — firefighters, police on bikes, 999 vs наши 112/101; literacy — text type an invitation: To/From/When/Where/Phone.
  • Personal. The invitation is a real product — kids take a REAL invitation home (to a parent-tea / class event if planned); safety numbers talk is serious-light: важно, но без страшилок.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the twister fast ×3;
  • tell 2 facts about UK emergency services + our number 112;
  • write a complete invitation with all 5 fields.

Block 3 · Literacy · Invitation
carpet → desks

Before you read. PB p.89: Who is it for? Who is it from?

Read (CD4 6). The invitation: To Jane · from Sam · when · where · phone. Find-and-point race per field.

Write YOUR invitation (AB p.86 format, on real card!). 5 fields checklist: To · Come to my party on · From · There's · Phone. Fill, check (5 stamps), THEN decorate. Address it to a real person (mum, friend).

Post it. Class “post office” box; two volunteers “deliver” — invitations go home for real.
