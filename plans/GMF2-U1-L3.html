Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow, read and act out the story «The very busy week» and talk about another person's sports: “Molly plays tennis on Monday. / She goes swimming.”
  • Subsidiary. Days of the week in context; value talk — Remember to organise your things; story skills: predict → listen → read along → sequence → act out; notice the 3rd-person -s.
  • Personal. Let the story breathe — big voices, pauses, prediction; the -s is NOTICED today, drilled properly in L4 — don't over-correct.
By the end, pupils can…
  • retell who does which sport on which day (2–3 facts);
  • read their role's lines from the book;
  • say “She plays/goes…” with the -s (emerging).

Structure
Molly plays (tennis) on (Monday). / She goes (swimming) on (Wednesday).  ·  recycle: Do you play…? — Yes, I do.
Form
he/she + verb + -s: plays, goes. “The -s jumps onto the verb when we talk ABOUT someone.” on + day.
Pron. watch
3rd-person endings: plays /z/, goes /z/; days: Wednesday /ˈwenzdeɪ/ (silent d!), Tuesday–Thursday minimal pair.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“I play — Molly play or playS?” → plays. “Who does the -s belong to — me or she?” → he/she.
“Busy week — is Molly bored or is she doing a lot?” → doing a LOT.

Assumptions: Do you…? and 10 sports are active from L1–L2. Days of the week are known from GMF1 but rusty — the story recycles them naturally. First full unit story of the year: act-out routine was modelled in Starter L3. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

10–20′
Story · listen & read
carpet

Predict. Books closed, first frame on screen/board: Look — what sport? What day? 2–3 guesses.

Listen 1 — gist (CD1 17, books closed). Listen: how many sports does Molly do? Check: count on fingers together.

Listen 2 — read along (books open pp.10–11). Finger under the text. Pause each frame: What does Molly play on Monday? — Tennis! She PLAYS tennis! Raise the -s card each time it sounds.

Days check. Point at the ladder: Monday — tennis! Wednesday — swimming! — class matches day → sport from memory, thumbs vote to confirm.

20–28′
Comprehension & -s noticing
carpet → desks

True/false paddles. Statements: Molly plays hockey on Monday… true or false? Every child shows ✔/✘ paddle (or thumbs). 6 statements — all voting, no spectators.

Sentence build (Showdown). Mini-whiteboards: you show [Molly 🎾 Monday] → all write Molly plays tennis on Monday. — Show me! Reveal together, self-check against the board model. 3 rounds (incl. one goes).

✔ Assess: the boards are your -s check — who writes “play”? They get the -s card as a desk reminder in L4.

💪 Support: copy-complete a gapped sentence: Molly ___ tennis on ___. ⭐ Extension: add a second sentence with and.

 paddles/thumbs · mini-whiteboards
28–40′
Act out
carpet, groups of 3–4

Model a frame. You + 2 pupils play the competition frame with big drama: Go, Molly, go!

Groups (Molly · Jake · narrator · Toby). Rehearse 2 frames with books as scripts; narrator reads captions, characters read bubbles. Rotate roles once.

Showtime. Each group performs ONE frame (30–40 sec each — fast carousel, no long waits). Audience task: shout the day of the frame at the end.

Value. Molly is busy AND organised. Are your school things organised? What helps? 3–4 hands, Russian OK, recast key words.
