Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Last unit test of the year: calm, model-first, celebrate a whole year of English · desks + side table

Verify them against the GMF1 Units 1–9 Standard Level Tests PDF (Unit 9) and cue the audio before pupils arrive — no fumbling with the CD on the last test day of the year.
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. Assess Unit 9 language — daily routines (wake up · get up · wash my face · brush my teeth · get dressed · comb my hair · have a shower/bath · make my bed · have breakfast/lunch/dinner · go to school · do homework · go to bed), “I (wash my face) every day / every morning”, “I have (dinner) in the (evening)”, time expressions in the morning / afternoon / evening — with the GMF1 Unit 9 Standard Level Test (Listening ×2 · Reading true/false · Writing word box · Reading & Writing order-and-write · Speaking), in a calm, confidence-first atmosphere.
  • Subsidiary. Phonics — Phonics Hero: марафон tricky words — one calm reading-and-writing lap through ALL the colour sets (Red · Yellow · Green · Brown, тетрадь strips стр.29 + КТП pairs); exam micro-skills: work alone, listen twice, check your page.
  • Personal. Model-first: show every task type before the paper is out; no competitive games today; keep voice low and pace unhurried; no child ever sits with nothing to do (quiet poster task during Speaking turns). It's the final test of the whole year → protect confidence and end on a proud high.
By the end, pupils can…
  • complete all written parts of the Unit 9 test independently;
  • answer the Speaking questions one-to-one (What do you do? · When? · in the morning / evening);
  • read the year's full tricky-word strips at speed and write 3 favourites;
  • leave smiling — the last test is a “show what I know” day, and a proud end to the year.

Phonics
Phonics Hero — марафон tricky words (все части): read the full strips at speed (the · a · I · is · said · he · me · to · you · have · we · are · was · go · my · do · no · here · there · they…) + write 3–4 favourites; calm, confidence-first on test day.
Concept checks (ICQs) · Assumptions
Before the paper: Do we work alone or with a friend? → alone! Is it OK to not know one? → yes — skip it, come back!
Listening: How many times do we listen? → two times — first just listen, then number.

Assumptions: all task types rehearsed in L6 (review + AB p. 98) and via the unit trainer at home; children write by copying from word boxes; by the ninth unit test anxiety is much lower than in October, but it is still the last test of the year → keep the breathing ritual + model-first + Speaky cheering, not judging. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Test nerves / “I can't!”Breathing ritual (smell the flower — blow the candle), model-first demo, Speaky: “You do these things EVERY day — you KNOW them!”; call it the “show-what-you-know game”.
“Order and write” (ex 5) is hard for emergent readersModel ONE on the board slowly — read the jumbled words, drag them into order, read the finished sentence. Leave the worked example on the board; let Support children copy the model shape and just swap the routine word.
True/false reading (ex 3) — can't read the sentenceRead each of the 5 sentences aloud to the whole class, mime it, then they circle true or false. Re-read privately beside children who need it.
Copying from a neighbourDesks apart before the lesson; gentle private reminder — never shame publicly on test day.
Red pens / marking in sight = “I got it wrong!” panicNo red pens on show. Mark later, out of the room; today only pencils and smiles. Collect papers face-down, no ticking in front of the child.
Listening pace too fast for somePlay each track (21 / 22) twice; pause between items; watch pencils — nobody left behind.
Speaking queue = waiting childrenOne-to-one at the side table while ALL others do the poster task; call children in a quiet rotation.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — arm low all lesson = whisper day.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
Breathing — smell the flower 🌸 → blow the candle 🕯 ×3.
Differentiation
  • 💪 Support (needs more). Seat these children within arm's reach; read every instruction aloud again right beside them, point to the worked example, keep word boxes visible, and give extra time — hold the next listening item until their pencil moves. For “order and write” let them copy the board model and change one word. Praise effort, not speed.
  • ⭐ Extension (fast finishers). After the poster: a bonus “My day” mini-poster (draw + label 3 routines, add a “…every morning / evening” caption) or a page in the class picture book; still buzzing → the calm Jenga ritual below. Never “more test”, always create + apply.
Self-evaluation (thinking)

At the very end each child evaluates their own effort with a traffic-light card — 🟢 easy · 🟡 okay · 🔴 tricky:

Show me your light. What was EASY today? What was a bit TRICKY? 2–3 children name one thing each. No right/wrong — a lovely last check-in on the whole year of routines.

Calm, non-competitive Jenga ritual (optional buffer): a fast finisher gently pulls one block → answers ONE Unit 9 question (What do you do? / When — morning, afternoon or evening?) → done. No tower race, no losers.

23–43′
UNIT 9 TEST — written parts
desks

Read every rubric aloud. They are emergent readers — before EACH exercise, read the instruction to the whole class and point to the example: Exercise 1 — listen and number. Like this. Re-read privately beside any child who needs it.

Listening (paced together). Ex. 1 Listen and number (Track 21) · ex. 2 Listen and number, then match (Track 22) — play each track TWICE: first listen, pencils down; second time number/match. Watch the room, pause between items.

Reading → Writing → Reading & Writing (own pace). Ex. 3 read & circle true / false — 5 sentences (I have lunch in the afternoon · I make my bed in the evening · I have breakfast in the afternoon · I have dinner in the morning · I go to school in the morning) · ex. 4 look & write the routines from the word box (get up · brush my teeth · go to bed · wash my face · get dressed · make my bed) · ex. 5 order & write the sentence (I have a shower every morning / I brush my teeth every evening / I comb my hair every morning / I go to school every day / I wash my face every morning / I go to bed every night). Circulate silently; encourage with a nod, never with answers: Take your time. You're doing great.

Countdown at the end: Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down. Collect written pages (keep the Speaking picture page).
