A1 young learners (~8–9 y.o., early readers · continuing line) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Talk Partners · carpet + desks
Week 30.11–06.12 · Lesson 27✨ NEW · What's he doing? — He's …ing (3rd person)~10 pupils · groups of 3–4Pupil's Book pp.42–43 · AB p.40
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow, read and act out the story «What's Toby doing?» and describe someone else's actions: “What's Toby doing? — He's running up the hill!”
  • Subsidiary. Story skills cycle; value — look at wild animals, but don't touch; he/she + is + -ing grows from the story (everyone watches Toby!); sequencing & retelling.
  • Personal. Toby's chaos is the comic engine — narrate with rising drama; the class becomes the asking chorus (“What's Toby doing NOW?!”).
By the end, pupils can…
  • retell 4–5 story events with He's …ing;
  • ask “What's he/she doing?” naturally;
  • read their role's lines.

Structure
What's (Toby) doing? — He's (running up the hill). / She's (walking).  ·  recycle: I'm …ing · Are you…?
Form
he/she + 's (is) + verb-ing. The 's is IS here (not has!) — flag lightly with the NOW-gesture.
Pron. watch
He's /hiːz/ links into the verb: He's_running; chasing /ˈtʃeɪsɪŋ/.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“He's running — now or every day?” → now!
“Toby chases the sheep — is that OK on a trail?” → no — look, don't touch/chase!

Assumptions: I/you continuous fresh from L2 — he/she is the natural next door, delivered by the story. Acting routine solid; groups semi-independent.

Story · listen & read
carpet

Predict. First frame: Look at Toby's nose! What's wrong? — whisper guesses.

Listen 1 — gist (CD2 22, books closed). Listen: what does Toby chase?

Listen 2 — read along (pp.42–43). Pause per frame; class asks the chorus question each time: What's Toby doing NOW?! → harvest: He's rolling down the hill! Raise the 's card on each instance.

Check. True/false paddles ×6 — all vote.
