Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 wild animals and describe them: “It's got (long hair). / It's (big and grey).”
  • Subsidiary. Sing the zoo song (CD1 50) + stickers chant (CD1 52); retrieve GMF1 animals, colours and “It's got…” (GMF1 U3), woven onto the new animals; read animal riddles (I've got four legs… I'm a…); animal mime — controlled TPR.
  • Personal. Animal noises are magnetic — agree the “sound budget” (noises only on cue); keep riddle answers full sentences.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 wild animals;
  • describe an animal with It's got… / It's…;
  • solve a written riddle and say the animal.

Structure
It's got (long hair / four legs / big ears).  ·  It's (big / grey).  ·  riddle: I've got two legs. I can't fly. I'm a (penguin).
Form
It's got + noun phrase (=у него есть); It's + adjective. I've got in riddles — same got-family.
Pron. watch
giraffe /dʒɪˈrɑːf/ stress on 2nd syllable; lizard /ˈlɪzəd/; crocodile 3 beats; penguin /ˈpeŋɡwɪn/.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“It's GOT long hair — about having or being?” → having (у него есть).
“Can a penguin fly?” → no — I CAN'T fly!

Assumptions: Pupils know cat/dog/farm animals (GMF1) and “has got” receptively from GMF1 U3 — now productive. Early readers handle the riddle texts with support. КТП list said parrot/zebra — по учебнику верно penguin/lizard. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Presentation · 10 animals
carpet → board

Flashcards. What's this? It's a… lion! Choral ×3 + a signature pose each; 2–3 solo. Stress traps drilled: gi-RAFFE, LI-zard.

Describe as you go. Per card one fact from the class: It's got a long neck! It's big! — you scribe key phrases under the cards.

▶ Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if it's got FOUR legs… penguin! …tiger! — everyone decides every time.

💪 Support: pose + word is enough. ⭐ Extension: two-fact descriptions: It's big and it's got big teeth.

28–36′
Riddle time (readers!)
carpet, pairs

Model a riddle (PB act. 3 format). Read aloud: I've got two legs. I'm black and white. I can't fly. What am I? — pairs whisper-agree, then all together: A penguin!

▶ Riddle cards in pairs. Each pair gets 3 riddle cards: A reads, B guesses with a full answer It's a hippo! — swap. Icons support weak readers.

✔ Assess: listen for reading fluency on the riddle cards — pair strong+developing next time accordingly.

36–44′
Mime safari
carpet, 3 teams

▶ Slow-motion safari mime. One player poses/mimes; team asks together: Is it a crocodile? (recycles GMF1 Is it…?) Mimer: Yes, it is! / No, it isn't! Rotate mimer — everyone mimes once. Sound effects ONLY on your cue — conduct them like an orchestra for 5 glorious seconds, then Freeze.
