🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 1 “Ready for school!” · Lesson 3 — Story: Toby goes to school
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Story: listen → follow → act out (drama) · carpet + desks
Week 21.09 · Lesson 6✨ STORY~12 pupils · small groups for drama
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow and understand the story “Toby goes to school” (Pupil's Book pp. 10–11, CD1 15) and act it out using key story language: “Where's my (book)? — It's in the bin / on the shelf.”, “Put the (pencil case) on the desk.”, “Bad dog, Toby! Tidy up.”, “Well done! Thank you!”.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — classroom objects + “Where's my…? — It's here.” and prepositions in / on / under (L1–L2) plus colours (Starter) — woven straight into the story work (Toby makes a mess, children find & tidy the objects). Phonics — blend & read CVC words revising all 26 sounds (sad · bad · dog · bin · big · sit · pen) + tricky words his · has · into. Values: keep the classroom tidy; say thank you.
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (gesture responses, prediction); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a small group at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 30-sec showcase.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (number the frames, answer with gestures);
  • say the key lines in role: “Where's my book? — It's in the bin!”, “Put the pencil case on the desk.”;
  • blend & read CVC words and read his · has · into by sight;
  • say “Thank you!” and keep the classroom tidy (values).

Structure
Where's my (black and white) book? — It's in the bin / on the shelf. · Put the (pencil case) on the desk. · Bad dog, Toby! Tidy up. · What a tidy desk! Well done! Thank you.
Form
Where 's (is) my + noun? → It 's + in / on / under + the + noun. Imperative: Put the … in / on the … (receptive here, full focus in L4).
Pron. watch
connected Where's /weəz/; weak in the /ɪn ðə/, on the /ɒn ðə/; sentence stress: It's in the BIN.
Phonics
Jolly (JP Book 2): blend & read CVC words, all 26 sounds — sad · bad · dog · bin · big · sit · pen (recognise → blend → segment) · tricky words: his · has · into.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Frame 1: “Is Toby happy?” → “No — he's sad.” (sad face) “Why?” → “Dogs don't go to school.”
Frames 5–6: “Oh no — a messy desk! Where's the book?” → “It's in the bin!” “And the pencil case?” → “It's on the shelf!”
Frame 8: “Now — what a tidy desk! The teacher says Well done. What does Toby say?” → “Thank you!”

Assumptions: classroom objects + Where's my…? and in / on / under known from L1–L2; colours known from the Starter unit; children know Toby the dog & the magic bike from the unit opener. Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures while listening; the teacher points, children don't read the speech bubbles alone.

“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.

Review → into the story
carpet, circle

Recycle L1–L2 woven into today. Speaky's things are lost. Class helps (rehearsed rhythm, clap-clap-ask): Where's my pencil? — teacher hides it → It's under the book! Do 3–4 objects, moving them in / on / under a box as you go.

Show-me — everyone moves. Three gestures: in (hand drops inside fist) · on (flat hand on top) · under (hand below). Call fast: Show me IN! Show me ON! Show me UNDER! — all react. Then flash colours on the objects: What colour? — Black! Purple!

Bridge. Someone in our story makes a BIG mess… his name is Toby. Who is Toby? Let's find out! Signal: Class? — Yes!

📺 Phonics — read CVC words
at the board

Recognise. Quick sound flash with Jolly actions — Do you remember these sounds? Point, children voice each sound (children make the sound, not the app). 6–8 sounds, snappy.

Blend — “Robot Toby” (all respond). Speaky talks like a robot dog, one sound at a time: /d/ /o/ /g/… class blends & shouts dog! then points to the picture. Do sad · bad · dog · bin · big · sit · pen — a picture appears each time for meaning.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Robot Toby Blender ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U1 “Flashcards” engine — one CVC card, letters split d · o · g, then a picture reveal on tap. · How: tap to sound each letter → tap to blend → tap to show the picture. · Pupils: voice each sound then blend chorally — dog! — and touch their nose if the picture matches. · Teacher: drive the taps, keep the beat, praise blends.

Read — thumbs check (all react). Flash CVC word cards; class blends chorally, then thumbs up = real word, thumbs down = silly word (mix in zog · lat). Every child shows a thumb.

▶ Alt phonics game (rotation, not every lesson): Fly-Swatters — “own swatter” re-skin. EVERY child holds a paper swatter (or just their open hand). Two words on the board — one real, one silly (dog / zog, bin / lat). Speaky robot-blends one → Swat the REAL word! → on your 3-2-1-GO! all swat their own mini-card / swat the air toward the correct side. No “two children at the board, the class watching”.

Segment — sky-write, keep it light. Say a word once (sad · bin · dog); class sky-writes the letters with a finger & sounds them out — no whole-class mini-whiteboard dictation (that written segment is the heaviest task for emergent readers and this is the fullest lesson of the unit). Then tricky words by sight: his · has · into — flash, chorus, air-write.

Support: keep the picture beside the word; ask for just the first & last sound. Extension: fast finishers may write sad · bin · dog on a mini-whiteboard → Show me!

 JP Book 2 · CVC word/picture cards · paper fly-swatters (alt game) · a few mini-whiteboards (extension only) · Speaky (robot voice)

24–34′
📺 Story — listen & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 10). Big story picture on screen / books open. What classroom objects can you see in the story? — pairs point & name 15 sec, then chorus a few. Pre-teach with gesture: Toby (dog paws “woof”) · magic bike (sparkle fingers) · tidy up (sweep hands).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “What's in Toby's classroom?” ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U1 “Find it!” engine on the big story picture. · How: Speaky asks, a child taps the object; it lights up. · Pupils: whole class points to their book / calls it — A book! A pencil case! A crayon! then It's a black and white book. · Teacher: 🦝 Where's the crayon? Point! — 4–5 objects, quick.

Predict (thinking) — before we listen. Toby is a dog… but he wants to go to school! What will happen? Will it be tidy or a BIG mess? Thumbs guess: tidy up vs. hands-on-cheeks “Oh no!”. Let's listen and check your guess!

Listen 1 — global (CD1 15). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: Is Toby a dog? Show me! (thumbs) Was your guess right — a mess?

Detail check — True or False? (thumbs up / down, all react). Say a sentence, class shows a thumb: Toby is a cat. (👎) · Toby is sad at the start. (👍) · The book is on the shelf. (👎 — It's in the bin!) · Toby tidies up. (👍). Re-point the frame to fix any wrong thumb.

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the story gestures at the right moments: sad (frame 1) · magic bike ride (pedal) · Sshh! finger (Be quiet, Toby!) · Oh no! hands on cheeks (frame 4) · point in the bin / on the shelf · sweep tidy up · cheer Well done! CCQs between frames (see box above). At frames 5–6 stop: Where's the book? — In the bin! Where's the pencil case? — On the shelf!

35–42′
▶ Build the story
desks, pairs

▶ Story Builders (Mido №54 “Reconstruction”, picture re-skin for non-readers — all pairs work at once). Each pair gets 8 mini frame-cards of the story, shuffled.

Order it. Put the story in order — one card each, take turns! (RallyRobin with cards: A places one, B places the next.) Countdown 2 min. ICQ: Do we grab all the cards or take turns? (→ take turns!)

Check with the audio. Replay CD1 15: pairs touch each card as they hear it — self-check, fix the order. Then choral retell, teacher holds up big frames one by one, class says the line: Look at Toby. He's sad. · Toby is on the magic bike! · Be quiet, Toby! · Oh no! My pencil case! · Where's my book? · It's in the bin! · Put the book in the bag. · What a tidy desk. Thank you!

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Toby Story-Sequence 1–5 🔨 СОБРАТЬ
Screen: the 5 key story frames (sad Toby → magic bike → “Oh no!” mess → in the bin / on the shelf → tidy desk “Thank you!”) appear shuffled in a row, each with an empty number badge 1–5. · How: tap two frames to swap them (or drag); when the order is right, a ✅ + a cheer plays. A “Check” button lights correct frames green. · Pupils: the class calls the order — Number one — Toby is sad! Number two — the magic bike! — and gesture each frame. · Teacher: take votes (Which is first? Hands up!), tap as they decide, then replay CD1 15 to confirm. Mirrors the paired frame-cards on screen for a whole-class check.

Support: hand that pair only 5 clearest frames (not 8) and number the first one for them. Extension: after ordering, ask them to say one whole line for each frame from memory before the audio check.

 8 story frame mini-cards per pair (print from PB pp. 10–11) · 5-frame set for support pairs · CD1 15 · big frame cards for the board
42–50′
Act out — drama
carpet, groups

Roles. Groups of 3–4: Toby the dog (blue badge, “Woof! Ring! Ring!”) · 2 children (red + green, find the objects) · teacher (yellow, says “Well done!”). Model once with a strong group.

All groups act at the same time. Narrate the story slowly, frame by frame; every group plays it in their own spot (magic-bike ride on the spot!): Poor Toby, dogs don't go to school… Look! The magic bike! Be quiet, Toby! Oh no — my pencil case! Where's my book? — It's in the bin! Put the pencil case on the desk. Bad dog, Toby! Tidy up. What a tidy desk. Well done! — Thank you!

Create (thinking) — make it yours. Before round 2: Toby made a mess with ONE more thing — you choose! What is it? Where does it go? Each group adds a line with their own object: Where's my crayon? — It's under the bag! Put the crayon in the bin! (support: point to a card; extension: invent the whole exchange).

Swap roles, run it again — keep it to 2 rounds (don't let the drama over-run; Freeze! the moment energy tips). Volunteers: ONE group shows 30 sec for the class — big applause. Values (PB p. 11 ex. 2): Keep the classroom tidy — what do we put in the bin? → Rubbish!

Support: quieter children get the “find the object” role (short lines + gesture); a line card in hand. Extension: confident children play Toby / teacher and lead the exchange.

50–55′
▶ Tidy up, Toby!
desks, all at once

▶ Tidy up, Toby! (targeted settler — prepositions + Put the… + values; every child places a card every round). Each child has a strip with 3 zones — bin · shelf · bag — and a few object cards.

🦝 Speaky gives Toby's orders: Put the book on the shelf! → all move that card onto the shelf zone; Put the rubbish in the bin! · Put the crayon in the bag! 6–7 orders, instant show-me check each time (Point to it! Where's the book? — On the shelf!).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “Tidy up, Toby!” (model & check) ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U1 “Find it!” board with three zones — bin · shelf · bag — and the objects. · How: Speaky says the order, a child taps the object onto the right zone; it snaps in place if correct. · Pupils: first move it on their own paper strip, then one child mirrors it on screen — Put the crayon in the bag! class checks: Yes! It's in the bag! · Teacher: use it to model order 1, then just for the show-me check so every child stays on their own strip.
