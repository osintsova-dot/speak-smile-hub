Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow, read and act out the story «The haunted house»: “There's a haunted (bedroom) with a haunted (bed)! / There aren't any skeletons!”
  • Subsidiary. Story cycle with delicious spooky drama; furniture returns inside rooms (sofa · armchair · bath · shower · bed · mirror · piano); There-grammar consolidated in narrative.
  • Personal. Spooky = fun-scary, never scary-scary: wobbly-ghost voice, giggles built in; check faces — one anxious child changes the temperature you set.
By the end, pupils can…
  • retell the haunted tour room by room;
  • combine room + furniture: “a haunted bathroom with a haunted bath”;
  • read their role's lines with ghost-voice joy.

27–40′
Act out · haunted tour
carpet, groups of 3–4

Model one room: tour-guide (you) + wobbling furniture-ghost (a pupil under a scarf!).

Groups: tour-guide · 2 visitors · furniture-ghost. Each group takes ONE room; rehearse the chunk + a scare-and-giggle beat.

Showtime — the full tour. Groups perform in room order; audience = brave visitors who chorus: Is there a ghost? — YES! Run! Finale all together: There aren't any skeletons! PHEW!

💪 Support: furniture-ghost is wordless — perfect opt-in for the shy. ⭐ Extension: guide adds a There-are line.
