Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to use ordinal numbers 1st–31st and ask & answer about birthdays: “When's your birthday? — My birthday is on the (ninth) of (May).”
  • Subsidiary. Months warmed back (GMF1/calendar talk); the class birthday calendar is BORN today (stays on the wall all year); listening — circle the date (CD3 10); first/second/third irregulars spotlighted.
  • Personal. Birthdays are emotional gold — every child's date gets celebrated aloud; keep the ordinal drills brisk and body-based.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say 1st, 2nd, 3rd + regular -th ordinals;
  • ask & answer the birthday question with a real date;
  • place their birthday on the class calendar.

Form
the + ordinal + of + month; irregular trio 1st/2nd/3rd; fifth/twelfth spelling quirks — say, don't drill spelling.
Pron. watch
-th endings /θ/ tongue out (fourth, fifth); the ordinal trio first/second/third stress; months' first-syllable stress.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“One, two, three — for counting. For DATES?” → first, second, third!
“My birthday is IN May or ON the ninth of May?” → on + the date.

Assumptions: Cardinal numbers to 50 solid (U2); months known patchily — the calendar wall fixes that over time. Fingers-and-tongue /θ/ work carried over from earlier th-words.

Presentation · ordinals
carpet → board

Podium trio. 🥇 first! 🥈 second! 🥉 third! Choral + finger-podium gesture. Then the -th family: fourth, fifth, sixth… — tongue-peek /θ/ on each; count the class line: You're first! You're second!

Date machine (act. 1, CD3 8–9). PB p.64: My birthday is on the NINTH of MAY. Build on the board: [on] [the] [ordinal] [of] [month]; back-chain the chunk.

▶ Ordinal aerobics: Touch your head FIRST… clap SECOND… jump THIRD! — sequence commands, all move.

💪 Support: podium trio + “-th” is enough. ⭐ Extension: say tricky ones: twelfth, twentieth, thirty-first.
