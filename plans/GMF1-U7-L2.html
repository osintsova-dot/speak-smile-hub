🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 7 “Let's explore my town!” · Lesson 2 — Grammar: There's a… / There are…
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Dialogue → controlled drill → freer → production · carpet + desks
Week 01.03 · Lesson 49 · Continuing group✨ GRAMMAR~12 pupils · pairs + 2 teams
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say what is in a town using There's a… for one place and There are (two)… for more than one: “There's a zoo. There are two sweet shops.” (Pupil's Book p. 73 dialogue + Grammar box), and act out Molly & Tim's “Welcome to Newtown” dialogue.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — L1 (yesterday's) 10 town places + “It's a…”, and Project 2's counting question “How many (lambs) can you see?” — woven into today's grammar (“How many cafés are there? — There are two.”); numbers 1–10 for counting places; colours review (green) — “There's a green park”; phonics — the ur /ɜː/ family (purple, burn, purse, turn, nurse), reviewing er/ir; listen for numbers (PB p. 73 ex. 2 “Listen and write the number”).
  • Personal. Insist on the WHOLE sentence (“There are two cafés”, not “Two cafés”); keep the plural -s hiss (“two cafés”); choose There's a vs There are by how many; every task keeps ALL children responding (own cards / point & say / all pairs at once) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say “There's a (zoo).” for one place and “There are (two) sweet shops.” for more;
  • ask & answer “How many (cinemas) are there? — There are two.” and act out the Newtown dialogue;
  • hear, blend & segment words with ur /ɜː/ and read the sight words there · are.

Structure
There's a (zoo).  ·  There are (two) sweet shops.  ·  How many (cafés) are there? — There are two.  ·  Welcome to (Newtown)!  ·  Great! I love (animals). Fantastic!
Form
There's = There is + a + ONE place. There are + number + place + -s (more than one). Question: How many + places + are there? Choice driven by quantity (1 → There's a / 2+ → There are).
Pron. watch
There's /ðeəz/ — one word; There are /ðeərə/ linked; hiss the plural -s /s/ (cafés, shops); keep the number in “There are two”; don't shrink the question to “How many café?”.
Phonics
Jolly focus: ur /ɜː/ — purple, burn, purse, turn, nurse (recognise → blend → segment) · review of er/ir /ɜː/ · sight words: there · are (today's Grammar-box words) · JP Book 2 ur set.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold up ONE zoo card: “One zoo — do I say There's a zoo or There are zoos?” → There's a zoo.
Hold up TWO café cards: “One or two?” → two → “So — There ARE two cafés.” (There are + number + -s)
“How do we ask the number?” → How many cafés are there? (fingers = ?)
ur: “u and r are friends — they growl /ɜː/ like a purring cat: purr… purple!”
⭐ Option — colour CCQ: hold a green park card: “It's a park. What colour?” → green → “There's a green park.” (Weaves the green review — use if the class is quick.)

Assumptions: the 10 town places + “It's a…” were met in L1 (yesterday); “How many … can you see?” & numbers 1–10 known from Project 2 / earlier units; er/ir /ɜː/ met before. Pupils are non-/emergent readers → picture grids, town scenes & audio, not printed text. Carpet routines & pair-work signals known.

Phonics — ur /ɜː/
at the board

Recognise — the growling ur. Show letters ur: u and r are FRIENDS. Together they growl /ɜː/ — like a purring cat! Say & act: ur… purple, burn, turn, nurse, purse! (curl hands like paws for the “purr”). Children copy, holding the lips round for /ɜː/.

/ɜː/ or not? — whole-class show-me (all react). Every child gets two cards: 🐱 (ur /ɜː/) and 🔤 (single sound). Say words one by one — purple · cat · turn · pen · nurse · sun · purse · dog · burn… → Show me! — all hold up 🐱 or 🔤. 8–10 words, fast; nobody just watches.

Blend (all together). Push the sounds together: /t/ /ur/ /n/… turn! Then purple, burn, nurse — picture for each (purple crayon · fire · nurse).

Segment · dictation (everyone writes). Now YOU write. Listen… word once → all write on mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & check together. Focus: turn, burn, nurse. Finish with the sight words we'll read today: there · are — flash, chorus, air-write (these are the words in our Grammar box!).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): ur Sorter ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the Unit 1 “Flashcards” engine)
Screen: a word + picture flips up one at a time (purple · cat · turn · pen · nurse · sun · purse · dog). Mechanic: tap the card to hear the word, then tap 🐱 or 🔤 to sort it; a green tick / red shake shows if right. Pupils: hold up their own 🐱/🔤 first, then a helper taps to check. Teacher: read the word, wait for hands, then reveal. Reuse: same engine as L1 Flashcards — just swap the card set.

◆ Support: in dictation, give sound-buttons (t-ur-n) — they only fill letters. ▲ Extension: fast writers add a word by ear (curl, hurt).

24–33′
Presentation · Dialogue
carpet → pairs

Model with Speaky. Two puppets/roles: Molly welcomes Tim. Teacher: Welcome to Newtown, Tim! — Thanks, Molly. — There's a big zoo here! — Great! I love animals. — And there are two sweet shops. — Fantastic! I love sweets, too! Big gestures: one finger for a zoo, two fingers for two sweet shops.

Listen & repeat — PB p. 73 ex. 1 (CD3 27). Books open / picture on screen: Molly & Tim at the zoo. Play the dialogue; children repeat line by line. Drill the Grammar box: There's a zoo. ↗ There are two sweet shops. Point at the box: One place → There's a. Two places → There are. — this is our grammar of the day.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Newtown Spinner ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the “What's this? spinner” engine)
Screen: a wheel of the 10 town places; each slice shows the place with a small number badge (×1 or ×2). Mechanic: tap to spin → it lands on, e.g., “café ×2”; that building pops up in the town. Pupils: BEFORE the reveal the whole class reads the number and says together There's a café! / There are two cafés! (choose by the badge). Teacher: spin, hold the reveal until you hear the FULL sentence with the right there's/there are. 🧠 Predict: One or two? Guess before it stops! — children show 1 or 2 fingers before the wheel lands.

Act out (ALL pairs at once). Pairs on the carpet, each with a small set of place cards (some singles, some doubles). A is the guide: Welcome to my town! There's a zoo. There are two cafés. B replies: Great! Fantastic! Swap roles, new cards. ICQ: One café — do you say “There's a” or “There are”? (→ There's a!)

◆ Support: give the sentence-frame cards There's a ___ / There are two ___ to point along. ▲ Extension: add a colour or “big” — There's a big green park!

▶ Slow reveal: What's in Newtown?
carpet / board
🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it in Newtown! ✅ ГОТОВ (reuse of the Unit 1 “Find it!” engine)
Screen: a big drawn town scene with places dotted around (2 cafés, 1 zoo, 2 cinemas, 1 hospital…). Mechanic: tap Speaky → he asks How many cafés are there?; children scan, count & point; tap the places → they glow and a counter fills. Pupils: There are two cafés! There's one zoo! (2–3 rounds, to MODEL counting before the physical game). Teacher: use this as the model, then run the Slow-reveal game.

▶ Slow reveal — “What's in Newtown?” (Игротека “Угадай по кусочку” — whole class predicts, nobody sits out). Cover a place card with a “?” cloud and reveal it bit by bit. Class guesses: Is there a zoo? Is there a café? When revealed, everyone says the full sentence — and you flash a number card (1 or 2): There's a zoo! / There are two cinemas!

Kagan — Numbered Heads energy: children whisper their guess to a partner first, then hands up together. Rule — guess → SAY the full sentence. Teacher feeds the language: One or two? Then — There ARE two! Reset if loud: Freeze! Run 5–6 cards; a 2nd round only if time allows (buffer — don't rush the pair game for it).

◆ Support: reveal more of the card faster for a slower child so they get the win too. ▲ Extension: add “How many?” — How many cinemas are there? — There are two!

⭐ Swap (rotation): if you ran Slow reveal recently, swap in Fly-swatters (Мухобойки) — place cards on the board with number badges; you call There are two cafés! → two team runners swat the café card, the whole class chants the sentence.
