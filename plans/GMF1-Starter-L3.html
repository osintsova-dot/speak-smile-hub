🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Starter “Hello, friends!” · Lesson 3 — Story: A big surprise
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuing group) · 60 min · Story: listen → read → act out (drama) · carpet + desks
Week 07–13.09 · Lesson 3✨ STORY~12 pupils · small groups for drama
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow and understand the story “A big surprise” (Pupil's Book p. 6, CD1 6) and act it out using key story language: “What's this, Dad? — It's a bike. It's for two children.”, “Let's clean the bike. Good idea!”, “Ring the bell again, Beth.”, “What colour is it? — It's green and red!”, “It's a magic bike! This magic bike is fantastic!”
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve real prior learning from the Starter — colours (Starter L1) and What's this? — It's a (bike) / shapes (Starter L2) — woven straight into the story (the magic bike changes colour, children name each colour). Phonics — Phonics Hero: повтор ур.7–9 (b·j·v · w·x·y · z·qu, тетрадь стр.17–25) — speed-reading reactivation, light (story lesson) + tricky words by sight he · she · we. Values: look after / clean your things; say thank you.
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (gesture + colour responses, prediction); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a small group at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 30-sec showcase.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (number the frames, answer with thumbs);
  • ask & answer in role: “What colour is it? — It's green and red!”, “What's this? — It's a bike.”;
  • recognise the sounds g · o · u · l · f · b and read he · she · we by sight;
  • name colours of an object and say “Thank you!” (values).

Structure
What colour is it? — It's green (and red). · What's this, Dad? — It's a bike. It's for two children. · Let's clean the bike. Good idea! · Ring the bell (again). · It's a magic bike! This magic bike is fantastic! · Thank you.
Form
What colour + is + it? → It's + colour (and colour). Recycled: What's this? → It's a + noun. Let's + verb (clean / ring) — receptive here.
Pron. watch
colour /ˈkʌlə/ (the ou = /ʌ/, weak -our); connected What colour's it?; linking green and red /griːn ən red/; sentence stress: It's GREEN and RED.
Phonics
Phonics Hero — повтор ур.7–9: sounds b·j·v · w·x·y · z·qu (тетрадь стр.17–25) — speed reading, light (story lesson); robot-blend bus · jam · box · zip for fun; qu — «неразлучные друзья /кв/». Tricky words by sight: he · she · we.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Frame 1: “Whose bike is it? — Dad gives it. Who is it for?” → “For two children!” (point to the tandem bike)
Frame 3: “They ring the bell — what happens?” → “The bike changes colour! It's a MAGIC bike!”
Frames 5–6: “Ring again! What colour is it now?” → “Green, red, orange, blue…!” “Is it a normal bike?” → “No — it's magic! It's fantastic!”

Assumptions: colours are known from Starter L1; What's this? — It's a (bike) and shapes from Starter L2; children can name a few colours already. Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures while listening; the teacher points, children don't read the speech bubbles alone.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Long lines block the story (magic bike · for two children)Pre-teach 3 things before listening with picture + gesture (magic = sparkle fingers, ring the bell = ring gesture + “ring-ring!”, clean = wipe hands).
Children shout colours over the audio“Listening hands” rule — hands cupped behind ears while the CD plays; call the colours only when it stops.
Colours confused (e.g. brown / blue, orange)Colour flashcards up first; fast “Show me RED! Show me GREEN!” — everyone touches something that colour before the story.
Drama turns to chaos (riding around the room)The bike ride is ON THE SPOT (pedal sitting/standing); roles fixed by colour badges; “Freeze!” resets.
Frame cards ordered by one child in the pairRule “one card each turn, take turns” (RallyRobin with cards); praise pairs where both hands work.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset (essential in drama).

📺 Phonics Hero — повтор ур.7–9 (light)
at the board

Speed reading — тетрадь стр.17–25 (ур.7–9), light. Strips b · j · v → w · x · y → z · qu: You know them ALL — there and back, fast! Remind the pair: q and u are best friends — together /кв/: qu! 2 quick rounds — children voice the sounds, not the app.

Robot blend — “Robot Speaky” (all respond, just for fun). 🦝 talks like a robot, one sound at a time: /b/ /u/ /s/… class blends & shouts bus! A handful only — bus · jam · box · zip — picture each time for meaning. No writing / segmenting today — keep the load light for the story.

Read — thumbs check (all react). Flash 3–4 words; class blends chorally, then thumbs up = real word, thumbs down = silly word (mix in vob · jix). Every child shows a thumb.

Tricky words by sight. Flash he · she · we — chorus, point, air-write once. We can't sound these out — we just KNOW them!

Support: keep the picture beside the word; ask for just the first sound. Extension: fast finishers think of one more word with b or j — Tell me a /b/ word! 🎮 Phonics Hub (speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/) — Station 1 «Звуки» / «⚡ Speed reading», ✅ готов; offline: тетрадь стр.17–25.

20–32′
📺 Story — listen & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 6). Big story picture on screen / books open. What's this? — A bike! What colour is it now? — pairs point & say 15 sec, then chorus. Pre-teach with gesture: magic (sparkle fingers) · ring the bell (ring + “ring-ring!”) · clean (wipe hands).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “What colour is the bike?” ✅ ГОТОВ · find-it-u1
Screen: the Starter “Find it!” engine on the story frames. · How: 🦝 asks, a child taps a colour on the bike; it lights up. · Pupils: whole class points to their book / calls it — Green! Red! Orange! then the full line It's green and red. · Teacher: 🦝 What colour is it here? Point! — 4–5 frames, quick.

Predict (thinking) — before we listen. It's a MAGIC bike. They ring the bell… what happens? Will it stay grey, or CHANGE? Thumbs guess: “change!” (jazz hands) vs. “stay the same” (flat hands). Let's listen and check your guess!

Listen 1 — global (CD1 6). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: Is it a magic bike? Show me! (thumbs) Was your guess right — it changes colour?

Detail check — True or False? (thumbs up / down, all react). Say a sentence, class shows a thumb: The bike is for two children. (👍) · Dad rings the bell. (👎 — Beth rings the bell!) · The bike stays grey. (👎 — It changes colour!) · The magic bike is fantastic. (👍). Re-point the frame to fix any wrong thumb.

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the story gestures & call the colours at the right moments: clean (wipe, frames 1–2) · ring the bell (ring gesture + ring-ring!) · at each frame shout the new colour — Green and red! …orange! …blue! …brown and yellow! · big cheer This magic bike is fantastic! CCQs between frames (see box above). At frames 5–6 stop: What colour is it NOW? — Green, red, orange, blue, brown and yellow!

33–40′
▶ Build the story
desks, pairs

▶ Story-Sequence (picture re-skin for non-readers — all pairs work at once). Each pair gets 6 mini frame-cards of “A big surprise”, shuffled.

Order it. Put the story in order — one card each, take turns! (RallyRobin with cards: A places one, B places the next.) Countdown 2 min. ICQ: Do we grab all the cards or take turns? (→ take turns!)

Check with the audio. Replay CD1 6: pairs touch each card as they hear it — self-check, fix the order. Then choral retell, teacher holds up big frames one by one, class says the line: What's this, Dad? · It's a bike. It's for two children. · Let's clean the bike. · Ring the bell! · Look! It's green and red! · This magic bike is fantastic!

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Magic-Bike Story-Sequence 1–6 🔨 СОБРАТЬ
Screen: the 6 story frames (get the bike → clean it → ring the bell / green & red → orange → blue → all the colours “fantastic!”) appear shuffled in a row, each with an empty number badge 1–6. · How: tap two frames to swap them (or drag); a “Check” button lights the correct order green + plays a cheer. · Pupils: the class calls the order — Number one — What's this, Dad? Number two — Let's clean the bike! — and gesture each frame. · Teacher: take votes (Which is first? Hands up!), tap as they decide, then replay CD1 6 to confirm. Mirrors the paired frame-cards on screen for a whole-class check.

Support: hand that pair only 4 clearest frames (get bike → clean → ring/colour → all colours) and number the first one for them. Extension: after ordering, say one whole line for each frame from memory before the audio check.

40–48′
Act out — drama
carpet, groups

Roles. Groups of 3–4: Dad (blue badge, gives the bike) · Beth (red, rings the bell) · 1–2 children (green + yellow, clean the bike & call the colours). Model once with a strong group.

All groups act at the same time. Narrate the story slowly, frame by frame; every group plays it in their own spot (bike ride on the spot!): What's this, Dad? — It's a bike. It's for two children! — Wow, thank you, Dad! Let's clean the bike. Good idea! Ring the bell, Beth… ring-ring! Look — it's green and red now! It's a MAGIC bike! Ring again… green, red, orange! This magic bike is fantastic!

Create (thinking) — make it yours. Before round 2: Ring the bell ONE more time — YOUR magic colour! What colour is your bike now? Each group adds a line with their own colour: Ring-ring! What colour is it? — It's pink and purple! (support: point to a colour card; extension: invent the whole exchange).

Swap roles, run it again — keep it to 2 rounds (don't let the drama over-run; Freeze! the moment energy tips). Volunteers: ONE group shows 30 sec for the class — big applause. Values: They CLEAN the bike and say thank you — we look after our things too!

Support: quieter children get the “clean the bike / call one colour” role (short line + gesture); a colour card in hand. Extension: confident children play Dad / Beth and lead the exchange.

 role badges ×4 colours · colour cards · toy bell (optional)

desks, all at once

▶ Colour the magic bike (targeted settler — What colour is it? — It's… + primes the homework; every child colours every round). Each child has a small bike outline (or a strip of blank shapes) and crayons.
