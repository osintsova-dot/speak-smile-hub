🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 7 “Let's explore my town!” · Lesson 3 — Story: The treasure hunt
Pre-A1 young learners (~7–8 y.o., emergent readers · continuing line) · 60 min · Story: listen → read → act out (drama) · carpet + desks
Week 08.03 · Lesson 50✨ STORY~12 pupils · small groups for drama🦸 Phonics Hero · диктант диграфов (ур.13–18)
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow and understand the story “The treasure hunt” (Pupil's Book pp. 74–75, CD3 29) and act it out using key story language: “Can we explore your town? — Yes, of course.”, “It's a treasure map. Let's look for the treasure!”, “There's a big, old tree next to the swimming pool.”, “There are two yellow rocks in front of the zoo.”, “How many steps are there? — There are 20.”
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve real prior learning — places in town (zoo · swimming pool · sweet shop · library · park) + “There's a… / There are…” (U7 L1–L2), prepositions in / on / under and colours (U1 / Starter, yellow rocks), numbers 1–20 (count 20 steps), Toby the dog & the Magic Bike (U1) — all woven straight into the treasure hunt. Phonics — Phonics Hero: диктант диграфов Части 2 (ур.13–18) — one big friendly dictation across ai·oa·ie·ee·or·ng·oo·ch·sh·th·ou·oi·ue·er·ar + sight/tricky words there · are · here (they run right through the story). Values: cross the road safely (red/yellow/green).
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (gesture responses, prediction, True/False thumbs); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a small group at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 30-sec showcase.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (order the frames, answer True/False with thumbs);
  • say key lines in role: “It's a treasure map!”, “There's a tree next to the swimming pool.”, “There are 20 steps!”;
  • write digraph words from dictation across Part 2 (rain · moon · ship · star) and read there · are · here by sight;
  • say why we cross the road when the man is green (values).

Structure
Can we explore your town? — Yes, of course. · It's a treasure map. Let's look for the treasure! · There's a (big, old tree) next to the (swimming pool). · There are (two yellow rocks) in front of the (zoo). · How many steps are there? — There are 20.
Form
There 's (is) + a + noun · There are + number + noun-s · location: next to / in front of + the + place (receptive here, full focus in L4). How many …s are there? — There are + number.
Pron. watch
connected There's /ðeəz/, There are /ðeərə/; sentence stress: There are TWO yellow ROCKS. · How many STEPS are there?
Phonics
Phonics Hero — диктант диграфов Части 2 (ур.13–18): 6–8 words across the digraph families (rain, goat, bee, fork, king, moon, ship, coin, star…), speed-strip warm-up, Hub Station 4 check · sight/tricky words: there · are · here + Brown-set review.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Frame 2: “What has Toby got in his mouth?” → “A treasure map!” “A dog's map or a pirate's map?” → (predict, thumbs)
Frames 3–4: “Where's the big, old tree?” → “Next to the swimming pool.” “Where are the two yellow rocks?” → “In front of the zoo!”
Frames 7–8: “How many steps are there?” → “There are 20.” “What's the treasure?” → “A big bone — it's a dog's treasure map!”

Assumptions: places in town + There's a… / There are… known from U7 L1–L2; in / on / under, colours and numbers 1–20 known from earlier units; children know Toby the dog & the Magic Bike from U1. next to / in front of are the story's new locations (receptive today). Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures while listening; the teacher points, children don't read the speech bubbles alone.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
New locations block the story (next to · in front of)Pre-teach with a big gesture on the treasure map before listening: next to = hands side by side, in front of = one hand steps in front; “Show me!” drill.
Children call answers over the audio“Listening hands” rule — hands cupped behind ears while the CD plays; talk only when it stops.
There's vs There are mixed upOne = There's a… (one finger); many = There are two… (two fingers + “-s” hiss). Show fingers, all react.
Drama turns to chaos (running to find “treasure”)The hunt happens ON THE SPOT (Magic-bike ride = pedal sitting; “20 steps” = march on the spot); roles fixed by colour badges; “Freeze!” resets.
Frame cards ordered by one child in the pairRule “one card each turn, take turns” (RallyRobin with cards); praise pairs where both hands work.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset (essential in drama).

22–33′
📺 Story — listen & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 74). Big story picture on screen / books open. What places can you see in the story? — pairs point & name 15 sec, then chorus a few (zoo · swimming pool). Pre-teach with gesture: treasure map (unroll hands) · yellow rocks (two fists) · cross the road (walk fingers) · steps (march fingers, count).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “What's in the story?” ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U1 “Find it!” engine on the big story picture. · How: Speaky asks, a child taps the thing; it lights up. · Pupils: whole class points in their book / calls it — The zoo! The swimming pool! Toby! The treasure map! · Teacher: 🦝 Where's the old tree? Point! — 4–5 things, quick. Offline reserve: “Point to it in your book!” + hold up the big picture.

Predict (thinking) — before we listen. Toby has a map… whose treasure is it? A pirate's? A child's? A DOG'S? Thumbs guess. Let's listen and find the treasure!

Listen 1 — global (CD3 29). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: Did they find the treasure? Show me! (thumbs) What was it? → A big bone!

Detail check — True or False? (thumbs up / down, all react). Toby has a treasure map. (👍) · The old tree is next to the sweet shop. (👎 — next to the swimming pool!) · There are two yellow rocks. (👍) · There are 20 steps. (👍). Re-point the frame to fix any wrong thumb.

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the story gestures at the right moments: unroll the map · “Well done, Toby!” pat a dog · point next to / in front of · Magic-bike ride (pedal) · cross the road (walk fingers, wait for green!) · count 20 steps · cheer It's a big bone! CCQs between frames (see box above). At frames 3–4 stop: Where's the tree? — Next to the swimming pool! Where are the rocks? — In front of the zoo!

34–41′
▶ Build the story
desks, pairs

▶ Story-Sequence (Mido №54 “Reconstruction”, picture re-skin for non-readers — all pairs work at once). Each pair gets the story's frame-cards, shuffled.

Order it. Put the treasure hunt in order — one card each, take turns! (RallyRobin with cards: A places one, B places the next.) Countdown 2 min. ICQ: Do we grab all the cards or take turns? (→ take turns!)

Check with the audio. Replay CD3 29: pairs touch each card as they hear it — self-check, fix the order. Then choral retell, teacher holds up big frames one by one, class says the line: Can we explore your town? · It's a treasure map! · There's a tree next to the swimming pool. · There are two yellow rocks in front of the zoo. · Count 20 steps! · Magic Bike, can you help? · How many steps are there? — There are 20. · It's a big bone!

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Treasure Story-Sequence 1–8 🔨 СОБРАТЬ
Screen: the 8 story frames (map found → “It's a treasure map!” → tree next to the pool → rocks in front of the zoo → count 20 steps → Magic Bike helps → cross & count → the big bone) appear shuffled in a row, each with an empty number badge 1–8. · How: tap two frames to swap them (or drag); a “Check” button lights correct frames green, then a cheer plays. · Pupils: the class calls the order — Number one — Toby finds a map! Number two — It's a treasure map! — and gesture each frame. · Teacher: take votes (Which is first? Hands up!), tap as they decide, then replay CD3 29 to confirm. Offline reserve: the 8 big frame-cards on the board — the class number them together with sticky-tack.

Support: hand that pair only 5 clearest frames (not 8) and number the first for them. Extension: after ordering, say one whole line for each frame from memory before the audio check.

41–50′
Act out — drama
carpet, groups

Roles. Groups of 3–4: Molly (red badge, host — “Welcome!”) · Tim (green, reads the map) · Toby the dog (blue, “Woof! Ring, ring — Magic Bike!”) · an explorer (yellow, counts the steps). Model once with a strong group.

All groups act at the same time. Narrate the story slowly, frame by frame; every group plays it in their own spot (Magic-bike ride & 20 steps on the spot!): Can we explore your town? — Yes, of course! Look — Toby has a map! It's a treasure map! There's a tree next to the swimming pool. There are two yellow rocks in front of the zoo. We can't cross the road… Magic Bike, can you help? Green man — cross! Count 20 steps… It's a big bone!

Create (thinking) — make it yours. Before round 2: Your map has ONE more clue — you choose! What's next to it? Each group adds a line with their own place: There's treasure next to the library! There are three rocks in front of the park! (support: point to a place card; extension: invent the whole clue + the number).

Swap roles, run it again — keep it to 2 rounds (Freeze! the moment energy tips). Volunteers: ONE group shows 30 sec for the class — big applause.

Support: quieter children play the “counting explorer” (short line + march); a line card in hand. Extension: confident children play Tim / Molly and lead the map-reading.

50–55′
▶ Follow the map! + Values
desks, all at once

▶ Follow the map! (targeted settler — next to / in front of + There's / There are; every child places a card every round). Each child has a mini “town map” strip (tree · pool · zoo · library) and a few object cards (rocks · treasure · bone).

🦝 Speaky gives map clues: There are two yellow rocks in front of the zoo! → all move that card in front of the zoo; There's treasure next to the tree! · There's a bone next to the pool! 5–6 clues, instant show-me each time (Point! Where's the treasure? — Next to the tree!).

Values (PB p. 75 ex. 2) — cross the road safely. Hold up red / yellow / green. Red man — do we cross? — NO! Green man — cross the road! Remember! Cross the road when the man is… — GREEN! Quick “red-light / green-light” freeze on the spot to seal it.
