🖐 Give Me Five! 2 · Starter “Let's go back to school!” · Lesson 2 — Grammar · School subjects
A1 young learners (~8–9 y.o., early readers · continuing line) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Talk Partners · carpet + desks
Week 07.09–13.09 · Lesson 2✨ NEW · 8 school subjects~10 pupils · pairs + 2 teamsPupil's Book p.5 · AB p.3
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 8 school subjects and ask & answer “What's your favourite subject? — My favourite subject is (English).”
  • Subsidiary. Chant the subjects with rhythm (CD1 4); run a real class survey (data → mini-bar-chart); recycle “What's his/her name?” from L1; genuine communication — pupils report a partner's answer.
  • Personal. Model the full answer sentence, but accept one-word answers from slower pupils and recast; keep the survey moving — max 30 sec per partner.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 8 school subjects;
  • ask & answer “What's your favourite subject?”;
  • report a friend's answer: “Her favourite subject is Music.”

Structure
What's your favourite subject? — My favourite subject is (English).  ·  report: His/Her favourite subject is (PE).
Form
What + 's + your + favourite + subject? Possessives switch: your → my in the answer (watch pupils echoing “your” back).
Pron. watch
stress fa-vourite (2 syllables — /ˈfeɪv.rət/); PE & ICT as letter names /piː iː/, /aɪ siː tiː/; Maths with /s/ at the end.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Favourite — the one I like MOST or a little?” → most.
“In the answer do I say YOUR or MY?” → my.

Assumptions: L1 review words are warming up; pupils know school things from GMF1 U1 — subjects are NEW as a category. They can copy & write familiar words. Survey format is new this year — model it fully first.

10–19′
Presentation · 8 subjects
carpet → board

Present with icon cards. Hold up each card: What's this? It's… Maths! Choral ×3, then 2–3 solo. One action per subject (Maths = count fingers, Music = air-guitar, PE = run, Art = paint, Science = “boom!”, ICT = typing, English = open book, Spanish = “¡Hola!”).

Chant (act. 1, CD1 4). Chant through twice with the actions — first listen & point at the board cards, then chant along.

▶ Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if you hear… Music! — flash cards in random order, everyone reacts every time.

💪 Support: icon carries the meaning — word optional at this stage. ⭐ Extension: read the word cards without icons.

19–27′
Dialogue · model
carpet

Listen & repeat (act. 2, CD1 5–6). PB p.5: What's your favourite subject? — My favourite subject is Maths. Choral, rows, pairs.

Form focus (board). Write the Q&A; circle your / my: I ask about YOU — you answer about… ME? No — about MY! Chest-tap on my. Back-chain the long answer.

ICQ: Do you answer with one word or the full sentence? (→ full sentence — but recast, don't force)

 Pupil's Book p.5 · CD1 5–6 · board
27–38′
Class survey
mingle → desks

Model first. Ask 2 pupils publicly, tally on the board: What's your favourite subject? — Music! One point for Music!

▶ Survey mingle (AB-style grid). Each child has a mini-grid (8 subjects). Music on — walk; music off — pair up: What's your favourite subject? Tick the answer. 4–5 partners.

Harvest (desks). Count ticks together: How many for PE? Count! One, two, three… Build the class bar-chart on the board — PE is the champion!

✔ Assess: circulate — is the my/your switch happening? Recast on the spot, note 1–2 names.

 survey mini-grids · music · board chart
38–47′
Report & guess
carpet, pairs → circle

Report a partner. Model: This is Masha. Her favourite subject is Art. Pairs prepare (30 sec), then popcorn round the circle — each child reports their partner in one sentence (recycles his/her from L1!).

▶ Guess who! — you read from the survey: His favourite subject is Science… who is it? Class guesses the classmate. 4–5 rounds, everyone scans their grids — all involved.

💪 Support: sentence frame on the board: His/Her favourite subject is ___. ⭐ Extension: add a reason — because it's fun!
