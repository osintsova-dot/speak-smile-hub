🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 4 “Fantastic food!” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuing line) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Phonics · carpet + desks
Week 23.11–29.11✨ NEWcontinuing line~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to identify & name 10 food words and say what is on a plate using “What's this? — It's (chicken).” and the song chunk “I've got (rice and eggs).”
  • Subsidiary. Recycle have got (U3), Is it a…? Yes/No (U2), colours & adjectives (U2/Starter) in the food topic; phonics magic e (a–e) recognise → blend → segment; seed preferences receptively (I like / I love / delicious) as a bridge to L2; pre-literacy & fine motor (chant, stickers).
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; ICQ before every task; run a stirrer→settler rhythm with songs & signals; name-check every child at least once.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 food words & sing “Delicious food”;
  • ask & answer “What's this? — It's (fish).”;
  • read & blend magic-e (a–e) words: cake, plate, grape;
  • say one food they like (emerging).

Structure
What's this? — It's (chicken).  ·  I've got (rice and eggs).  ·  seed (receptive): I like / I love (ice cream). It's delicious!
Form
It + 's (is) + a/-.   I + 've (have) + got + food (from U3).   “ice cream” = two words (two claps).
Pron. watch
/tʃ/ in chicken & chips (contrast pair); final /z/ in eggs; linked “I've got”.
Phonics
Jolly / JP Book 2 — magic e: a–e (cake · plate · grape · name · game · snake). Recognise → blend → segment.
Tricky words
aftercover — learn by heart (can't sound out).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“I love ice cream” — a lot or a little? → a lot (love > like).
“It's delicious” — yummy or yucky? → yummy 😋
magic e “cake” — do we say the e? → no, it's silent; it makes a say its name /eɪ/.

Assumptions: pupils know the 26 sounds + digraphs and can blend/segment CVC; the tricky-word routine is known; U1–U3 language is available — classroom objects & Where's my…? (U1), toys & Is it a…? Yes/No + adjectives (U2), body & have got (U3); numbers & colours from the Starter. Emergent readers → lean on pictures & audio; single-word reading only.

10–21′
Phonics — magic e
at the board

Recognise. Write cap then add a silent e → cape: Watch the magic e! It's silent, but it makes the a say its name — /eɪ/. Wave a “magic-e wand” over the e. Show cards: name · game · cake · plate · grape · snake (cake/plate/grape link to food!).

▶ Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if you hear the LONG a — caaake!… Sit if it's short — cat! (mix a few: cake / cat / name / man / plate / plan). Everyone moves every time.

Blend. Push them together: c–a–k–e… cake! Children blend chorally + show the picture for meaning.

Segment · dictation. Now YOU write. Listen… say a word once → children write on mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & check. Words: cake · name · plate. Finish with tricky words — flash after cover: We can't sound these out — we just KNOW them. After! Cover!

23–32′
Presentation · Vocabulary
carpet → teams

Curiosity — mystery plate. ▶ Guess-by-a-slice — reveal each food card edge by edge: What's this? If unknown, model: It's chicken. Chicken. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 solos. Do all 10: rice · eggs · chicken · fish · chips · salad · fruit · cake · ice cream · meat.

Contrast the tricky pair (hold both): This is chicken. These are chips. Gesture: peck vs. dip a chip. “ice cream” → two claps.

▶ What's this? spinner (projector, food wheel) — spin → class chants It's [salad]! ×6–8 for fast recognition.

▶ Fan-N-Pick (teams of 4, mini food-cards): ① fans · ② picks & asks What's this? · ③ answers It's [fish]. · ④ praises Yummy! Well done! Rotate roles each turn.

ICQ: Do you say “What's this?” or “Where's my…?” (→ What's this?)
