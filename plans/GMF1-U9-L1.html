🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 9 “Day and night” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuing line) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Phonics · carpet + desks
Week 19.04–25.04✨ NEW · 10 daily routines~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4Pupil's Book p.92 · AB p.90
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 daily routines and say them in the first person — “I (get up)”, “I (brush my teeth)” — and answer “What's this? — It's (get up)” in the day-and-night context.
  • Subsidiary. Sing the coursebook song It's a beautiful day; retrieve colours (U1), numbers (U2), “Is it a…? — Yes/No” (U2), “can” (U3/U5), “What's this?” (U6) & “There's a…” (U7) woven onto the bedroom / day scene; phonics ‘ph’ = /f/ (recognise → blend → segment); tricky words water, who; pre-literacy & fine motor (sticker chant); preview “I (wash my face) every day.” for L2.
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; ICQ before every task; run a stirrer→settler rhythm with songs & signals; catch & recast the silent-b comb /kəʊm/ and the /θ/ in brush my teeth.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 daily routines (get up · get dressed · wash my face · brush my teeth · comb my hair · make my bed · have a shower · go to school · go to bed · sleep);
  • say & mime “I get up / I brush my teeth…” & answer “What's this? — It's…”;
  • hear & blend ‘ph’ = /f/ words.

Structure
What's this? — It's (get up).  ·  I (get up).  ·  (song) I open the window and I say “It's a beautiful day.”  ·  preview (L2): I (wash my face) every day.
Form
First-person routine chunks: I + verb phrase. “have a (shower)” / “go to (bed / school)” fixed patterns; possessive my. Weak a /ə/ in have a shower.
Pron. watch
silent-b comb /kəʊm/; brush my teeth /θ/; three-word chunks wash-my-face · make-my-bed; get up vs get dressed (stress the 2nd word); ‘ph’ /f/.
Phonics
Digraph ‘ph’ = /f/: phone · elephant · dolphin · photo (recognise → blend → segment). Tricky words: water · who.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“When you get up, are you in bed or out of bed?” → out → get up. “Do we brush our teeth or our hair?” → teeth → brush my teeth. “Do we sleep in the day or at night?” → night → sleep.
‘ph’: “How many letters?” → two. “How many sounds?” → one → /f/.

Assumptions: colours (U1), numbers 1–10 (U2), “Is it a…? — Yes/No” (U2), “can” (U3/U5), “What's this?” (U6) & “There's a…” (U7) and the 26 sounds + core digraphs are known (continuing line, late-year). Pupils are non-/emergent readers → rely on pictures, mime & audio, not printed text. Carpet & team routines are known.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
comb said with a hard /b/Model the silent b: comb — /kəʊm/, no /b/; touch lips, hold up the “b” card, cross it out.
Confuse wash my face ↔ have a shower ↔ brush my teethOne clear mime each (splash 💦 / rain-down 🚿 / scrub 🪥); say the first word loud.
get up ↔ get dressed mixed upContrast the mimes: stretch “out of bed” vs pull-on-clothes; stress the 2nd word.
‘ph’ read as /p/+/h/Slide two fingers together: two letters, ONE sound — /f/.
Over-excited “wake up!” energy on the carpetFreeze! + volume-control arm; follow every stirrer with a settler.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

10–22′
Phonics · ‘ph’ = /f/
at the board

Recognise. Two letters — p, h — but ONE sound: /f/! Show phone · elephant · dolphin · photo. ▶ Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if you hear /f/… phone!… cat!… elephant! (mix in non-‘ph’ words — everyone reacts every time).

🎮 Interactive (projector): Flashcards — ‘ph’ reveal. A picture uncovers slowly; class shouts the first sound before the reveal — /f/… phone! Then all together /f/ /əʊ/ /n/… phone! Every child points a “slide finger” = all blend at once. Offline reserve: hold A5 ‘ph’ picture cards, reveal by hand.

Blend. Push the sounds together with a finger slide + picture for meaning: /f/ /əʊ/ /n/… phone! Repeat with photo (/f/ /əʊ/ /t/ /əʊ/). Point to the ‘ph’: Here's our /f/!

Segment · sort. Where's the /f/? Show me! Say a word once → children draw the ‘ph’ shape in the air OR write ph on mini-whiteboards, then hold up: Show me! Words: phone · photo · dolphin · elephant. Then flash tricky words water · who — These are tricky — we just know them!

💪 Support: a picture beside each word; they only find & trace the ‘ph’. ⭐ Extension: where is the /f/ — at the start or in the middle? (phone → start · dolphin → middle).

✔ Assess: the “Show me!” boards are your live check — scan for who split ‘ph’ into /p/+/h/; recast on the spot.

24–33′
Presentation · Vocabulary
carpet → teams

Present — 10 daily routines. Do the mime + hold up each A4 card: What's this? If unknown, model with the action: Watch me… I get up! Get up. Choral drill ×3 (say + do), then 2–3 children solo. Routines: get up · get dressed · wash my face · brush my teeth · comb my hair · make my bed · have a shower · go to school · go to bed · sleep. Contrast the tricky trio (mime them): I wash my face. I have a shower. I brush my teeth.

🎮 Interactive (projector): Daily-routines spinner (колесо рутин). Spin → a routine lands. A rotating child taps to spin; the whole class does the action and chants I brush my teeth! Predict: before the reveal — Is it “sleep”? — Yes/No! (recycles U2). Every child shows a thumb 👍/👎 = all engaged. Offline reserve: shuffle the flashcard stack, flip the top card.

▶ Fan-N-Pick (teams of 4, mini-cards): ① fans the cards · ② picks & asks What's this? · ③ answers with the mime It's “comb my hair”. · ④ praises Well done! Rotate roles every turn — everyone asks, answers, praises.

ICQ: Do you say “What's this?” or “How many?” (→ What's this?)

💪 Support: pair a shy child with the “praiser” role first; keep the mime going. ⭐ Extension: answer in the first person — I comb my hair! 		A4 flashcards ×10 · 3 mini-sets · Routines spinner (board) · ▶ Fan-N-Pick

44–52′
Mime & Find
carpet → pairs
🎮 Interactive (projector): “Find it!” — the morning scene. Tap Speaky — 🦝 asks What's she doing? Can you find someone who is “brushing her teeth”? the class scans the bedroom scene on screen, points & answers It's “brush my teeth”! (2–3 rounds, to model the phrase for all). Offline reserve: the big Pupil's Book p.92 scene — point.

▶ Mime & Guess · What's this? — in pairs, on the carpet. Each pair has 5 routine cards face-down. Child 1 turns one card so only they see it, then mimes it silently. Child 2 guesses: Is it “make my bed”? — Yes! / No, try again.

Correct → keep the card & say it together Make my bed! Then swap roles. (Most cards wins.) Everyone is miming or guessing the whole time — no one waits. Reset if loud: Freeze!

✔ Assess: stand behind one pair at a time — can both produce the routine name unprompted from the mime? That's the lesson's success check.

💪 Support: 3 cards, not 5; keep a small picture strip visible to point to. ⭐ Extension: guess in the first person — You make your bed! / answer I make my bed every day! (previews L2). 		5 routine cards per pair · Find it! (board, model) · Speaky
