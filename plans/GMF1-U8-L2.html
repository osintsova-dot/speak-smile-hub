🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 8 “Camping fun!” · Lesson 2 — Grammar: What are you wearing? I'm wearing…
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Dialogue → controlled drill → freer → production · carpet + desks
Week 29.03 · Lesson 56✨ GRAMMAR~12 pupils · pairs + 2 teams · продолжающая
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to ask what someone is wearing and say what they are wearing using the present continuous: “What are you wearing? — I'm wearing (shorts) and a (T-shirt).” (Pupil's Book p. 83 dialogue, CD3 45), and act out the camping “Is it cold?” dialogue.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — the 10 clothes words from L1 (T-shirt, shorts, skirt, shoes, sweater, trousers, trainers, shirt, sun hat, swimsuit) + Starter colours — woven into today's structure (“I'm wearing a red T-shirt!”); phonics — the vowel sound /oɪ/ spelled “oy” (boy, toy, enjoy, joy), reviewing “oi” from L1 (coin, join); listen for detail (PB p. 83 ex. 2 “Listen and match”, CD3 46).
  • Personal. Insist on the FULL question (“What are you wearing?”, not “Wearing?”); keep I'm wearing in the answer (“I'm wearing shorts”, not just “shorts”); every task keeps ALL children responding (own cards / point & say / all pairs at once) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • ask “What are you wearing?” about someone's clothes;
  • answer “I'm wearing (shorts) and a (T-shirt).” and act out the camping dialogue;
  • hear, blend & segment words with /oɪ/ and know it can be spelled oi (middle) or oy (end).
Target language
Lexis
T-shirtshortsskirtshoessweatertrouserstrainersshirtsun hatswimsuit (recycled from L1 · + colours: red, blue…)
Structure
What are you wearing? — I'm wearing (shorts) and a (T-shirt).  ·  Is it cold? — No, it isn't. It's hot today.  ·  Hurry up! — I'm coming.
Form
Present continuous: I am / 'm wearing + clothes. Question What are you wearing? Note: shorts / trousers / trainers / shoes = plural → no “a” (“I'm wearing shorts”); singular → “a” (“a T-shirt”). Rising tone on the question ↗, falling on the answer ↘.
Pron. watch
wearing /ˈweərɪŋ/ — clear -ing ending; keep I'm (not bare “shorts”); linking “What are you” /wɒtəjə/; don't let the question shrink to one word.
Phonics
Jolly focus: /oɪ/ → oy — boy, toy, enjoy, joy (recognise → blend → segment) · review “oi” from L1 (coin, join, spoil) · rule: oi in the middle, oy at the end · tricky words: are, you (JP Book 2 p. 44).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Point at your own T-shirt: “Am I wearing it now?” → yes → “So I say: I'm wearing a T-shirt.” (present continuous = happening now)
Hold up shorts: “One thing or two legs?” → two → “So we say shorts — no ‘a’!” Hold up a T-shirt → “a T-shirt” (one → a).
oy: “Where is the /oɪ/ — in the middle or at the END of boy?” → at the end → “At the end we use oy; in the middle we use oi — coin.”
⭐ Option — weather CCQ: mime shivering: “Is it hot or cold?” → cold → “What do you wear? A sweater!” mime sun → hot → “Shorts and a T-shirt!” (Ties the dialogue's Is it cold? to the clothes — use only if the class is quick today.)

Assumptions: the 10 clothes words and the song “What are you wearing?” were first met in L1; colours known from the Starter; “oi” /oɪ/ met in L1. Pupils are non-/emergent readers → picture grids and audio, not printed text. Carpet routines & pair-work signals known.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
One-word answer: “shorts” instead of “I'm wearing shorts”Point-and-say rule: hand on the clothes + I'm wearing shorts! — the answer always starts with I'm wearing; back-chain …shorts → wearing shorts → I'm wearing shorts.
Drops -ing: “I wear shorts”Exaggerate the ending with a gesture (roll hands) — wear-ING; chorus 3× before the pair task.
Adds “a” to plurals: “a shorts / a trousers”Two-fingers gesture for plural clothes (two legs) vs one finger for “a T-shirt”; sort a few together: shorts — no ‘a’! T-shirt — a T-shirt!
oy said as /o/ + /y/, or confused with “oi”“oi and oy are twins — both say /oɪ/”; round the lips: oy… boy, toy; anchor the position rule (oy at the END).
Mingle game: child stays silent / just shows clothesRule: “Meet → ASK → ANSWER, then swap.” No high-five until both said the full line; teacher gestures “speak” before praise.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.
Procedure — staging
highlighted = exact words to say (teacher / class) 🦝 = Speaky lines ▶ = game […] = fill-in slot 🎮 = on-screen (projector) interactive
⏱ Pacing — honest 60 min. This is a full plate, so pick, don't cram. The core = dialogue model (24–33′) + one production game (Mingle) + Listen & match. Buffers to drop first if you're short: the “Dress the Camper” relay, the “What's he/she doing?” preview (53–55′), Timed Pair Share. Better one line said well by every child than three stages half-done — never rush the model and the pair practice.

Phonics — /oɪ/ oy
at the board

Recognise — meet the “oy twins”. Show oi from L1: Remember /oɪ/? — coin, join! Now show oy: This is oy — the TWIN of oi. It also says /oɪ/: oy… boy, toy, enjoy, joy! Action: point far away and call Boy! Toy! Children copy, rounding the lips.

oi or oy? — whole-class show-me (all react). Every child gets two cards: 🔵 oi and 🟡 oy. Say words one by one — boy · coin · toy · join · joy · spoil · enjoy · oil… → Show me! — all hold up 🔵 (middle) or 🟡 (end). Anchor the rule: /oɪ/ in the MIDDLE → oi; at the END → oy. 8–10 words, fast; nobody just watches.

Blend (all together). Push the sounds together: /b/ /oy/… boy! /t/ /oy/… toy! Then enjoy, joy — picture for each (boy · toy car · smiley “I enjoy!” · happy face).

Segment · dictation (everyone writes). Now YOU write. Listen… word once → all write on mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & check together. Focus: boy, toy, joy. Finish with tricky words by sight: are · you — flash, chorus, air-write (they're in today's question!).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): oy Sorter ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the Unit 1 “Flashcards / qu Sorter” engine)
Screen: a word + picture flips up one at a time (boy · coin · toy · join · joy · spoil · enjoy · oil). Mechanic: tap the card to hear the word, then tap 🔵 oi or 🟡 oy to sort it; a green tick / red shake shows if right. Pupils: hold up their own 🔵/🟡 first, then a helper taps to check. Teacher: read the word, wait for hands, then reveal. Reuse: same engine as the U1 Sorter — just swap the card set (oi/oy).

◆ Support: in dictation, give the sound-buttons (b-oy) — they only fill letters. ▲ Extension: fast writers add a 4th word by ear (Roy, soil).

Presentation · Dialogue

Model with Speaky. 🦝 Speaky hides in a “tent” (a chair with a cloth). Teacher: Is it cold, Speaky? — 🦝 No, it isn't. It's hot today! — What are you wearing? — 🦝 I'm wearing shorts and a T-shirt. Hurry up! — I'm coming!

Listen & repeat — PB p. 83 ex. 1 (CD3 45). Books open / picture on screen: Jake at the campsite. Play the dialogue; children repeat line by line. Drill: rising tone on What are you wearing? ↗, point-and-say I'm wearing shorts and a T-shirt! ↘ Back-chain the question. Point at the Grammar box: What are you wearing? I'm wearing a T-shirt. — this is our question of the day.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): What are you wearing? Spinner ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the “What's this? / Where's my…? spinner” engine)
Screen: a wheel of the 10 clothes items next to a plain camper character. Mechanic: tap to spin → it lands on 2 items; tap → the camper puts them ON (reveal + “ta-da” sound). Pupils: BEFORE the reveal the whole class asks together What are you wearing? ↗ → on reveal the class answers for the camper I'm wearing shorts and a T-shirt! ↘. Teacher: spin, hold the reveal until you hear the FULL question, then tap. 🧠 Predict: What will the camper wear? Point & guess! — children point before the wheel stops.

Act out (ALL pairs at once). Pairs on the carpet; each pair gets 2 clothes cards face-down. A: What are you wearing? B looks at their cards and answers: I'm wearing (a skirt) and (shoes)! Swap cards, change roles, repeat. ICQ: Do you say “shorts” or “I'm wearing shorts”? (→ the full answer!)

◆ Support: let the weaker child answer first with the card in view + you mouth I'm wearing…. ▲ Extension: add a colour — I'm wearing a red T-shirt and blue shorts!

whole room

▶ Clothes Mingle (Игротека — walk-and-meet, our U8 version — the whole class moves and talks, nobody sits out). Each child holds a secret “outfit” card (2 clothes items pictured). Music on → everyone walks; music stops → Freeze! Find a partner!

Kagan — Mix-Pair-Share: in each pair, A asks What are you wearing? → B reads their card: I'm wearing (a sweater) and (boots)! → swap → high-five → new partner when the music starts. Teacher feeds the language: Good! What ARE you wearing? Say the whole answer!
