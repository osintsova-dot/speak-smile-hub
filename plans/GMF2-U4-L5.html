Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the or/al tongue twister («The horse in shorts walks and talks in a storm»), talk about British forest schools, and read an adventure story & write their own with the story-chain frame.
  • Subsidiary. Textbook phonics: or/al = /ɔː/; culture — dens, mud pies, minibeast hunts vs our outdoor games; literacy — text type an adventure story (Adam & the treasure), sequencing verbs.
  • Personal. The adventure-story writing is the deliverable — protect it; forest-school block invites “что мы делаем на улице” talk — keep it in English frames.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the twister fast ×3;
  • name 3 forest-school activities;
  • write a 4-step adventure story from the choice-frame.

Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“horse and walks — same sound? Same letters?” → same /ɔː/, different letters (и l молчит!).
“An adventure story — boring or exciting?” → exciting — danger + treasure!

Assumptions: Prepositions + trail words active. Adventure schema (map→danger→treasure) is universally known from cartoons — writing rides on it. or/al recognition only. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Block 3 · Literacy · Adventure story
carpet → desks

Before you read. PB p.47 picture: What's he got? A map! Where's the X?

Listen & read (CD2 33). Echo-read line by line; mime each move (sail! cross the rope bridge! tiptoe the passage! RUN from the mummies!).

Order check. You read events shuffled — class shouts the right order numbers.

Write your adventure (notebooks, AB p.44 format). Choice-frame: sails across the (river/lake/pool) → walks across the (grass/bridge/sand) → goes along the (path/road/secret passage) → sees the (monkeys/mummies/monsters) → finds the (map/treasure/key)! Pairs choose a path, write 4–5 lines, title it. Two pairs read theirs — class mimes along.

💪 Support: circle choices in the printed frame, copy into lines. ⭐ Extension: add a twist line: But the treasure is… a banana!
