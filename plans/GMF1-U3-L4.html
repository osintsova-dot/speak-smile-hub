🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 3 “At the circus” · Lesson 4 — Vocab+Grammar: Describing hair & eyes · He's / She's got green eyes and short hair!
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers — CONTINUING line) · 60 min · New vocab + structure together (PPP + TPR) · carpet + desks
Week 09.11–15.11 · Lesson 21✨ VOCAB+GRAM~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4 + pairs🦸 Phonics Hero ур.19a · Magic E: краткий/долгий
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to describe a person's hair and eyes — 6 hair words long · short · blonde · brown · black · red hair (Pupil's Book p. 32) — and use the structure He's / She's got (green eyes) and (short hair) / My friend has got… to talk about a friend or a circus clown.
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve real prior learning — body/face words from U3 L1 (head, eyes, ears, nose, mouth, hair), I've got a (red nose) from L2, the story Fun at the circus where Toby “got purple hair” (L3), colours from the Starter — all woven into today's target: “Molly the clown has got curly red hair and big eyes!”; phonics — Phonics Hero ур.19 (a) первое знакомство: Magic E part 1 — «Краткий ✊ или долгий 🖐?» (the vowel's two voices: ran → rain, kit → kite); sing the coursebook song My friend (CD2 2); value: we're all different — and that's OK.
  • Personal. Split SAY vs DO clearly in every game; give an ICQ before each game; use one gesture per word (hair = frame the head ✋ · eyes = point to eyes 👀); every task keeps ALL children responding (own face-cards / all point at once / pair barrier game / each draws their OWN friend).
By the end, pupils can…
  • name 6 hair words with a gesture (long, short, blonde, brown, black, red hair);
  • describe a face: “She's got long hair. He's got green eyes.”;
  • ask & describe a friend: “My friend has got black hair.”;
  • hear & show the difference between a short ✊ and a long 🖐 vowel (kit — kite, ran — rain) and say the five vowel names.
Target language
Lexis
long hairshort hairblonde hairbrown hairblack hairred hair (+ eyes via grammar: green · brown · blue eyes; recycled: head, ears, nose, mouth, big/small)
Structure
He's got (green eyes). · She's got (long hair). · She's got (brown eyes) and (short hair). · Talk Partners: My friend has got black hair. My friend has got green eyes.
Form
He / She + 's got (= has got) + adjective + noun. Chunk: has got for description. Join two facts with and.
Pron. watch
blonde = /blɒnd/ (one beat, silent “e” — NOT “blond-ee”); hair /heə/ (not “her”); She's got = /ʃiːz ɡɒt/ (contracted has → 's /z/); eyes /aɪz/.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.19 (a) первое знакомство — Magic E, part 1: «Краткий ✊ или долгий 🖐?» — every vowel has TWO voices: short (cat, kit, hop) and long = its alphabet name (rain, kite, hope); vowel names a /эй/ · e /и:/ · i /ай/ · o /оу/ · u /ю:/ (тетрадь стр.53) · sight words: other · were.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Show a long-hair face vs a short-hair face. “Is her hair long or short?” (frame it with hands).
Point to a boy card / a girl card. “This is a girl — do I say He's got or She's got?” → She's got.
Hold up green vs brown. “The clown's eyes — are they green or brown?” (point + name).

Assumptions: face/body words + I've/He's/She's got are solid from U3 L1–L3; colours from the Starter. Emergent readers → real faces, pictures & audio, minimal print. Phonics: all Part-2 digraphs (ai…ar) finished last lesson (ур.18) → today opens Part 3 · Magic E (ур.19a, стр.53). Note: КТП/BigBen listed curly/straight hair + blue/brown/green eyes, but PB p. 32 ex. 1 core vocab is long/short/blonde/brown/black/red hair; eyes (green/brown/blue) come through the grammar & the song → plan follows the book.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Mix up He's got / She's got (boy vs girl)Always point to a boy card or girl card first, THEN say the sentence. Chant: girl → She's… ✋girl · boy → He's… ✋boy. Hide the word — keep the point.
Drop “got”: “She long hair”Model the whole chunk She's-got as ONE word; back-chain …long hair → got long hair → She's got long hair.
blonde said “blond-ee”; hair → “her”Model /blɒnd/ and /heə/, exaggerate; picture appears for meaning; praise self-corrections.
Over-excitement drawing / cutting facesSoft pace, “Freeze!” resets; ICQ before each craft (Do we draw now or listen?).
Short vs long vowel sounds merge (kit = kite)Anchor gestures: ✊ fist = short knock, 🖐 open palm = the vowel “says its name”; exaggerate the contrast in pairs (kit — kiiite), all show the hand every time.

“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.

Phonics Hero — Magic E: краткий/долгий (a)

Speed reading — the WHOLE Part 2 (тетрадь стр.53 №1). The big strip ai · oa · ie · ee · or · ng · oo · ch · sh · th · ou · oi · ue · er · ar — all fifteen! You finished ALL the digraphs — read them there and back… fast! Whole class, victory lap.

The vowel secret (стр.53, Спики). 🦝 Speaky whispers: Every vowel has TWO voices! Short — squeeze a fist ✊, a little knock: a — /э/ — cat! Long — open the palm 🖐, the vowel says its NAME: a — /эй/ — rain! All do fist/palm with each example (стр.53 №2 «Два голоса гласной»).

Vowel names — стр.53 №3. Read the five name-cards together: a /эй/ · e /и:/ · i /ай/ · o /оу/ · u /ю:/. The long vowel sounds like its alphabet name! Chorus ×2 with open palms 🖐.

Short or long? — стр.53 №4 (all vote, then circle). Read each pair together — ran/rain · cot/coat · kit/kite · bed/bead · cub/cube… — every child shows ✊ or 🖐 for each word, then circles the LONG one in the тетрадь. 5–6 pairs in class, the rest for home. Sight words to finish (whole-word flash): other · were.

💪 Support: teacher exaggerates the pair first (kit — kiiite); child only shows the hand. ⭐ Extension: think of another “name-sayer” word (cake? bee?).
🎮 Phonics Hub (проектор): speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/ — набор «Краткий или долгий? 🖐» Screen: word pairs flash up; tap to hear, tap to reveal short ✊ / long 🖐. Pupils: every child shows the fist/palm BEFORE the reveal — the app checks, children voice the words. Teacher: hold the reveal until all hands are up; celebrate the tricky ones (bed/bead). ✅ ГОТОВ — Offline: тетрадь стр.53 №4 + your own fist/palm do the same job.

20–28′
Presentation · hair + He's/She's got
carpet

Present hair vocab (PB p. 32 ex. 1, CD2 1). Listen–point–say the 6 faces: long hair · short hair · blonde hair · brown hair · black hair · red hair. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children. Fix the tricky word: blonde = /blɒnd/ (one beat).

Present the grammar. Hold a girl card, point: She's got long hair. She's got brown eyes. Hold a boy card: He's got short hair. He's got green eyes. Say → point to girl/boy → children copy. CCQ (see box): A girl — He's got or She's got? (→ She's got!)

Sing the coursebook song (PB p. 32 ex. 2, CD2 2). ▶ «My friend» — listen & point, then sing by ear with pictures. Key lines to own: He's got green eyes. He's got short hair. He's fantastic! · value line: We're different, and that's OK — we're friends! · 🎮 u3-song-friend

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): What's this? spinner · 🎮 whats-this-spinner-u3 Screen: a wheel of the 6 hair pictures; the arrow spins and lands on one. Mechanics: tap Spin → arrow stops on long / short / blonde / brown / black / red hair → whole class answers, then a named child. Language: What hair? — Long hair! · She's got long hair. Teacher: spin 6–8×, speed up; land on blonde to re-run the /blɒnd/ model. ✅ ГОТОВ — U1 "What's this?" spinner (swap deck to hair). Offline: shuffle the 6 hair cards, flip one at a time.

▶ Make my clown — barrier game
desks, in pairs

▶ Barrier / Talk-Partners game (PB p. 32 ex. 3 model first; Kagan — RallyCoach; both children speak & build every round). Model on the board with 🦝: My clown has got short hair. And she's got big eyes.

Each pair: a stand-up book as a screen + 2 identical clown-face bases + loose hair & eye pieces. Pupil A builds a clown & describes: My clown has got red hair and green eyes. Pupil B builds the SAME clown on their side from the words only. Lift the screen → Same? — Yes! cheer & high-five → swap roles. ICQ: Do you SHOW your clown, or SAY the words? (→ Say the words!)

🎮 Digital option (projector): body-board — S&S dice board game in teams: tap the die, hop along the board, and the whole team says the square aloud — I've got ten fingers! A quick consolidation round if the screen is free.
