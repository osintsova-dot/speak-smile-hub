🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 9 “Day and night” · Lesson 5 — Phonics · Culture · Literacy: A Day-and-Night Poem
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Phonics + CLIL culture + guided literacy · carpet + desks
Week 03.05–09.05 · Lesson 66L5 Phon+Cult+Litcontinuing group · ~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4🦸 Phonics Hero · итоговый диктант Частей 1–2
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the /θ/ sound (Toby's tongue twister “Three thin tigers have a bath on Thursday”), understand what British children do when a tooth falls out (the Tooth Fairy — box under the pillow, comes at night, leaves a coin) and read & act out a day-and-night poem (“Morning time”), then make their own day/night mini-poem card.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning inside the topic — Unit 9 daily routines + meal times + “I (brush my teeth) every day. — Me, too.” (L1–L4) pulled straight into the /θ/ words (teeth · bath · Thursday · three); Phonics Hero — итоговый диктант: звуки и диграфы Частей 1–2 (CVC + ai·ee·oo·sh·ou·ar…), the year's writing check in a friendly game shape; tricky words that also hide a th: the · they · this · that + sight words day · night.
  • Personal. Keep the craft stage tight (timer + ONE line per card); model writing letter-by-letter on the board; every child responds in every task (own card / show-me “th” tongue / thumbs vote) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tongue twister with a clear English /θ/ and spot words with the ‘th' sound;
  • name 2–3 things about the Tooth Fairy in Britain (box · pillow · night · coin);
  • read a short day-and-night poem and make their own mini-poem card (copy one line + draw);
  • write the year's dictation — one word per sound family (cat · rain · moon · ship · star…).

Structure
th – th – th. Three thin tigers have a bath on Thursday.  ·  In Britain, the Tooth Fairy visits children at night. I put my tooth under my pillow.  ·  Poem: It's morning time. The sun is in the sky. Get up, children — it's a new day!  ·  I (brush my teeth) every day. — Me, too.
Form
th + word (initial & final: thin · bath). Poem = short rhyming lines. Fixed chunks, copied not built.
Pron. watch
English /θ/ — tongue tip gently between the teeth, soft air out: not Russian «с/ф/т». Keep the tongue visible in three, thin, bath, Thursday, teeth. (Voiced pair /ð/ hides in the · this · they.)
Phonics
Coursebook sound: th (PB p. 97, CD4 18–19) — the digraph from Phonics Hero ур.16! · Phonics Hero — итоговый диктант Частей 1–2: one word per family — cat · sun · rain · bee · moon · ship · out · star (+ this for th). Tricky words: the · they · this · that; sight words day · night.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
English ‘th' — do we hide the tongue, or show it? → Show it — between the teeth! Bath — /b/ /a/ … and the last sound? th!
The Tooth Fairy — does she come in the day or at night? → At night! Where do you put your tooth? → Under the pillow!
In the poem — morning: the sun says hello or goodbye? → Hello! (the moon and stars say goodbye).

Assumptions: daily routines + meal times + “I … every day / Me, too” known from L1–L4; children can copy letters but not write freely → all writing is trace/copy from a board frame. Emergent readers: the poem is read together, finger-following. The Tooth Fairy may be new — model warmly; some children may have a different custom (a little mouse in Russia) → celebrate it.

Can't tell th-words from othersPicture anchors (three·tooth·teeth·think·mouth·bath) + tongue-out show-me; exaggerate the ‘th'; use the tooth anchor card.
Poem copying slow / words unknownBoard frame written LARGE, ONE line only; dotted-line strips for weakest writers; teacher scribes.

A child is upset (“no Tooth Fairy at my home”)Frame it as In Britain… then And in Russia? — warmly celebrate their own custom (мышка). No “right” answer.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

10–22′
Phonics — th + sound review
at the board

Recognise th (~2½′). Toby's tongue twister (PB p. 97, CD4 18): th – th – th. Three thin tigers have a bath on Thursday! Tongue-between-teeth gesture; soft air, tongue visible — not «с/ф». Choral ×3: slow → normal → fast. 2–3 brave solos with the “microphone”. Support: hold up the th picture-anchor card (a tooth) as the sound cue — weak pupils watch it, not the letters.

“th or not th?” — whole-class show-me (~2′, all react). Pictures one by one — three · mouth · bed · think · tooth · door · teeth · thin (AB p. 94 set). Has the ‘th' sound → everyone stands and sticks the tongue out “th”; no ‘th' → sit, hands on knees. Show me! Fast, nobody just watches.

🎮 Projector: Flashcards — “th or not th?” ✅ ready
  • Screen: big picture flashcards, one tap = next card.
  • Kids: whole class stands+“th” for th-words, sits for the rest — every child reacts at once.
  • Teacher: use the existing Flashcards tool; exaggerate the ‘th'.
  • Offline: paper picture cards from the AB p. 94 set, held up one by one.

Blend for meaning (~1½′). Push sounds together with picture support: /th/ /i/ /n/… thin! /b/ /a/ /th/… bath! /t/ /ee/ /th/… teeth! Children echo + do the action.

🦸 Итоговый диктант — Части 1–2 (~4′, everyone writes, game shape). The BIG year dictation — show what you know! One word per family, word once → segment on fingers together → all write on mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & self-check. Words (pick 6–8): cat · sun · rain · bee · moon · ship · out · star + this (today's th!). A 5-second shake-out between words — a game, not a test; every correct board earns a team point.

🎮 Projector: Phonics Hub — speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/ · Station 4 «Сегментинг (диктант)» ✅ ready
  • Screen: after each word the letters appear one by one for self-check.
  • Kids: write first on their board — the Hub is the reveal; children voice everything, not the app.
  • Offline: teacher reveals on the board letter by letter.

Tricky words (~1½′). Flash cards the · they · this · that + sight words day · night: Look — these have ‘th' too, but we just KNOW them: the… they… this… that. Choral ×2 + air-write. Then straight to the song — keep the pace up.

Culture — the Tooth Fairy in Britain
carpet → circle

Listen & look (PB p. 98, CD4 20). Photo by photo: In Britain, the Tooth Fairy visits children's bedrooms at night. When my tooth falls out, I put it in a box. I put the box under my pillow. The Tooth Fairy comes at night — she takes my tooth when I'm sleeping. In the morning, I find a letter: “Thank you, Lucy!” — and money in my money box!

▶ Tooth-Fairy mime — everyone joins in (role-play). ICQ: Night — quiet or loud? — Quiet! All mime: put the tooth in the box → box under the pillow → curl up and “sleep” → (Speaky flutters the fairy over) → morning: stretch, find the coin! Every child mimes, all together.

Think about YOUR culture (all vote — thumbs). Have you got a Tooth Fairy in YOUR country? What do you do with your tooth? — thumbs up / down + one-word answers; celebrate the Russian custom (мышка) warmly. Everyone answers.

35–47′
Literacy — “Morning time” poem
desks

Before you read (PB p. 99, CD4 21). Listen and number — day or night? Point: sleep · owl · cockerel · children → each time say day! or night! — everyone calls out.

Listen & read the poem (CD4 22) — fingers follow. Read “Morning time” together (emergent readers follow the picture + line): It's morning time. The sun is in the sky. The moon and the stars say goodbye. The owl goes to sleep… and the cockerel crows. Get up, children — it's a new day! CCQ: Morning — sun: hello or goodbye? — Hello!

▶ Act out the poem (Ex 3, everyone). Owl sleeps (whoo), cockerel crows (cock-a-doodle-doo), all stretch: Get up, children — it's time to play!

Make YOUR day/night card (literacy product). Fold an A5 card: one side NIGHT (moon · stars · owl — cool colours), one side DAY (sun · cockerel · children — warm colours). Each child copies ONE line from the board frame (written LARGE) and draws it. Support: dotted-line strips to trace + teacher scribes. Extension: copy a second line, or add their own: It's night time… Timer 8 min, countdown finish: 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down!
