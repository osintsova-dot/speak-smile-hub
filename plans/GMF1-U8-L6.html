🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 8 “Camping fun!” · Lesson 6 — Review: Clothes & What are you wearing? · 🏆 Flagship Quiz
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Consolidation + flagship team quiz with reward · carpet + desks
Week 12.04–18.04 · Lesson 60L6 Review🏆 Quiz day~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will have consolidated all Unit 8 language — the clothes (T-shirt, shorts, trousers, skirt, shirt, sweater, shoes, trainers, sun hat, swimsuit + coat/boots), “What are you wearing? — I'm wearing (shorts) and a (T-shirt)”, “What's he/she wearing? — He's/She's wearing…”, the outdoor activities (riding a horse, climbing a tree, swimming, fishing, sailing a boat, playing the guitar, painting a picture, putting up a tent) and “What's he/she doing? — He's/She's (fishing)” (present continuous) — and can use it confidently in the review tasks and the quiz.
  • Subsidiary. Jolly track — read the soft “c” (face, cycle, circus, race, city) and recap the unit's “v” sound (Vicky the vet loves volleyball); tricky words there, were; self-assessment (My progress traffic light, AB p. 87 ex. 3); listening for detail (PB p. 90 ex. 1, CD4 05).
  • Personal. Run the flagship quiz by PIES: every child writes/shows every answer (Showdown with mini-boards), the responder is picked by Numbered Heads — no “captains answer everything”; keep the reward moment short so language time wins.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the clothes and answer “What are you wearing?” without support;
  • ask & answer “What's he/she doing? — He's/She's (fishing)” (present continuous);
  • ask & answer “What's he/she wearing? — He's/She's wearing (a sweater)”;
  • read words with the soft “c” sound and recap the “v” sound;
  • self-assess with the traffic light — ready for the Unit 8 test.

Structure
What are you wearing? — I'm wearing (shorts) and a (T-shirt). · What's he/she doing? — He's/She's (fishing). · What's he/she wearing? — He's/She's wearing (a sweater).
Form
Present continuous be + verb-ing (now): He 's swimming. · wear + ing = wearing. Contractions I'm / He's / She's.
Pron. watch
clear -ing /ɪŋ/ (not “swim”); linking “He's_swimming / She's_riding”; soft c = /s/ in face, city.
Phonics
Jolly focus: soft “c” /s/ (face, cycle, circus, race, city) + recap “v” (Vicky the vet loves volleyball). Tricky words: there, were.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Point to a boy fishing: Is he fishing NOW or every day? → now → “He's fishing.” (present continuous = happening now).
Quiz rule ICQ: Who writes the answer — one child or EVERYBODY? → everybody, on their own board!

Assumptions: all Unit 8 language met in L1–L5; present continuous (“What's he/she doing?”) met in L2 & L4; the “v” sound was taught in L5 — today we read the soft c. Teams of 4 with numbered members (1–4) are routine. The flagship quiz with reward runs only on Review day — children know it's special.

Phonics — soft “c” + recap “v”
📺 board → open space

Recap “v” (fast, 30 sec). Tongue twister: v – v – v. Vicky the vet loves volleyball! ×2 with the “v” lip-bite (from L5, PB p. 87).

Blend soft “c” (all together, word first — picture after). Write f-a-ce: arm-blending gesture — /f/… /a/… /s/… push them together — face! Then race, city, cycle, circus. Reveal “c before i / e / y says /s/”. Picture appears only AFTER the class reads — reading, not guessing.

📺 Interactive · projector · “Flashcards” (Genki/S&S engine)  ✅ READY
Screen: the soft-“c” word fills the screen in big letters — face · race · city · cycle · circus — tap to flip and reveal the picture.
Mechanic: word shows first (letters only) → class blends chorally → tap → picture confirms. Never picture-first.
Target language: /s/… blend it… circus!
Teacher: tap in rhythm, keep the arm-blend gesture; no projector → paper word cards work identically.

▶ Take Off–Touch Down (all move): Stand up if “c” says /s/! Say a mix: face · cat · city · cake · circus · cup · cycle. Everyone stands / sits every time — nobody watches.

▶ Swat It! — мухобойки (fly-swatters, whole class reads). 5–6 soft-“c” words on the board. Point to one → the WHOLE class reads it chorally first (c-i-r-c-us… circus!) → THEN two children race to swat it. Quick rotation, new pair each word, so many get a turn while the class is always the reading “jury”.

Tricky words. Flowers there · were: Tricky words — we just KNOW them! There… were. Choral ×2 + air-write (these two appear in our postcard: There's a lake…).

17–24′
Review in motion
📺 open space · pairs

▶ Camping Charades — What are you doing? (everyone mimes, nobody waits). 🦝 Speaky whispers an activity to the class: Everybody — you're swimming! → all mime. Then freeze one child: What's he doing? Think–Pair first: everyone whispers to a neighbour. Call a number → that child answers: He's swimming! Class confirms chorally: Yes! He's swimming! Cycle 5–6 activities — riding a horse · climbing a tree · fishing · sailing a boat · playing the guitar.

▶ RallyRobin — What are you wearing? (pairs, both talk). Partners take turns naming one thing they're wearing: I'm wearing trousers. — I'm wearing a T-shirt. Every child speaks every round. Then flip to the model song song Genki «What Are You Doing?» as a movement recap of the -ing structure (mime along).

Option — big room? Secret-Word Stations. Set 4 corner stations (Clothes · What are you wearing? · Activities · What's he/she doing?). Teams rotate; clear one mini-task per station (name it / answer / mime it) and earn ONE letter. Four stations → four letters → unscramble the secret word (e.g. T·E·N·T / C·A·M·P). First team to read the word wins — a lively rotation alternative when space allows.

Support ▸ weaker pupils keep an activity picture card in hand + answer with a partner (echo the chunk).   Extension ▸ stronger pupils give the full sentence — He's riding a horse and he's wearing a sun hat.
📺 Interactive · projector · “What's this? Spinner” + “Find it!” (U8 review engines)  ✅ READY
Screen: Spinner lands on a random clothes or activity picture; or Find it! shows a busy campsite scene and highlights a person doing something.
Mechanic: Spinner → What's this? — It's a swimsuit! / He's fishing! · Find it! → point & say She's sailing a boat! (whole class points at the screen every round).
Target language: What are you wearing? / What's he/she doing? — He's/She's (fishing).
Teacher: use as a warm station or when a mime round runs dry; keep it to 60–90 sec so movement stays the star.

33–51′
🏆 FLAGSHIP QUIZ with reward
📺 teams · projector

Set-up (2 min). 3 teams of 4, numbered heads 1–4, one mini-whiteboard EACH. Quiz grid on screen — categories: Clothes 👕 · What are you doing? 🏕️ · Sounds & spelling 🔤 · Camp & culture ✨. Rules + ICQ: Who writes? — Everybody! When do you show? — On “Showdown!”

Round flow (Kagan Showdown + Numbered Heads — all answer every question). Question up (e.g. clothes picture: What is it? · activity picture: What's he doing? · sound: Write the word — f-a-ce! · read a soft-“c” word · camp culture: What do you sleep in? — a tent!). Every child writes/draws → Showdown! → all boards up → call a number: Number 3! — that child of the playing team answers aloud in a full sentence.

Predict (thinking). On a half-hidden clothes/activity picture, before revealing: Look — just a corner! What do you THINK it is? Write your guess! Then reveal — Were you right? Turns each round into a little guess-and-check.

Reward shot. Correct = the answering child takes the shot: animated football penalty on screen — a GOAL = team points, class counts them in English: One, two, three… GOAL! (No projector → paper goal + soft ball, ≈ Mido №91 Bowling for Animal reskin.) Rotate teams; over ~10–12 questions every child shoots at least once.

Support ▸ word-box / picture bank stays visible on a side card so weaker writers copy, not freeze; accept one correct word from them.   Extension ▸ when a strong pupil is “Number”, require the full sentence — He's fishing. He's wearing a sun hat. — not just the verb.
🔨 Flagship interactive · projector · BUILD · “Unit 8 Team Quiz + Reward”  🔨 BUILD
Screen 1 — Grid: 4×3 board of numbered tiles under the 4 category headers (Clothes 👕 / What are you doing? 🏕️ / Sounds & spelling 🔤 / Camp & culture ✨). Tap a tile → it flips to the question (clothes pic / half-hidden item / activity pic / audio word / soft-“c” word / culture prompt). Used tiles grey out.
Screen 2 — Answer: big “Show your boards!” + a 5-second timer bar; tap ✔ / ✗ to score; a full-sentence model appears for choral echo.
Screen 3 — Reward mini-game: football penalty — child “kicks”, goalie dives, animated GOAL (or a bowling reskin). One shot = +points.
Screen 4 — Scoreboard: 3 team bars, huge numbers readable from the back row, plus a “Teamwork ⭐” toggle for best cooperation.
Mechanic: pick tile → question → everyone writes → Showdown → Numbered Head answers → score → reward shot → rotate team. ~10–12 tiles ≈ 15 min.
Target language: all Unit 8 — What are you wearing? — I'm wearing… / What's he/she doing? — He's/She's (fishing) / What's he/she wearing? + read a soft-“c” word.
Teacher: host only — YOU tap tiles & call the number so no captain dominates. Spec is a self-contained HTML slide (S&S purple #6C3FC5 / honey #F5C842). Offline reserve: printed grid + paper goal + soft ball works identically.

Finish with totals + teamwork star: Give me five, champions!
