Genki preschoolers (~3.5–5 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Adventure level · Lesson 6 of 7✨ thank you · please · you’re welcome + finale song12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to use polite chunks — Thank you! · Please. · You’re welcome! · Goodbye — giving & receiving, and to sing the Thank You! song, which becomes the everyday finale ritual of every lesson.
  • Subsidiary. Remember-time recycle of the whole level (Please game with all commands/verbs/directions); folder worksheet; the S&S thank-you card. Present the Thank You! song as our closing ritual.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; model politeness warmly all lesson; every child both gives & receives; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say Thank you! when they receive;
  • answer You’re welcome!;
  • ask with …please;
  • sing the Thank You! song with bows;
  • make & give a thank-you card.

Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: (Pass-the-gift) “Get the gift → say Thank you?” → yes.
ICQ: (Please game) “Do it only if I say…?” → please.

Assumptions: children had L1–L5 (all level chunks). Politeness taught through giving & receiving real objects + L1 prompt, not abstract talk. Non-readers. Group = 12. This lesson launches the Thank You! song as the finale ritual for all lessons.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Won’t give up the gift/propMake giving the fun part — the giver gets the You’re welcome! line & applause. Speaky models generous giving.
“Please/Thank you” droppedNever hand anything over until the polite chunk comes; wait warmly with a smile.
Please game → everyone moves anywaySlow down; exaggerate “no please” pauses; Freeze! resets; praise those who waited.
Shy child won’t give a cardGiving to Speaky or the teacher counts; a bow without words counts too.
Transitions get chaoticTrain: Come with me! — one signal, one destination.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 👋 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…” with fingers shown.
“Come with me!” 🚊 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana’s real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🌱 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Finale = cumulative Q&A chain + the Thank You! song ritual.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello song (chorus), “choose the greeting → jump/run/swim/fly to your chair”.
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives & shakes hands with each child (Hello! How are you?).
  • Remember time — cumulative review with A4 flashcards / memory / bridge game / app on the board.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, topic song, board app, one active game.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheets, a different type each lesson (listen & tick / colour by command / trace / match соедини / draw & tell / put-in-order / mini-craft), always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🌱 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Remember time 2 + Closing — run the growing Q&A chain, Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
