Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Consolidation + flagship team quiz with reward · carpet + desks
Week 26.10 · Lesson 16L6 Review🏆 Quiz day~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will have consolidated all Unit 2 language — the 10 toys, “What is it? / It's a…”, “Is it a…? — Yes, it is / No, it isn't”, “What's your favourite toy?” and the adjectives big/small · old/new · fast/slow — and can use it confidently in the review tasks and the quiz.
  • Subsidiary. Jolly track — read CVC words with short vowels (blending; JP Book 2, p. 7); tricky words there, once; self-assessment (My progress traffic light, AB p. 23); listening for detail (PB p. 26 ex. 1).
  • Personal. Run the flagship quiz by PIES: every child writes/shows every answer (Showdown), the responder is picked by Numbered Heads — no “captains answer everything”; keep the reward moment short so language time wins.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name all 10 toys and answer “What is it?” without support;
  • ask & answer “Is it a…?” with full short answers;
  • say “My favourite toy is a… It's (old and brown)”;
  • blend and read simple CVC words (bed, six, jam, sock, duck);
  • self-assess with the traffic light — ready for the Unit 2 test.

Form
Short answers keep is / isn't; adjective BEFORE noun in riddles: a big red robot.
Pron. watch
keep -n't in isn't; linking “It's a” /ˈɪtsə/; clear final /t/ in it.
Phonics
Jolly focus: reading CVC words — short vowels (JP Book 2, p. 7: bed, six, jam, sock, duck, black). Tricky words: there, once.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold a doll behind the box: Is it a robot? → No → “No, it isn't.” — full answer, not just “no”.
Quiz rule ICQ: Who writes the answer — one child or EVERYBODY? → everybody, on their own board!

Assumptions: all Unit 2 language met in L1–L5; CVC blending started in L5 (short-vowel discrimination) — today we read whole words. Teams of 4 with numbered members (1–4) are routine. The flagship quiz with reward runs only on Review day — children know it's special.

▶ Change! (Mido №44 — everyone moves, anyone can be asked). Chairs in a circle, numbered 1–12. Ask a question — flashcard up: What is it? / card hidden: Is it a doll? / What's your favourite toy? — then: Change! → ALL swap seats. Think-Pair first: everyone whispers the answer to their new neighbour. Then write a number on the board — that seat answers aloud; class confirms chorally: Yes! It's a train!

5–6 rounds, mix all three Unit 2 questions + one adjective riddle: It's big and purple! Is it a ball?

Support ▸ weaker pupils keep a toy picture card in hand + echo the answer with their neighbour before speaking.   Extension ▸ stronger pupils add an adjective unprompted — Yes! It's a big new train!

33–51′
🏆 FLAGSHIP QUIZ with reward
📺 teams · projector

Set-up (2 min). 3 teams of 4, numbered heads 1–4, one mini-whiteboard EACH. Quiz grid on screen — categories: Toys 🧸 · Is it a…? ❓ · Adjectives ✨ · Sounds 🔤. Rules + ICQ: Who writes? — Everybody! When do you show? — On “Showdown!”

Round flow (Kagan Showdown + Numbered Heads — all answer every question). Question up (e.g. picture half-hidden: Is it a scooter? · riddle: It's old and brown! · sound: Write the vowel — six! · read a CVC word). Every child writes/draws → Showdown! → all boards up → call a number: Number 3! — that child of the playing team answers aloud in a full sentence.

Predict (thinking). On a half-hidden toy, before revealing: Look — just a wheel! Is it a car or a bike? Write your guess! Then reveal — Were you right? Turns each round into a little guess-and-check.

Reward shot. Correct = the answering child takes the shot: animated bowling / football on screen — knocked pins = team points, class counts them in English: One, two, three… five! (No projector → soft-toy pins + ball, ≈ Mido №91 Bowling for Animal.) Rotate teams; over ~10–12 questions every child throws at least once.

Support ▸ a toy / word bank stays visible on a side card so weaker writers copy, not freeze; accept one correct word from them.   Extension ▸ when a strong pupil is “Number”, require the full sentence — No, it isn't. It's a big red robot. — not just the word.
🔨 Flagship interactive · projector · BUILD · “Unit 2 Team Quiz + Reward”  🔨 BUILD
Screen 1 — Grid: 4×3 board of numbered tiles under the 4 category headers (Toys 🧸 / Is it a…? ❓ / Adjectives ✨ / Sounds 🔤). Tap a tile → it flips to the question (half-hidden toy / picture / riddle / audio word / CVC word). Used tiles grey out.
Screen 2 — Answer: big “Show your boards!” + a 5-second timer bar; tap ✔ / ✗ to score; a full-sentence model appears for choral echo.
Screen 3 — Reward mini-game: bowling — child “rolls”, pins fall, animated strike (or a football penalty reskin). One shot = +points.
Screen 4 — Scoreboard: 3 team bars, huge numbers readable from the back row, plus a “Teamwork ⭐” toggle for best cooperation.
Mechanic: pick tile → question → everyone writes → Showdown → Numbered Head answers → score → reward shot → rotate team. ~10–12 tiles ≈ 15 min.
Target language: all Unit 2 — What is it? — It's a (robot). / Is it a…? — Yes, it is. / No, it isn't. / My favourite toy is a… It's (old). + read a CVC word.
Teacher: host only — YOU tap tiles & call the number so no captain dominates. Spec is a self-contained HTML slide (S&S purple #6C3FC5 / honey #F5C842). Offline reserve: printed grid + soft-toy pins + ball works identically. Flagship quiz runs only on Review day.

Finish with totals + teamwork star: Give me five, champions!
