🖐 Give Me Five! 2 · Starter “Let's go back to school!” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary
A1 young learners (~8–9 y.o., early readers · continuing line) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Talk Partners · carpet + desks
Week 03.09–06.09 · Lesson 1✨ FIRST LESSON of the year~10 pupils · pairs + 2 teamsPupil's Book p.4 · AB p.2
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to retrieve and use Level 1 vocabulary (animals, toys, classroom objects, places, clothes, food, shapes, numbers 1–20) and ask & answer “What's his/her name? — His/Her name is (Jake / Molly).”
  • Subsidiary. Re-establish class routines & signals after the summer; sing “We've got our school bags” (CD1 1); meet the course characters (Jake, Molly, Beth & Toby the dog); early literacy — read & write familiar words (AB follow-up).
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; give an ICQ before every task; re-train the signals patiently — this lesson sets the tone for the whole year.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name Level 1 words in 8 categories (3+ per category);
  • ask & answer “What's his name? — His name is Jake.”;
  • sing the school-bags song with actions.

Structure
What's his/her name? — His/Her name is (Jake / Molly).  ·  recycle: What's this? — It's a (…) · I've got (a pencil).
Form
What + 's (is) + his/her + name? → His/Her name + 's + [name]. Contrast his (👦) / her (👧) with a gesture each.
Pron. watch
his /hɪz/ vs her /hɜː/; name /neɪm/ (long ай); numbers 13–20 stress on the last syllable (thirteen).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“His — for a boy or a girl?” → a boy. “Her?” → a girl.
“Do I ask about MY name or ANOTHER person's name?” → another person's.

Assumptions: Pupils finished GMF1 in May: greetings, colours, numbers 1–10, toys, animals, food, clothes are passive after the summer — expect slow recall, plan extra wait-time. They can read & write simple familiar words (early readers). Carpet & team routines need re-training after the break.

11–20′
Retrieval · Level 1 words
carpet, standing

Category mingle. ▶ Mix–Pair–Share: music → Stand up, hand up, pair up!

R1: Tell your partner three ANIMALS. R2: Three TOYS! R3: Three FOOD words! R4 RallyRobin (take turns): Count together — one, two, three… to twenty!

Whole-class harvest. Board split into 8 category columns; teams call words, you write 2–3 per column — the “word wall” stays up all lesson.

✔ Assess: listen as you circulate — which categories are weakest? Give those extra recycling in the next stages.

 music · board “word wall” · ▶ Mix-Pair-Share + RallyRobin
20–28′
PB p.4 · Find & say + characters
desks

Open your books at page 4. Look — a new school year! Find and say (act. 1). Find… a cat! Point! All point, then chorus: It's a cat! 6–8 items across categories, pairs check each other.

Meet the characters. Point to the kids on the page: Who's this? His name is Jake. Her name is Molly. And this is Beth. And the dog? His name is Toby! Choral echo each name.

Act out (act. 3). Listen & repeat the dialogue: What's his name? — His name is Jake. What's her name? — Her name is Molly. Boys ask, girls answer — swap.

Song · We've got our school bags
carpet, standing

Stand up — let's sing! Sing «We've got our school bags» with actions (pack the bag · swing it on · march). Second run: half the class sings, half mimes — swap.

Freeze between verses to keep control: And… freeze!

34–43′
Team word race
desks, 2 teams

Showdown — spelling from memory (readers!). Each pupil has a mini-whiteboard. You flash a picture card: Write the word… show me! All write at once, all reveal together. Team point per correct board. 8 rounds across categories.

ICQ: Do you shout the word or WRITE it? (→ write, then show)

💪 Support: word's first letter given on the board. ⭐ Extension: add the article or a colour — a red pen.

 mini-whiteboards · picture cards · ▶ Showdown
43–52′
Ask & answer carousel
carpet, pairs

▶ Quiz-Quiz-Trade: each child gets a character/picture card (Jake, Molly, Beth, Toby + famous cartoon characters). A asks: What's his name? B answers: His name is [Sponge Bob]! — then B asks about their card, they TRADE cards and find a new partner.

Every child asks and answers many times — no spectators. Reset if loud: Freeze!

✔ Assess: stand in the flow — can they choose his/her correctly without prompting? Note names for tomorrow's recycling.
