🎄 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Christmas · Lesson 1 — Genki Christmas
Genki preschoolers (~3.5–5 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Christmas block · Lesson 1 of 7✨ Christmas words + Merry Christmas!12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to name the Christmas things — snowman · Santa Claus · reindeer · Christmas tree · Christmas stocking · candy cane · presents · Christmas card — inside the question What's this? — It's a snowman! and greet with Merry Christmas!
  • Subsidiary. Remember-time recycle of all earlier topics (greetings · feelings · numbers · family). New vocab lives ONLY in context, never as a word list. Folder worksheet: listen & tick.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; model every line first; every child mimes/says every round; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘; keep the festive magic high.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say Merry Christmas! with a wave;
  • answer What's this? — It's a snowman! (8 items);
  • mime & name a Christmas thing on Ho, ho, ho!;
  • join the Genki Christmas song with gestures;
  • tick the picture they hear.

Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: “When I say Ho ho ho — mime AND say the word?” → yes.
ICQ: (worksheet) “I say a word — you TICK one picture or all?” → one.

Assumptions: children had the whole autumn block (greetings→family). Christmas is a fresh, high-magic topic — most is new but very picturable. Near-zero English + L1 prompt. Non-readers. Group = 12.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Long words (Christmas stocking / candy cane)Chunk with the gesture; say it WITH the mime; the chant carries it, not accuracy.
Over-excitement (Christmas = big feelings)🧘 settler + volume-control arm after each ⚡; Freeze! = statue.
Only a few shout, others watchEveryone mimes/says at once; cards go to EVERY child in games; ICQ first.
They mime but stay silentSay the word WITH the mime every time; Speaky whispers the word first.
Transitions get chaoticTrain: Come with me! — one signal, one destination.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…” with fingers shown.
“Come with me!” 🚌 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana's real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🟢 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Cumulative Q&A recycle lives at the start (Remember time); the final game is on today's Christmas language.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello song (chorus), “choose the greeting → jump/fly to your chair”.
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives from the bus-box & shakes hands with each child (Merry Christmas! How are you?).
  • Remember time — cumulative review with A4 flashcards / what's missing? / app on the board.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, topic song, board app, active Genki games.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheet, a different type each lesson (this lesson: listen & tick), always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🟢 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Final game ⚡ — back to the carpet for a fast, fun game on today's language, so the lesson ends on a high note.
  • Closing — Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
