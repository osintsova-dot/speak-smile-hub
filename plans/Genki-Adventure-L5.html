🦝 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Adventure · Lesson 5 — Pronouns: I, you, he, she, we
Genki preschoolers (~3.5–5 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Adventure level · Lesson 5 of 7✨ pronouns + am/is/are happy12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to match a pronoun to who they point at and say the be chunk — I am happy · You are happy · He/She is happy · We are happy · Everybody is happy!
  • Subsidiary. Remember-time recycle of L4 (directions) & L3 (superhero); folder worksheet (listen, point & say); the S&S group-photo collage; recycle L2 feelings inside the frame.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; embody the grammar with grouping (I = alone, we = pair, everybody = all); every child points/speaks every round; don’t correct am/are/is at 5.
By the end, pupils can…
  • point to me / you / us / everybody on cue;
  • say I am happy / We are happy!;
  • chant Everybody is happy! as a group;
  • recognise he / she by pointing;
  • stick & tell We are happy!

Chunks
I am happy. · You are happy. · He/She is happy. · We are happy! · Everybody is happy!  ·  (recycle) I’m happy / great.
TPR gesture
I point self · you point partner · we arms round two · he/she point aside · everybody big arms round all.
Pron. watch
the tiny swap am / are / is — model, don’t drill; I’m / we’re as one word; everybody a joyful 4-beat clap: EV-ry-bo-dy.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: (grouping) “We — alone or with a friend?” → with a friend.
ICQ: (point) “Point AND say it?” → yes.

Assumptions: children had L1–L4 (feelings solid). Pronouns/be are abstract at 5 — taught physically (who you point at) with the L1 prompt, NOT as grammar. Don’t correct am/are/is. Non-readers. Group = 12.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Pronouns feel abstractNever explain — show with the body: I = me, we = us, everybody = the circle. The pointing IS the meaning.
They mix am/are/isDon’t correct at 5 — the song fixes it. Praise the pronoun + feeling; model the chunk back warmly.
Grouping game → clumpingPractise “find one friend” calmly first; Freeze! between calls; bubble-arms.
Shy child won’t pair upSpeaky or teacher is the instant partner; the gesture counts more than speaking.
Transitions / over-excitement🧘 settler after each ⚡; train: Come with me!
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 👋 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…” with fingers shown.
“Come with me!” 🚊 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana’s real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🌱 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Finale = cumulative Q&A chain + the Thank You! song ritual.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello song (chorus), “choose the greeting → jump/run/swim/fly to your chair”.
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives & shakes hands with each child (Hello! How are you?).
  • Remember time — cumulative review with A4 flashcards / memory / bridge game / app on the board.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, topic song, board app, one active game.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheets, a different type each lesson (listen & tick / colour by command / trace / match соедини / draw & tell / put-in-order / mini-craft), always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🌱 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Remember time 2 + Closing — run the growing Q&A chain, Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
