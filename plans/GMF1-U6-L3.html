🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 6 “A day in the park” · Lesson 3 — Story: Hide and seek
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Story: listen → read → act out (drama) · carpet + desks
Week 08.02 · Lesson 43✨ STORYпродолжающие · ~12 pupils · small groups for drama🦸 Phonics Hero ур.30a · /ё:/ er·ir·ur
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow and understand the story “Hide and seek” (Pupil's Book pp. 62–63, CD3 6) and act it out using key story language: “Where's Toby? — He's on the slide.”, “Where are Jake and Molly? — They're behind the tree.”, “He isn't under the bush / in the long grass.”, “You can't play in here.”, “You can have a ride on the magic bike.”
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — Where's…? / Where are…? + He's / She's / They're and prepositions on · behind · under (U6 L1–L2) plus can / can't (U5) — woven straight into the story work (the children can't find Toby, they hunt for him in / on / under park places). Phonics — Phonics Hero ур.30 (a) первое знакомство, kept light: sound /ё:/ in three houses er · ir · ur (her, bird, turn; trace girl, turn) + tricky words of · also. Values (PB p. 63 ex. 2): respect the signs around you (No dogs in the playground).
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (gesture responses, prediction); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a small group at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 30-sec showcase.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (order the frames, answer with gestures);
  • say the key lines in role: “Where's Toby? — He's on the slide!”, “They're behind the tree!”;
  • read their first /ё:/ house-words (her · bird · turn) and trace girl · turn; read of · also by sight;
  • explain the value: respect the signs — “No dogs in the playground.”

Structure
Where's (Toby)? — He's on the slide. · Where are (Jake and Molly)? — They're behind the tree. · He isn't under the bush / in the long grass. · You can't play in here. · You can have a ride on the magic bike.
Form
Where 's (is) + name? → He / She 's + prep + the + noun. · Where are + names? → They're + prep + the + noun. · can / can't + verb (receptive here, recycled from U5).
Pron. watch
connected Where's /weəz/, Where are /weər ə/; weak on the /ɒn ðə/, behind the /bɪˈhaɪnd ðə/; sentence stress: He's on the SLIDE.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.30 (a) первое знакомство: sound /ё:/ — three houses er (her, sister) · ir (bird, girl) · ur (turn, purple); trace girl · turn (тетрадь стр.83), light — story lesson · tricky words: of · also.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Frame 1: “Can Toby play in the playground?” → “No, he can't!” “Why?” → “The sign! No dogs!” (values — respect the signs).
Frames 3–4: “Where are Jake and Molly?” → “They're behind the tree!” → “Got you!”
Frames 5–7: “Oh no — where's Toby? Is he under the bush?” → “No!” “In the long grass?” → “No!” “Where is he?” → “He's on the slide!”

Assumptions: park objects + Where's…? / Where are…? and on / behind / under known from U6 L1–L2; can / can't known from U5 (Fun on the farm); children know Toby the dog & the magic bike from earlier units. Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures while listening; the teacher points, children don't read the speech bubbles alone. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Review → into the story
carpet, circle

Recycle U6 L1–L2 + U5, woven into today. Speaky hides a toy dog in the room. Class asks (clap-clap-ask): Where's Toby? — a child finds it → He's under the chair! / He's on the shelf! / He's behind the box! Do 3–4 places, moving the dog on / behind / under as you go.

Show-me — everyone reacts. Where's = one finger up · Where are = two fingers up. Call fast: Where's Toby? — one finger! Where are Jake and Molly? — two fingers! Then recycle can/can't (U5): Can a dog run? — Yes, he can! Can a dog fly? — No, he can't! (thumbs).

Bridge. Some children play hide and seek in the park… and Toby the dog wants to play too! But look — a sign. Can Toby play? Let's find out! Signal: Class? — Yes!

Speed reading — тетрадь стр.83 №1 (quick). Last lesson's /ау/·/ой/ words: out · cow · coin · boy · town · toy — There and back — fast! (Light — this is the fullest lesson of the unit.)

Meet the three houses of /ё:/ (стр.83 №2). 🦝 Speaky: One lazy sound /ё:/ — three outfits! 🏠 er — her, sister · 🏠 ir — bird, girl · 🏠 ur — turn, purple. Lazy-mouth gesture (no rolled r — just /ɜː/); all copy each word ×2, one gesture per house.

Read — thumbs check (all react). Flash the house words; class blends chorally, then thumbs up = real word, thumbs down = silly word (mix in zirl · burd-nonsense). Every child shows a thumb.

Write it — стр.83 №3 «Пишем» (desks, ~2′). Trace and write: girl · turn — whisper each as you write. Then tricky words by sight: of · also — flash, chorus, air-write.

📺 Story — listen & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 62). Big story picture on screen / books open. What can you see in the playground? — pairs point & name 15 sec, then chorus a few. Pre-teach with gesture: hide and seek (cover eyes, count “8, 9, 10!”) · magic bike (sparkle fingers) · the No dogs sign (cross arms “Toby, no!”).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “What's in the playground?” ✅ ГОТОВ · 🎮 find-it-u6
Screen: the GMF1 “Find it!” engine on the big story picture. · How: Speaky asks, a child taps the object; it lights up. · Pupils: whole class points to their book / calls it — A swing! A slide! A tree! A bush! · Teacher: 🦝 Where's the slide? Point! — 4–5 objects, quick. · Offline reserve: point to your open Pupil's Book and play the same “Point to the…!” with the whole class.

Predict (thinking) — before we listen. Toby the dog wants to play hide and seek. But there's a sign… Can Toby play in the playground? Thumbs guess: 👍 yes vs. 👎 no. And later — Toby gets LOST! Where will they find him? Under a bush? On the slide? Let's listen and check your guess!

Listen 1 — global (CD3 6). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: What game do they play? Show me! (cover eyes = hide and seek) Was your guess right — can Toby play? (👎 — the sign!)

Detail check — True or False? (thumbs up / down, all react). Say a sentence, class shows a thumb: The children play football. (👎 — hide and seek!) · Jake and Molly are behind the tree. (👍) · Toby can play in the playground. (👎 — No dogs!) · Toby is on the slide. (👍). Re-point the frame to fix any wrong thumb.

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the story gestures at the right moments: count “8, 9, 10!” · cover eyes (hide) · “No dogs!” cross arms · “Got you!” grab · “Oh no!” hands on cheeks (Toby lost) · point under the bush / in the long grass / on the slide · magic-bike ride (pedal). CCQs between frames (see box above). At frames 3 & 7 stop: Where are Jake and Molly? — Behind the tree! Where's Toby? — On the slide!

▶ Build the story

▶ Story Builders (Mido №54 “Reconstruction”, picture re-skin for non-readers — all pairs work at once). Each pair gets 8 mini frame-cards of “Hide and seek”, shuffled.

Order it. Put the story in order — one card each, take turns! (RallyRobin with cards: A places one, B places the next.) Countdown 2 min. ICQ: Do we grab all the cards or take turns? (→ take turns!)

Check with the audio. Replay CD3 6: pairs touch each card as they hear it — self-check, fix the order. Then choral retell, teacher holds up big frames one by one, class says the line: Let's play hide and seek! · Sorry, Toby. You can't play in here. · 8, 9, 10! 42–50′
Act out — drama
carpet, groups

Roles. Groups of 3–4: Beth (red badge, “Let's play hide and seek!”) · Jake & Molly (green + blue, the hiders) · Toby the dog (yellow, “Woof! Ring! Ring!”). Model once with a strong group.

All groups act at the same time. Narrate the story slowly, frame by frame; every group plays it in their own spot (hiding is MIMED on the spot!): Let's play hide and seek! Sorry, Toby, you can't play in here — look, a sign! 8, 9, 10! Ready or not, here I come! Where are Jake and Molly? — They're behind the tree! Got you! … Oh no! Where's Toby? He isn't under the bush. He isn't in the long grass. Magic Bike, can you help? Now I can see him — he's on the slide! You can't go in the playground, Toby, but you can have a ride on the magic bike. Let's go!

Create (thinking) — make it yours. Before round 2: Toby hides in a NEW place — you choose! Where is he? Each group adds a line with their own place: Where's Toby? — He's behind the rock! / He's next to the bush! (support: point to a picture card; extension: invent the whole exchange with a preposition of their choice).

Swap roles, run it again — keep it to 2 rounds (don't let the drama over-run; Freeze! the moment energy tips). Volunteers: ONE group shows 30 sec for the class — big applause. Values (PB p. 63 ex. 2): Look at the sign — No dogs! We respect the signs around us. Can Toby go in? → No!

Support: quieter children get the “hider” role (short lines + gesture); a line card in hand. Extension: confident children play Beth / the seeker and lead the “Where are…? — They're…” exchange.
