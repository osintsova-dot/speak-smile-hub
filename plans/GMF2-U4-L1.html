Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 nature-trail features and say what they can do there: “I can (swim) in the (lake). / I can (climb) the (hill).”
  • Subsidiary. Sing the nature-trail song (CD2 17) + stickers chant (CD2 19); retrieve “I can + verb” (GMF1) and zoo animals (U3 — who lives in the forest?), woven onto nature; imaginary trail walk (guided TPR).
  • Personal. This is a movement unit — build the trail journey ritual today (walk-climb-swim-jump mime sequence); rein it in with Freeze discipline.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 nature features;
  • say “I can run in the grass / swim in the lake”;
  • read the nature words and match to pictures.

Form

Pron. watch
leaves long /iː/ + /vz/; path /pɑːθ/ with th; bridge /brɪdʒ/; river short /ɪ/.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Can I swim in the grass?” → no! In the LAKE. (place must match the action)
“One leaf — many…?” → leaves!

Assumptions: I can + verb is old friendly ground (GMF1) — the news is the nature places. Winter outside, summer trail in the book — enjoy the contrast. Readers manage word cards. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Presentation · 10 nature words
carpet → board

Flashcards + trail map. Build a “trail” across the board left→right as you present: What's this? It's a… forest! Choral ×3 + gesture (trees-arms, hill-climb, river-swim…). Contrast pair road/river held together.

Walk the trail (guided TPR). Narrate, all mime on the spot: We walk in the forest… we climb the hill… we swim in the lake… we jump in the leaves! And… freeze!

▶ Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if you can SWIM there… lake! …road! — absurd ones get the big NO laugh.

💪 Support: gesture + word. ⭐ Extension: chain two places: I can walk in the forest and jump in the leaves.
