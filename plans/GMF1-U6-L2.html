🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 6 “A day in the park” · Lesson 2 — Grammar: Where's (Beth)? He's / She's on the swing.
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Dialogue → controlled drill → freer → production · carpet + desks
Week 08.02 · Lesson 42✨ GRAMMARпродолжающая · ~12 pupils · pairs + 3 teams🦸 Phonics Hero ур.29b · /ау/·/ой/
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to ask where a person is and say where he or she is, choosing he / she correctly: “Where's (Beth)? — She's on the swing.” / “Where's (Jake)? — He's on the roundabout.” (Pupil's Book p. 61 dialogue & Grammar box), and act out the “lost sister / lost brother” dialogue.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — U6 L1 playground objects (slide, swing, roundabout, seesaw, climbing frame) + U1 “Where's my…? — It's here” and the prepositions in / on / under — woven into today's question (“Where's the girl? — She's on the slide!”); meet 5 nature words for the matching task (grass, flower, tree, bush, rock); phonics — Phonics Hero ур.29 (b) закрепление: /ау/ ou·ow + /ой/ oi·oy (word grid → sort → dictation → stories «Корова и цветок» и «Мальчик и игрушка»); listen for detail (PB p. 61 ex. 2 “Listen and match”); gently preview behind / next to / in front of for Lesson 4.
  • Personal. Insist on the FULL question (“Where's Beth?”, not “Beth?”); make pupils choose he or she and keep on the in the answer (“He's on the roundabout”, not “roundabout”); every task keeps ALL children responding (own cards / point & say / all pairs at once) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • ask “Where's (Beth)?” about a person in the park;
  • answer with He's / She's on the (swing), choosing he or she, and act out the lost-sister / lost-brother dialogue;
  • read, sort & write /ау/–/ой/ house-words (out, cow, coin, boy) and read the story «Корова и цветок».
Target language
Lexis

Structure
Where's (Beth)? — She's on the swing.  ·  Where's (Jake)? — He's on the roundabout.  ·  I'm lost!  ·  Look! Come on. Let's play, too.  ·  preview: They're (behind / next to) the (tree).
Form
Where's = Where is; short answer He's (= He is, a boy) / She's (= She is, a girl) + on the + place. Rising tone on the question ↗, falling on the answer ↘. Choice point: boy → he, girl → she.
Pron. watch
He's /hiːz/ vs She's /ʃiːz/ — hear the difference; keep on the (not bare “swing”); linking “She's on the” /ʃiːzɒnðə/; don't let the question shrink to one word.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.29 (b) закрепление: word grid cloud, mouth, cow, owl, oil, coin, boy, toy · sort ou/ow/oi/oy · dictation out, cow, town, coin, boy, toy · stories «Корова и цветок» + «Мальчик и игрушка» (тетрадь стр.80–82) · tricky words: they, eight.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Point to a boy card: “Is it a boy or a girl?” → boy → “So we say HE. Where's Jake? — He's on the slide.”
Point to a girl card: “Boy or girl?” → girl → “So we say SHE. Where's Beth? — She's on the swing.”
Put Speaky on the swing: “Is he on the swing or under it?” → on → “He's on the swing.”
⭐ Option — ow CCQ: show snow and cow: “Same letters ow — same sound?” → NO! /оу/ snow, /ау/ cow — “read it, then check the meaning!”

Assumptions: the 5+ playground objects and the song It's fun in the park were met in L1; “Where's my…? — It's here” and in / on / under come from Unit 1; the /ау/·/ой/ houses (ou·ow·oi·oy) were met in L1 (ур.29a, стр.79) — today consolidates. Pupils are non-/emergent readers → picture grids, models and audio, not printed text. Carpet routines & pair-work signals known. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Mixes he / she (“Where's Beth? — He's on the swing”)Anchor gestures: point to hair / dress for SHE, cap for HE; hold a boy card & a girl card and drill in fast pairs — girl → she, boy → he; correct on the spot, class echoes.
One-word question: “Beth?” instead of “Where's Beth?”Hands-behind-back rule: no answer from Speaky until the FULL question; back-chain …Beth? → where's Beth? → Where's Beth?
Drops on the: answers just “swing”Point-and-say drill: finger on the place card + She's on the swing! — the answer always has on the; model with a big pointing gesture.
oi/oy houses mixed in writing (“boi”)Anchor rule: “oy likes the END of the word — boy, toy!”; house cards stay on the rail, point before writing.
Put it! / pair game: child places the figure but stays silentRule: “Move it → SAY it.” No turn counts until He's on the slide! is said; partner is the checker (RallyCoach); teacher gestures “speak” before praising.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.
Procedure — staging

⏱ Pacing — honest 60 min. This is a full plate, so pick, don't cram. The core = dialogue model (24–33′) + the Put it! production game + Listen & match. Recall the two sounds (gestures). Forest call: ow! (ау!) · surprised sailor: oy! (ой!) — all copy ×2. Word grid — тетрадь стр.80 №1: read together with pictures — cloud · mouth · cow · owl · oil · coin · boy · toy. Kagan — RallyRobin: pairs take turns down the grid.

Sort — стр.80 №2 «Раздели слова» (all vote first). Four houses on the rail: ou · ow · oi · oy. Words out · cow · oil · boy · cloud · town · coin · toy… → every child points to the right house → check → write it in the column. 6 words in class, the rest go home.

Dictation — стр.80 №3 (everyone writes). Now YOU write. Listen: out… word once → segment on fingers → all write → Show me! → reveal & self-check. Words: out, cow, coin, boy (+ town, toy if quick). Finish with tricky words by sight: they · eight — flash, chorus, air-write.

Stories — стр.81 «Корова и цветок» (settler). Read the 5 frames like a comic, chorus each line: A brown cow. The cow eats grass… ⭐ Star task: count the flowers (3). Colour the star «Я молодец!» (стр.80). («Мальчик и игрушка» стр.82 — tonight's home read-aloud.)

Presentation · Dialogue

Model with Speaky. 🦝 Speaky looks around, lost. Teacher voices him: I'm lost! Where's my big sister? — point to the girl on the swing card — She's on the swing. Look! — And where's my big brother? — He's on the roundabout. Come on. Let's play, too!

Listen & repeat — PB p. 61 ex. 1 (CD3 4). Books open / picture on screen: the park scene. Play the dialogue; children repeat line by line. Drill: rising tone on Where's my sister? ↗, point-and-say She's on the swing! ↘ Back-chain the question. Point at the Grammar box: Where's my sister? She's on the swing. Where's my brother? He's on the roundabout. CCQ: Sister — he or she? (→ SHE!) Brother — he or she? (→ HE!)

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Where's …? Spinner ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the “What's this? spinner” engine) · 🎮 whats-this-spinner-u6
Screen: a wheel of park places (swing · slide · roundabout · seesaw · climbing frame) with a boy 👦 or girl 👧 sprite. Mechanic: tap to spin → it lands on a place; tap → the child pops onto it (reveal + sound). Pupils: BEFORE the reveal the class asks together Where's (Beth)? ↗ → on reveal She's on the swing! ↘ (choose he / she from the sprite). Teacher: spin, hold the reveal until you hear the FULL question AND the right he / she. 🧠 Predict: Which place? Point & guess! — children point before it stops.

Act out (ALL pairs at once). Pairs on the carpet; give each pair a boy card + a girl card + 2 place cards. A: Where's (Jake)? B points: He's on the slide! Swap names / places, change roles, repeat. ICQ: Boy — he or she? (→ he!)

◆ Support: let the weaker child be B (points + He's/She's on the…) first, then swap. ▲ Extension: A adds the second line — And where's (Beth)? — She's on the swing, too!

33–41′
▶ Put it! Where's he / she?
carpet → pairs
🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Put it! / Where's he hiding? 🔨 СОБРАТЬ (small drag-&-reveal engine)
Screen: a park picture (swing · slide · tree · bush · rock) + a boy 👦 and girl 👧 sprite. Mechanic: DRAG the child onto a spot — on the swing / on the slide / behind the tree / under the bush / next to the rock; the matching phrase pops up and Speaky's voice says it. Pupils: whole class asks Where's he? → after the drop, all answer He's on the slide! Teacher: use this as the MODEL, then hand out the paper mats. To build: 1 park background + boy & girl sprites, 5 snap-spots, drag-to-place, matching phrase + Speaky voice line; reuse the spinner's reveal sound.

▶ Put it! — “Where's he / she?” (controlled → freer pair drill — every pair plays at once, nobody waits). Give each pair a mini park mat (swing, slide, tree, bush, rock drawn on it) + a boy figure + a girl figure. Kagan — RallyCoach: partner A places a figure on a spot and hides it under a hand; partner B asks Where's (Beth)? A reveals: She's on the swing! B is the checker — praise the right he / she. Swap roles each turn.

Teacher monitors, feeds fast finishers a preposition upgrade: She's BEHIND the tree! He's NEXT TO the rock! Reset if loud: Freeze! Run one round; add a 2nd round only if time allows (buffer — don't rob the Listen & match for it).

🧠 Create — your own hider: children choose a spot themselves and invent the sentence — Where's she? — She's under the bush! Their choice, their sentence.

◆ Support: give a picture-word strip (on / behind / under) so B always has the phrase. ▲ Extension: use TWO figures — Where are they? — They're behind the tree! (primes L4). ⭐ Offline reserve (no board): big park poster on the wall + boy/girl magnets — you hide, the class asks & answers together.
