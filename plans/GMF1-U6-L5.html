🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 6 “A day in the park” · Lesson 5 — Phonics · Culture · Literacy: How to Grow Flowers
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Phonics + CLIL culture + guided literacy · carpet + desks
Week 15.02–21.02 · Lesson 45L5 Phon+Cult+Lit~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4🦸 Phonics Hero ур.31a · c·g·ph + air·ear·are
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the /w/ sound (Toby's tongue twister “w–w–w. Wally the worm washes windows”), understand what British children do in the park (play cricket on the grass, feed the ducks on the pond, ride on the train, the playground zip wire) and read a set of instructions and make their own “grow flowers” instruction strip (Push the seeds… / Water the seeds… / Put the pot in a sunny place…).
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning inside the topic — Unit 6 playground & nature words + “Where's…? / Where are…? — He's/She's/They're + prepositions of place” (L1–L4) reused to hide & find in the park; Phonics Hero ур.31 (a) первое знакомство — хитрые буквы: soft c /с/ (face), soft g /дж/ (giant), ph /ф/ (phone); trace face, phone (тетрадь стр.86); tricky words father · after + recycle they · their.
  • Personal. Keep the writing stage tight (timer + only 3 steps to copy); model writing word-by-word on the board; every child responds in every task (own strip / show-me “w-w-w” / thumbs vote) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tongue twister with a clear English /w/ and spot words that begin with /w/;
  • name 2–3 things British children do in the park;
  • read simple instructions and make their own “grow flowers” instruction strip (order 3 steps + draw);
  • read the хитрые буквы: soft c (face), soft g (giant), ph (phone) — and trace face · phone.

Structure
w–w–w. Wally the worm washes windows.  ·  Where's/Where are (Beth)? — He's/She's/They're (behind/next to) the (tree).  ·  In the park we (play cricket / feed the ducks / ride on the train).  ·  Instructions: Push the seeds into the soil. Water the seeds. Put the pot in a sunny place.
Form
Initial /w/ + word. Instructions = imperatives (Verb + …!) in sequence (first → then → next). Fixed chunks, copied & ordered, not built.
Pron. watch
English /w/ — round the lips and blow gently, a soft “wuh”, not the Russian «в» (no teeth on the lip). Keep it rounded in Wally, worm, washes, windows, water.
Phonics
Coursebook sound: /w/ (PB p. 65, CD3 11–12) · Phonics Hero ур.31 (a) первое знакомство: хитрые буквы — before e·i·y, c reads /с/ (face, city) and g reads /дж/ (giant, gym); ph = /ф/ (phone, dolphin); trace face · phone (тетрадь стр.86). Tricky words: father · after; recycle they · their.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold the “My garden” page: Is this a story or instructions? → Instructions! Step 1 — do we put the soil or the water first? → Soil!
Worm sound — teeth on the lip like «в», or round lips and blow? → round lips, blow w-w-w (English /w/), no teeth.

Assumptions: playground & nature words + “Where's…?” + prepositions known from L1–L4; children can copy words but not write freely → all writing is trace/copy from a board frame. Emergent readers: the instructions are read together, finger-following. The park culture (cricket, feeding ducks, park train, zip wire) is new — model & mime first. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Phonics — /w/ + sound review
at the board

Recognise /w/ (~2½′). Toby's tongue twister (PB p. 65, CD3 11): w – w – w. Wally the worm washes windows! Round-lips “blow” gesture; soft wuh, no teeth on the lip — not «в». Choral ×3: slow → normal → fast. 2–3 brave solos with the “microphone”. Support: hold up the /w/ picture-anchor card (a window / a worm) as the sound cue — weak pupils watch it, not the letter.

“w or not w?” — whole-class show-me (~2′, all react). Pictures one by one — walk · water · goat · Wednesday · week · wall · weather · ruler (AB p. 62 set). Begins with /w/ → everyone stands and blows w-w-w; not /w/ → sit, hands on knees. Show me! Fast, nobody just watches.

Хитрые буквы — тетрадь стр.86 №2 «Домики» (~3′). 🦝 Speaky's spy secret: Before e, i, y the letter c goes soft — /с/: face! And g goes soft — /дж/: giant! And a new pair: ph = /ф/ — phone! Three houses on the rail: 🏠 c — face, ice, city · 🏠 g — giant, gym, orange · 🏠 ph — phone, dolphin, photo. Read each house chorally; gesture per house (call-a-phone for ph!).

Which house? — whole-class show-me (~2′, all react). Say a word — face · phone · giant · ice · photo · orange… → every child points to the right house → chorus. Fast, nobody just watches.

Write it — стр.86 №3 «Пишем» (~2′, desks). Trace and write: face · phone — whisper each as you write. Support: trace the big model letters with a finger first. Extension: write city on the spare line. (The air·ear·are houses, sort & dictation of ур.31 come next lesson — today is first meeting only.)

Literacy — how to grow flowers
desks

Before you read (PB p. 67). What do you need to grow flowers? Look and point! — circle the right ones: soil… seeds… water… sun (not grass / a book).

Listen & read the instructions (CD3 16) — fingers follow. Read “My garden” together — Instructions text type (a ladybird helps!): 1 Put the soil in a pot. 2 Push the seeds into the soil with your finger. 3 Water the seeds. 4 Put the pot in a sunny place. 5 Put the plants in the garden. CCQ: Step 3 — do we water or eat the seeds? — Water!

Make YOUR instruction strip (literacy product). On a fold strip with 3 boxes, each child orders and copies 3 key steps from the board frame (written LARGE, line by line) and draws each one — like the book: Push the seeds → Water the seeds → Put the pot in a sunny place. Support: picture cut-outs to lay in order + dotted-line words to trace + teacher scribes. Extension: copy all 5 steps, or add step numbers 1–5. Timer 8 min, countdown finish: 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down!

Create — your own /w/ thing (fast finishers, links to phonics). On the back of the strip: Draw something that starts with w-w-w — and tell me its name! (window · worm · water · wall…). They show & say it at the door — a mini creative check of the sound.
