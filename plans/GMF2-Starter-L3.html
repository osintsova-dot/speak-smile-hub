Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the alphabet a–z, spell familiar words (“How do you spell English? — E-N-G-L-I-S-H”) and follow & act out the story «The storm» using weather language: “It's (cloudy) today. / Is it (raining)? — Yes, it is.”
  • Subsidiary. Sing the alphabet song (CD1 8); revise weather words in context; story skills — predict, listen, sequence, act out; meet the magic bike (the course story engine!).
  • Personal. Keep the alphabet snappy — letter NAMES only (no phonics drilling: this line works строго по учебнику); give every child a story role.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say/sing the alphabet a–z;
  • spell their name and 1–2 familiar words aloud;
  • act out a story scene with weather lines.

Structure
How do you spell (English)? — E-N-G-L-I-S-H.  ·  It's (cloudy) today. · Is it (raining)? — Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.
Form
Is it + -ing? → Yes, it + is / No, it + isn't. Letter NAMES for spelling (not sounds): a /eɪ/, e /iː/, i /aɪ/…
Pron. watch
letter names g /dʒiː/ vs j /dʒeɪ/; e /iː/ vs i /aɪ/ (classic traps); stormy with long /ɔː/.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Spell — do I say the WORD or the LETTERS?” → the letters.
“Is it raining? — look outside: yes or no?” → real answer!

Assumptions: Pupils know letter names patchily from GMF1; weather words met in GMF1 U8. First story of the year — the act-out routine needs a full model. Early readers: they follow the story text in the book while listening.

10–22′
Alphabet & spelling
carpet → board

Sing & point (act. 1–2, CD1 7–8). Sing twice: first all together pointing in the book, then boys/girls alternate lines.

Tricky letters. Board: g–j, e–i, a–r. Look at my mouth: g… j… Point-and-say drill — YOU point randomly, class calls the letter name. Every child answers every time.

Alphabet aerobics (stirrer). Make a T with your arms! Make an O! An X! — body letters, 60 seconds.

Spelling (act. 3, CD1 9). Model: How do you spell English? — E-N-G-L-I-S-H! ▶ Talk Partners: pairs spell from their name tags: How do you spell your name? — partner checks against the tag. Then 2 words chorally: cat, book.

💪 Support: spell with the tag in view (copy-spell). ⭐ Extension: spell a subject word from memory.

✔ Assess: listen for g/j, e/i — recast immediately; these letter names carry the whole year's spelling work.

Story · The storm (listen & read)
carpet

Predict. PB p.7, books closed, cover picture on the board: Look! What's the weather like? Where are Jake and Molly? Take 2–3 guesses (L1 OK, recast).

Listen 1 (books closed, CD1 11). Gist: Listen: WHAT do they find? → a bike! …a MAGIC bike!

Listen 2 (books open — read along). Pupils follow the text with a finger. Pause per frame: Is it raining? — Yes, it is! What's this? — It's a bike!

Comprehension. Thumbs up/down statements: It's sunny in the story… thumbs! The bike is magic… thumbs! — every child votes.

31–41′
Act out
carpet, groups of 3

Model the scene. You + 2 strong pupils play frame 3–4: weather lines + finding the bike. Big drama voice!

Groups of 3 (roles: Jake · Molly · storm/sound effects). Each group rehearses the scene with the book open (script support for readers). Rotate roles once.

Showtime. 2 groups perform (volunteers first); audience job: listen for the weather word and call it back at the end — everyone has a task.

💪 Support: sound-effects role carries no lines — perfect for the shyest child today. ⭐ Extension: add an extra line: “Let's go home!”

Weather spin & spell
carpet, pairs

▶ Weather Tic-Tac-Toe (offline, board). 3×3 grid with weather/story pictures. Two teams; to claim a cell the WHOLE team chorus-answers: Is it raining? — Yes, it is! + one player spells the word: R-A-I-N. Rotate the speller every turn — everyone spells once.

ICQ: Does one person answer or the whole team? (→ team answers, one spells)

✔ Assess: the spelling turn is your check — who still guesses letter names? They get the support card next lesson.
