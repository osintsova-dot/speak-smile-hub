Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the ow/ou tongue twister («A brown cow and a loud mouse run around the house»), compare British kids' bedrooms with theirs, and read & perform a scene from the play script «Cinderella».
  • Subsidiary. Textbook phonics: ow/ou = /aʊ/ (⚠ ow was /əʊ/ in U5 — same letters, DIFFERENT sound; flag the fun); culture — bedrooms & hobbies; literacy — text type a play script: characters, lines, stage directions.
  • Personal. Cinderella is the year's theatre peak — cast generously, keep scenes tiny; the ow-two-sounds moment is a delight, not a problem.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the twister fast ×3;
  • tell 2 facts about UK bedrooms and compare;
  • perform a play-script line in role.

Block 3 · Literacy · Play script
carpet → stage

Before you read. PB p.79: Who's this? What's the story? What does she lose?

Listen & read (CD3 39). Follow the script format: names, lines, brackets; chorus the famous line: Whose is this shoe?!

Cast & rehearse. 3 casts × tiny scenes (dress moment · the ball · the shoe). Colour-highlight lines; 3-minute rehearsal; badges on.

Showtime. Scenes in order = the whole tale; audience joins the shoe-fitting chorus: It's mine! — No, it's MINE! Bows + applause.

💪 Support: mice/clock chorus roles are wordless-friendly. ⭐ Extension: the fairy adds an invented line.

Write (AB p.76 seed). One favourite line copied + illustrated in notebooks.
