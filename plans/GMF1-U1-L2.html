🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 1 “Ready for school!” · Lesson 2 — Grammar: Where's my…? It's here.
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Dialogue → controlled drill → freer → production · carpet + desks
Week 14.09 · Lesson 5✨ GRAMMAR~12 pupils · pairs + 2 teams
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to ask where a lost school object is and say where it is: “Where's my (pencil)? — It's here.” (Pupil's Book p. 9 dialogue), and act out the “lost pencil” dialogue.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — L1 classroom objects + “What is it? — It's a…”, and Starter colours — woven into today's question (“Where's my red crayon? — It's here!”); phonics — the digraph qu /kw/ (queen, quick, quiet, quack), reviewing the 26 single sounds; listen for detail (PB p. 9 ex. 2 “Listen and find. Circle”); gently preview prepositions in / on / under for Lessons 3–4.
  • Personal. Insist on the FULL question (“Where's my pencil?”, not “Pencil?”); keep It's in the answer (“It's here”, not just “Here”); every task keeps ALL children responding (own cards / point & say / all pairs at once) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • ask “Where's my (crayon)?” about a lost school object;
  • answer “It's here.” and act out the lost-pencil dialogue;
  • hear, blend & segment words with qu /kw/ and tell it from the single sounds.

Structure
Where's my (pencil)? — It's here.  ·  And where's my (book)? — It's here, too. Look!  ·  Thank you!  ·  preview: It's (in / on / under) the (bag).
Form
Where's = Where is; Where's + my + noun? → short answer It's here (It's = It is). Rising tone on the question ↗, falling on the answer ↘.
Pron. watch
Where's /weəz/ — one word, not “where is”; keep It's (not bare “Here”); linking “Where's my” /weəzmaɪ/; don't let the question shrink to one word.
Phonics
Jolly focus: qu /kw/ — queen, quick, quiet, quack (recognise → blend → segment) · review of the 26 single sounds · tricky words: you, your (JP Book 2 p. 10).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hide a pencil behind your back: “Can you see my pencil?” → no → “So I ask: Where's my pencil?”
Speaky spots it in the bag: “Where's my pencil?” → “It's here!” (points + shows it) — not just “Here”.
qu: “Do q and u come together?” → yes, best friends → they say /kw/ — queen.
⭐ Option — box CCQ: put a pen ON a box: “Is it inside or on top?” → on top; drop it IN → “And now?” → inside. (Primes in / on / under for L3–4 — use only if the class is quick today.)

Assumptions: the 10 classroom objects, “What is it? — It's a…” and “Where's my…? — It's here” were first met in L1 & the Starter; the 26 single sounds (incl. q) are known. Pupils are non-/emergent readers → picture grids and audio, not printed text. Carpet routines & pair-work signals known.

Classroom management — signals

“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.

Phonics — qu /kw/
at the board

Recognise — meet the “best-friends” qu. Show letter q: This is q. And q ALWAYS brings a friend — u! Together they say /kw/: qu… queen, quick, quiet, quack! Action: flap arms like a duck for quack! Children copy, rounding the lips for /w/.

/kw/ or not? — whole-class show-me (all react). Every child gets two cards: 👑 (qu /kw/) and 🔤 (single sound). Say words one by one — queen · cat · quick · pen · quack · sun · quiet · dog… → Show me! — all hold up 👑 or 🔤. 8–10 words, fast; nobody just watches.

Blend (all together). Push the sounds together: /qu/ /i/ /ck/… quick! Then queen, quack, quiet — picture for each (crown · duck · finger-on-lips “shh”).

Segment · dictation (everyone writes). Now YOU write. Listen… word once → all write on mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & check together. Focus: queen, quick, quack. Finish with tricky words by sight: you · your — flash, chorus, air-write.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): qu Sorter ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the Unit 1 “Flashcards” engine)
Screen: a word + picture flips up one at a time (queen · cat · quick · pen · quack · sun · quiet · dog). Mechanic: tap the card to hear the word, then tap 👑 or 🔤 to sort it; a green tick / red shake shows if right. Pupils: hold up their own 👑/🔤 first, then a helper taps to check. Teacher: read the word, wait for hands, then reveal. Reuse: same engine as L1 Flashcards — just swap the card set.

◆ Support: in dictation, give the sound-buttons (qu-i-ck) — they only fill letters. ▲ Extension: fast writers add a 4th word by ear (quiz).

24–33′
Presentation · Dialogue
carpet → pairs

Model with Speaky. 🦝 Speaky pats his pockets, worried. Teacher: Oh no! Where's my pencil? — peek in the bag, find it — It's here! — And where's my book? — It's here, too. Look! — Oh! Thank you, Speaky!

Listen & repeat — PB p. 9 ex. 1 (CD1 13). Books open / picture on screen: the school-gate scene. Play the dialogue; children repeat line by line. Drill: rising tone on Where's my pencil? ↗, point-and-say It's here! ↘ Back-chain the question. Point at the Grammar box: Where's my pencil? It's here. — this is our question of the day.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Where's my…? Spinner ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the “What's this? spinner” engine)
Screen: a wheel of the 10 school objects sits next to Speaky's closed bag. Mechanic: tap to spin → it lands on one object; tap the object → it pops out of the bag (reveal + “ta-da” sound). Pupils: BEFORE the reveal the whole class asks together Where's my (pen)? ↗ → on reveal It's here! ↘. Teacher: spin, hold the reveal until you hear the FULL question, then tap. 🧠 Predict: Which one will it land on? Point & guess! — children point before the spin stops.

Act out (ALL pairs at once). Pairs on the carpet; each pair gets a “lost thing” (one object card A hides in a fist / under a book). A: Oh no! Where's my […]? B points: It's here! Swap the card, change roles, repeat with a new object. ICQ: Do you say “pencil” or “Where's my pencil”? (→ the full question!)

◆ Support: let the weaker child be B (points + It's here!) first, then swap. ▲ Extension: A adds a second line — And where's my book? — It's here, too!

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — Where's my…? ✅ ГОТОВ (reuse of the Unit 1 “Find it!” engine)
Screen: a drawn classroom with objects tucked around it (on the shelf, under the chair, in the bag). Mechanic: tap Speaky → he asks; children scan & point on the picture; tap the object → it glows + “It's here!” pops up. Pupils: Where's my ruler? — It's here! ▶ Hide & Seek — “Where's my…?” (Игротека “Прятки”, our U1 version — the whole class moves and searches, nobody sits out). Class covers eyes: Close your eyes… no peeking! 🦝 Speaky helps you hide 6–8 object cards around the room (on the shelf, under a chair, in the bag).

Kagan — Quiz-Quiz-Trade energy: everyone hunts at once. Rule — find it → SAY it: whoever finds a card holds it up high and shouts Where's my ruler? — It's here! and brings it to the carpet. Teacher feeds the language: Good! WHERE's my ruler? Say the whole question!

When all cards are found, sort them on the carpet: What is it? — It's a crayon! Reset if loud: Freeze! Run once; re-hide and run a 2nd round only if time allows (buffer — don't rush the pair game for it).

◆ Support: quietly steer a slower child to an easy-to-spot card so they get the win too. ▲ Extension: finders add where it was — It's here — on the shelf!

⭐ Swap (rotation): if you ran Hide & Seek last lesson, swap in Tic-tac-toe — 9 object cards in a grid; a team wins a square by asking Where's my (rubber)? and the other team answering It's here!

▶ Pair “Lost & found”
desks, pairs

▶ Lost & Found (controlled pair drill — every pair plays at once, nobody waits). Each pair: a small set of real school objects (pencil, rubber, ruler, crayon) + one “hiding spot” (under the book / in the pencil case).

Model on the board first. A closes eyes; B hides ONE object under the book. A opens eyes and asks: Where's my crayon? B: if right — It's here! (reveal); if wrong — No! Try again! Then swap roles. Keep the FULL question every time.

Kagan — RallyCoach: partner = checker; praise the full question: Good question! It's HERE! Teacher monitors, feeds fast finishers colour + object: Where's my RED crayon? — It's here!

🧠 Create — your own thing: children hide a REAL object of their own (a real pencil, a hair-clip) and invent the question about it: Where's my (clip)? — It's here! Their word, their hiding spot.

◆ Support: give a picture-card menu on the desk so they always have an object to name. ▲ Extension / fast finishers: add the borrowing chunk — May I borrow your rubber? — Of course! Thank you!
