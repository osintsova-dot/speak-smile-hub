🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Starter Unit “Hello, friends!” · Lesson 2 — Grammar: What's your name? — I'm (Jake).
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Greetings → name role-play → classroom instructions (TPR) · carpet + desks
Week 07.09 · Lesson 2 (Starter)✨ GRAMMAR~12 pupils · pairs + mingle
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to greet a friend and exchange names — ask “What's your name?” and answer “I'm (Jake).” in a short meeting role-play (Pupil's Book p. 4 song “What's your name?”, CD1 1), and respond to core classroom instructions — Stand up · Sit down · Listen · Look · Open / Close your book.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — Starter L1 numbers 1–10, colours & “What's this? — It's a…” — woven into the greeting (“Hello! How many? — Five! What's your name?”); phonics — Phonics Hero: повтор ур.4–6 (h·r·m · d·g·o · u·l·f, тетрадь стр.10–16) — speed-reading reactivation + short routine dictation; tricky words go, no, so by sight; listen & respond through TPR.
  • Personal. Insist on the FULL exchange (“What's your name?”, not “Name?”; “I'm Jake”, not bare “Jake”); classroom commands = whole-body TPR — every child moves; every task keeps ALL children responding (mingle / ball / show-me cards) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • greet a friend and ask “What's your name?”;
  • answer “I'm (name).” and act out a short meeting;
  • hear & say the sounds c/k · e · h · r · m · d and respond to Stand up / Sit down / Listen / Look / Open your book.

Structure
What's your name? — I'm (Jake).  ·  Hello! / Hi! — Goodbye!  ·  Nice to meet you!  ·  Imperatives: Stand up! · Sit down! · Listen! · Look! · Open your book.
Form
What's = What is; I'm = I am. Question with a friendly rising tone ↗, answer falling on the name ↘. Command = bare verb (+ please), always with a gesture.
Pron. watch
“What's your” /wɒtsjɔː/ links into one chunk — not “what is your”; keep I'm (not bare “Jake”); stress the verb in each command (STAND up, SIT down) so ears catch it.
Phonics
Phonics Hero — повтор ур.4–6: sounds h·r·m · d·g·o · u·l·f (тетрадь стр.10–16) — speed reading, blends hat, dog, sun, leg, короткий диктант-ритуал. Знакомые звуки — реактивация, не презентации. Tricky words: go, no, so.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
🦝 Speaky hides his eyes: “I don't know you! What do I ask?” → “What's your name?” → child: “I'm ___!” (not just the name).
Point to yourself: “I'm the teacher. Now YOU — I'm…?” → “I'm (name).” Every answer starts with I'm.
Commands: say “Stand up!” and do it → “Do I sit or stand?” → stand; “Sit down!” → sit. Listen → DO.
c/k: “c and k are twins — same sound /k/ — cat, kite!”
⭐ Option — greetings by time: add Good morning! / Good afternoon! with a wave — use only if the class is quick today.

Assumptions: Hello/Goodbye, numbers 1–10, colours and “What's this? — It's a…” were met in Starter L1; Group 1 sounds s a t i p n are known; pupils are non-/emergent readers → pictures & audio, not printed text. Carpet routines & pair-work signals known.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Answers just “Jake”, drops I'mHand-on-chest gesture: the answer starts with I'm; back-chain Jake → I'm Jake; model with a big “I'm” + point to self.
Question shrinks to “Name?”Hands-up rule: Speaky won't answer until the FULL question; back-chain …your name? → what's your name? → What's your name?
c/k said as different sounds (or /s/ for c)“c and k are twins — same sound /k/”: cat, kite start the same; punch the /k/ with a fist.
Commands: child says the word but doesn't move (or moves without listening)Rule “Listen → DO”; big teacher gesture with every command; in Speaky Says, moving without “Speaky says” = Freeze!
Mingle chaos / keeps the same partnerStandUp-HandUp-PairUp rules: hand stays UP until you find a new partner; music = walk, stop = pair; quiet voices; Freeze! to reset.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.
Procedure — staging
highlighted = exact words to say (teacher / class) 🦝 = Speaky lines ▶ = game […] = fill-in slot 🎮 = on-screen (projector) interactive
⏱ Pacing — honest 60 min. Two grammar strands today (name role-play + classroom commands) plus phonics — a full plate, so pick, don't cram. The core = the name-exchange model (24–33′) + one mingle + the classroom-commands TPR. Buffers to drop first if you're short: the “Who's behind the door?” reveal (53–55′), Timed Pair Share, a 2nd mingle round. Better one exchange said well by every child than three stages half-done — never rush the model or the mingle.

Phonics Hero — повтор ур.4–6

Speed reading — тетрадь стр.10–16 (ур.4–6). Strips h · r · m → d · g · o → u · l · f: You KNOW these — there and back, fast! Vary the voice; every child reads every sound. (Реактивация после лета — НЕ новые презентации.)

First-sound show-me (all react). Letter cards on the ledge. Say a word — hat · red · dog · sun · leg · mug · fun… → Show me the FIRST sound! — all point to the letter / say the sound. 8–10 words, fast; nobody just watches.

Blend (all together). Push the sounds: /h/ /a/ /t/… hat! /d/ /o/ /g/… dog! /s/ /u/ /n/… sun! /l/ /e/ /g/… leg! — picture for each.

Dictation — the year's routine (short, everyone writes). Now YOU write. Listen… 2 words only, once each: dog · sun → mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & self-check. Finish with tricky words by sight: go · no · so — flash, chorus, air-write.

Presentation · Name role-play
carpet → circle

Model with Speaky. Teacher shakes Speaky's paw: Hello! What's your name? — 🦝 Speaky: I'm Speaky! What's your name? — Teacher: I'm Ms [name]! Nice to meet you!

Listen & sing — PB p. 4 “What's your name?” (CD1 1). Picture on screen: the market scene. Play the song; children point and sing, patting their chest on I'm ___. Drill: rising tone on What's your name? ↗, hand-on-chest I'm (Jake)! ↘ Back-chain the question.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): What's your name? Spinner ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the “What's this? spinner” engine)
Screen: a wheel of cartoon kids, each with a hidden name badge. Mechanic: tap to spin → it lands on one child; tap the child → the name badge pops up (reveal + chime). Pupils: BEFORE the reveal the whole class asks together What's your name? ↗ → on reveal the character “says” I'm Jake! and the class echoes. Teacher: spin, hold the reveal until you hear the FULL question, then tap. 🧠 Predict: Who will it stop on? Guess the name! — children point before the wheel stops.

Chain drill (ALL in the circle). Turn to your neighbour round the circle: What's your name? → I'm ___! — the question passes right round (▶ RallyRobin, everyone speaks). ICQ: Do you say “Jake” or “I'm Jake”? (→ start with I'm!)

◆ Support: let a shy child just answer I'm ___ while you ask. ▲ Extension: add Nice to meet you! after the name.
