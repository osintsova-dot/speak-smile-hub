🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 2 “Happy birthday!” · Lesson 5 — Phonics · Culture · Literacy: A Birthday Card
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Phonics + CLIL culture + guided writing · carpet + desks
Week 19.10 · Lesson 15L5 Phon+Cult+Lit~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4🦸 Phonics Hero ур.16a · ch·sh·th
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the /s/ sound (Toby's tongue twister “Six snakes on a slow scooter”), understand British birthday-party traditions (fancy dress, cards, party games, cake) and read & make their own birthday card (To… / Happy Birthday! / From…).
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning inside the topic — Unit 2 toys + “It's a…” / “Is it a…?” (L1–L2), adjectives big/small/old/new/fast/slow (L4), Starter colours & numbers (candles!); Phonics Hero ур.16 (a): digraphs ch·sh·th — meet, blend & trace (тетрадь стр.43–44), a perfect sound-contrast to today's /s/ (snake sss vs quiet shhh); tricky words said, here.
  • Personal. Keep the craft stage tight (timer + simple template); model writing letter-by-letter on the board; every child responds in every task (own card / show-me / stand-hiss) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tongue twister with a clear /s/ and spot /s/ words;
  • name 2–3 things British children do at a birthday party;
  • read a simple birthday card and make their own (copy To… / Happy Birthday! / From…);
  • hear which short vowel (a·e·i·o·u) is inside a CVC word.
Target language
Lexis
birthdaypartycardcakecandlespresentballoonsfancy dresspirates (recycled: 10 toys · big/small/old/new/fast/slow · colours · numbers 1–10)
Structure
Happy Birthday!  ·  It's a (robot). / Is it a (kite)?  ·  card frame: To (Beth), Happy Birthday! Lots of love, From (Mum and Dad).
Form
Card layout: To + name (top) → greeting → From + name (bottom). Fixed chunks, copied not built.
Pron. watch
/s/ is a long hiss (snake!), not /z/ or Russian “сь”; keep it unvoiced in six, snakes, slow, scooter.
Phonics
Coursebook sound: /s/ (PB p. 23, CD1 38–39) · Phonics Hero ур.16 (a) первое знакомство: digraphs ch /tʃ/ · sh /ʃ/ · th («язычок между зубами!») — chip, chin, shop, ship, fish, moth (тетрадь стр.43–45). Sight words: said, here.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold the model card: Who is the card for? → point to “To Beth” → Beth! Who is it from? → “From Mum and Dad”.
Snake sound — voice on or off? → hand on throat → off, just a hiss: sss.

Assumptions: toys + “Is it a…?” known from L1–L2, adjectives from L4; children can copy letters but not write freely → all writing is trace/copy from a board frame. Pass-the-circle games are new — model first. Emergent readers: card text is read together, finger-following. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Phonics — /s/ + Phonics Hero ch·sh·th (a)
board → desks

Recognise /s/ (~2½′). Toby's tongue twister (PB p. 23, CD1 38): s – s – s. Six snakes on a slow scooter! Snake-arm gesture, hand on throat — no voice, just a hiss. Choral ×3: slow → normal → fast. 2–3 brave solos with the “microphone”. 💪 Support: hold up the snake picture-anchor card as the sound cue — weak pupils watch it, not the letters.

Speed reading — Phonics Hero тетрадь стр.43 №1 (~1′). Strip ai · oa · ie · ee · or · ng · oo: There and back — fast! Whole class, left→right then right→left.

Meet ch · sh · th (стр.43 №2, ~2′) — the /s/ family of “hissy” sounds grows! Three pictures: the train ch — ch-ch-ch!; the quiet-finger sh — shhh!; the clown th — «язычок между зубами». Contrast with today's snake: sss — snake… shhh — quiet! (don't let s and sh merge). Children copy each sound + gesture; quick show-me: teacher says sock / shop / chip / three — all do the matching gesture.

“s or not s?” — whole-class show-me (~2′, all react). Pictures one by one — seven · socks · cat · scooter · swim · sing · snail · crayon (AB p. 20 set). Starts with /s/ → everyone stands and hisses sss; not /s/ → sit, hands on knees. Show me! Fast, nobody just watches.

🎮 Projector: Flashcards — “s or not s?” ✅ ready
  • Screen: big picture flashcards, one tap = next card.
  • Kids: whole class stands+“sss” for s-words, sits for the rest — every child reacts at once.
  • Teacher: use the existing Flashcards tool; exaggerate the hiss.
  • Offline: paper picture cards from the AB p. 20 set, held up one by one.

Blend the new digraphs (~2′) — Hub Station 3 / тетрадь стр.44 №1. Sound windows open one by one: /ch/ /i/ /p/… chip! /sh/ /i/ /p/… ship! Then chin, shop, fish, moth — picture for each; every child slides a “blend finger” in the air, all blend chorally. Sneak the contrast: chip… ship — same? No! /ch/ train, /sh/ quiet!

🎮 Projector: Phonics Hub — speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/ · набор ch·sh·th ✅ ready
  • Station 1 «Звуки»: old + new sound cards — children voice each sound, not the app.
  • Station 3 «Блендинг»: the six words in sound windows (chip · chin · shop · ship · fish · moth) — all blend at once.
  • Offline: тетрадь стр.44 №1 «Соединяем звуки» — the same six words, finger under each window.

Trace — тетрадь стр.43 №3 (~2′, desks, settle). Trace by the arrows, start at the yellow dot: ch · sh · th · chip · ship — whisper each as you write. 💪 Support: trace the big model letters with a finger first. ⭐ Extension: write fish on the spare line. (The sort, dictation & story «Fish and chips» of ур.16 come next lesson — today is first meeting only.)

Tricky words (~1½′). Flower cards said · here: We can't sound these out — tricky words! Look, say: said… here. Choral ×2 + air-write. Then straight to the song — keep the pace up.

Optional / fast finishers: the PB pair game What's my word? (AB p. 20 ex 3 / PB p. 23 ex 4) — everyone plays in pairs: A thinks of a picture, B asks Is it a snail? — No, it isn't. Is it a scooter? — Yes, it is. then swap (Kagan RallyRobin tempo). · A floor Hopscotch of picture squares — kids hop only on the /s/ pictures — works as a playground stir if space allows.

🎮 Reading-game option: read-race — ⚔ battle mode, level «Диграфы»: a word pops up, everyone reads it aloud, the fastest correct team races ahead. Swap it in for the speed-reading strip when the screen is free.
