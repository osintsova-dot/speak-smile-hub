🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 7 “Let's explore my town!” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuing line) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Phonics · carpet + desks
Week 01.03–07.03✨ NEW · 10 places in town~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4Pupil's Book p.72 · AB p.70
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 places in town and ask & answer using “What's this? — It's a (zoo)”, “There's a (cinema)” and “Let's go to the (café)” in the town context.
  • Subsidiary. Sing the coursebook song Let's go to town; retrieve colours (Starter/U1), numbers 1–10, “Is it a…? — Yes/No” (U2), “can” (U5) & the park objects of U6 woven onto town places; phonics ‘ir’ = /ɜː/ (recognise → blend → segment); tricky words her, were; pre-literacy & fine motor (sticker chant); preview “There are (two sweet shops). / How many … are there?” for L2.
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; ICQ before every task; run a stirrer→settler rhythm with songs & signals; catch & recast the tricky 3-syllable supermarket and the squashed restaurant.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 places in town (cinema · bookshop · sweet shop · toyshop · supermarket · café · zoo · hospital · restaurant · swimming pool);
  • ask & answer “What's this? — It's a…” & say “There's a (zoo)” / “Let's go to the (café)”;
  • hear, blend & segment ‘ir’ = /ɜː/ words.

Structure
What's this? — It's a (zoo).  ·  There's a (cinema).  ·  Let's go to the (café).  ·  preview (L2): There are (two sweet shops). / How many (shops) are there?
Form
There + 's (is) + a + noun. “Let's go to the…” = Let-us chunk from AB & song. Weak a /ə/, the /ðə/.
Pron. watch
3-syllable supermarket /ˈsuːpəmɑːkɪt/; squashed restaurant /ˈrestrɒnt/ (silent au); two-word swimming pool; café /ˈkæfeɪ/; long ‘ir’ /ɜː/.
Phonics
Digraph ‘ir’ = /ɜː/: bird · girl · shirt · first (recognise → blend → segment). Tricky words: her · were.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Do we watch a film there?” → yes → cinema. “Do we buy sweets there?” → yes → sweet shop. “Do we see the animals there?” → yes → zoo.
‘ir’: “How many letters?” → two. “How many sounds?” → one → /ɜː/.

Assumptions: colours, numbers 1–10, “Is it a…? — Yes/No” (U2), “can” (U5), the park objects & “What's this?” (U6) and all 26 phonic sounds + short/long vowels are known (continuing line, mid-year). Pupils are non-/emergent readers → rely on pictures & audio, not printed text. Carpet & team routines are known.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
supermarket squashed / mis-stressedClap the 4 beats: su–per–mar–ket; back-chain market → super-market.
Confuse sweet shop ↔ toyshop ↔ bookshopOne clear picture cue each: sweets 🍭 / toy 🧸 / book 📖; say the first word loud.
“There's” dropped to “there a…”Model & stress the ‘s: There'S a cinema. short choral drill.
‘ir’ read as two sounds /i/+/r/Slide two fingers together: two letters, ONE sound — /ɜː/.
Over-excited “town” energy on the carpetFreeze! + volume-control arm; follow every stirrer with a settler.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

10–22′
Phonics · ‘ir’ = /ɜː/
at the board

Recognise. Two letters — i, r — one sound: /ɜː/! Show bird · girl · shirt · first · third · skirt. ▶ Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if you hear /ɜː/… bird!… cat!… girl! (mix in non-‘ir’ words — everyone reacts every time).

Blend. Push the sounds together with a finger slide: /b/ /ir/ /d/… bird! + picture for meaning. Repeat with girl, shirt, first.

Segment · dictation. Now YOU write. Listen… say a word once → children write on mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & check. Words: bird · girl · shirt · first. Then flash tricky words her · were — These are tricky — we just know them!

💪 Support: pre-write “ir” on their board; they add the first & last sound. ⭐ Extension: add one more — third · skirt · dirt.

✔ Assess: the “Show me!” boards are your live check — scan for who reversed or split ‘ir’; recast on the spot.

24–33′
Presentation · Vocabulary
carpet → teams

Present — 10 places in town. Hold up each A4 card: What's this? If unknown, model: It's a zoo. Zoo. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children solo. Cards: cinema · bookshop · sweet shop · toyshop · supermarket · café · zoo · hospital · restaurant · swimming pool. Contrast the tricky trio (hold them up): This is a sweet shop. This is a toyshop. This is a bookshop.

🎮 Interactive (projector): “What's this?” spinner — колесо мест (town wheel). Spin → a place in town lands. A rotating child taps to spin; whole class answers chorally It's a (café)! Predict: before the reveal — Is it a zoo? — Yes/No! (recycles U2). Every child shows a thumb 👍/👎 = all engaged.

▶ Fan-N-Pick (teams of 4, mini-cards): ① fans the cards · ② picks & asks What's this? · ③ answers It's a (supermarket). · ④ praises Well done! Rotate roles every turn — everyone asks, answers, praises.

ICQ: Do you say “What's this?” or “How many?” (→ What's this?)

💪 Support: pair a shy child with the “praiser” role first. ⭐ Extension: answer in a chunk — There's a big zoo! 		A4 flashcards ×10 · 3 mini-sets · What's this? spinner (board) · ▶ Fan-N-Pick

44–52′
Ask & Find
carpet, in pairs
🎮 Interactive (projector): “Find it!” — town scene. Tap Speaky — 🦝 asks What's this? Can you find the (café)? the class scans the town on screen, points & answers It's a café! (2–3 rounds, to model the phrase for all).

▶ Memory · What's this? — in pairs, on the carpet. Each pair has 5 town cards. Child 1 shows a card, names it — It's a zoo. — and places it face-down. Repeat for all 5 (show → name → place).

Then Child 1 asks: What's this? pointing at a face-down card; Child 2 — from memory, first try — names it, then turns it over to check. Correct → keep the card. Then swap roles. (Most cards wins.) Reset if loud: Freeze!

✔ Assess: stand behind one pair at a time — can both produce the place name unprompted? That's the lesson's success check.

💪 Support: 3 cards, not 5; keep a small picture strip visible. ⭐ Extension: answer with the chunk — There's a supermarket! (previews L2). 		5 flashcards per pair · Find it! (board, model) · Speaky
