🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 6 “A day in the park” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuing line) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Phonics · carpet + desks
Week 01.02–07.02✨ NEW · 10 park objects~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4Pupil's Book p.60 · AB p.58🦸 Phonics Hero ур.29a · /ау/ ou·ow + /ой/ oi·oy
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 park objects and ask & answer using “What's this? — It's a (swing)” and “We can play on the (slide)” in the park context.
  • Subsidiary. Sing the coursebook song It's fun in the park; retrieve colours (Starter/U1), numbers 1–10, “Is it a…? — Yes/No” (U2) & “can” (U5) woven onto park objects; phonics — Phonics Hero ур.29 (a) первое знакомство: sounds /ау/ (ou·ow: out, cow) and /ой/ (oi·oy: coin, boy); trace cow, boy; tricky words does, their; pre-literacy & fine motor (sticker chant); preview “Where's (Beth)? — She's on the (swing)” for L2.
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; ICQ before every task; run a stirrer→settler rhythm with songs & signals; catch & recast the 3-syllable roundabout and the silent b in climbing frame.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 park objects (swing · slide · seesaw · roundabout · climbing frame · tree · flower · grass · bush · rock);
  • ask & answer “What's this? — It's a…” & say “We can play on the (slide)”;
  • hear the difference /ау/ (cow, out) vs /ой/ (boy, coin) and trace cow · boy.

Structure
What's this? — It's a (swing).  ·  We can play on the (slide). / We can climb a (tree).  ·  preview (L2): Where's (Beth)? — She's on the (swing).
Form
It + 's (is) + a + noun. “We can play on the…” = able-chunk from the song. Weak a /ə/.
Pron. watch
3-syllable roundabout /ˈraʊndəbaʊt/; silent b in climbing; /iː/ in seesaw / tree; /aʊ/ in roundabout — our new sound!
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.29 (a) первое знакомство: TWO sounds today — /ау/ («ау!» в лесу): ou (out, house) · ow (cow, owl, town); /ой/ («ой!»): oi (oil, coin) · oy at the end (boy, toy); trace cow · boy (тетрадь стр.79). Tricky words: does · their.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Do we sit on it and go up-down-up-down?” → yes → seesaw. “Does it turn round and round?” → yes → roundabout.
“cow — forest call /ау/ or surprised /ой/?” → /ау/! “And boy?” → /ой/! (gesture check).

Assumptions: colours, numbers 1–10, “Is it a…? — Yes/No” (U2), “can” (U5) and all 26 phonic sounds + short/long vowels are known (continuing line, mid-year). Pupils are non-/emergent readers → rely on pictures & audio, not printed text. Carpet & team routines are known. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
roundabout squashed to “roundbout”Clap the 3 beats: round–a–bout; back-chain bout → a-bout → round-a-bout.
Confuse swing ↔ seesawOne clear gesture each: swing = sway arms; seesaw = up-down two hands.
climbing said with the /b/Model & smile: climb — no b sound! short choral drill.
ow confusion: /оу/ (snow) vs /ау/ (cow)Speaky's rule: “read it, then CHECK the meaning — a snow-cow doesn't moo!”; anchor pictures snow/cow stay on the board.
Over-excited “park” energy on the carpetFreeze! + volume-control arm; follow every stirrer with a settler.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

10–22′
Phonics Hero — /ау/ ou·ow + /ой/ oi·oy (a)
board → desks

Speed reading — тетрадь стр.79 №1. Last week's /ю:/ words: blue · new · cube · glue · stew · mule — There and back — fast!

Meet TWO sounds (стр.79 №2). 🦝 Speaky: /ау/ — call into the forest: ow! (ау!) — houses 🏠 ou (out, house, mouse) · 🏠 ow (cow, owl, town). /ой/ — the surprised sailor: oy! (ой!) — houses 🏠 oi (oil, coin) · 🏠 oy at the END (boy, toy). Gesture per sound; all copy ×3. Speaky's secret: ow can say /оу/ (snow) AND /ау/ (cow) — read and check the meaning!

Which sound? — whole-class show-me (all react). Say a word — cow · boy · out · coin · owl · toy · house · oil… → every child does the matching gesture (forest-call vs surprised-oy) → chorus. Fast; nobody just watches.

Write it — стр.79 №3 «Пишем» (desks). Trace and write: cow · boy — whisper each as you write. Then flash tricky words does · their — These are tricky — we just know them!

💪 Support: gesture only for cow / boy / out; trace the big letters first. ⭐ Extension: write out or toy on the spare line.

✔ Assess: the gestures + tracing lines are your live check — note who mixes /ау/ and /ой/; recast with the forest-call on the spot.

Presentation · Vocabulary
carpet → teams

Present — 10 park objects. Hold up each A4 card: What's this? If unknown, model: It's a swing. Swing. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children solo. Cards: swing · slide · seesaw · roundabout · climbing frame · tree · flower · grass · bush · rock. Contrast the tricky pair (hold both): This is a swing. This is a seesaw.

🎮 Interactive (projector): “What's this?” spinner — колесо парка. Spin → a park object lands. A rotating child taps to spin; whole class answers chorally It's a (slide)! Predict: before the reveal — Is it a swing? — Yes/No! (recycles U2). Every child shows a thumb 👍/👎 = all engaged. · 🎮 whats-this-spinner-u6

Ask & Find
carpet, in pairs
🎮 Interactive (projector): “Find it!” — park scene. Tap Speaky — 🦝 asks What's this? Can you find the (seesaw)? the class scans the park on screen, points & answers It's a seesaw! (2–3 rounds, to model the phrase for all). · 🎮 find-it-u6

▶ Memory · What's this? — in pairs, on the carpet. Each pair has 5 park cards. Child 1 shows a card, names it — It's a slide. — and places it face-down. Repeat for all 5 (show → name → place).

Then Child 1 asks: What's this? pointing at a face-down card; Child 2 — from memory, first try — names it, then turns it over to check. Correct → keep the card. Then swap roles. (Most cards wins.) Reset if loud: Freeze!

✔ Assess: stand behind one pair at a time — can both produce the object name unprompted? That's the lesson's success check.

🎮 Digital option (projector): park-memo — S&S memory pairs on screen, 1–4 teams: a rotating child flips two cards, the whole class names every picture aloud — It's a slide! Swap it in for one round of this game when the screen is free.

💪 Support: 3 cards, not 5; keep a small picture strip visible. ⭐ Extension: answer with the song chunk — We can play on the roundabout! (previews L2). 		5 flashcards per pair · Find it! (board, model) · Speaky · 🎮 park-memo · 🎮 find-it-u6
