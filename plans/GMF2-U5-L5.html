Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the ow/oa tongue twister («A snowman and a goat in a coat row a yellow boat to the rainbow»), compare UK winter activities with ours, and read the fable of the Ants and the Grasshopper and act its two seasons.
  • Subsidiary. Textbook phonics: ow/oa = /əʊ/; culture — dry ski slope, ice rink, tubing, toboggan vs Norilsk winter (we're the experts!); literacy — text type a fable: story + MORAL.
  • Personal. The fable moral is gold — land it gently (help and work matter), no moralising lecture; Norilsk winter pride moment in the culture block.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the twister fast ×3;
  • name UK winter activities and compare with ours;
  • retell the fable's two scenes and say its moral simply.

Block 3 · Literacy · The fable
carpet → desks

Before you read. PB p.57 pictures: Summer picture, winter picture — what's different?

Listen & read (CD2 53). Echo-read the dialogue; spot our old chunks: What are you doing? Can I have some food? — hands up each time you hear a “known friend”.

Act the two seasons. Half the class = ants (working mime), half = grasshopper (dancing); play summer scene, then SWAP for winter scene — everyone plays both sides.

The moral, gently. Winter comes. Who has food? Why? Kids' words; you distil: Work first, play after — and help your friends. Write the class-agreed moral line in notebooks (AB p.54 seed).

💪 Support: mime roles carry non-readers. ⭐ Extension: read the grasshopper's lines solo with feeling.
