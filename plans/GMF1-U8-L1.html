🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 8 “Camping fun!” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuing line) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Phonics · carpet + desks
Week 22.03–28.03✨ NEW · 10 clothes items~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4Pupil's Book p.82 · AB p.80🦸 Phonics Hero · повтор ур.25 /и:/
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 items of clothing and ask & answer using “What's this? — It's a (sweater)” and “What are you wearing? — I'm wearing (shorts) and a (T-shirt)” in the camping context.
  • Subsidiary. Sing the coursebook song What are you wearing?; retrieve colours (Starter/U1), numbers 1–10, “Is it a…? — Yes/No” (U2), “I've got…” (U3) & “Where's my…? — It's here” (U1) woven onto today's clothes; phonics — Phonics Hero повторение ур.25: sound /и:/ in its houses ee·ea·e-e·y (speed grid, sort, dictation, story «Счастливый щенок», тетрадь стр.69–71); tricky words put, saw; pre-literacy & fine motor (sticker chant); preview “What's he/she doing? — He's/She's (riding a horse)” for L2/L4.
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; ICQ before every task; run a stirrer→settler rhythm with songs & signals; catch & recast the always-plural items (shorts · shoes · trousers · trainers — no “a”) and the two-word sun hat.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 clothes items (T-shirt · shorts · sun hat · shirt · trousers · trainers · skirt · sweater · shoes · swimsuit);
  • ask & answer “What's this? — It's a…” & say “I'm wearing (shorts) and a (T-shirt)”;
  • read, sort & write /и:/ house-words (bee · sea · meat · happy) at review speed.

Structure
What's this? — It's a (sweater).  ·  What are you wearing? — I'm wearing (shorts) and a (T-shirt).  ·  preview (L2/L4): What's he/she doing? — He's/She's (riding a horse).
Form
I'm = I am; wearing = present-continuous chunk. a + singular (a T-shirt, a skirt) vs plural, no “a” (shorts · shoes · trousers · trainers — always two). Weak a /ə/.
Pron. watch
two-word sun hat; swimsuit /ˈswɪmsuːt/; sweater /ˈswetə/ (short e — not “sweeter”); plural /z/ on shoes · trousers; long /iː/ in jeans, T-shirt — our review sound!
Phonics
Phonics Hero — повтор ур.25: /и:/ — houses ee (bee, tree) · ea (sea, meat) · e-e (these) · y (happy) — speed grid, sort, dictation bee, sea, meat, happy, story «Счастливый щенок» (тетрадь стр.69–71). Tricky words: put · saw.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Do we wear it when it's hot and sunny?” → yes → sun hat / shorts / T-shirt. “Do we wear it to swim?” → yes → swimsuit. “Is it one thing or two?” (shoes/trousers) → two → plural, no “a”.
“bee, sea, happy — same sound /iː/?” → yes! “Same letters?” → NO — different houses!

Assumptions: colours, numbers 1–10, “Is it a…? — Yes/No” (U2), “I've got…” (U3), “Where's my…? — It's here” (U1), places in town (U7) and all phonic sounds so far (incl. ‘oa’ U6, ‘ir’ U7) are known (continuing line, spring term). Pupils are non-/emergent readers → rely on pictures & audio, not printed text. Carpet & team routines are known.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
“a short / a trouser” — plural item made singularHold up TWO: shoeS, trouserS — always two, no “a”. Wiggle two fingers.
sweater said “sweeter”Short e: clap swe–ter; back-chain ter → swe-ter.
Confuse shirt ↔ T-shirt ↔ skirtCue: T-shirt = the T shape 👕; skirt = a girl spins; say the first sound loud.
“I'm wearing” dropped to “I wearing”Model & stress: I'M wearing a T-shirt. short choral drill.
ee/ea houses mixed in the sortSay the word, point to the house card FIRST, then write; pairs check each other (RallyCoach).
Over-excited “camping” energy on the carpetFreeze! + volume-control arm; follow every stirrer with a settler.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

10–22′
Phonics Hero — повтор ур.25 /и:/
board → desks

House recall + speed grid (тетрадь стр.70 №1). Four houses on the rail — ee · ea · e-e · y: One sound /iː/ — four houses. Read the grid — there and back, fast! bee · tree · green · sea · meat · leaf · happy · funny; whole class, pictures beside. ▶ Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if you hear /iː/… sea!… cat!… happy! — all react.

Sort lap (стр.70 №2, all vote first). Say a word (tree · sea · happy · meat · funny · leaf) → every child points to the right house → chorus & write 3–4 into the columns.

Dictation. Now YOU write. Listen: bee… word once → segment on fingers → all write on mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & self-check. Words: bee · sea · meat · happy. Then flash tricky words put · saw — These are tricky — we just know them! (Story «Счастливый щенок» стр.71 — home re-read tonight.)

💪 Support: pre-write the house letters on their board; they add the rest. ⭐ Extension: add one more — green · three.

✔ Assess: the “Show me!” boards are your live check — scan for who mixes the ee/ea houses; recast on the spot.

24–33′
Presentation · Vocabulary
carpet → teams

Present — 10 clothes items. Hold up each A4 card: What's this? If unknown, model: It's a sweater. Sweater. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children solo. Cards: T-shirt · shorts · sun hat · shirt · trousers · trainers · skirt · sweater · shoes · swimsuit. Contrast the tricky trio (hold them up): This is a shirt. This is a T-shirt. This is a skirt. Flag the plurals: shoeS — two! trouserS — two!

🎮 Interactive (projector): “What's this?” spinner — колесо одежды (clothes wheel). Spin → a clothes item lands. A rotating child taps to spin; whole class answers chorally It's a (skirt)! Predict: before the reveal — Is it a hat? — Yes/No! (recycles U2). Every child shows a thumb 👍/👎 = all engaged. Offline reserve: face-down flashcard pile — a child draws one, class names it.

▶ Fan-N-Pick (teams of 4, mini-cards): ① fans the cards · ② picks & asks What's this? · ③ answers It's a (swimsuit). · ④ praises Well done! Rotate roles every turn — everyone asks, answers, praises.

ICQ: Do you say “What's this?” or “How many?” (→ What's this?)

💪 Support: pair a shy child with the “praiser” role first. ⭐ Extension: answer with a colour — It's a blue swimsuit! 		A4 flashcards ×10 · 3 mini-sets · Clothes wheel spinner (board) · ▶ Fan-N-Pick

Ask & Find
carpet, mingle
🎮 Interactive (projector): “Find it!” — campsite scene. Tap Speaky — 🦝 asks What's this? Can you find the (swimsuit)? the class scans the campsite on screen, points & answers It's a swimsuit! (2–3 rounds, to model the phrase for all). Offline reserve: A3 campsite poster — point & name.

▶ Quiz-Quiz-Trade · Kagan — everyone mingles & talks. Each child holds ONE clothes picture card. Stand up, hand up, pair up! Child A shows the card — What's this? — Child B answers It's a (sweater)! A praises Well done! Swap roles, then trade cards and find a new partner. Keep going — Now trade! New partner!

Reset if loud: Freeze! Everyone is asking, answering & praising the whole time — no one waits.

✔ Assess: stand near one pair at a time — can both produce the clothes name unprompted, no picture peeking? That's the lesson's success check.
