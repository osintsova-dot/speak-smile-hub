🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 2 “Happy birthday!” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary: Toys
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuing line) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Phonics · carpet + desks
Week 05.10 · Lesson 11✨ NEW · 10 toys~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4Pupil's Book p.12 · AB p.16🦸 Phonics Hero ур.14a · ee·or
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 toys and identify & check them using “What is it? — It's a (robot).” and “Is it a (kite)? — Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.”
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — Unit 1 school things & “Where's my…? — It's here!”, Starter colours & numbers — and fold it into the new toys topic (“Where's my ball? — It's here!”, “It's a red car”, “How many kites?”); phonics — Phonics Hero ур.14 (a): digraphs ee /iː/ · or /ɔː/ (meet the sounds → blend → trace & write); listen for detail (order the toys chant); pre-literacy & fine motor (word–picture, stickers).
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; give an ICQ before every task; run a stirrer→settler rhythm; every task keeps ALL children responding (own card / show-me / stand-sit) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 toys;
  • ask & answer “What is it? — It's a…” and “Is it a…? — Yes, it is / No, it isn't”;
  • blend & read their first ee/or words (bee, tree, feet, green, fork, corn) and trace the digraphs.

Structure
What is it? — It's a (robot).  ·  Is it a (kite)? — Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.
Form
Is + it + a + noun? → Short answers: Yes, it is. / No, it isn't. Linking “It's a” /ˈɪtsə/.
Pron. watch
or = ONE sound /ɔː/ — no rolled r; ball /ɔː/ vs doll /ɒ/ (they don't rhyme!); keep -n't in isn't; falling tone on the answer.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.14 (a) первое знакомство: digraphs ee /iː/ · or /ɔː/ — «ослик кричит ee-or!» — bee, tree, feet, green, fork, corn (тетрадь стр.36–38) · sight words: what, when.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Show a doll, ask “Is it a robot?” → No → “No, it isn't.”
“Do I know the toy, or am I guessing?” → guessing → “Is it a…?”

Assumptions: numbers, age (How old are you?), colours & school things known from Starter + Unit 1. Pupils are non-/emergent readers → rely on pictures & audio, not printed text. Phonics: digraphs ai·oa·ie met last week (Phonics Hero ур.13, стр.33–35) → today's speed-reading strip. Carpet routines & team roles are known.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Confuse rhyming ball / dollPresent as a contrasting pair, one clear gesture each; drill slowly.
Digraph read as two letters: ee = /е-е/, or = /о-р/“Two letters — ONE sound”: merge two fingers into one fist; the donkey cry ee-or! glues the pair. Anchor cards stay on the board.
“Is it a…” said too fastBack-chain: …a kite → it a kite → Is it a kite?
Drop -n't: “No, it is”Exaggerated choral drill of No, it isn't with a head-shake.
Teams grab the mystery bag / cardsAssign the 4 roles inside each team; hands on knees until it's your turn.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Review → into toys
carpet, circle

Recycle Unit 1 + Starter (everyone has a card). Deal one picture card per child — mostly Unit 1 school things (pencil, book, ruler, bag…) with a few toys mixed in. RallyRobin in pairs: A hides the card behind their back — Where's my pencil? · B: It's here! — then swap. All pairs talk at once, no one waits.

Colours & numbers — whole-class show-me. Hold up a colour card: It's a red…? everyone points to a red thing / calls the colour. Numbers on fingers: How many? — Five! — every child shows the fingers.

Bridge into toys. 🦝 Speaky: On your birthday you get… TOYS! Preview the same frames: Where's my ball? — It's here! It's a red car! How many kites? Signal: Class? — Yes!

✔ Assess: listen as pairs swap — who still misses a colour/number word from Starter/U1? Note 1–2 names to seat near you at Presentation.

💪 Support: give the pair a card with a strong single object + colour cue. ⭐ Extension: stretch to two words — It's a big red car! (previews U2 L4 adjectives). 		1 picture card per pupil (Unit 1 things + a few toys) · colour cards · Speaky · ▶ RallyRobin

10–22′
Phonics Hero — ee·or (a)
board → desks

Speed reading — warm the old sounds (тетрадь стр.36 №1). Strip ai · oa · ie (last week's digraphs) on the board / in the тетрадь. Read with me — there and back… fast! Whole class reads left→right, then right→left; race the clock, everyone reads every sound.

Meet the new sounds — the donkey (стр.36 №2). 🦝 Speaky shows the donkey pictures: Listen! The donkey cries: ee-or! ee-or! ONE donkey — TWO new sounds: long ee /iː/ then or /ɔː/. Cry like donkeys — ee-or! (all bray ×3 — the pair sticks together). Check: hold up ee — Two letters… how many sounds? → every child shows ONE finger.

🎮 Phonics Hub (projector): speakandsmile.ru/gmf1/phonics/ — набор ee·or. Station 1 «Звуки»: flash old + new sound cards — children voice each sound, not the app. Station 3 «Блендинг»: sound windows open one by one — /b/ /ee/… bee! — every child slides a “blend finger” in the air = all blend at once. Words: bee · tree · feet · green · fork · corn (+ picture for meaning). Status ✅ ready. Offline reserve: тетрадь стр.37 №1 «Соединяем звуки» — the same six words in sound windows, finger under each window.

Write it — тетрадь стр.36 №3 «Пишем» (desks). Phonics Hero time! Open page 36. Trace ee and or by the dots, then the arrows show the stroke — start at the yellow dot and write: ee · or · bee · fork. Walk & monitor pencil grip; children whisper each word as they write.

Sight words. Flash what · when: chorus ×3, air-write. Look — “what” is OUR question today: What is it? (today's grammar word — now they see it in print).

Balloon finisher (60 sec, stir). Hub Station 6 «🎈 Шарики» — letters float up; the whole class names each sound, one rotating child pops the balloon. End on the cheer.

💪 Support: finger-trace the big letters first; blend only the three ee-words. ⭐ Extension: write green or corn on the spare line.

✔ Assess: the choral blends + tracing lines are your live check — note who reads ee as two sounds /е-е/; recast with the donkey cry on the spot.

44–53′
Guess & check
carpet → teams
🎮 Interactive (projector): Find it! — the birthday scene. Screen: a birthday-party scene full of toys. Tap Speaky — 🦝 hides one behind a box. Mechanics: whole class guesses, points on screen; reveal on correct. Language: Is it a robot? — No, it isn't! … Is it a kite? — Yes, it is! (2–3 rounds — models the question + both answers for all). Teacher: every child points at their own toy card while guessing = no watchers. Status ✅ ready (reuse Find it! engine, toys scene). Offline reserve: real hide-box + toy card on the board.

▶ Mystery Bag — Is it a…? — in teams of 4 (all guess). A feely bag holds toy cards / small toys. Child 1 secretly holds one; all three others guess — quick fire round the group, each with a toy in front of them to point at: Is it a car? Holder answers honestly — No, it isn't. / Yes, it is! The correct guesser takes the next turn, so the bag keeps moving and no one sits idle. Rotate the holder each round.

Reset if it gets loud: Freeze!

✔ Assess: stand behind one team at a time — can each child produce Is it a…? and the right short answer unprompted? That's the lesson's success check.

🎮 Digital option (projector): toys-memo — S&S memory pairs on screen, 1–4 teams: a rotating child flips two cards, the whole class names every picture aloud — It's a robot! Swap it in for one round of this game when the screen is free.

💪 Support: keep a picture strip of the toys visible to point to while guessing. ⭐ Extension: create a clue — It's red. Is it a car? (adds a colour before the guess). 		feely bag + toy cards/small toys per team · Find it! (board, model) · Speaky · 🎮 toys-memo
