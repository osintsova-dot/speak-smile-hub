🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 2 “Happy birthday!” · Lesson 1 — Vocabulary: Toys
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuing line) · 60 min · PPP + TPR + Phonics · carpet + desks
Week 05.10 · Lesson 11✨ NEW · 10 toys~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4Pupil's Book p.12 · AB p.16
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 toys and identify & check them using “What is it? — It's a (robot).” and “Is it a (kite)? — Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.”
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — Unit 1 school things & “Where's my…? — It's here!”, Starter colours & numbers — and fold it into the new toys topic (“Where's my ball? — It's here!”, “It's a red car”, “How many kites?”); phonics — voiced th /ð/ (recognise → blend → segment); listen for detail (order the toys chant); pre-literacy & fine motor (word–picture, stickers).
  • Personal. Keep teacher-talk low; give an ICQ before every task; run a stirrer→settler rhythm; every task keeps ALL children responding (own card / show-me / stand-sit) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 toys;
  • ask & answer “What is it? — It's a…” and “Is it a…? — Yes, it is / No, it isn't”;
  • hear, blend & segment words with voiced th /ð/.

Structure
What is it? — It's a (robot).  ·  Is it a (kite)? — Yes, it is. / No, it isn't.
Form
Is + it + a + noun? → Short answers: Yes, it is. / No, it isn't. Linking “It's a” /ˈɪtsə/.
Pron. watch
voiced /ð/ in this/that (not “z”/“d”); keep -n't in isn't; falling tone on the answer.
Phonics
Jolly focus: voiced th /ð/ — this, that, with, them (recognise → blend → segment).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Show a doll, ask “Is it a robot?” → No → “No, it isn't.”
“Do I know the toy, or am I guessing?” → guessing → “Is it a…?”

Assumptions: numbers, age (How old are you?), colours & school things known from Starter + Unit 1. Pupils are non-/emergent readers → rely on pictures & audio, not printed text. Carpet routines & team roles are known.

Review → into toys
carpet, circle

Recycle Unit 1 + Starter (everyone has a card). Deal one picture card per child — mostly Unit 1 school things (pencil, book, ruler, bag…) with a few toys mixed in. RallyRobin in pairs: A hides the card behind their back — Where's my pencil? · B: It's here! — then swap. All pairs talk at once, no one waits.

✔ Assess: listen as pairs swap — who still misses a colour/number word from Starter/U1? Note 1–2 names to seat near you at Presentation.

10–22′
Phonics — th /ð/
at the board

Recognise. Model the Jolly th action (tongue between teeth, soft buzz): th… this, that. Children copy the sound & gesture (children voice the sound, not the app).

🎮 Interactive (projector): Flashcards — th reveal. Screen: a th-word picture (this · that · them) uncovers slowly. Mechanics: class shouts the first sound before the reveal, then blends. Language: /th/… this! then /th/ /i/ /s/… this! Teacher: tap to uncover; every child points a “slide finger” = all blend at once. Status ✅ ready (reuse Flashcards-reveal engine, th deck). Offline reserve: A5 th picture cards, reveal by hand.

“th or not th?” — whole-class show-me (all react). Give every child a th-card (tongue-between-teeth picture). Say words one by one — this · sun · that · dog · them · with · red… If it has voiced th /ð/ → everyone stands up & holds up the th-card + buzzes; if not → sit, hands on knees. Show me! Fast, 8–10 words — nobody is just watching.

▶ Fly Swatters — team relay (rotates fast). Cards on the board (this, that, them, with + distractors). Teams line up; each round one runner per team swats the word you call — Swat “this”! — while the whole seated class holds up their matching th-card & says the word chorally. New runners every round so all get a turn in ~2 min.

Blend (all together). Let's push them together: /th/ /i/ /s/… this! Then that, them + a picture for meaning.

Segment · find the th (everyone shows). Say a word once → children draw the th shape in the air OR trace th on a mini-whiteboard → Show me! → reveal & check. Today's focus: voiced th — this, that, with, them. (October, emergent readers — trace the digraph, not the whole word.)

💪 Support: a picture beside each word; they only find & trace the th. ⭐ Extension: where is the /ð/ — at the start (this) or in the middle (mother)?

✔ Assess: the “Show me!” boards are your live check — scan for who says /ð/ as “z”/“d”; recast on the spot.

Presentation · Vocabulary
carpet → mingle

Presentation — toys. ▶ Peek-a-boo cards — slowly slide each toy flashcard out of the “present” bag: What is it? Reveal fully → model It's a robot. Robot. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children individually.

Contrast the tricky rhyming pair (hold both): It's a ball. It's a doll.

🎮 Interactive (projector): What is it? — toy spinner (колесо toys). Screen: a wheel of the 10 toys; a rotating child taps to spin. Mechanics: Predict before the reveal — Is it a kite? — Yes / No! (recycles U2 Is it a…?); the wheel lands → whole class chants. Language: What is it? — It's a robot! Teacher: every child shows 👍/👎 on the predict = all engaged; tap-to-spin rotates each round. Status ✅ ready (reuse What's this? spinner, toys deck). Offline reserve: shuffle the flashcard stack, flip the top card.

▶ Quiz-Quiz-Trade (Kagan — stand up, one card each): pupil A holds a card to their chest, asks What is it? · B answers It's a [kite]. · praise Well done! · swap cards, find a new partner. Music stops = freeze.

ICQ: Do you show your card or hide it? (→ hide it — hold it to your chest.)

44–53′
Guess & check
carpet → teams
🎮 Interactive (projector): Find it! — the birthday scene. Screen: a birthday-party scene full of toys. Tap Speaky — 🦝 hides one behind a box. Mechanics: whole class guesses, points on screen; reveal on correct. Language: Is it a robot? — No, it isn't! … Is it a kite? — Yes, it is! (2–3 rounds — models the question + both answers for all). Teacher: every child points at their own toy card while guessing = no watchers. Status ✅ ready (reuse Find it! engine, toys scene). Offline reserve: real hide-box + toy card on the board.

▶ Mystery Bag — Is it a…? — in teams of 4 (all guess). A feely bag holds toy cards / small toys. Child 1 secretly holds one; all three others guess — quick fire round the group, each with a toy in front of them to point at: Is it a car? Holder answers honestly — No, it isn't. / Yes, it is! The correct guesser takes the next turn, so the bag keeps moving and no one sits idle. Rotate the holder each round.

Reset if it gets loud: Freeze!

✔ Assess: stand behind one team at a time — can each child produce Is it a…? and the right short answer unprompted? That's the lesson's success check.

💪 Support: keep a picture strip of the toys visible to point to while guessing. ⭐ Extension: create a clue — It's red. Is it a car? (adds a colour before the guess). 		feely bag + toy cards/small toys per team · Find it! (board, model) · Speaky
