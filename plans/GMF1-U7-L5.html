🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 7 “Let's explore my town!” · Lesson 5 — Phonics · Culture · Literacy: Town Mouse and Country Mouse
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Phonics + CLIL culture + guided literacy (a fable) · carpet + desks
Week 15.03–21.03 · Lesson 52L5 Phon+Cult+Litcontinuers line~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4🦸 Phonics Hero · повтор Magic E ур.21–22
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the /z/ sound (Toby's tongue twister “z – z – z. There's a lazy zebra in the zoo”), understand how British children travel — by bike (cycling class at school, riding to the town centre, a three-wheeled rickshaw) and read & understand a fable — Town Mouse and Country Mouse (PB p. 79) — then make their own “town or country” mini-card (I live in the …).
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve real prior learning inside the topic — Unit 7 transport + places + “There's a… / There are… / How many … are there?” + numbers 11–20 (L1–L4) recycled through a transport ▶ Bingo; accumulative sound retrieval — contrast the NEW voiced /z/ with the known unvoiced /s/ (sun · sit vs zip · zoo) so the sound bank grows, not resets; Phonics Hero повторение Части 3, ур.21–22 — Magic E i-e / o-e·u-e columns + stories (тетрадь стр.58–63); tricky/sight words the · a · in · out.
  • Personal. Keep the fable read-aloud brisk (finger-follow, one frame at a time); model writing letter-by-letter on the board; every child responds in every task (own Bingo card / stand-and-buzz show-me / thumbs vote / own town-or-country card) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tongue twister with a clear voiced /z/ and spot words that have /z/ (zip · zoo · zebra · zigzag);
  • name 2–3 ways British children travel by bike (cycling class · to the town centre · rickshaw);
  • follow & understand a simple fable and say where each mouse likes to live;
  • write the first letter-sound they hear (incl. Z z) and copy “I live in the (town / country).”

Structure
z – z – z. There's a lazy zebra in the zoo.  ·  How many (cars) are there? — There are (fourteen).  ·  We travel by bike. / How do you travel in your town?  ·  I live in the (town / country).
Form
Initial/medial /z/ + word. Culture chunk travel by + transport. Fable read as fixed text; literacy line I live in the ___ copied from a board frame, not built.
Pron. watch
English /z/ is voiced — a buzzing “zzz” like a bee, throat ON. Russian speakers tend to devoice it to /s/ → keep zoo (not “soo”), zebra (not “sebra”), lazy buzzing to the end.
Phonics
Coursebook sound: /z/ (PB p. 77, CD3 34–35), contrast with known /s/ · Phonics Hero — повтор Части 3, ур.21–22: Magic E i-e и o-e·u-e — columns at speed + re-read stories «Майк и воздушный змей» / «Милый мул» (тетрадь стр.58–63). Tricky/sight words: the · a · in · out. Culture PB p. 78 (CD3 36) · Literacy fable PB p. 79 (CD3 38).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold the zebra picture: Zoo — a buzzing z-z-z, or a hissing s-s-s? → buzzing z. Bee sound or snake sound? → Bee (z)!
Culture: In Britain, do the children go to the town centre by car or by bike? → Bike!
Fable: Does Country Mouse like the town or the country? → The country — “I like a quiet life.”

Assumptions: transport, places in town, numbers 11–20, “There's a / There are / How many…?” known from L1–L4; children can copy letters but not write freely → all writing is trace/copy from a board frame. Emergent readers: the fable is read together, finger-following. The fable is new — model & read one frame at a time. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Likely problemWhat you do
/z/ devoiced to /s/ (“soo” for zoo)Hand on throat: “feel it buzz like a bee — zzz”; contrast sun / …/ zip; exaggerate the voiced start.
Can't tell z-words from s-wordsPicture anchors (zip·zebra·zoo·zigzag·lizard) + stand-and-buzz / sit show-me; drag out the first sound.
Fable too long / lose the threadONE frame at a time, finger-follow; a quick point-check after each picture; act it in mouse voices.
Card copying slow / Z unknownBoard frame LARGE (“I live in the ___”), ONE word to choose; dotted-line strips; teacher scribes.
Bingo gets rowdyCross-out (no cutting), best of the round, Say Bingo! only when a line is full; reset with Freeze!

“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Phonics — /z/ + s/z retrieval
at the board

Recognise /z/ (~2½′). Toby's tongue twister (PB p. 77, CD3 34): z – z – z. There's a lazy zebra in the zoo! Bee-wings gesture; hand on throat — feel it BUZZ (voiced), not a hissing snake. Choral ×3: slow → normal → fast. 2–3 brave solos with the “microphone”. Support: hold the /z/ picture-anchor (a buzzing bee / a zebra) as the sound cue — weak pupils watch it, not the letter.

“z or not z?” — whole-class show-me (~2′, all react). Pictures one by one — zip · puzzle · cinema · zoo · zero · zigzag · seesaw · lizard (AB p. 74 set). Has /z/ → everyone stands and buzzes z-z-z; no /z/ → sit, hands on knees. Show me! Fast, nobody just watches.

🎮 Projector: Flashcards — “z or not z?” ✅ ready
  • Screen: big picture flashcards, one tap = next card.
  • Kids: whole class stands+buzzes for /z/ words, sits for the rest — every child reacts at once.
  • Teacher: use the existing Flashcards tool; exaggerate the first sound.

Blend for meaning (~1½′). Push sounds together with picture support — recycle the /s/–/z/ pair: /z/ /i/ /p/… zip! /z/ /oo/… zoo! /s/ /u/ /n/… sun! Children echo + do the action; hear the buzz turn to a hiss.

35–46′
Literacy — a fable
desks

Before you read (PB p. 79 ex. 1) — predict. Two mice pictures: Where do you think they live — town or country? Point! Everyone points; read the labels together.

Listen & read the fable (CD3 38) — fingers follow, ONE frame at a time. Town Mouse and Country Mouse: Town Mouse visits the country — I don't like apples. The country is horrible. Country Mouse visits the town — This food is delicious. The town is fantastic! Then a noise — Don't worry, it's only a cat! — Country Mouse runs and runs home: The food is good in the town, but I like a quiet life. Point-check after each frame: Happy or scared? Town or country?

Make YOUR “town or country” card (literacy product — analyse + create). Fold an A5 card. Each child CHOOSES: Do YOU like the town or the country? — draws it (tall buildings + cars, OR trees + a quiet house) and copies ONE line from the board frame: I live in the (town / country). Support: dotted-line strip to trace + teacher scribes. Extension: add “I like …” + a /z/ thing hidden in the picture (a zebra at the zoo, a lizard). Timer 8 min, countdown: 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down!
