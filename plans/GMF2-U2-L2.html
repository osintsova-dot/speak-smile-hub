Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to choose a / an / some correctly in shop requests: “Can I have a peach / an apple / some bread, please?”
  • Subsidiary. Understand the shop dialogue (CD1 34); listen & circle (CD1 35); consolidate the polite ritual; write a shop conversation (AB format).
  • Personal. Teach a/an/some as three doors with clear rules-of-thumb, not grammar lecture; drill until the article comes out automatically with the noun.
By the end, pupils can…
  • sort nouns into a / an / some groups;
  • make correct shop requests with all three;
  • act a 4-line shop dialogue from memory.

Structure
Can I have a (peach) / an (apple) / some (bread), please? — Here you are. / Of course!  ·  Thank you very much. — You're welcome.
Form
a + consonant sound (a peach) · an + vowel sound (an apple, an egg, an orange) · some + plural or uncountable (some carrots, some bread).
Pron. watch
linking: an_apple /ənˈæpl/; weak some /səm/; Can I /kənaɪ/ at speed.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“AN — before which sounds? a-e-i-o-u — vowels!” → an apple, an egg.
“Bread, water — can we count them 1-2-3?” → no → SOME.

Assumptions: 10 food words fresh from L1; the chunk “Can I have…, please?” is already in the mouth — today we make the article slot accurate. Pupils read the dialogue bubbles. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Dialogue & grammar box
carpet → board

Listen & repeat (act. 1, CD1 34). Line-by-line echo with roles (shopkeeper voice vs customer voice).

Grammar box — three doors. Board: дверь a 🚪 (a peach) · дверь an 🚪 (an apple — vowel sounds a-e-i-o-u!) · дверь some 🚪 (some bread, some carrots). Sort 6 cards as a class — every child points to the door BEFORE the card moves.

Chain drill. Card deck decides: pupil draws, asks the next: Can I have an egg, please? — next answers Here you are! and draws. All watch the deck.

ICQ: “An” before peach or apple? (→ apple!)

34–44′
Shop role-play · round 2
two corners

Now with articles for real. Two counters again; frame card ON the counter: Can I have a / an / some ___, please? Everyone buys twice, serves once (rotation).

Twist: shopkeeper gives the WRONG item on wrong article — An apple? Here — a peach! — customer must politely re-ask. Self-correction built in.

💪 Support: frame card + door colours on the food cards. ⭐ Extension: add Thank you very much! — You're welcome! closers.

💪 Support: gap-fill frame from the board. ⭐ Extension: 6 lines including a refusal: Sorry, no lemons today!
