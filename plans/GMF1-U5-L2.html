🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 5 “Fun on the farm” · Lesson 2 — Grammar: Can you see a…? Yes, I can. / No, I can't.
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Dialogue → controlled drill → freer → production · carpet + desks
Week 11.01 · Lesson 35✨ GRAMMAR~12 pupils · pairs + 2 teamsПродолжающая line
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to ask if someone can see a farm animal and answer yes / no: “Can you see a (cow)? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't.” (Pupil's Book p. 51 dialogue & Grammar box), and act out the “find Toby the dog” dialogue.
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — the 10 U5-L1 farm animals + “What is it? — It's a…” and Starter colours — woven straight into today's question (“Can you see a brown cow? — Yes, I can!”); phonics — the split digraph e–e /iː/ “magic-e” (Pete, eve, these, Steve, theme), contrasting L1's ea /iː/; listen for detail (PB p. 51 ex. 2 “Listen and colour the animals they can see”, CD2 41); gently preview can / can't for ability (“Cows can't fly!”) for Lessons 3–4.
  • Personal. Insist on the FULL question (“Can you see a cow?”, not “Cow?”); keep the short answer complete (“Yes, I can.” / “No, I can't.”, not bare “Yes”); every task keeps ALL children responding (own cards / point & say / all pairs at once) — never “2 play, 10 watch”.
By the end, pupils can…
  • ask “Can you see a (duck)?” about a farm animal;
  • answer “Yes, I can.” / “No, I can't.” and act out the lost-dog dialogue;
  • hear, blend & segment e–e /iː/ magic-e words and tell them from short-vowel words.

Structure
Can you see a (cow)? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't.  ·  Can you see Toby? — No, I can't.  ·  The cat is here. But where's Toby?  ·  preview: Cows can't fly. / Ducks can swim.
Form
Can you see + a + noun? → short answer Yes, I can / No, I can't (can't = cannot). Keep the pronoun+verb: not bare “Yes”. Rising tone on the question ↗, falling on the answer ↘.
Pron. watch
weak can /kən/ in the question vs strong can /kæn/ & can't /kɑːnt/ in the answer; linking “Can you” /kænjuː/; make the /t/ in can't clear so it isn't heard as “can”.
Phonics
Jolly focus: split digraph e–e /iː/ (magic-e) — Pete, eve, these, Steve, theme (recognise → blend → segment) · contrast short-e words (pet, hen, red) · tricky words review: Red list — I · the · he · she · we · me · be · you · your (JP Book 2 p. 35).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Cover the farm picture with your hands: “Can you see the cow?” → no → uncover a bit: “Now? Can you see a cow? → Yes, I can!”
Point where there is NO pig on the farm: “Can you see a pig?” → “No, I can't!” (shake head) — the answer must have can't, not just “No”.
e–e: “There's a magic e at the end — it jumps over and makes e say its name: /iː/ — Pete!” (contrast pet → Pete).
⭐ Option — ability CCQ: flap arms: “Can a bird fly?” → yes → Birds can fly!; “Can a cow fly?” → no → Cows can't fly! (Primes can/can't for ability, L3–4 — use only if the class is quick today.)

Assumptions: the 10 farm animals and “What is it? — It's a…” were met in L1; “Can you see a…? Yes, I can!” was heard in the L1 song On the farm; the single sounds & magic-e from Unit 4 (a-e, i-e, o-e) are known. Pupils are non-/emergent readers → picture grids and audio, not printed text. Carpet routines & pair-work signals known.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
One-word question: “Cow?” instead of “Can you see a cow?”Hands-over-eyes rule: no answer from Speaky until the FULL question; back-chain …a cow? → see a cow? → Can you see a cow?
Bare answer: just “Yes” / “No”Two-thumbs drill: 👍 = Yes, I can!, 👎 = No, I can't! — the answer always keeps I can / I can't; model with a big gesture.
can't heard as can (drops the /t/)Over-articulate the /t/: can — can-T!; pair it with a firm head-shake so meaning is never lost; contrast pairs can ↔ can't.
e–e read as short e (says /e/ not /iː/)“Magic-e makes the vowel say its NAME” — sweep a finger from e to e: P-e-t → Pete; contrast pet ↔ Pete on the board.
Guess game: child spots the animal but stays silent / just pointsRule: “See it → SAY it.” No card counts until Yes, I can! is said; teacher gestures “speak” before giving the point.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Buffers to drop first if you're short: the 2nd guessing round, the can/can't ability preview (53–55′), Timed Pair Share. Phonics — e–e /iː/ (magic-e)
at the board

Recognise — the magic-e jump. Write pet: p-e-t… pet! Now add a magic e at the end → Pete: Watch! Magic-e jumps over the t and touches the middle e — now e says its NAME: /iː/ — Pete! Sweep your finger e→e. More: eve, these, Steve, theme. Children copy, stretching /iː/.

Magic-e or short-e? — whole-class show-me (all react). Every child gets two cards: 🪄 (e–e /iː/) and short-e 🔤. Say/show words one by one — Pete · pet · these · hen · eve · red · Steve · net… → Show me! — all hold up 🪄 or 🔤. 8–10 words, fast; nobody just watches.

Blend (all together). Push the sounds together: /P/ /iː/ /t/… Pete! Then eve, these, theme — a picture/name card for each. Contrast pairs on the board: pet ↔ Pete.

Segment · dictation (everyone writes). Now YOU write. Listen… word once → all write on mini-whiteboards → Show me! → reveal & check together. Focus: Pete, eve, these. Finish with tricky words review by sight — flash, chorus, air-write: I · the · he · she · we · me · be · you · your (Red list).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Magic-e Sorter ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the Unit 1 “Flashcards” engine)
Screen: a word flips up one at a time (Pete · pet · these · hen · eve · red · Steve · net). Mechanic: tap the word to hear it, then tap 🪄 (magic-e /iː/) or 🔤 (short-e) to sort it; a green tick / red shake shows if right; on 🪄 words the final e lights up and “jumps”. Pupils: hold up their own 🪄/🔤 first, then a helper taps to check. Teacher: read the word, wait for hands, then reveal. Reuse: same engine as L1 Flashcards — just swap the card set. Offline: two paper cards 🪄/🔤 per child + a word list.

◆ Support: in dictation, give the sound-buttons (P-e-t-e) — they only fill letters. ▲ Extension: fast writers add the magic-e themselves: pet → Pete.

24–33′
Presentation · Dialogue
carpet → pairs

Model with Speaky. 🦝 Speaky hides a toy dog behind the farm poster. Teacher peers around: Can you see Toby? — look, no dog — No, I can't! — spot the cat — Can you see the black cat? — Yes, I can! The cat is here. But where's Toby?

Listen & repeat — PB p. 51 ex. 1 (CD2 40). Books open / picture on screen: the farm-gate scene, the children looking for Toby. Play the dialogue; children repeat line by line. Drill: rising tone on Can you see a cow? ↗, thumbs on Yes, I can! ↘ / No, I can't! ↘. Back-chain the question. Point at the Grammar box: Can you see Toby? Yes, I can. No, I can't. — this is our question of the day.

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Can you see a…? Spinner ✅ ГОТОВ (reskin of the “What's this? spinner” engine)
Screen: a wheel of the 10 farm animals sits next to a foggy farm. Mechanic: tap to spin → it lands on one animal; tap the farm → the fog clears and the animal either appears (“ta-da!”) or is NOT there (empty spot). Pupils: BEFORE the reveal the whole class asks together Can you see a (goat)? ↗ → on reveal Yes, I can! ↘ or No, I can't! ↘. Teacher: spin, hold the reveal until you hear the FULL question, then tap. 🧠 Predict: Will it be there? Thumbs up or down first! — children vote before the fog clears. Offline: spin a paper arrow on an animal circle; a helper hides/shows the matching card behind a paper fence.

Act out (ALL pairs at once). Pairs on the carpet; each pair gets a small farm picture with some animals on it. A points somewhere: Can you see a […]? B checks the picture: Yes, I can! / No, I can't! Swap the picture, change roles, repeat. ICQ: Do you say “cow” or “Can you see a cow”? (→ the full question!)

◆ Support: let the weaker child be B (yes/no with 👍/👎) first, then swap. ▲ Extension: A adds a colour — Can you see a grey horse? — Yes, I can!

▶ Peek-a-farm — “Can you see a…?” (Игротека “Угадай по кусочку”, farm reskin — the whole class reacts every reveal, nobody sits out). Hold an animal card behind a paper “fence”; slide it up slowly, showing just a bit — an ear, a tail. Class shouts the guess-question: Can you see a cow? — No, I can't! … Can you see a goat? — Yes, I can! Rule — see it → SAY it: every child holds up 👍/👎 AND says the answer. Reveal fully to check.

Kagan — Numbered Heads energy: before the full reveal, table teams whisper their guess; you call a number, that child says the whole question for the team. Keeps every table thinking, not just the fastest hand. Reset if loud: Freeze!

Run 6–8 cards; a 2nd round only if time allows (buffer — don't rush the pair game for it).

◆ Support: reveal a bigger piece for a slower child so they get the win too. ▲ Extension: guessers add a colour — Can you see a brown cow?

⭐ Swap (rotation): if you ran a peek game last lesson, swap in Fly-swatter listening — 4 animal cards on the board; you say Can you see a duck?, two racers swat the duck while everyone else holds up their own card & shouts Yes, I can! — fast ROTATION so all get a turn.

▶ Pair “Farm detectives”
desks, pairs

▶ Farm Detectives (controlled pair drill — every pair plays at once, nobody waits). Each child has their OWN secret farm card (4–5 animals on it, held so the partner can't see).

Model on the board first. A asks about their partner's hidden farm: Can you see a sheep? B looks at their own card: if it's there — Yes, I can!; if not — No, I can't! Then swap roles. Keep the FULL question every time.

Kagan — Fan-N-Pick / RallyRobin: partners take strict turns (ask ↔ answer ↔ swap), so both speak equally; partner = checker, praises the full question: Good question! Yes, I can! Teacher monitors, feeds fast finishers colour: Can you see a grey horse? — Yes, I can!

🧠 Create — draw your own farm: children draw one animal of their choice in a box (as in AB p. 49 ex. 2) and quiz their partner about it: Can you see a (rabbit)? — Yes, I can! Their animal, their card.

◆ Support: give a picture-card menu on the desk so they always have an animal to ask about. ▲ Extension / fast finishers: add the ability line — Can it fly? — Yes! Birds can fly!
