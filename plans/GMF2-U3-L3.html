Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow, read and act out the story «The mysterious egg» and ask about plural animals: “Have they got (four legs)? — Yes, they have. / No, they haven't.”
  • Subsidiary. Story skills: predict → gist → read along → sequence → act; value — help your friends and family; plural got-questions grow naturally from the story (penguins!).
  • Personal. Mystery pacing — don't reveal the egg's secret early; train the “ooooh?” prediction pause.
By the end, pupils can…
  • retell the story in 4–5 steps;
  • ask & answer “Have they got…?” about penguins/animals;
  • read their role's lines.

Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“ONE penguin — has or have? MANY penguins?” → has / have.
“The kids find the penguin's family — is that helping?” → yes — we help friends!

Assumptions: Has it got…? is fresh (L2) — they-form is a small hop. Acting routine solid. Egg/mystery lexis carried by pictures. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).
