Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to ask & answer about sports: “Do you play (volleyball)? / Do you go (skateboarding)? — Yes, I do. / No, I don't.”
  • Subsidiary. Understand the dialogue (CD1 15, Molly's goggles); listen for detail — Listen & tick (CD1 16); consolidate the play/go split from L1; write a mini-conversation (AB follow-up).
  • Personal. Drill the short answers until they're automatic — full “Yes, I do”, never bare “yes”; keep the interview stage strictly timed.
By the end, pupils can…
  • ask “Do you play/go…?” with the right verb;
  • answer “Yes, I do. / No, I don't.” truthfully;
  • complete a listen-and-tick task on first listening.

Structure
Do you play (volleyball)? — Yes, I do. / No, I don't.  ·  Do you go (rollerblading)?
Form
Do + you + play/go + sport? Short answers with do/don't — never “Yes, I play”. The -s appears only in L3 (he/she) — don't pre-teach.
Pron. watch
weak Do you /djə/ at speed; don't with a clean /əʊ/; rising intonation on the question ↗.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Do you play tennis? — can I answer just ‘yes’?” → better: Yes, I do!
“Is the question about me or about you?” → about YOU — so answer about yourself, truthfully.

Assumptions: 10 sports + play/go are fresh from L1 (checked in warm-up). Yes/No questions with do existed receptively in GMF1; production is new. Pupils can read the dialogue speech bubbles. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Listen & tick
desks

Act. 3 (CD1 16). Pencils down. First — just listen: which sports do you hear? Second listen — tick the sports (cycling · volleyball · rollerblading · hockey · swimming). Model item 1 together, check in pairs, then whole-class check with the card held up.

✔ Assess: collect a quick thumbs-check per item — who ticked late/wrong? They join your group in the interview stage.

26–34′
Tic-Tac-Toe · grammar
desks, 3 teams

▶ Noughts & Crosses — sports. 3×3 grid of sport pictures. Team claims a cell by asking the OTHER team the question — Do you play baseball? — and the other team answering truthfully in chorus: Yes, we do! / No, we don't! Both sides speak every turn. Rotate the asker.

🎮 Interactive (projector): «Крестики-нолики» — sports. Same game on screen, auto-checks the line. A rotating child taps the cell; the WHOLE team says the question aloud. · 🎮 sports-ttt

ICQ: Who says the question — one player or the team? (→ the team, together)

 🎮 sports-ttt · offline grid backup
34–44′
Sports interview
mingle

▶ Quiz-Quiz-Trade. Every child holds a sport card. Music → mingle; music off → pair: A Do you play tennis? (their card) — B answers truthfully Yes, I do! / No, I don't. B asks theirs. TRADE cards, new partner. 5–6 exchanges — everyone asks/answers ~6 times.

Tally twist. Before starting: Count your YES answers! Harvest at the end: Who got three yes? Four?

💪 Support: card shows the full question as text (readers' support). ⭐ Extension: follow-up: When? — On Saturdays!

 sport cards · music · ▶ Quiz-Quiz-Trade
44–52′
Write a mini-dialogue
desks, pairs

Talk Partners → notebooks. Pairs write a 4-line dialogue from the board frame: — Do you play/go…? — Yes, I do. — Do you play/go…? — No, I don't. Both write; both sign. Then act it out to another pair (previews AB p.7 ex.2 format).

💪 Support: frame printed on a half-sheet — fill 2 gaps only. ⭐ Extension: add a third exchange with a subject: Do you like PE?
