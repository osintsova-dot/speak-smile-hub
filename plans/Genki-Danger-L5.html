🦝 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Danger · Lesson 38 — Doctor, Doctor! — head, arm, leg, hand, foot, tummy
Genki preschoolers (~5–6 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games + role-play (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Danger level · L38 · Lesson 5 of 8✨ Body parts + role-play · My … hurts12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to name 6 body parts (head, arm, leg, hand, foot, tummy) and role-play a doctor visit — Doctor, doctor! My leg hurts! — Get well soon!
  • Subsidiary. Cumulative Remember-time recycle of greetings, numbers, sea, face & monster — woven into "the poorly monster/teddy"; the S&S big-book; a listen-&-plaster folder sheet.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; body parts always in the phrase (My arm hurts!, not a bare list); every child mimes / speaks every round; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name head, arm, leg, hand, foot, tummy;
  • say My … hurts! holding the part;
  • reply Get well soon!;
  • play doctor & patient in a pair;
  • put a plaster on the part that hurts.

TPR gesture

Pron. watch
tummy soft t · hurts /hɜːts/ · arm long /ɑː/; keep Get well soon warm & caring.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: "Do we HOLD the sore part & say ow?" → yes.
ICQ: "Doctor is kind — do we say Get well soon?" → yes.

Assumptions: children know body parts from L35 & the "add-a-part" games → now in a caring role-play. Meaning from mime, a toy doctor kit & the L1 prompt. Non-readers/non-writers. Attention 5–7 min → ⚡/🧘. Group = 12.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
"hurts" feels sad/scaryKeep it playful — a poorly TEDDY, big pretend "ow", instant Get well soon! + hug fixes it.
Confusing arm/hand, leg/footHold & shake each: arm (whole), hand (fingers); leg (whole), foot (stomp); name 3×.
Everyone wants to be the doctorPairs swap roles: doctor ↔ patient each round; Speaky is the first patient so all watch a model.
Role-play gets loud🧘 gentle voice for doctor; Freeze! + volume-control arm.
Transitions get chaoticTrain: Come with me! — one signal, one destination.
Classroom management — signals
"Class? — Yes!" — you: "Class!" → children stop & echo "Yes!"
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
"Speaky says…" — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
"Freeze!" / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — "5… 4… 3… 2… 1…" with fingers shown.
"Come with me!" 🚌 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana's real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🟢 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Finale = cumulative Q&A chain + the Thank You! song ritual.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello / Good Morning song, "choose the greeting → jump/run/swim/fly to your chair".
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives on the bus-box & shakes hands with each child.
  • Remember time — cumulative Q&A of everything so far (thumb-up/down like a game), growing lesson by lesson, woven into today's topic.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, Genki topic song, board app, active games / role-play.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheets, a different type each lesson (listen & plaster / draw & tell / build & stick / colour-by-command / match соедини / sort & stick / count), always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🟢 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Remember time 2 + Closing — run the growing Q&A chain, Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
