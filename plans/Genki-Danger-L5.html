🦝 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Danger · Lesson 5 — Doctor, Doctor! Body Parts
Genki preschoolers (~5–6 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Danger level · Lesson 5 of 7✨ body parts + My … hurts12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to say where it hurts & play doctor — Doctor, doctor! My arm hurts. Where does it hurt? Get well soon! — using head · arm · leg · hand · foot · tummy.
  • Subsidiary. Remember-time recycle of all past topics + L2/L3 (body & face parts); folder worksheet (match соедини); a doctor-patient role-play.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; gentle caring tone; every child is doctor AND patient; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name head · arm · leg · hand · foot · tummy;
  • say My … hurts. & ask Where does it hurt?;
  • play doctor & patient in a pair;
  • match the hurt part to the bandage (worksheet);
  • say Get well soon!

Chunks

TPR gesture
hold the sore part & wince “ow!” · doctor mimes bandaging · Get well soon! pat gently. Speaky models both roles.
Pron. watch
keep hurts soft; tummy playful. Caring meaning & the exchange matter more than perfect sounds.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
CCQ: (hold your leg & wince) “My head hurts?” → no, my leg hurts!
ICQ: “Doctor is gentle or rough?” → gentle. “Patient says…?” → My … hurts.

Assumptions: children had Adventure→Christmas + Danger L1–L4 (body & face parts primed). Near-zero English + L1 prompt. Non-readers. Group = 12.

Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…” with fingers shown.
“Come with me!” 🚌 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana’s real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🌳 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Finale = a fast game on today’s language + the Thank You! song ritual.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello song (chorus), “choose the greeting → swim/fly/jump to your chair”.
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives from the bus-box & shakes hands with each child (Hello! How are you?).
  • Remember time — cumulative Q&A chain of ALL past topics + a review game (What’s missing / Karuta / board app). The whole-course recycle lives HERE, at the start.
  • Focus time — new topic presented from the magic bus-box, topic song, board app, 2–3 active games.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheet, a different type each lesson (listen & tick / colour / trace / match / draw & tell / sort / mini-craft), always listen → do → say.
  • Reading time 🌳 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Final game ⚡ — back to the carpet for a fast, fun game on today’s language, ending on a high note (kids don’t want to leave). Not the whole-course recycle — that was at the start.
  • Closing — Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
