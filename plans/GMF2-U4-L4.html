Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to use 4 movement prepositions (up · down · along · across) with Present Continuous: “He's running up the hill. / She's swimming across the lake.”
  • Subsidiary. Sing «What's he doing?» (CD2 24); Talk Partners game (CD2 25); cooperative song «Calm down» (CD2 26) — the settle-tool of the season; Yes-he-is/No-she-isn't short answers.
  • Personal. Prepositions live in the body — every drill has an arm-path gesture; keep the obstacle course orderly (one mover per lane).
By the end, pupils can…
  • show & say the 4 prepositions with a gesture each;
  • make He's/She's + -ing + preposition + place sentences;
  • answer “Is he…? — Yes, he is / No, he isn't.”

Structure
He's (riding a horse) across the (sand). / She's (walking) up the (hill).  ·  Is he (walking) up a hill? — Yes, he is. / No, he isn't.
Form
preposition + the + place; questions with Is he/she…? → Yes, he is / No, she isn't.
Pron. watch
across /əˈkrɒs/ weak first syllable; along vs across minimal-ish pair — gesture separates them.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Up — show me with your arm! Down? Along? Across?” → four clean paths.
“Is she running down? (picture: up)” → No, she isn't — she's running UP!

Assumptions: He's/She's + -ing fresh from the story; nature places active. Four prepositions only (по учебнику — up/down/along/across, без over/through). Gestures carry the meaning. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
along/across blurredArm-paths: along = follow the line, across = cut the line; drill as contrast pair with a rope on the floor.
Preposition dropped in sentencesSentence builder cards physically ordered: [He's] [running] [up] [the hill].
Obstacle course over-excitesOne mover per lane, walk-speed rule, Calm down song ready as the settle tool.
Short answers drift (“Yes, is”)Twins card Yes-he-is / No-he-isn't; beat drill.
Fast finishers in writingExtension: second sentence with a different preposition.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Present with the rope-line. Rope on the floor = the path. Walk it: along the path! Cut it: across the path! Stairs mime: up! down! Choral + arm-path ×3 each.

Obstacle course

Mini course: rope (path) · blue cloth (lake) · chair bridge · book hill. One mover per lane walks it while the CLASS narrates: She's walking along the path… she's going across the bridge! Everyone moves once, everyone narrates constantly.

✔ Assess: narration is the check — who supplies the preposition unaided?

Settle: Calm down song gesture → carpet.
