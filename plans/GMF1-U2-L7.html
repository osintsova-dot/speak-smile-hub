Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Assessment day: calm, model-first, no competition · desks + side table

⚠️ Before you print & before class: the track numbers (07 / 08) and test page numbers (Unit 2 = Page 1–3 of the test booklet) in this plan are from cross-checking the PDF. Verify them against the GMF1 Units 1–9 Standard Level Tests PDF (Unit 2) and cue the audio before pupils arrive — no fumbling with the CD on test day.
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. Assess Unit 2 language — toys, “What is it? / It's a…”, “Is it a…? — Yes, it is / No, it isn't”, “My favourite toy is a… It's (old)” — with the GMF1 Unit 2 Standard Level Test (Listening · Reading · Writing · Read & write · Speaking), in a calm, confidence-first atmosphere.
  • Subsidiary. Jolly track — write CVC words with short vowels (dictation; JP Book 2, p. 7); tricky words like, have; exam micro-skills: work alone, listen twice, check your page.
  • Personal. Model-first: show every task type before the paper is out; no competitive games today; keep voice low and pace unhurried; no child ever sits with nothing to do (quiet poster task during Speaking turns).
By the end, pupils can…
  • complete all written parts of the Unit 2 test independently;
  • answer the Speaking questions one-to-one (What is it? · Is it a…? · What's your favourite toy?);
  • write short CVC words from dictation (bed, six, jam, duck, sock);
  • leave smiling — the test is a “show what I know” day, not a scary day.

Pron. watch
Speaking part: accept clear meaning over perfect accent; gently remodel, don't correct mid-test.
Phonics
Jolly focus: writing CVC words — short vowels (JP Book 2, p. 7). Tricky words: like, have.
Concept checks (ICQs) · Assumptions
Before the paper: Do we work alone or with a friend? → alone! Is it OK to not know one? → yes — skip it, come back!
Listening: How many times do we listen? → two times — first just listen, then write.

Assumptions: all task types rehearsed in L6 (review + AB p. 23) and via the unit trainer at home; children write by copying from word boxes; first “real” test of the year for some → anxiety possible, hence breathing ritual + model-first + Speaky cheering, not judging.

Differentiation
  • 💪 Support (needs more). Seat these children within arm's reach; read every instruction aloud again right beside them, point to the worked example, keep the word box visible, and give extra time — hold the next listening item until their pencil moves. For ex. 4/5 let them copy the board model and just swap the toy word. Praise effort, not speed.
  • ⭐ Extension (fast finishers). After the poster: a bonus “My favourite toys” mini-poster (draw + label 2–3 toys with a “big / old / fast” caption) or a page in the class picture book; still buzzing → the calm Jenga ritual below. Never “more test”, always create + apply.
Self-evaluation (thinking)

At the very end each child evaluates their own effort with a traffic-light card — 🟢 easy · 🟡 okay · 🔴 tricky:

Show me your light. What was EASY today? What was a bit TRICKY? 2–3 children name one thing each. No right/wrong — a friendly check-in, not a score.

Calm, non-competitive Jenga ritual (optional buffer): a fast finisher gently pulls one block → answers ONE Unit 2 question (What is it? / Is it a robot? / What's your favourite toy?) → done. No tower race, no losers.

23–43′
UNIT 2 TEST — written parts
desks

Read every rubric aloud. They are emergent readers — before EACH exercise, read the instruction to the whole class and point to the example: Exercise 1 — listen and match. Like this. Re-read privately beside any child who needs it.

Listening (paced together). Ex. 1 Listen and match (Track 07) · ex. 2 Listen and tick ✓ (Track 08) — play each track TWICE: first listen, pencils down; second time draw the line / tick. Watch the room, pause between items.

Reading → Writing → Reading & Writing (own pace). Ex. 3 read & circle — 6 items (It's a ball. It's old. · scooter–fast · teddy–new · doll–small · robot–big · car–slow) · ex. 4 look & write the short answer (Is it a teddy? — Yes, it is / No, it isn't) · ex. 5 read & write from the word box (small · fast · robot · train · slow · scooter) — My favourite toy is a… It's… Circulate silently; encourage with a nod, never with answers: Take your time. You're doing great.

Countdown at the end: Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down. Collect written pages (keep the Speaking picture page).
