🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 9 “Day and night” · Lesson 4 — Vocab+Grammar: Meal times · I have dinner in the evening!
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · New vocab + structure together (PPP + TPR) · carpet + desks
Week 26.04–02.05 · Lesson 65✨ VOCAB+GRAM~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4 + pairs🦸 Phonics Hero · свободное чтение: истории Части 4
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 4 meal-time chunks — breakfast in the morning, lunch in the afternoon, dinner in the evening, a snack at night (Pupil's Book p. 96) — and say when they have each meal: “I have dinner in the evening.”
  • Subsidiary. Recycle real prior learning — daily routines get up / wash my face / brush my teeth / have breakfast (L1), “I (wash my face) every day” (L2), and the story The wobbly tooth where Jake “has breakfast every morning” and the family sit down for dinner (L3) — woven into today's target: “Jake has breakfast in the MORNING and dinner in the EVENING — just like you!”; phonics — Phonics Hero свободное чтение: истории Части 4 (тетрадь стр.66–90) — the “big books” carousel; sing the coursebook song Are you hungry? (CD4 15); values: eat well through the day.
  • Personal. Match each meal to a time-of-day picture (sun-up ☀️ · sun-high 🔆 · sunset 🌇 · moon 🌙); watch in the morning/afternoon/evening BUT at night; give an ICQ before every game; every task keeps ALL children responding (all mime at once / stand-sit True–False / pair info-gap / own “My day” poster).
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 4 meals + time of day (breakfast/morning, lunch/afternoon, dinner/evening, snack/night);
  • answer “When do you have breakfast?” → “In the morning!”;
  • say a full chunk: “I have dinner in the evening.”;
  • read a “big-book” story from Part 4 aloud with a partner.
Target language
Lexis
breakfastlunchdinnera snackin the morningin the afternoonin the eveningat night (recycled: get up · wash my face · brush my teeth · have breakfast)
Structure
I have (breakfast) in the (morning). · I have (a snack) at night. · I have (lunch) at school. · Talk Partners: I have lunch in the morning. — No! · I have lunch in the afternoon. — Yes!
Form
I + have + (meal) + time phrase. Present simple for routines — no “-s” (I / you). Time: in the morning/afternoon/evening · at night.
Pron. watch
breakfast = /ˈbrekfəst/ (two beats, NOT “break-fast”); weak “the” /ðə/; linking have_a snack; contrast in the vs at night.
Phonics
Phonics Hero — свободное чтение, истории Части 4 (стр.66–90): «Счастливый щенок» (стр.71) · «Ночь и свет» (стр.74) · «Корова и цветок» (стр.81) · «Мальчик и игрушка» (стр.82) · «Мальчик и море» (стр.85) · «Добрый великан» (стр.88) · «Телефон и дельфин» (стр.89) · «День рождения» (стр.90) — почти настоящие книги!
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Hold up ☀️ then 🌙. “Breakfast — in the morning or at night?” (point to the picture) → morning.
“Lunch — at school in the afternoon, or in bed at night?” → afternoon (at school).
“A snack — a big meal or a little one?” → little! (at night). “Do I say in the night or at night?” → at night!

Assumptions: daily routines + have breakfast are solid from L1; “I … every day” from L2; the L3 story already used breakfast every morning and dinner — today those meals get pinned to a time of day and used in “I have … in the …”. Emergent readers → pictures, audio & a short decodable text. КТП suggested Genki Eat! Drink! Dance!, but the coursebook song Are you hungry? (p. 96) IS the exact target structure → plan uses it; Genki Good Morning bridges the times of day.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Say in the night instead of at nightFreeze the 4 time-cards on the board: morning ☀️ · afternoon 🔆 · evening 🌇 all take in the; night 🌙 takes at. Chant 3× and point; over-stress “AT night”.
Mix up lunch / dinnerAnchor: lunch = afternoon, at school; dinner = evening, at home with family. Mime each (tray at school vs table at home).
breakfast said “break-fast” (2 clear words)Model /ˈbrekfəst/, back-chain: …fast → brek-fast → breakfast; clap two beats, not three.
Child names the meal but drops the time phraseSentence frame on the board — I have ___ in the ___. — + point to the time-of-day picture as they speak; give the frame, they finish.
Over-excitement in the mime game“Freeze!” resets; one command → one calm mime; Volume-control arm to settle.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

21–28′
Presentation · meal times + I have…
carpet

Present meal times (PB p. 96 ex. 1, CD4 14). Listen–point–say the 4 picture-chunks: breakfast in the morning · lunch in the afternoon · dinner in the evening · a snack at night. Choral drill ×3, then 2–3 children. Fix the tricky word: breakfast = /ˈbrekfəst/ (back-chain, two beats).

Present the grammar — meal + time of day. Hold a bowl-card under the sun ☀️: I have breakfast IN THE morning. · under the moon 🌙: I have a snack AT night. Say → point to the time-card → children copy the full chunk. Drill the in the / at switch.

▶ When do you…? call-response (all react). You hold up a meal-card; class shouts the time: Dinner — when? — IN THE EVENING! Speed up. CCQ (see box): A snack — in the night, or AT night? (→ AT night!)

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): What's this? spinner Screen: a wheel of the 4 meal pictures + a time-of-day icon on each; the arrow spins and lands on one. Mechanics: tap Spin → arrow stops on breakfast / lunch / dinner / snack → whole class says the full chunk, then a named child. Language: What's this? — Breakfast in the morning! Teacher: spin 6–8×, speed up; land on breakfast to re-run the /ˈbrekfəst/ back-chain, and on snack to drill at night. ✅ ГОТОВ — U1 "What's this?" spinner (swap deck to the 4 meal-time cards).

▶ Speaky says: Meal time mime
standing at desks

▶ Speaky says — Meal time mime (TPR command game; every child mimes at once → all move, nobody watches). Each child has 4 mini meal-cards fanned in their hand + the 4 time-of-day cards on the desk.

🦝 Speaky commands, all mime the meal AND touch the right time-card: Speaky says have breakfast in the morning! · Speaky says have dinner in the evening! · Speaky says have a snack at night! Trick them (no “Speaky says” → freeze): Have lunch at night! — that's wrong-time silly, so freeze & giggle. ICQ: Do you move if I DON'T say “Speaky says”? (→ No — freeze!)

Kagan — RallyRobin (pairs, take turns): Partner A gives ONE meal command, Partner B mimes it & says the full chunk I have dinner in the evening! — swap, swap, swap. Everyone is the boss.

💪 Support: the time-of-day cards stay face-up on the desk as a prompt; teacher's ☀️🌙 gesture stays visible. ⭐ Extension: the "boss" gives a 2-meal command — Have breakfast in the morning AND lunch in the afternoon.
