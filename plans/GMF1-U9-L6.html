Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will have consolidated all Unit 9 language — the daily-routine verbs (get up, wash my face, brush my teeth, comb my hair, have a shower, get dressed, make my bed, go to school, go to bed, sleep + have breakfast / lunch / dinner), the times of day (in the morning / afternoon / evening · at night) and the structures “I (wash my face) every day.” / “I have (breakfast) in the (morning).” / “I sleep at night.” — and can retrieve and use them confidently in the review tasks and the quiz.
  • Subsidiary. Phonics — Phonics Hero итоговый диктант: Magic E и написания (Части 3–4) (cake, bike, home, cube, rain, night, snow, girl…) + Toby's “th” tongue twister; self-assessment (My progress traffic light, AB p. 97 ex. 3); listening for detail (PB p. 100 ex. 1, CD4 24).
  • Personal. Run the flagship quiz by PIES: every child writes/shows every answer (Showdown on mini-boards), the responder is picked by Numbered Heads — no “fastest child answers everything”; keep the reward moment short so language time wins.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the daily-routine actions and act them out on cue;
  • say “I (brush my teeth) every day.” without support;
  • match a meal / action to its time — “I have breakfast in the morning. I sleep at night.”;
  • write the year's Magic-E & sound-house dictation (cake · night · snow · girl) and say the “th” twister;
  • self-assess with the traffic light — ready for the Unit 9 test.

Structure
I (wash my face) every day. · I have (breakfast) in the (morning). / I sleep at night. · When do you (get up)? — In the morning.
Form
Present simple I + verb + every day. Time phrase: in the morning/afternoon/evening · at night. Connected “in the” /ɪnðə/.
Pron. watch
clear /θ/ in three, thin, bath, teeth (tongue between teeth, no /f/, no /t/); linking “in the”; stress on the action verb.
Phonics
Phonics Hero — итоговый диктант Частей 3–4: Magic E (cake, bike, home, cube) + написания (rain, night, snow, girl) + sentence I like my bike. · recap “th” twister: “Three thin tigers have a bath on Thursday.”
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Show a sun / a moon: Is it morning or night? Do I have breakfast or do I sleep? → morning → “I have breakfast in the morning.”
Quiz rule ICQ: Who writes the answer — one child or EVERYBODY? → everybody, on their own board!

Assumptions: all Unit 9 language met in L1–L5; single sounds + CVC blending done — today we read the th digraph. Teams of 4 with numbered members (1–4) are routine. The flagship quiz with reward runs only on Review day — children know it's special. This is the last unit of the year → a celebratory tone is welcome. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Quiz over-excitement / shouting outRules first + ICQ; “WiFi” 🖐 between questions; points only when the team is quiet-ready.
Strong pupils dominate answersNumbered Heads: YOU call the number (1–4) after boards are up — anyone can be the voice.
Confuse in the (morning) vs at nightFreeze the phrase: “morning / afternoon / evening = in the; night = at.” One gesture per time; demo before asking.
“th” said as /f/ or /t/ (free / tree)Tongue-out mirror trick: “tongue peeps out — th!”; show word FIRST, blend chorally, picture only to check.
Ex. 1 (PB p. 100) writing too slowWord bank is on the page — copy, don't invent; countdown + “finish at home is OK”.
Losing team deflatesBonus “teamwork star” for best cooperation; everyone gets a shot at the reward at least once.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.

Review in motion
📺 open space · corners

▶ Mime the Routine — charades (everyone acts, nobody waits). Think–Pair first: each child silently mimes one routine to a neighbour, partner guesses the full chunk: You brush your teeth! → swap. Then call a number → that child mimes for the class; class answers chorally: He gets dressed! I get dressed every day!

▶ Time-of-day Corners (Kagan Corners — whole class moves). Label 4 corners: morning ☀️ · afternoon 🌤 · evening 🌆 · night 🌙. Call an action: I have breakfast! → EVERY child runs to morning and says I have breakfast in the morning! Mix: I go to school… I have dinner… I sleep… I have lunch. Attention reset: Class? — Yes!

Support ▸ weaker pupils hold a picture card of the action + echo the chunk with a partner.   Extension ▸ stronger pupils add the time unprompted — I brush my teeth in the morning AND in the evening.

33–51′
🏆 FLAGSHIP QUIZ with reward
📺 teams · projector

Set-up (2 min). 3 teams of 4, numbered heads 1–4, one mini-whiteboard EACH. Quiz grid on screen — categories: Daily routines 🪥 · Times of the day 🕐 · Sounds & spelling (th) 🔤 · Class rules ✨. Rules + ICQ: Who writes? — Everybody! When do you show? — On “Showdown!”

Round flow (Kagan Showdown + Numbered Heads — all answer every question). Question up (e.g. mime on screen: What is she doing? · time: When do you have breakfast — morning, afternoon or night? · sound: Write the word — t-ee-th! · read a “th” word · class rule: We put our hand up to… ?). Every child writes/draws → Showdown! → all boards up → call a number: Number 3! — that child of the playing team answers aloud in a full sentence.

Predict (thinking). On a half-hidden picture, before revealing: Look — just the sky! Morning or night? Write your guess! Then reveal — Were you right? Turns each round into a little guess-and-check.

Reward shot. Correct = the answering child takes the shot: animated football penalty on screen — a GOAL = team points, class counts them in English: One, two, three… GOAL! (No projector → paper goal + soft ball, ≈ Mido №91 Bowling for Animal reskin.) Rotate teams; over ~10–12 questions every child shoots at least once.

Support ▸ picture / word bank stays visible on a side card so weaker writers copy, not freeze; accept one correct word from them.   Extension ▸ when a strong pupil is “Number”, require the full sentence — She brushes her teeth. She brushes her teeth in the morning. — not just the verb.
🔨 Flagship interactive · projector · BUILD · “Unit 9 Team Quiz + Reward”  🔨 BUILD
Screen 1 — Grid: 4×3 board of numbered tiles under the 4 category headers (Daily routines 🪥 / Times of the day 🕐 / Sounds & spelling 🔤 / Class rules ✨). Tap a tile → it flips to the question (mime clip / picture / half-hidden sky / audio word / “th” word / rule prompt). Used tiles grey out.
Screen 2 — Answer: big “Show your boards!” + a 5-second timer bar; tap ✔ / ✗ to score; a full-sentence model appears for choral echo.
Screen 3 — Reward mini-game: football penalty — child “kicks”, goalie dives, animated GOAL (or a bowling reskin). One shot = +points.
Screen 4 — Scoreboard: 3 team bars, huge numbers readable from the back row, plus a “Teamwork ⭐” toggle for best cooperation.
Mechanic: pick tile → question → everyone writes → Showdown → Numbered Head answers → score → reward shot → rotate team. ~10–12 tiles ≈ 15 min.
Target language: all Unit 9 — I (get up / brush my teeth) every day. / I have (breakfast) in the (morning). / I sleep at night. + read a “th” word.
Teacher: host only — YOU tap tiles & call the number so no captain dominates. Spec is a self-contained HTML slide (S&S purple #6C3FC5 / honey #F5C842). Offline reserve: printed grid + paper goal + soft ball works identically.

Finish with totals + teamwork star: Give me five, champions!

🎮 Ready-made engine for this quiz: routines-quiz — the S&S bowling/basketball quiz on this unit, 1–4 teams: a picture question on screen → the team answers chorally — Do you brush your teeth every day? → Yes, I do! → one child makes the throw on screen; the score counts itself. Stretch round: review3-quiz — mixed questions from Units 7–9.
