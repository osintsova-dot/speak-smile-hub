🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 4 “Fantastic food!” · Lesson 3 — Story: I'm hungry
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Story: listen → follow → act out (drama) · carpet + desks
Week 30.11–06.12 · Lesson 27✨ STORYcontinuing line · ~12 pupils · small groups for drama🦸 Phonics Hero ур.22a · Magic E: o-e·u-e
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow and understand the story “I'm hungry” (Pupil's Book pp. 42–43, CD2 23) and act it out using key story language: “I'm hungry.”, “Let's ring the bell.”, “I like ice cream.”, “I love chocolate cake — it's my favourite.”, “I don't like oranges / fruit.”, “Do you like peaches? — No, I don't.”, “Robot, you're amazing! Thank you.”
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve real prior learning — food nouns + I like / don't like / love… and Do you like…? — Yes, I do / No, I don't (U4 L1–L2, immediate) plus What's your favourite…? → “It's my favourite” (U2) and Here you are / please / thank you (Starter) — woven straight into the restaurant story (the magic robot serves food the children like / don't like). Phonics — Phonics Hero ур.22 (a) первое знакомство: Magic E o-e · u-e (hop → hope, cub → cube; trace nose, cube), kept light; tricky words by sight made · does. Values: it's important to eat fruit every day.
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (face gestures, prediction); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a small group at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 30-sec showcase.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (order the frames, answer True/False with thumbs);
  • say the key lines in role: “I love chocolate cake — it's my favourite!”, “Do you like peaches? — No, I don't.”;
  • blend & read Magic-e words kite · bike · smile and read made · does by sight;
  • say “Thank you!” and know it's important to eat fruit (values).

Structure
I'm hungry. · I like ice cream. · I love chocolate cake — it's my favourite. · I don't like oranges / fruit. · Do you like peaches? — No, I don't. · Here you are. · Robot, you're amazing! Thank you.
Form
I like / love / don't like + noun (statement of taste). Do you like + noun? → Yes, I do / No, I don't. (met in L1–L2, consolidated here in story context.)
Pron. watch
weak Do you /dʒə/; contraction don't /dəʊnt/; sentence stress: I DON'T like ORANGES. · exclaim Mmm! Yuck! Amazing! with feeling.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.22 (a) первое знакомство — Magic E: o-e /оу/ · u-e /ю:/ — the last two magics: hop → hope · not → note · cub → cube · tub → tube; trace nose · cube (тетрадь стр.61). Tricky words by sight: made · does.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Frame 2: “Is the food real?” → “No — it isn't real.” “Are they hungry?” → “Yes!” “What do they do?” → “Ring the bell!”
Frame 4: “Does Beth like ice cream?” → “Yes, she likes ice cream.” “What's Molly's favourite?” → “Chocolate cake!”
Frames 5–6 & 8: “Does Jake like fruit?” → “No! He doesn't like fruit.” “What does the robot make?” → “Fruit juice!” “Does Jake like it?” → “Yes — he LOVES it!”

Assumptions: food nouns + I like / don't like / love and Do you like…? Yes I do / No I don't known from U4 L1–L2; What's your favourite…? from U2; Here you are / thank you from the Starter. Phonics: Magic E a-e (U3) and i-e (L1–L2) are known — today the last two, o-e·u-e (ур.22a). Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures while listening; the teacher points, children don't read the speech bubbles alone.

10–20′
📺 Phonics Hero — Magic E: o-e·u-e (a)
board → desks

Speed reading — тетрадь стр.61 №1. All the magic words so far: cake · bike · kite · time · five · late — There and back — fast! Wave the wand on each silent e. Keep it snappy — this is the story lesson, phonics stays light.

The last two magics (стр.61 №2 «Волшебное превращение»). Today the magic e works on o and u! hop… hope! The o says its name — /оу/! cub… cube! The u says — /ю:/! Four transformations, all read both words ✊→🖐: hop → hope · not → note · cub → cube · tub → tube. Kagan — RallyRobin: A reads the short word, B the magic word, swap.

Blend — “Robot Chef” (all respond). 🦝 Speaky robot-blends one sound at a time: /n/ /оу/ /z/… class blends & shouts nose! then points to their own nose. Do nose · bone · cube · mule (picture each time for meaning — the robot waiter has a red NOSE, story link!).

Write it — стр.61 №4 «Пишем» (desks, ~2′). Trace and write: nose · cube — don't forget the magic e! Then tricky words by sight: made · does — flash, chorus, air-write.

Support: keep the picture beside the word; ask for just the vowel “name” (o = /оу/). Extension: fast finishers read one extra magic word from a card (home · cute · flute) → Show me!

22–33′
📺 Story — listen & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 42). Big story picture on screen / books open. What food can you see in the restaurant? — pairs point & name 15 sec, then chorus a few. Pre-teach with gesture: restaurant (chef-hat mime) · ring the bell (ding finger) · robot waiter (stiff robot arms).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “What's in Beth's restaurant?” ✅ ГОТОВ
Screen: the U4 “Find it!” engine on the big story picture (pp. 42–43). · How: Speaky asks, a child taps the food; it lights up. · Pupils: whole class points to their book / calls it — Ice cream! Chocolate cake! An orange! Grapes! · Teacher: 🦝 Where's the ice cream? Point! — 4–5 foods, quick. Оффлайн-резерв: hold up paper food cards, children point in their books & chorus.

Predict (thinking) — before we listen. The food is not real… but a MAGIC bell rings! What will happen? Will the food be real? Thumbs guess: yes (magic!) vs. no. Let's listen and check your guess!

Listen 1 — global (CD2 23). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: Is the waiter a robot? Show me! (thumbs) Was your guess right — is the food real now?

Detail check — True or False? (thumbs up / down, all react). Say a sentence, class shows a thumb: Molly loves chocolate cake. (👍) · Jake likes oranges. (👎 — He doesn't like oranges!) · The waiter is a cat. (👎 — It's a robot!) · Jake loves fruit juice. (👍). Re-point the frame to fix any wrong thumb.

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the story gestures at the right moments: rub tummy (I'm hungry!) · ring the bell · robot arms (frame 3) · hands to heart (I love chocolate cake!) · push away “Yuck!” (I don't like oranges!) · blend the juice (whir!) · cheer Amazing! CCQs between frames (see box above). At frames 5–6 stop: Do you like peaches? — No, I don't! Do you like grapes? — No, I don't!

42–50′
Act out — drama
carpet, groups

Roles. Groups of 3–4: Beth the owner (yellow badge, chef hat, “Welcome to my restaurant!”) · Molly + Jake (red + green, the hungry customers) · the robot waiter (blue, robot arms, “Here you are!”). Model once with a strong group.

All groups act at the same time. Narrate the story slowly, frame by frame; every group plays it in their own spot (robot-walk on the spot!): Welcome to my restaurant! This food isn't real — I'm hungry! Let's ring the bell! Wow, the food is real! Here you are — I love chocolate cake! And here's an orange, Jake. But I don't like oranges! Do you like peaches? — No, I don't! No problem — one minute… Here's a fruit juice! Mmm, amazing! Thank you, robot!

Create (thinking) — make it yours. Before round 2: The robot brings ONE more food — you choose! Do you like it? Each group swaps in their own food: Here's a pizza! — Mmm, I love pizza! It's my favourite! or Here's fish! — Yuck, I don't like fish! (support: point to a food card; extension: invent the whole exchange with Do you like…?).

Swap roles, run it again — keep it to 2 rounds (don't let the drama over-run; Freeze! the moment energy tips). Volunteers: ONE group shows 30 sec for the class — big applause. Values (PB p. 43 ex. 2): It's important to eat… ice cream, meat or FRUIT every day? → Fruit!

Support: quieter children get the robot waiter role (short repeated line “Here you are!” + gesture); a line card in hand. Extension: confident children play Jake / Beth and lead the Do you like…? exchange.
