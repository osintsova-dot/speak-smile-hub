🦝 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Unit 1 “Start speaking” · Lesson 4 — Revision 1 (Lessons 1–3)
Pre-A1 preschoolers (~5 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · REVIEW — stations + team games + reward challenge · chairs + carpet + tables
Week 14.09–20.09 · Lesson 4 of 72🔁 Revision — every 4th lesson12 pupils · whole-group + 3 teams of 4🎧 All by ear — no text, no phonics
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will have consolidated Lessons 1–3 and be more confident/fluent using them in fresh games: warm-up commands, greetings (What's your name? · Hello/Goodbye), feelings (How are you? — I'm…), actions (I can…) and directions (Left! Right!).
  • Subsidiary. Recycle-in-context via 4 revision stations (Kagan rotation) & ONE flagship team reward game (this is the review lesson where a competitive reward game belongs); a language-linked craft settler (magnet/puzzle sort by category — say as you sort); a draw-&-tell finish (pick a favourite & tell).
  • Personal. No new language — pure confidence & fluency; keep teams 4 max (12 = 3×4), fast rotation so nobody waits; every child speaks/moves every round; alternate ⚡/🧘; hold to max 3 game-peaks.
By the end, pupils can…
  • respond to the warm-up commands quickly;
  • ask/answer What's your name? — My name is…;
  • answer How are you? with a feeling;
  • do & say I can… actions and directions;
  • draw & tell one favourite chunk to teacher/partner.

Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ (stations): “When the bell rings — do we STAY or move to the next friend-station?” → move.
ICQ (reward game): “Do the action & say it → THEN roll the ball?” → yes!

Assumptions: children have had L1–L3 → this recycles, no new words. Still near-zero English beyond Unit 1 chunks — keep everything gesture/picture-based. Non-readers/non-writers. Attention 5–7 min → constant ⚡/🧘. Group = 12; the reward game uses 3 teams of 4 (first time we team up — keep it cooperative, everyone scores). Losing is fine: Try again!

Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone freezes, mid-move reset.
Bell / countdown — station change; “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…”.
“Come with me!” 🚂 — train to change zone.
Lesson framework — the shape of EVERY Genki preschool lesson (all 72)

Fixed skeleton — reuse this order in every plan. Keep the ⚡/🧘 rhythm, but max 2–3 game-peaks per lesson. Review lesson = stations + ONE flagship reward game (bowling / basketball) — this is the only lesson type where a competitive reward game belongs.

  • 1 · Greeting 🚪 🧘 — door welcome + Speaky arrives.
  • 2 · Warm-up song ⚡ — Genki Disco Warm Up + song medley.
  • 3 · Core games ⚡/🧘 — revision stations + flagship reward game.
  • 4 · Craft / worksheet block 🧘 — language-linked, here a sort by category.
  • 5 · 📖 Story time 🧘 — S&S big-book (Speaky), choral refrain.
  • 6 · Workbook worksheet — draw & tell 🧘 — draw & tell.
  • 7 · Reflection + Goodbye + Exit 🧘.

▶ Speaky Bowling (review reward game — only in review lessons). 3 teams of 4. Speaky flips a card (action / feeling / command); the player does it & says the chunk — I can swim! / I'm hungry! / Bow! — THEN rolls a soft ball to knock the skittles. Every knocked skittle = a cheer for the whole team.

Well done! Come with me! 🚂 To the tables. Language link — listen → do → say. Give each child a set of small picture cards + a base sheet with 3 labelled “baskets” (a smiley = feelings · a running figure = actions · an arrow = directions).

Mechanic. Speaky names a picture — children sort it into the right basket and say it: happy → I'm happy! / fly → I can fly! / left → Left! Sort & say — every child engaged, calm settle after the reward game.
