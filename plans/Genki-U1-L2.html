🦝 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Unit 1 “Start speaking” · Lesson 2 — What's your name? & How are you?
Pre-A1 preschoolers (~5 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + games + TPR (no reading / writing) · chairs + carpet + tables
Week 07.09–13.09 · Lesson 2 of 72✨ First words: names & feelings12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 All by ear — no text, no phonics
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to ask & answer two social questions — What's your name? — My name is… and How are you? — I'm happy / sad / tired… — and recognise/name 8 feeling words (happy · sad · tired · hungry · cold · great · good · OK).
  • Subsidiary. Keep the door-Speaky-goodbye routine; recycle the L1 warm-up commands inside the Disco song; a language-linked craft settler (sticker-match feelings — match & name); a draw-&-tell finish — draw yourself & tell the partner My name is… I'm happy! (early production).
  • Personal. Every child speaks in a pair every round (Mix-Pair-Share — no single-file queue); give an ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡ stirrer / 🧘 settler; hold to max 3 game-peaks.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say their name with My name is… when asked;
  • ask a partner What's your name?;
  • show & name a feeling and say I'm tired / happy…;
  • answer How are you? with one feeling;
  • draw & tell one chunk (name + feeling) to teacher/partner.

Chunks
What's your name? · My name is… · Nice to meet you!  ·  How are you? · I'm happy! · I'm sad.  ·  Hello / Goodbye
TPR gesture
each feeling = one clear face + body (Speaky models): happy = big smile, sad = rub eyes, tired = yawn, hungry = rub tummy, cold = shiver, great = two thumbs up.
Pron. watch
contraction I'm /aɪm/ as ONE happy word (not “I am”); tired two beats /ˈtaɪəd/; hungry not “angry”; keep name long /neɪm/.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ (Mingle): “When the music stops — do we RUN or do we TALK to a friend?” → talk.
ICQ (feelings): “Show the face AND… say the word?” → yes!

Assumptions: children had Lesson 1 (know Speaky · Disco commands · hello/goodbye) but are still near-zero English → meaning comes from face, body, tone, pictures — NOT translation. Non-readers/non-writers (no phonics, no text worksheets — “writing” = drawing & sticking). Attention 5–7 min → constant ⚡/🧘 rhythm. Group = 12, mostly whole-group on the carpet + shoulder-partner; team games max 4 (12 = 3×4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Shy child won't say their name to a partnerSpeaky answers for them first (My name is Speaky!); the child can just point to themselves — doing counts. Whisper the name together, praise any sound.
They say only the name (“Masha”), drop the chunkModel the whole ribbon every time: My name is Masha. Tap your chest on “My”, big smile — the melody carries the words.
Feelings blur togetherOne clear gesture per feeling, always word + face together, 3×. Play “copy Speaky's face” before naming.
Mingle turns into running / bumpingWalk to slow music with bubble arms (space); Freeze! = stop & find the nearest friend. Model one calm round first.
Over-excitement after a stirrerAlways follow ⚡ with a 🧘 settler; volume control arm; sit back on the carpet.
Transitions (carpet → tables) get chaoticMove as a train: Come with me! 🚂 — one signal, one destination.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…” with fingers shown.
“Come with me!” 🚂 — line up as a train to change zone.
Lesson framework — the shape of EVERY Genki preschool lesson (all 72)

Fixed skeleton — reuse this order in every plan. Keep the ⚡/🧘 rhythm, but max 2–3 game-peaks per lesson — don't over-game.

  • 1 · Greeting 🚪 🧘 — door welcome + Speaky arrives.
  • 2 · Warm-up song ⚡ — always Genki Disco Warm Up (ritual + recycles today's/earlier language).
  • 3 · Core games ⚡/🧘 — 2–3 games max from the Genki bank, each with its song. Everything else = optional/for variety.
  • 4 · Craft / worksheet block 🧘 — every craft carries a language link (listen → do → say). Rotates: applique-by-command · draw-&-tell · magnet/puzzle sort · sticker-match.
  • 5 · 📖 Story time 🧘 — our own S&S big-book (Speaky), Brown-Bear mechanic, choral refrain.
  • 6 · Workbook worksheet — draw & tell 🧘 — child draws (in topic) & tells teacher/partner. Every lesson.
  • 7 · Reflection + Goodbye + Exit 🧘 — “what did we learn” · goodbye song · exit ticket + reward sticker.

Well done! Come with me! 🚂 To the tables. Language link — listen → do → say. Hand out an S&S feelings strip: 4 empty faces on a base sheet + a set of colour feeling-face stickers (happy · sad · tired · hungry).

Mechanic. Speaky commands one feeling — Find… happy! — each child peels the matching sticker, sticks it on a face, and the class choruses I'm happy! Work through the feelings. Match → stick → say — every child engaged.

Countdown: 5… 4… 3… 2… 1… stick it down.
