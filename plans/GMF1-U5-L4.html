🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 5 “Fun on the farm” · Lesson 4 — Vocab+Grammar: Action verbs · Birds can fly, cows can't!
Pre-A1 young learners (~7–8 y.o., emergent readers, continuing group) · 60 min · New vocab + structure together (PPP + TPR) · carpet + desks
Week 18.01–24.01 · Lesson 37✨ VOCAB+GRAM~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4 + pairs
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 6 action verbs — fly, swim, run, walk, climb, jump (Pupil's Book p. 54) — and say what animals can & can't do with can / can't: “Birds can fly. · Cows can't climb. · Horses can swim. · Ducks can't fly.”
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve real prior learning — Unit 5 farm animals (cow, horse, sheep, pig, duck, chicken, goat, dog, cat, frog — L1), “Can you see a…? — Yes, I can / No, I can't” (L2), the story Animal magic where cows can't fly and this sheep can climb trees (L3) — woven into today's target: the animals they know now DO things. Phonics — reading the split digraph i–e /ie/ (bike · slide · drive · kite, JP Book 2); sing the coursebook song Cats can't fly (CD2 44); value: respect what every animal can do.
  • Personal. Fix ONE gesture per verb and one thumb-signal for can 👍 / can't 👎; give an ICQ before every game; every task keeps ALL children responding (all mime at once / stand–sit true–false / RallyRobin pairs / own answer card).
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 6 action verbs with a gesture (fly, swim, run, walk, climb, jump);
  • say an animal ability: “Ducks can swim.” / “Cows can't fly.”;
  • judge true/false: hear “Fish can fly” → No! → correct it;
  • read short i–e words: bike · slide · drive · kite.
Target language
Lexis
flyswimrunwalkclimbjump (recycled: cow, horse, sheep, pig, duck, chicken, goat, dog, cat, frog)
Structure
(Birds) can fly. · (Cows) can't climb. · (Horses) can swim. · (Ducks) can't fly. · Talk Partners: Cats can fly. — No! · Cats can swim. — Yes!
Form
Plural animal + can / can't + base verb (no “-s”, no to): Birds can fly — NOT can flies / can to fly. can = able to.
Pron. watch
weak can /kən/ vs strong can't /kɑːnt/ — hear the final /t/; silent “b” in climb = /klaɪm/; /w/ start in walk /wɔːk/ (not “valk”).
Phonics
Jolly focus: reading split digraph i–e → /ie/ (bike · slide · drive · kite, JP Book 2) · colour sight-word review: green (feeds “It's green”).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Show a bird in the sky vs a cow in a field. “Can a bird fly? 👍 or 👎” → 👍 can. “Can a cow fly?” → 👎 can't.
“Fish can swim — is that YES or NO?” → Yes! (👍) · “Fish can walk?” → No! (👎)
“can = it's OK, it's able to. can't = no, it's not able to.” Thumb up / thumb down every time.

Assumptions: the farm animals are solid from L1–L3; “Can you see a…? — Yes/No” is fresh from L2; can/can't for ability was already felt in the L3 story (cows can't fly · this sheep can climb trees) — today the verbs get named and the sentences get built & used. Emergent readers → real pictures, mime & audio, minimal print. КТП listed sleep/eat/drink, but PB p. 54 core is fly·swim·run·walk·climb·jump → plan follows the book.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
can't sounds the same as canOver-stress the final /t/ and add a big thumb-down 👎: can't! /kɑːnt/. Chant “can 👍 / can't 👎” before the games; a wrong thumb = fix it, don't say the word.
Adds “-s” or “to”: “A bird can flies / to fly”Recast to the plural chunk: Birds can fly. Keep animals PLURAL on the cards (no he/she, no agreement) — the pattern stays clean.
Mixes walk / run or climb / jumpOne clear gesture per verb: fly 🕊✋wave · swim 🏊 · run 👟fast · walk 🚶slow · climb 🧗up · jump ⬆️. Chant with the gesture, then hide the word — only the gesture.
Reads i–e like short i (bike → “bik”)Point to the “magic e”: e at the end makes i say its NAME — /ie/! bike! Contrast bit/bite · kit/kite on the board.
Over-excitement in mime gamesFreeze! resets mid-movement; one animal → one mime → sit; soft voices with the volume-arm signal.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… sit down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset.
