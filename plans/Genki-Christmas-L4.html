Genki preschoolers (~3.5–5 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Christmas block · Lesson 4 of 7✨ shapes + I can see a…!12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to name shapes in Christmas things — triangle · circle · diamond · oval · rectangle · heart · square · star — inside What shape is it? — I can see a star!
  • Subsidiary. Remember-time recycle of all earlier topics + Christmas words so far (colours L3, decorations L2), reused with NEW shape language. New vocab lives ONLY in context. Folder task: match соедини.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; model every line first; every child traces the shape in the air & says it every round; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name 8 shapes with an air-trace gesture;
  • answer What shape is it? — It's a star!;
  • spot a shape: I can see a triangle!;
  • join the Christmas Shapes song;
  • match a shape to the ornament of that shape.

Chunks
What shape is it? · I can see a triangle! · I can see a star!  ·  (recycle) What colour is it? — It's gold! · It's a Christmas tree!
TPR gesture
air-trace each shape with a finger: circle round · triangle tree · star 5 points · heart two humps · square/rectangle box · diamond tilt.
Pron. watch
keep I can see a…! as one bright chunk; a happy point on star!. Don't drill single shapes — tracing & spotting carry it, not accuracy.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: “I show a star — you trace it AND say the shape?” → yes.
ICQ: (worksheet) “Draw a line: shape → the ornament of THAT shape?” → yes.

Assumptions: children had L1–L3 Christmas + the autumn block; circle/star may be familiar, diamond/oval/rectangle likely new. Near-zero English + L1 prompt. Non-readers. Group = 12.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Diamond vs square / oval vs circle mix-upAir-trace big & slow together; “tilt = diamond, stretch = oval”; accept near-misses.
Can't find the shape in the picturePoint & trace it ON the ornament first; then they hunt.
Only a few speak, others watchEveryone traces & says at once; each child has shape cards; ICQ first.
Over-excitement after ⚡🧘 settler + volume-control arm; Freeze! = statue.
Transitions get chaoticTrain: Come with me! — one signal, one destination.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…” with fingers shown.
“Come with me!” 🚌 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana's real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🟢 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Cumulative Q&A recycle lives at the start (Remember time); the final game is on today's Christmas language.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello song (chorus), “choose the greeting → jump/fly to your chair”.
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives from the bus-box & shakes hands with each child (Merry Christmas! How are you?).
  • Remember time — cumulative review with A4 flashcards / what's missing? / app on the board.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, topic song, board app, active Genki games.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheet, a different type each lesson (this lesson: match соедини), always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🟢 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Final game ⚡ — back to the carpet for a fast, fun game on today's language, so the lesson ends on a high note.
  • Closing — Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
