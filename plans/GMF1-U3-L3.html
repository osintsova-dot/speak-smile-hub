🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 3 “At the circus” · Lesson 3 — Story: Fun at the circus
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers · continuers line) · 60 min · Story: listen → read → act out (drama) · carpet + desks
Week 09.11 · Lesson 20✨ STORY~12 pupils · small groups for drama🦸 Phonics Hero ур.18b · ue·er·ar
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow and understand the story “Fun at the circus” (Pupil's Book pp. 30–31, CD1 52) and act it out using key story language: “I've got big feet and I've got purple hair.”, “She's fantastic! He's very strong.”, “Welcome to the circus. This circus is very big.”, “Be careful, Jake!”, “Are you OK? — Yes, I am.”
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve real prior learning — parts of the body + I've / He's / She's got… (L1–L2 this week) woven straight into the story (the children describe the circus performers), PLUS the Magic Bike and Let's make a… from U1's Toby story, and adjectives + colours from U2 (big feet, purple hair, red nose). Phonics — Phonics Hero ур.18 (b) закрепление: digraphs ue·er·ar (match → mini-dictation → tricky strip so, be → story «Звезда над фермой»), kept light + sight words want · because. Values: ride a bike safely — wear a helmet, two hands (PB p. 31).
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (gesture responses, prediction); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a small group at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 30-sec showcase. Keep phonics light — this is the fullest lesson of the unit.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (number the frames, answer with gestures);
  • say the key lines in role: “I've got big feet!”, “She's fantastic!”, “Be careful, Jake!”;
  • describe a circus performer: “He's got purple hair.”, “She's got long hair.”;
  • read & write ue/er/ar words (car · star · glue), read the phonics story «Звезда над фермой» and want · because by sight;
  • say why we ride a bike safely — helmet + two hands (values).

Structure
I've got (big feet). I've got (purple hair). · He's / She's got (purple hair / long hair). · She's fantastic! He's very strong. · Welcome to the circus. This circus is very big. · Be careful, Jake! · Are you OK? — Yes, I am.
Form
I've / He's / She's got + (adjective) + noun. have got for possession/description — met in L2, retrieved & used in context here (full focus was L2, extended in L4).
Pron. watch
connected I've got /aɪv gɒt/, He's got / She's got /hiːz gɒt/ /ʃiːz gɒt/ (one beat, don't split); sentence stress: I've got BIG FEET. · He's got PURPLE hair.
Phonics
Phonics Hero ур.18 (b) закрепление: ue /uː/ · er /ɜː/ · ar /ɑː/ — match car, star, glue, fern · dictation her, car, star, glue · tricky strip so, be · story «Звезда над фермой» (тетрадь стр.50–51) · sight words: want · because. Kept light — story is the core today.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Frame 1: “Can Beth juggle?” → “No — I can't juggle.” “What does Jake say?” → “Let's make a circus! Magic Bike, can you help?” (the Magic Bike is back!)
Frame 4: “Look at Molly the clown! Point — big feet?” (👍) “What colour is her hair?” → “Purple!” → She's got purple hair.
Frames 6–7: “Jake has ONE hand on the bike — is that safe?” → “No! Be careful, Jake!” → CRASH! Frame 8: “Is Jake OK?” → “Yes, I am!” “And Toby?” → “He's got purple hair!” (funny twist)

Assumptions: parts of the body + I've / He's / She's got known from L1–L2 this week; adjectives (big/long) + colours (purple/red) from U2; children know Toby the dog & the Magic Bike from U1's story. Phonics: ue·er·ar met last lesson (Phonics Hero ур.18a, стр.49) — today consolidates. Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures while listening; the teacher points, children don't read the speech bubbles alone. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
New words block the story (acrobat · juggle · performer)Pre-teach 3 with picture + gesture before listening: acrobat (arms out, balance) · juggle (hands toss) · clown (big smile + honk nose). Say & do together.
Children call answers over the audio“Listening hands” rule — hands cupped behind ears while the CD plays; talk only when it stops.
I've got vs He's/She's got mixed upAnchor gestures: I've got = point to self · He's/She's got = point to the performer. “Show me!” drill before the story.
Drama turns to chaos (running around)Movements are ON THE SPOT (magic-bike ride = pedal sitting; acrobat = balance in place); roles fixed by colour badges; “Freeze!” resets.
Frame cards ordered by one child in the pairRule “one card each turn, take turns” (RallyRobin with cards); praise pairs where both hands work.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — raise/lower your arm = louder/quieter → settler.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
“Freeze!” — statue, mid-movement reset (essential in drama).

3–8′
Retrieval → into the story
carpet, circle

Retrieve L1–L2 woven into today. Point to yourself, quick rhythm (touch & say): I've got a nose! I've got two eyes! I've got big feet! Class copies with actions. Then point to Speaky: He's got a red nose! He's got big ears! Do 4–5 body parts fast — everyone touches the part on themselves.

Show-me — everyone reacts (U2 adjectives + colours). Call & all touch/show: Show me BIG feet! Show me a purple crayon on your hair! Point to your red nose! Then: He's got… what colour hair? — Purple! (retrieves colours + adjectives from U2 into body-part talk).

Bridge (U1 link). Remember Toby the dog… and the MAGIC BIKE? It's back! Today Jake says: “Let's make a circus!” What happens? Let's find out! Signal: Class? — Yes!

 Speaky · body-part flashcards · a purple crayon · (Magic Bike picture from U1)
8–10′
🎵 Bridge
stand at carpet

Let's make a funny circus monster! Sing Genki «Let's Make A Monster» (25) with actions — I've got two heads… three arms… four eyes! This primes the exact story pattern I've got + (number/adjective) + body part, then → Sit down — sound time!

10–18′
🦸 Phonics Hero — ue·er·ar (b, light)
board → desks

Recall the three anchors (quick). Doctor's a-a-ar! · whirring mixer errr! · sticky glue ue! — all copy each sound + gesture. Then blend the six words chorally (тетрадь стр.50 №1 «Соединяем звуки»): /g/ /l/ /ue/… glue! /s/ /t/ /ar/… star! — glue · fern · her · car · star · farm, finger under each window.

Match — стр.50 №2 «Прочитай и соедини». Read car · star · glue · fern together, then every child draws the line to the picture in their own тетрадь. RallyCoach: partner checks — Good job!

Mini-dictation — стр.50 №3 (kept short). Now YOU write. Listen: car… segment on fingers together → all write → Show me! → reveal & self-check. Two–three words from her, car, star, glue — story is the core today.

Tricky strip — стр.50 №4. so · be — read for speed: left→right, then right→left! + today's sight pair want · because — chorus ×3, air-write, in a sentence: I want to go to the circus, because it's fantastic!

Story — стр.51 «Звезда над фермой» (settler). Read the 5 frames like a comic, finger under the words, chorus each line: It is winter. A star in the dark… The draw-task («Читаю и рисую: A star in the dark») goes home tonight. Finish: colour the star «Я молодец!» (стр.50).

20–31′
📺 Story — listen & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 30). Big story picture on screen / books open. Before you read: can you see a clown in the story? (PB prompt) — pairs point 10 sec. Pre-teach 3 with gesture: acrobat (arms out, balance) · juggle (toss hands) · clown (big smile, honk nose).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “Who's at the circus?” ✅ ГОТОВ · 🎮 find-it-u3
Screen: the U1 “Find it!” engine on the big story picture. · How: 🦝 asks, a child taps; it lights up. · Pupils: whole class points to their book / calls it — The clown! The acrobat! Toby! The magic bike! then describe: She's got purple hair. He's got big feet. · Teacher: Where's the acrobat? Point! What has the clown got? — 4–5 taps, quick. Offline reserve: point on the big printed picture / in books.

Predict (thinking) — before we listen. Jake rides the magic bike with ONE hand. Uh-oh… What will happen? Safe — or a big CRASH? Thumbs guess: two hands “safe” vs. hands-on-cheeks “Oh no!”. Let's listen and check your guess!

Listen 1 — global (CD1 52 / animated story). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: Is it a real circus… or the magic bike again? Was your guess right — a crash? (thumbs)

Detail check — True or False? (thumbs up / down, all react). Say a sentence, class shows a thumb: Beth can juggle. (👎 — I can't juggle!) · Molly the clown has got purple hair. (👍) · Toby is very strong. (👍) · Jake has got two hands on the bike. (👎 — one hand!) · At the end, Toby has got purple hair. (👍). Re-point the frame to fix any wrong thumb.

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the story gestures at the right moments: “I can't juggle” (toss + shrug) · magic-bike ride (pedal, “Ring! Ring!”) · clap for the acrobat (She's fantastic!) · honk for the clown · strong arms for Toby · “Be careful!” (wag finger) · CRASH! (hands on cheeks) · point to Toby's purple hair & laugh. Stop at frame 4: What has Molly got? — Big feet and purple hair!

32–40′
▶ Build the story
desks, pairs

▶ Story Builders (picture re-skin for non-readers — all pairs work at once). Each pair gets 8 mini frame-cards of “Fun at the circus”, shuffled.

Order it. Put the story in order — one card each, take turns! (RallyRobin with cards: A places one, B places the next.) Countdown 2 min. ICQ: Do we grab all the cards or take turns? (→ take turns!)

Check with the audio. Replay CD1 52: pairs touch each card as they hear it — self-check, fix the order. Then choral retell, teacher holds up big frames one by one, class says the line: I can't juggle. Let's make a circus! · Welcome to the circus! · Here's Annie the acrobat. She's fantastic! · I've got big feet and purple hair! · Here's amazing Toby! He's very strong. · Jake and his magic bike! · Oh no — CRASH! · Are you OK? Yes, I am! Toby's got purple hair!

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Circus Story-Sequence 1–8 ✅ ГОТОВ · 🎮 u3-story-circus
Screen: the 8 story frames (make a circus → welcome → acrobat → clown → strong Toby → Jake & the bike → CRASH! → “Are you OK?” + purple-hair Toby) appear shuffled in a row, each with an empty number badge 1–8. · How: tap two frames to swap them (or drag); a “Check” button lights the correct frames green, a ✅ + cheer plays when the order is right. · Pupils: the class votes the order — Number one — “I can't juggle!” Number two — the circus! — and gesture each frame. · Teacher: take votes (Which is first? Hands up!), tap as they decide, then replay CD1 52 to confirm. Mirrors the paired frame-cards for a whole-class check. Offline reserve: big printed frame-cards on the board, magnets, class numbers them.

Support: hand that pair only the 5 clearest frames (make a circus → clown → strong Toby → crash → “Are you OK?”) and number the first one. Extension: after ordering, say one whole line for each frame from memory before the audio check.

40–49′
Act out — drama
carpet, groups

Roles. Groups of 3–4: Ringmaster (red badge, “Welcome to the circus!”) · Molly the clown (yellow, “I've got big feet and purple hair!”) · Jake on the magic bike (blue, “Look! One hand!”) · Beth / audience (green, “Wow! She's fantastic! Be careful, Jake!”). Model once with a strong group.

All groups act at the same time. Narrate slowly, frame by frame; every group plays it in their own spot (bike ride & acrobat balance ON THE SPOT): Let's make a circus! Magic Bike, help! · Welcome to the circus! · Here's the acrobat — she's fantastic! · Here's the clown — I've got big feet and purple hair! · Here's amazing Toby — he's very strong! · Jake and his magic bike — one hand! Be careful! · CRASH! Oh no! · Are you OK? — Yes, I am!

Create (thinking) — make it yours. Before round 2: Add ONE more circus performer — you choose! What has your performer got? Each group adds a line: Here's Max! He's got long arms and red hair! He's fantastic! (support: point to a body-part card; extension: invent the whole introduction).

Swap roles, run it again — keep it to 2 rounds (Freeze! the moment energy tips). Volunteers: ONE group shows 30 sec for the class — big applause. Values (PB p. 31 ex. 2): Jake used one hand and crashed! When you ride a bike, what do you wear? What do you use? → A helmet! Two hands!

Support: quieter children play the audience (short lines Wow! Be careful! + gesture); a line card in hand. Extension: confident children play the Ringmaster / clown and lead the exchange.

 role badges ×4 colours · body-part cards · a “magic bike” prop / hoop · PB p. 31 (values)
49–54′
▶ Describe the performer
desks, all at once

▶ Guess my performer! (targeted settler — He's/She's got… + body + colour; every child works every round). Each child has a strip with a blank clown/acrobat outline and a few coloured body-part cards (hair · nose · feet · eyes).

🦝 Speaky describes; all build on their strip: My clown has got purple hair! → all place purple hair; He's got a red nose! · She's got big feet! 5–6 clues, instant show-me each time (Point to it! What's got purple hair?). Then pairs: A describes their performer, B builds it — My acrobat has got long hair and green eyes. (RallyRobin, take turns).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “Build the clown” (model & check) ✅ ГОТОВ · 🎮 find-it-u3
Screen: the U1 “Find it!” board with a blank clown + coloured body-part pieces. · How: Speaky says the clue, a child taps the piece onto the clown; it snaps if correct. · Pupils: first build it on their own paper strip, then one child mirrors on screen — She's got purple hair! class checks: Yes! Purple hair! · Teacher: use it to model clue 1, then just for the show-me check so every child stays on their own strip. Offline reserve: paper strip + cards only.

54–57′
Reflection + Closing
carpet

▶ RallyRobin retell (pairs, take turns). Big frames stay on the board in order. Tell the story to your partner — one line each, take turns! A: Let's make a circus! → B: Welcome to the circus! → A: She's fantastic! … (support: point & say one word per frame; extension: full lines to the end).

Then: Which performer do you like — the acrobat, the clown or strong Toby? Point and tell your partner! (20 sec each). “I can…” badge: Colour your badge — I can act out the circus story!
