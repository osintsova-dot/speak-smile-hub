🦝 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Danger · Lesson 36 — Make a Face — nose, mouth, eyes, ears, hair
Genki preschoolers (~5–6 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Danger level · L36 · Lesson 3 of 8✨ Make a Face · Put on the… / Draw a…12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to name 5 face parts (nose, mouth, eyes, ears, hair) and build a face — Put on the nose! Draw a big mouth!
  • Subsidiary. Cumulative Remember-time recycle of greetings, numbers, sea & body parts — woven into the face; the S&S big-book; a build-a-face stick & say folder sheet.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; face parts always in a command (Put on the eyes!, not a bare list); every child touches / places a feature every round; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name nose, mouth, eyes, ears, hair;
  • follow Put on the…! Draw a…!;
  • touch each face part on command;
  • say what's missing on a face;
  • build & name their own funny face.

TPR gesture
point to your own nose / mouth / eyes / ears / hair; put on = mime placing; draw = finger-draw in the air.
Pron. watch
mouth /maʊθ/ (tongue out for -th) · hair /heə/ (not "here") · keep Put on the… a light chunk.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: "Do we PLACE the part or watch?" → place.
ICQ: "Put on AND say the part?" → yes.

Assumptions: children know body parts from L35 (head · eyes · ears) → the face is a natural zoom-in. Meaning from pointing, felt features & the L1 prompt. Non-readers/non-writers. Attention 5–7 min → ⚡/🧘. Group = 12.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Mixing eyes/ears (from L35)Point & touch together; eyes = see, ears = hear (mime); the song repeats both.
"mouth" said without -thModel tongue-tip playfully; over-do it; accept a good try.
Everyone wants to place a feature at onceEach child holds ONE feature card; place in a quick ROTATION when their part is called.
Face gets silly / off-taskSilly is fine & motivating — just always name the part in English as you place it.
Transitions get chaoticTrain: Come with me! — one signal, one destination.
Classroom management — signals
"Class? — Yes!" — you: "Class!" → children stop & echo "Yes!"
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
"Speaky says…" — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
"Freeze!" / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — "5… 4… 3… 2… 1…" with fingers shown.
"Come with me!" 🚌 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana's real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🟢 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Finale = cumulative Q&A chain + the Thank You! song ritual.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello / Good Morning song, "choose the greeting → jump/run/swim/fly to your chair".
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives on the bus-box & shakes hands with each child.
  • Remember time — cumulative Q&A of everything so far (thumb-up/down like a game), growing lesson by lesson, woven into today's topic.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, Genki topic song, board app, active games.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheets, a different type each lesson (build & stick / colour-by-command / draw & tell / listen & tick / match соедини / sort & stick / count), always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🟢 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Remember time 2 + Closing — run the growing Q&A chain, Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.

▶ Build-a-face relay. Big blank face on the board. Each child holds ONE feature card. Speaky calls Put on the nose! → the child with the nose runs, places it & everyone shouts Nose! Quick rotation until the face is complete.

▶ Touch your face. Speaky says Touch your hair! Touch your mouth! — all 12 touch & name. Sometimes a trick (no "Speaky says") → freeze.

▶ What's missing? (Kim's game). Show a full face → close eyes → remove one feature → What's missing? — The mouth! Everyone guesses chorally, then a child re-places it.
