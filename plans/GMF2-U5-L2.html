Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to ask & answer about group actions: “What are you doing? — We're (skating)! / Are you (dancing)? — Yes, we are. / No, we aren't.”
  • Subsidiary. Understand the frozen-lake dialogue (CD2 41); listen & tick (CD2 42); team-mime interviews; write a we-dialogue (AB format).
  • Personal. Plural short answers full form (Yes, we are — never “Yes we're”); pairs answer TOGETHER — that's the whole point of we.
By the end, pupils can…
  • ask “What are you doing? / Are you…?” to a group;
  • answer as a pair: “Yes, we are / No, we aren't”;
  • run a team mime-interview.

Structure
What are you doing? — We're (making a snowman).  ·  Are you (eating)? — Yes, we are. / No, we aren't.
Form
are + we/you(pl.) + -ing; short answers: Yes, we are / No, we aren't. Contrast with singular I'm-answers.
Pron. watch
aren't /ɑːnt/ one beat; weak are questions /əjə/; chorus timing — answering together IS the drill.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Are you skating? — you two, together. Answer?” → Yes, we are!
“Can we answer ‘Yes, we're’?” → no — Yes, we ARE.

Assumptions: We're + actions fresh from L1. Are-you questions known in singular (U4) — plural answer is the step. Pair-answering needs choral discipline, which the class now has. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).
