Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say what they are doing right now and ask: “What are you doing? — I'm (climbing). / Are you (swimming)? — Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.”
  • Subsidiary. Understand the trail dialogue (CD2 20); listen & tick (CD2 21); -ing spelling notice (run→running, swim→swimming — just flag); mime-based guessing — the natural home of Present Continuous.
  • Personal. Ground every sentence in a VISIBLE action — continuous means NOW, so someone must always be doing it; short answers full form.
By the end, pupils can…
  • ask “What are you doing? / Are you…?” correctly;
  • answer “I'm …ing” about a real/mimed action;
  • give true short answers “Yes, I am / No, I'm not.”

Structure
What are you doing? — I'm (climbing a hill).  ·  Are you (swimming)? — Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
Form
am/are + verb-ing. Doubling flag: run→running, swim→swimming. Short answers: Yes, I am (never “Yes, I'm”).
Pron. watch
-ing /ɪŋ/ no hard g; I'm /aɪm/ smooth; weak are in What_are_you /wɒtəjə/.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“I'm climbing — am I doing it NOW or every day?” → now, this second!
“Are you swimming? — you're sitting. Answer?” → No, I'm not!

Assumptions: can-sentences + nature words active (L1). Present Continuous heard in GMF1 stories receptively — production is new and is THE grammar of units 4–5. Mime makes it concrete. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Dialogue & grammar box

Listen & repeat (act. 1, CD2 20). Echo with role voices (puffing climber!).

Grammar box (act. 2). Board: What are you doing? — I'm …ing. · Are you …ing? — Yes, I am / No, I'm not. Circle am/are; raise the -ing card. NOW-gesture explained.

Live drill. YOU mime, class asks together: What are you doing? → you answer; then a pupil mimes, class asks, mimer answers. 5 quick rotations — the question gets automatic through repetition.
