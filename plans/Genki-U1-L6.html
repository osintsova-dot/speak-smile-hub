Pre-A1 preschoolers (~5 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + role-play + puppets (no reading / writing) · chairs + carpet + tables

Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to use 6 polite phrases in the right moment — Thank you! · Please! · You're welcome! · I'm sorry! · Excuse me! · Goodbye! — through song, gift-passing & simple puppet role-plays.
  • Subsidiary. Fold politeness into the daily routine (say Thank you to Speaky/friends all lesson); a language-linked craft settler (make a simple stick-puppet, then it speaks the phrases); a draw-&-tell finish — draw someone waving goodbye & tell Goodbye! Thank you!
  • Personal. Model manners warmly all lesson (be the example); every child speaks in a pair every round; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘; hold to max 3 game-peaks.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say Thank you! when they get something;
  • ask with Please! and reply You're welcome!;
  • say I'm sorry! / Excuse me! at the right moment;
  • make a puppet speak the phrases;
  • draw & tell Goodbye! Thank you! to teacher/partner.

TPR gesture
thank you = hand on heart, small bow · please = praying hands · sorry = hand on chest, sad face · excuse me = tap shoulder · you're welcome = open palms · goodbye = wave.
Pron. watch
Thank you soft /θ/ (tongue tip — but don't over-drill at 5); You're welcome as one warm phrase; Excuse me /ɪkˈskjuːz/ — keep it light & polite, not shouted.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ (gift pass): “When I give you the gift — what do we say?” → Thank you!
ICQ (puppet): “Does YOUR puppet talk, or you?” → the puppet (hides shyness).

Assumptions: children have had L1–L5; feelings/greetings solid. Politeness is best taught situationally (real moments) not as a list — weave it through the lesson. Near-zero English beyond unit chunks · non-readers/non-writers · attention 5–7 min · group = 12, whole-group + shoulder-partner. The puppet lowers speaking anxiety — shy children “speak” through it.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
Phrases blur (thank you vs sorry)Anchor each to a clear gesture + a mini-situation (drop something = sorry; get a gift = thank you). Act it out, don't explain.
Shy child won't speak the phraseThe puppet speaks for them; or whisper it together. Doing the gesture counts.
Gift-pass gets grabby / “I want it!”Set the rule: gentle hands, please to ask, thank you to receive, pass on. Model one calm round first; music controls the pace.
Puppet-making mess / off-taskPre-cut parts; one instruction at a time; Speaky commands each step; countdown to finish.
Over-excitement after ⚡🧘 settler after each ⚡; volume-control arm; Freeze!
TransitionsTrain Come with me! 🚂 — one signal, one destination.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter.
“Speaky says…” — do it only when Speaky says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone freezes, reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…”.
“Come with me!” 🚂 — train to change zone.
Lesson framework — the shape of EVERY Genki preschool lesson (all 72)

Fixed skeleton — reuse this order in every plan. Keep the ⚡/🧘 rhythm, but max 2–3 game-peaks per lesson — don't over-game.

  • 1 · Greeting 🚪 🧘 — door welcome + Speaky arrives.
  • 2 · Warm-up song ⚡ — Genki Disco Warm Up (ritual + recycles earlier language).
  • 3 · Core games ⚡/🧘 — 2–3 games max, each with its song.
  • 4 · Craft / worksheet block 🧘 — language-linked. Here: make a stick-puppet that speaks the phrases.
  • 5 · 📖 Story time 🧘 — S&S big-book (Speaky), choral refrain.
  • 6 · Workbook worksheet — draw & tell 🧘.
  • 7 · Reflection + Goodbye + Exit 🧘.

Well done! Come with me! 🚂 To the tables. Language link — listen → do → say. Each child makes a simple S&S stick-puppet (pre-cut Speaky/friend face on a lolly stick; stick on eyes/smile).

Mechanic (as they build). Speaky commands a step + a phrase: Give your puppet a smile — say “Thank you!” When done, the puppet bows & speaks: children make their puppet say Thank you! · Please! · Goodbye! to a neighbour's puppet. Build → say — every child engaged; the puppet is used right away.
