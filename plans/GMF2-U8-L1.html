Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to name 10 places in a town and say what we do there: “We buy bread at the (baker's). / We catch a train at the (train station).”
  • Subsidiary. Sing the town song (CD3 44) + stickers chant; retrieve There's/There are (U7) and food shopping (U2), woven onto the town; place ↔ function matching (readers); build the classroom TOWN MAP that lives through the unit.
  • Personal. The floor town-map is the unit's engine — build it today with ceremony; possessive-'s shop names (baker's, chemist's) are chunks, not grammar.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name the 10 places;
  • match place ↔ what we do there;
  • say “There's a bank in our town.”

Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“Where do we buy medicine — the baker's or the chemist's?” → the chemist's!
“Baker'S — why the little 's? It's the baker's SHOP.” → у пекаря!

Assumptions: Shopping language (U2) and There-grammar (U7) feed straight in. Norilsk kids know these places in their town — real-map talk lands. chemist's/baker's are exotic — pictures anchor them. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

Presentation · build the town
carpet → floor map

Floor map ceremony. Big paper “streets” on the floor. Present each place card, then a child places it on the map: What's this? It's the… fire station! Where does it go? Choral ×3 + sound/gesture each (siren! stamp letters! shhh-museum!).

Function link. Per place: What do we do here? We buy bread! — function card laid beside; class echoes the pair.

▶ Take Off–Touch Down: Stand up if we buy something there… baker's! …car park!

💪 Support: place + sound is enough. ⭐ Extension: full function sentence: We catch a train at the train station.
