Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to say the oo/ui tongue twister («A kangaroo and a goose in a swimsuit drink fruit juice by the pool»), talk about British garden wildlife, and read fact files & write their own animal fact file.
  • Subsidiary. Textbook phonics: oo/ui = /uː/; culture — birds, hedgehogs, foxes, squirrels in UK gardens vs our yard; literacy — text type fact files (gorillas / polar bears), got-language in real information.
  • Personal. Fact-file writing is the deliverable — protect 15 minutes; keep the culture mime bursts short.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the twister fast ×3;
  • name 3 UK garden animals and compare with ours;
  • write a 5-line fact file about a chosen animal.

Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“goose and juice — same sound? Same letters?” → same /uː/, different letters.
“A fact file — a story or TRUE information?” → true information.

Assumptions: got-family complete after L1–L4; animals active. Fact-file reading needs the two texts read chorally with support. Garden wildlife maps onto “кто живёт у нас во дворе”.

Block 3 · Literacy · Fact files
carpet → desks

Before you read. PB p.35 photos: Where do gorillas live — snow or rainforest? Guess!

Listen & read (CD2 10). Both fact files chorally with finger-tracking; after each: quick quiz — They eat fish and meat — gorillas or polar bears? — all point to the photo.

Write your fact file (notebooks, AB p.32 format). Frame: Name · Habitat · Colour · Size · They've got… · They eat… Pairs pick any animal (unit words or beyond), fill all six slots, draw. Two pairs present; class guesses the animal before the name is read!

💪 Support: word bank per slot on the board. ⭐ Extension: add a “They can…” bonus line.
