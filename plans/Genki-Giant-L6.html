🦝 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Giant · Lesson 60 — What time is it? — half past · quarter past · quarter to
Genki preschoolers (~6 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Giant level · L60 · Lesson 6 of 8🕜 half past · quarter to / past12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to tell half & quarter times — What time is it? — It's half past two! It's quarter to three!
  • Subsidiary. Cumulative recycle of o'clock (L59) & numbers — woven into a daily-routine chain; the S&S big-book; a draw-the-hands folder sheet.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; a time always on the clock with the hand shown; every child sets & says every round; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show o'clock, half past, quarter to / past on a clock;
  • ask What time is it?;
  • answer It's half past two!;
  • link a time to a daily routine;
  • draw the hands to show a time.

Chunks
What time is it? — It's half past two! · It's quarter past three! · It's quarter to four!  ·  (recycle) o'clock 1–12
TPR gesture
hands as clock: half past = down · quarter past = right · quarter to = left; point to the routine picture.
Pron. watch
half /hɑːf/ (silent l) · quarter /ˈkwɔːtə/ · keep the whole time as a smooth chunk.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: "Do we SHOW the time on the clock or guess?" → show it.
ICQ: "Say the WHOLE time (half past two)?" → yes.

Assumptions: children can do o'clock from L59 → half & quarter are the next step on the same clock. Meaning from the moving hand, routine pictures & the L1 prompt. Non-readers/non-writers. Attention 5–7 min → ⚡/🧘. Group = 12.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
"half past" too abstractShow the hand pointing straight DOWN every time; a routine picture anchors it.
quarter to / quarter past mixedLeft = to, right = past; big body-clock arms; slow & repeat.
Long chunk forgottenChant "half — past — two" in three beats; the song carries it.
Bored if too hardKeep it playful with routines (breakfast / bath / bed time); lots of moving hands.
Transitions get chaoticTrain: Come with me! — one signal, one destination.
Classroom management — signals
"Class? — Yes!" — you: "Class!" → children stop & echo "Yes!"
Volume control 🖐 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
"Speaky says…" — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
"Freeze!" / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — "5… 4… 3… 2… 1…" with fingers shown.
"Come with me!" 🚌 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana's real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🟢 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Finale = cumulative Q&A chain + the Thank You! song ritual.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello / Good Morning song, "choose the greeting → jump/run/swim/fly to your chair".
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives on the bus-box & shakes hands with each child.
  • Remember time — cumulative Q&A of everything so far (thumb-up/down like a game), growing lesson by lesson, woven into today's topic.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, Genki topic song, board app, active games.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheets, a different type each lesson (map-build / match соедини / colour-by-command / count & stick craft / make-a-clock / draw the hands / draw & tell / sort & stick), always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🟢 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Remember time 2 + Closing — run the growing Q&A chain, Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
