Genki preschoolers (~5–6 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Giant level · Lesson 6 of 8✨ half past · quarter to / past12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to tell times past the hour — What time is it? — It’s half past two. It’s quarter to three. — recognising the half-past clock face & answering (o’clock recycled).
  • Subsidiary. Remember-time recycle (o’clock from L5 · numbers · daily routine); folder worksheet (put-in-order); a moving-hands clock.
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; accept one-word answers, model the full chunk back; every child speaks/moves every round; ICQ before each game; alternate ⚡/🧘.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say It’s half past …;
  • recognise the half-past hand-down clock;
  • order daily-routine pictures by time;
  • ask & answer What time is it?;
  • join the whole What time is it? song.

Chunks
What time is it? — It’s half past two. · It’s quarter to three.  ·  (recycle) It’s … o’clock.
TPR gesture
o’clock hand points up · half past hand points down · quarter past hand right · quarter to hand left — big arm as the hand.
Pron. watch
keep half past together as one chunk; don’t over-drill quarter to/past — recognition is enough at this age.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: “Half past — hand UP or DOWN?” → down.
ICQ: “Do we say the number OR It’s half past …?” → the whole chunk.

Assumptions: children secured o’clock in L5. Half past is the main new item; quarter to/past kept to recognition only. Some English + L1 prompt. Non-readers — clock faces only. Group = 12.

Lesson framework — Oksana’s real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🌱 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Cumulative Q&A recycle lives at the start (Remember time); the final game is on today’s language.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello song (chorus), “choose the greeting → jump/run/swim/fly to your chair”.
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives & shakes hands with each child (Hello! How are you?).
  • Remember time — cumulative review with A4 flashcards / Genki Karuta / app on the board.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, topic song, board app, active games.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheet, a different type each lesson, always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🌱 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Final game ⚡ — back to the carpet for a fast, fun game on today’s language, ending on a high note.
  • Closing — Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
