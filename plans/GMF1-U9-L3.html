🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 9 “Day and night” · Lesson 3 — Story: The wobbly tooth
Pre-A1 young learners (~7 y.o., emergent readers) · 60 min · Story: listen → read → act out (drama) · carpet + desks · ⭐ FINAL story of the course — the Magic Bike's last ride
Week 26.04–02.05 · Lesson 64✨ STORY~12 pupils · small groups for dramaПродолжающая линия🦸 Phonics Hero · свободное чтение: истории Части 3
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to follow, understand and act out the story “The wobbly tooth” (Pupil's Book pp. 94–95, CD4 13) using key story language: “Good morning. Are you ready for breakfast? — Yes, I am. I have breakfast every morning.”, “What's the matter? — I've got a wobbly tooth!”, “Let's play volleyball!”, “Can you see my tooth? — No, it isn't in the grass.”, “It's looking for your tooth. — Magic Bike, you're incredible!”.
  • Subsidiary. Retrieve real prior learning across the whole course, woven straight into the story: prepositions in / on / under (U1 · U6 — searching for the lost tooth), body parts tooth · teeth · hands · feet (U3 — Values), food & I'm hungry (U4 — breakfast/dinner), outdoor play volleyball (U8) + present-simple daily routines from this unit (I have breakfast every morning · wash your hands · brush your teeth · go to bed). Phonics — Phonics Hero свободное чтение: истории Части 3 (Magic E, тетрадь стр.53–65), light + the story-tied th words (tooth · teeth) + sight words you · your · are. Values: look after your teeth — brush before bed.
  • Personal. Keep story listening active (gestures, prediction); manage drama noise with Freeze; every child acts in a small group at the same time — no “2 on stage, 10 watching” until the final 30-sec showcase. Give this last story a warm, celebratory send-off.
By the end, pupils can…
  • show they understand the story (number the frames 1–5, answer True/False with thumbs);
  • say the key lines in role: “I've got a wobbly tooth!”, “Can you see my tooth? — No, it isn't in the grass.”, “Magic Bike, you're incredible!”;
  • read a Magic-E story (Part 3) aloud with a partner and read you · your · are by sight;
  • ask “Have you got a wobbly tooth?” and answer “Yes, I have / No, I haven't” (Values: brush your teeth before bed).

Form
Present simple routine: I have + meal + every morning. · Negative It isn't + in / on / under + the + place. · Have got: I've got a … / Have you got …? (receptive; recycled from U3).
Pron. watch
voiceless th /θ/ — tooth · teeth · three · thin (tongue between teeth, not /t/ or /f/); weak in the /ɪn ðə/; sentence stress: It isn't in the GRASS.

Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Frame 2: “What's the matter with Jake?” → “He's got a wobbly tooth!” (wobble a finger)
Frame 4: “Oh no! They play volleyball… now where's the tooth?” → “It's lost / gone!” “Is it in the grass?” → “No — it isn't in the grass!”
Frames 7–8: “Who is looking for the tooth?” → “The Magic Bike!” “Where is the tooth in the end?” → “Under his pillow!”

Assumptions: daily routines + I have breakfast every morning known from U9 L1–L2; in / on / under from U1/U6; tooth/teeth/hands/feet & I've got from U3; hungry/breakfast/dinner from U4; volleyball from U8; children know Jake, Toby the dog & the recurring Magic Bike. Emergent readers → they FOLLOW the pictures & the teacher's pointing while listening; they don't decode the speech bubbles alone. Retrieve the WHOLE course, woven into today. Fast “My day” mime chain — you call, ALL do the routine action (from U9 L1–L2): Wake up! Wash your face! Brush your TEETH! Have breakfast! Go to school! Snap the beat.

Show-me — everyone moves (U1 / U6 prepositions). Speaky “loses” his tooth in a little box: Where's my tooth? Is it IN the box? ON the box? UNDER the box? — three gestures: in (hand inside fist) · on (flat on top) · under (below). Call fast, all react.

Body + food quick-fire (U3 · U4). Point to your teeth! Your hands! Your feet! then Are you hungry? What do you have for breakfast? — thumbs / one-word answers.

📺 Story — listen, read & follow
carpet, books/screen

Before you read (PB p. 94). Big story picture on screen / books open. Pre-question from the book: Where is the magic bike in the story? Let's look for it! Pairs point & name what they see 15 sec, then chorus. Pre-teach with gesture: wobbly tooth (wobble finger) · magic bike (sparkle fingers) · volleyball (mime a bounce) · incredible (thumbs-up “Wow!”).

🎮 Интерактив (проектор): Find it! — “What's in Jake's day?” ✅ ГОТОВ · 🎮 find-it-u9
Screen: the U9 “Find it!” engine on the big story picture. · How: Speaky asks, a child taps the object; it lights up. · Pupils: whole class points to their book / calls it — Breakfast! The garden! The volleyball! The magic bike! · Teacher: 🦝 Where's the magic bike? Point! — 4–5 things, quick (find the hidden bike in an early frame — hook).

Predict (thinking) — before we listen. Jake's tooth is wobbly… I think it will fall out! Who will find it? The dog? Mum? Or… the MAGIC BIKE? Thumbs vote (dog / mum / bike). Let's listen and check your guess!

Listen 1 — global (CD4 13). “Listening hands” behind ears. Teacher points frame by frame; children follow the pictures. Gist check: Does the tooth fall out? Show me! (thumbs) Who finds it — was your guess right?

Detail check — True or False? (thumbs up / down, all react). Say a sentence, class shows a thumb: Jake has got a wobbly tooth. (👍) · They play football in the garden. (👎 — volleyball!) · The tooth is in the grass. (👎 — It isn't in the grass!) · The Magic Bike finds the tooth. (👍) · The tooth is under the bed. (👎 — under his pillow!). Re-point the frame to fix any wrong thumb.

Listen 2 — active (all respond). Play again; children do the story gestures at the right moments: “Good morning!” stretch (frame 1) · wobble finger (frame 2) · bounce volleyball (frames 3–4) · hands searching the grass/flowers (frame 5) · wash hands / “I'm hungry” (frame 6) · sparkle fingers magic bike (frames 7–8) · big thumbs-up You're incredible! CCQs between frames (see box above). Stop at frame 5: Is the tooth in the grass? — No! In the flowers? — No!

42–50′
Act out — drama
carpet, groups

Roles. Groups of 4–5: Jake (yellow badge, wobbly tooth) · Dad/Mum (blue, “Are you ready for breakfast?” / “Wash your hands!”) · 2 friends (red + green, play volleyball & search) · the Magic Bike + dog (purple, sparkle & “Woof!”). Model once with a strong group.

All groups act at the same time. Narrate the story slowly, frame by frame; every group plays it in their own spot (volleyball ON THE SPOT!): Good morning, Jake! Are you ready for breakfast? — Yes, I have breakfast every morning! · What's the matter? — I've got a wobbly tooth! · Let's play volleyball! — Wait for me! · I win! …Jake, where's your tooth? · Can you see it? — No, it isn't in the grass. · Come and wash your hands, we're hungry! · Look — the magic bike! It's looking for your tooth… · It's under your pillow! — Magic Bike, you're incredible!

Create (thinking) — make it yours. Before round 2: Jake loses ONE more thing — you choose! Where is it? Where does the magic bike find it? Each group adds a line: Where's my sock? — It isn't under the bed… it's under the pillow! (support: point to a card; extension: invent the whole exchange).

Swap roles, run it again — keep it to 2 rounds (Freeze! the moment energy tips). Volunteers: ONE group shows 30 sec for the class — big applause for the Magic Bike's last ride of the course. Values (PB p. 95 ex. 2): Look after your teeth! Brush your ___ before you go to bed. → teeth!

Support: quieter children get the “friend / search” role (short lines + gesture); a line card in hand. Extension: confident children play Jake / the Magic Bike and lead the exchange.
