Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. Assess Unit 4 language — food (chicken, eggs, chips, ice cream, salad, fish, cake…) & fruit (oranges, grapes, cherries, lemons, apples, bananas…), “I like / I don't like / I love…”, “My favourite food is…”, “Do you like…? — Yes, I do / No, I don't” — with the GMF1 Unit 4 Standard Level Test (Listening ×2 · Reading · Writing · Reading & Writing · Speaking), in a calm, confidence-first atmosphere.
  • Subsidiary. Jolly track — write words with ay (dictation; JP Book 2, p. 15): play, hay, tray, spray; tricky words also, of; exam micro-skills: work alone, listen twice, check your page.
  • Personal. Model-first: show every task type before the paper is out; no competitive games today; keep voice low and pace unhurried; no child ever sits with nothing to do (quiet poster task during Speaking turns). It's a real test before the winter break → protect confidence, finish on a high.
By the end, pupils can…
  • complete all written parts of the Unit 4 test independently;
  • answer the Speaking questions one-to-one (What's this? · Do you like…? · What's your favourite food?);
  • write short ay-words from dictation (play, hay, tray, spray);
  • leave smiling — the test is a “show what I know” day, not a scary day.

Structure
I like / I don't like (chips). · I love (ice cream). · My favourite food is (fish). · Do you like (grapes)? — Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
Form
Test answers: number · tick (✓) happy/sad face · match & circle · copy from the picture / word box · put the words in order · speak.
Pron. watch
Speaking part: accept clear meaning over perfect accent; gently remodel, don't correct mid-test. Watch grapes / cherries.
Phonics
Jolly focus: the ay sound /ai/ — play, hay, tray, spray (JP Book 2, p. 15). Tricky words: also, of.
Concept checks (ICQs) · Assumptions
Before the paper: Do we work alone or with a friend? → alone! Is it OK to not know one? → yes — skip it, come back!
Listening: How many times do we listen? → two times — first just listen, then number / tick.

Assumptions: all task types rehearsed in L6 (review + AB p. 46) and via the unit trainer at home; children write by copying from the pictures / word box; a happy face = I like, a sad face = I don't like — met all unit. Anxiety possible, hence breathing ritual + model-first + Speaky cheering, not judging.

Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → pupils stop & echo “Yes!”
“WiFi” 🖐 — say “WiFi” + raise a spread hand → pupils silently mirror & tune in.
Volume control — arm low all lesson = whisper day.
“Give me five” — eyes · ears · mouth · hands · feet.
Countdown — “Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down.”
Breathing — smell the flower 🌸 → blow the candle 🕯 ×3.
Differentiation
  • 💪 Support (needs more). Seat these children within arm's reach; read every instruction aloud again right beside them, point to the worked example, keep the picture / word box visible, and give extra time — hold the next listening item until their pencil moves. Praise effort, not speed.
  • ⭐ Extension (fast finishers). After the poster: a bonus “My favourite food” mini-poster (draw + label 3 more foods, add an “I like / I don't like” caption) or a page in the class picture book; still buzzing → the calm Jenga ritual below. Never “more test”, always create + apply.
Self-evaluation (thinking)

At the very end each child evaluates their own effort with a traffic-light card — 🟢 easy · 🟡 okay · 🔴 tricky:

Show me your light. What was EASY today? What was a bit TRICKY? 2–3 children name one thing each. No right/wrong — it tells YOU what to fold into the Unit 5 warm-ups.

Calm, non-competitive Jenga ritual (optional buffer): a fast finisher gently pulls one block → answers ONE Unit 4 question (What's this? / Do you like it?) → done. No tower race, no losers.

Quiet whole-class choral round — no teams, no points, no timer. A confidence warm-up: “we already know every one of these.”
Language
What's this? — It's (chicken)! Do you like it? — Yes, I do! One card up, everyone answers together, indoor voices, ~6–8 cards only.
Teacher
Slow, smiling: See? You know EVERY one. Easy-peasy — now let's show it on paper. Then close the board and hand out the test.

23–43′
UNIT 4 TEST — written parts
desks

Read every rubric aloud. They are emergent readers — before EACH exercise, read the instruction to the whole class and point to the example: Exercise 1 — listen and number. Like this. Re-read privately beside any child who needs it.

Listening (paced together). Ex. 1 Listen and number the food — steak/beef, chicken, eggs, ice cream, fruit, chips (Track 11) · ex. 2 Listen and tick 😀/🙁 — fruit a girl & a boy like / don't like: oranges, apple & orange & kiwi, grapes, cherries, lemons (Track 12). Play each track TWICE: first listen, pencils down; second time number / tick. Watch the room, pause between items.

Reading → Writing → Reading & Writing (own pace). Ex. 3 read & match, circle 😀/🙁 (My favourite food is fish · I like fruit · I love chicken · I don't like eggs · My favourite food is chips · I don't like salad) · ex. 4 look & write from the picture (I like eggs and chips · chicken and salad · cake) · ex. 5 read & order, write (Do you like grapes? · Yes, I do. · Do you like cherries? · No, I don't. · Do you like peaches? · Yes, I do.). Circulate silently; encourage with a nod, never with answers: Take your time. You're doing great.

Countdown at the end: Finishing in 5…4…3…2…1… pencils down. Collect written pages (keep the Speaking picture strip, p. 3).

Speaking (1-to-1) + quiet task

Whole class — quiet poster (no one waits empty). On each desk: A5 sheet — Draw your favourite food. Copy: “I like ______.” (caption frame on the board). Finished → whisper-share with a neighbour. Finisher ladder for the very fast: bonus “My favourite food” poster / a page in the class picture book → and, as a calm buffer, the non-competitive Jenga ritual (pull one block, answer ONE Unit 4 question, no winners).
