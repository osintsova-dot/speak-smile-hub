🦝 GenkiEnglish Preschool · Challenge · Lesson 20 — Vegetables — I like / I don’t like
Genki preschoolers (~5–6 y.o., non-readers) · 60 min · songs + TPR + games (no reading / writing / phonics) · chairs · carpet · tables · board (Genki app)
Challenge level · Lesson 20 of 8✨ veggies · I like / don’t like12 pupils · whole-group + shoulder-partner🎧 Bilingual L2/L1 · by ear
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, pupils will be better able to name 5 vegetables (onion, carrot, peas, potato, tomato) and express likes/dislikes — I like carrots! I don’t like onions!
  • Subsidiary. Cumulative Remember-time recycle of animals, numbers & Do you like…?; the S&S big-book; a veggie-soup cut-&-glue craft (Oksana’s real activity).
  • Personal. Bilingual L2/L1; veggies always in context (mmm/yuck faces); counting recycled in the soup game; every child asks/cooks every round; alternate ⚡/🧘.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name onion, carrot, peas, potato, tomato;
  • say I like… / I don’t like…;
  • ask & answer Do you like tomatoes?;
  • count veggies for the soup four potatoes!;
  • cut, glue & name veggies in a pan.

TPR gesture
I like rub tummy + “mmm” · I don’t like push away + “yuck”; each veggie = a mime (peel a potato, pop peas).
Pron. watch
vegetables /ˈvedʒtəbəlz/ (3 beats) · don’t crisp; keep mmm/yuck big & clear.
Instruction checks (kept tiny) · Assumptions
ICQ: “Like = which face?” → mmm/smile. “Don’t like?” → yuck.
ICQ: “Cut ON the lines?” → yes.

Assumptions: children know animals & Do you like…? from before → meaning from real-look veggies, faces, music & the L1 prompt. Non-readers/non-writers. Attention 5–7 min. Group = 12. Check food allergies before tasting talk.

Anticipated problems & solutions
Likely problemWhat you do
“I don’t like” forgottenPair every veggie with a clear mmm/yuck face; over-do the gesture, the song drills it.
Scissors tricky for somePre-score the lines; help hand-over-hand; praise any cut.
Everyone wants to grab the cardOnly the two runners grab; the rest ASK & cheer — give them the question job.
Muddling potato/tomatoSlow it, tap-count syllables, show both cards side by side.
Glue mess / over-excite🧘 calm table voice; one glue per pair; wet-wipes ready.
Classroom management — signals
“Class? — Yes!” — you: “Class!” → children stop & echo “Yes!”
Volume control 👋 — raise arm = louder, lower = quieter → instant settler.
“Speaky says…” — do the action only when Speaky the raccoon says it.
“Freeze!” / statue — everyone becomes a statue, mid-movement reset.
Countdown — “5… 4… 3… 2… 1…” with fingers shown.
“Come with me!” 🚊 — line up as a train (паровозик) to change zone.
Lesson framework — Oksana’s real Genki preschool stages

Practice-proven skeleton — reuse in every plan. Bilingual teacher talk (L2 English / L1 русская подсказка). Zones: chairs 🪑 / carpet 🌱 / tables 🪑 / board 📺 (Genki app). Finale = cumulative Q&A chain + the Thank You! song ritual.

  • Opening time 🚪 — door greeting (train), Hello / Good Morning song, “choose the greeting → jump/run/swim/fly to your chair”.
  • Warm up ⚡ — Disco Warm Up song; Speaky arrives on the bus-box & shakes hands with each child.
  • Remember time — cumulative Q&A of everything so far (thumb-up/down like a game), growing lesson by lesson, woven into today’s topic.
  • Focus time — presentation from the magic bus-box, Genki topic song, board app, active games.
  • Table time 🪑 — own folder worksheets, a different type each lesson (draw & tell / mini-craft / listen & tick / match соедини / spinner / sort & stick / count), always language-linked (listen → do → say).
  • Reading time 🌱 — our S&S big-book (Speaky), read actively with gestures & choral refrain.
  • Remember time 2 + Closing — run the growing Q&A chain, Thank You! + Goodbye song, exit ticket + reward sticker on the folder.
