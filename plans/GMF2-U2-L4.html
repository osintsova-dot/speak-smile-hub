Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will be better able to count 10–50 by tens and mixed and ask & answer about prices: “How much is this (lemon)? — It's (thirty) cents. / How much are these (carrots)? — They're (forty) cents.”
  • Subsidiary. Sing «How much is this?» (CD1 38); is/are split with singular/plural (builds on a/an/some); market role-play with paper coins; write prices (AB format).
  • Personal. -ty vs -teen is THE trap: exaggerate the stress difference every time; keep coin-handling rules tight before the market opens.
By the end, pupils can…
  • say the tens 10–50 and prices like twenty-six cents;
  • choose is/are with this/these correctly;
  • run a market exchange with real coin counting.

Structure
How much is this (lemon)? — It's (thirty) cents.  ·  How much are these (carrots)? — They're (forty-five) cents.
Form
this + is (one) / these + are (many) — echo of a/an vs some; numbers: twenty + one…nine composites.
Pron. watch
-ty stress on the FIRST syllable (THIRty) vs -teen on the last (thirTEEN) — drill as minimal pairs; forty (no u!).
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
“THIRty or thirTEEN — show fingers!” → 30 vs 13, stress tells us.
“These carrots — is or are?” → are (many!).

Assumptions: Numbers 1–20 known from GMF1/Starter review; tens are new. this/these singular/plural intuition seeded by a/an/some. Coin props are exciting — rules first. Группы: команда — максимум 4; при другой численности делим на команды по 3–4 (12=3×4 · 10=3+3+4 · 9=3×3 · 8=2×4 · 7=3+4).

36–47′
Market role-play
two stalls

Market opens! Two stalls with price-tagged food cards; each child gets coins (paper tens). Buy: How much are these peaches? — They're thirty cents. — Here you are! — Thank you! Count coins into the seller's cup.

Rotation. Sellers swap every 2–3 minutes — everyone sells AND buys.

💪 Support: frame card at the stall; round-ten prices. ⭐ Extension: haggling line: Twenty cents, please! — No, thirty!
