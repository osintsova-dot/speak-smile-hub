🦝 Give Me Five! 1 · Unit 6 “A day in the park” · Lesson 6 — Review: Playground & Where's/Where are? · 🏆 Flagship Quiz
Pre-A1 young learners (~7–8 y.o., emergent readers, continuing group) · 60 min · Consolidation + flagship team quiz with reward · carpet + desks
Week 22.02–28.02 · Lesson 46L6 Review🏆 Quiz day~12 pupils · 3 teams of 4
Aims & objectives
  • Main aim. By the end, learners will have consolidated all Unit 6 language — the playground objects (slide, swing, roundabout, seesaw, climbing frame) + nature/park words (grass, flower, tree, bush, rock, butterfly, bird), “Where's (Beth)? — He's / She's (on the swing)”, “Where are (Molly and Jake)? — They're (behind / in front of / next to / between / near) the (tree)” and the prepositions of place — and can use it confidently in the review tasks and the quiz.
  • Subsidiary. Jolly track — read the /or/ sound (storm, fork, sort, corn, horn) and recap the unit's /oa/ vowel family (coat, grow, rose); tricky words cover, love; self-assessment (My progress traffic light, AB p. 65 ex. 3); listening for detail (PB p. 68 ex. 1, CD3 17).
  • Personal. Run the flagship quiz by PIES: every child writes/shows every answer (Showdown on mini-boards), the responder is picked by Numbered Heads — no “captains answer everything”; keep the reward moment short so language time wins.
By the end, pupils can…
  • name all the playground / park objects and answer “Where's…?” without support;
  • ask & answer “Where's (Beth)? — He's/She's (on the swing)” and “Where are…? — They're (behind the tree)”;
  • use prepositions in / on / under / behind / in front of / next to / between / near correctly;
  • read words with the /or/ sound and blend simple CVC words;
  • self-assess with the traffic light — ready for the Unit 6 test.

Structure
Where's (Beth)? — He's / She's (on the swing). · Where are (Molly and Jake)? — They're (behind / in front of / next to / between / near) the (tree).
Form
Where + 's (is) → one person → He's / She's; Where + are → two+ → They're + preposition + the + noun.
Pron. watch
linking “He's on / She's in”; They're /ðeə/ (not “they-er”); clear /r/ in the /or/ words fork, corn.
Phonics
Jolly focus: /or/ sound (storm, fork, sort, corn, horn) + recap /oa/ family (coat · grow · rose). Tricky words: cover, love.
Concept checks (CCQs) · Assumptions
Put a toy behind a book: Where's the toy? Is it in front of or BEHIND? → behind → “It's behind the book.”
One child sits: One person — He's or They're? → He's. Two children: Two people — They're! (singular vs plural check).
Quiz rule ICQ: Who writes the answer — one child or EVERYBODY? → everybody, on their own board!

Assumptions: all Unit 6 language met in L1–L5; the /oa/ vowel family and CVC blending are done — today we read the new /or/ sound. Teams of 4 with numbered members (1–4) are routine. The flagship quiz with reward runs only on Review day — children know it's special.

Target language: /or/… blend it… corn!
Teacher: tap in rhythm, keep the arm-blend gesture; no projector → paper word cards work identically.

▶ Take Off–Touch Down (all move): Stand up if you hear /or/! Say a mix: fork · sun · storm · dog · corn · red · horn. Everyone stands / sits every time — nobody watches.

Review in motion
🎮 open space · teams

▶ Where's Beth? — Park Spy Hunt (everyone answers, nobody waits). Before the lesson 🦝 Speaky “hid” park picture-figures (Beth, Jake, a butterfly, a bird) on / behind / in front of / next to / between the “tree”, “bush”, “rock” (chairs + card props). Ask: Where's Beth? Think–Pair first: everyone whispers the answer to a neighbour. Then call a number → that child answers aloud: She's behind the tree! Class confirms chorally: Yes! She's behind the tree!

Mix singular & plural across 5–6 rounds to force the choice: Where's Jake? — He's on the rock! · Where are the birds? — They're in the tree! · Where are Beth and Jake? — They're next to the bush! Attention reset: Class? — Yes!

Support ▸ weaker pupils hold the picture card of the hidden figure + answer with a partner (echo the chunk).   Extension ▸ stronger pupils add a second clause — She's behind the tree, next to a flower.

Mechanic: Spinner → What's this? — It's a roundabout! · Find it! → point & say She's on the swing! / They're behind the tree! (whole class points at the screen every round).
Target language: What's this? / Where's…? / Where are…? — He's/She's/They're (prep) the…
Teacher: use as a warm station or when the real-prop round runs dry; keep it to 60–90 sec so movement stays the star.

33–51′
🏆 FLAGSHIP QUIZ with reward
🎮 teams · projector

Set-up (2 min). 3 teams of 4, numbered heads 1–4, one mini-whiteboard EACH. Quiz grid on screen — categories: Park objects 🎢 · Where's / Where are? 📍 · Sounds & spelling 🔤 · Class rules ✨. Rules + ICQ: Who writes? — Everybody! When do you show? — On “Showdown!”

Round flow (Kagan Showdown + Numbered Heads — all answer every question). Question up (e.g. picture half-hidden: What is it? · position: Where's Beth? — behind / in front of / next to? · number check: One girl or two — He's, She's or They're? · sound: Write the word — c-or-n! · read an /or/ word · class rule: We wait next to the… ?). Every child writes/draws → Showdown! → all boards up → call a number: Number 3! — that child of the playing team answers aloud in a full sentence.

Predict (thinking). On the half-hidden picture, before revealing: Look — just a corner! What do you THINK it is? Write your guess! Then reveal — Were you right? Turns each round into a little guess-and-check.

Reward shot. Correct = the answering child takes the shot: animated football penalty on screen — a GOAL = team points, class counts them in English: One, two, three… GOAL! (No projector → paper goal + soft ball, ≈ Mido №91 Bowling for Animal reskin.) Rotate teams; over ~10–12 questions every child shoots at least once.

Support ▸ word-box / picture bank stays visible on a side card so weaker writers copy, not freeze; accept one correct word from them.   Extension ▸ when a strong pupil is “Number”, require the full sentence — It's a roundabout. She's on the roundabout. — not just the noun.
🔨 Flagship interactive · projector · BUILD · “Unit 6 Team Quiz + Reward”  🔨 BUILD
Screen 1 — Grid: 4×3 board of numbered tiles under the 4 category headers (Park objects 🎢 / Where's–Where are? 📍 / Sounds & spelling 🔤 / Class rules ✨). Tap a tile → it flips to the question (picture / half-hidden object / audio word / /or/ word / rule prompt). Used tiles grey out.
Screen 2 — Answer: big “Show your boards!” + a 5-second timer bar; tap ✔ / ✗ to score; a full-sentence model appears for choral echo.
Screen 3 — Reward mini-game: football penalty — child “kicks”, goalie dives, animated GOAL (or a bowling reskin). One shot = +points.
Screen 4 — Scoreboard: 3 team bars, huge numbers readable from the back row, plus a “Teamwork ⭐” toggle for best cooperation.
Mechanic: pick tile → question → everyone writes → Showdown → Numbered Head answers → score → reward shot → rotate team. ~10–12 tiles ≈ 15 min.
Target language: all Unit 6 — What's this? / Where's…? — He's/She's… / Where are…? — They're (behind/in front of/next to/between/near) the… + read an /or/ word.
Teacher: host only — YOU tap tiles & call the number so no captain dominates. Spec is a self-contained HTML slide (S&S purple #6C3FC5 / honey #F5C842). Offline reserve: printed grid + paper goal + soft ball works identically.

Finish with totals + teamwork star: Give me five, champions!
